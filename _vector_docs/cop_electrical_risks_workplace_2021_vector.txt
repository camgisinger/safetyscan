CHUNK 1: Voltage Thresholds and Metallic Items Prohibition
Section: CoP s1.2, s1.3; ES Act s18, s30
Topic: Voltage definitions and prohibited metallic personal items during electrical work

VOLTAGE THRESHOLDS:
- Extra-low voltage: Not exceeding 50V AC or 120V ripple-free DC
- Low voltage: Exceeds extra-low voltage but does not exceed 1000V AC RMS or 1500V ripple-free DC
- High voltage: Above 1000V AC RMS or 1500V DC
- Serious consequences can occur from contact with as little as 50V AC or 120V DC
- Fault current can flow at up to 20 TIMES the rated current of the supply transformer

PROHIBITED ITEMS:
- Workers MUST NOT wear metallic personal items (watches, jewellery, body piercings) while working near exposed energised parts
- Workers MUST NOT wear metal spectacle frames during electrical work near live parts

FIRE EXTINGUISHER PROHIBITION:
- Water, foam, and wet chemical extinguishers MUST NOT be used on electrical fires
- CO2 or dry powder extinguishers must be used on electrical fires

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing watch, rings, or visible piercings near exposed energised parts = non-compliant
- Worker wearing metal-framed glasses during live electrical work = non-compliant
- Water or foam fire extinguisher as the only extinguisher near electrical equipment = non-compliant
- Conductive material (concrete, wet timber, water) providing a path to earth near exposed parts = high-risk indicator

CHUNK 2: Cable Management and Work Area Security
Section: CoP s1.3
Topic: Physical cable protection and work area isolation requirements

CABLE MANAGEMENT:
- Leads or flexible cables running across floors, through doorways, or over sharp edges MUST have physical protection
- Unfinished electrical work areas MUST be tagged and taped off

TEST EQUIPMENT:
- Test instruments with cracked or broken insulation on leads, probes or clips = unsafe test gear
- Conductive tools (metallic tape measures, rulers, wire-reinforced ladders) MUST NOT be used near exposed energised parts

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Leads or flexible cables running across floors, through doorways, or over sharp edges without protection = non-compliant cable management
- Unfinished electrical work areas not tagged and taped off = non-compliant
- Test instruments with cracked or broken insulation on leads, probes or clips = unsafe test gear
- Conductive tools (metallic tape measures, rulers, wire-reinforced ladders) near exposed energised parts = non-compliant
- Unlicensed worker performing wiring connections = primary duty breach

CHUNK 3: Unsafe Equipment Disconnection and Test Tags
Section: CoP s3.1, s3.2; ES Regulation s101, s194
Topic: Mandatory disconnection of unsafe equipment and test tag requirements

UNSAFE EQUIPMENT — MUST be:
- DISCONNECTED OR ISOLATED from its electricity supply immediately
- NOT RECONNECTED until repaired or tested by a competent person and found safe, or replaced or permanently removed

TEST AND TAG INTERVALS (ES Regulation s194):
- Construction and demolition sites: most frequent interval
- Manufacturing environments: regular interval
- Amusement industries: regular interval
- Service and office environments: least frequent interval
- Hire equipment: MUST be inspected and tested before use

NEW EQUIPMENT:
- Does not have to be tested before first use but SHOULD BE VISUALLY INSPECTED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Electrical equipment labelled "UNSAFE" or "OUT OF SERVICE" but still connected to power = regulatory breach
- Missing test tags on equipment in construction or manufacturing environment = non-compliance
- Equipment with visible discolouration, broken insulation, or loose cord anchors = unsafe indicator
- Test tag date expired = equipment overdue for inspection
- No test tag present on portable electrical equipment on construction site = non-compliant

CHUNK 4: Safety Switches (RCDs) — Performance Thresholds
Section: CoP s3.3; AS/NZS 3190
Topic: RCD performance specifications and faulty device tagging (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

CLASSES OF SAFETY SWITCHES:
- Type I: Residual current rating NOT EXCEEDING 10 MILLIAMPS; tripping time WITHIN 40 MILLISECONDS (used for equipment directly connected to people)
- Type II: Residual current rating GREATER THAN 10 MILLIAMPS BUT NOT EXCEEDING 30 MILLIAMPS; tripping time WITHIN 300 MILLISECONDS

FAULTY DEVICES — MUST:
- Have a DURABLE TAG IMMEDIATELY ATTACHED warning people not to use the safety switch
- Be IMMEDIATELY WITHDRAWN FROM USE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Safety switch without a "Test" or "Trip" button = non-compliant device
- Non-portable socket outlet protected by an RCD not labelled "Safety Switch Protected" = identification failure
- Faulty RCD in use without a warning tag = serious safety breach
- No RCD visible on portable electrical equipment in use on construction site = non-compliant
- RCD board without individual circuit labels = poor practice

CHUNK 5: Isolation Verification and Locking Off
Section: CoP s4.1, s5.1; ES Act s55; ES Regulation s13–16
Topic: Test for dead principle and isolation point security

MANDATORY PRINCIPLE: "TEST FOR DEAD BEFORE YOU TOUCH" — apply at all times.

ASSUMPTION: ALL conductors and electrical components MUST BE ASSUMED energised until proven de-energised.

SECURING ISOLATION:
- High-voltage: Each high-voltage exposed part MUST BE EARTHED after being de-energised
- Locking off: Isolation points should be fitted with control mechanisms (hasps, locks) to prevent inadvertent re-energising
- Each worker should apply their own lock

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using a panel voltmeter as the sole method to determine if a part is de-energised = unsafe/non-compliant method
- Danger tag or lock MISSING from an isolation point while work is in progress = serious isolation failure
- Out-of-service or caution tags not removed by a competent person after repair = procedure failure
- Multiple workers on same isolation point with only one lock = inadequate isolation
- Isolation board with no locks or tags visible while electrical work is in progress = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Energised Work Prohibition and "Near" Definition
Section: CoP s6.1, s6.2; ES Regulation s12, s14, s19
Topic: Live work prohibition and proximity threshold (KEY MEASUREMENT)

PROHIBITION: Work MUST NOT be carried out on or near energised electrical equipment UNLESS:
- It is necessary in the interests of health and safety
- It is necessary for the work to be carried out properly
- It is necessary for testing under section 15(1)
- There is no reasonable alternative means

PERFORMING WORK FOR CONVENIENCE ALONE IS ILLEGAL.

"NEAR" DEFINITION: Near, in relation to electrical equipment, means WITHIN 3 METRES of an exposed energised part of the equipment.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Electrical work being performed within 3 metres of live parts without an SWMS on site = regulatory breach
- Worker performing live work alone without safety observer = non-compliant (see Chunk 7)
- No risk assessment record available for live electrical work = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Energised Work Planning and Area Clearance
Section: CoP s6.3; ES Regulation s22
Topic: Mandatory planning requirements before live work commences

PLANNING REQUIREMENTS — before energised work commences:
- A RISK ASSESSMENT must be conducted by a competent person and RECORDED
- The area must be CLEAR OF OBSTRUCTIONS for easy access and exit
- The isolation point must be CLEARLY MARKED OR LABELLED and CAPABLE OF BEING OPERATED QUICKLY
- The work must be carried out in accordance with a SAFE WORK METHOD STATEMENT (SWMS)

SAFETY BARRIERS:
- Safety barrier made of conductive material MUST be correctly earthed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Isolation point blocked by equipment or materials = obstruction breach
- No risk assessment record available for recent live electrical work = non-compliant
- Safety barrier made of conductive material that is not correctly earthed = non-compliant
- No SWMS on site during live electrical work = regulatory breach

CHUNK 8: Safety Observer Requirements and Competency
Section: CoP s6.3; ES Regulation s22
Topic: Mandatory safety observer presence and competency thresholds (KEY MEASUREMENT)

MANDATORY OBSERVER: Energised electrical work MUST be carried out WITH A SAFETY OBSERVER PRESENT.

OBSERVER COMPETENCY REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be competent to implement control measures AND rescue and resuscitate the worker
- Must have been assessed as competent to rescue and resuscitate a person IN THE PREVIOUS 12 MONTHS

EXEMPTION: Safety observer not required if:
- Work consists ONLY OF TESTING; AND
- Risk assessment shows NO SERIOUS RISK

OBSERVER DUTIES:
- MUST NOT carry out any other work or function that compromises their role
- MUST NOT be situated in the work basket of an elevating work platform (EWP)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Safety observer helping with manual electrical work instead of watching the operator = duty compromise
- Safety observer standing in the same EWP basket as the worker = compliance failure
- No safety observer present during live fault-finding where a serious risk is present = violation
- Safety observer who cannot demonstrate current rescue/resuscitation competency = non-compliant
- Safety observer using a mobile phone while worker is performing live work = duty compromise

CHUNK 9: Test Instrument Ratings and Proximity Voltage Testers
Section: CoP s7.2; ES Regulation s22, s23
Topic: Test instrument category ratings and prohibited sole-use devices

TEST INSTRUMENT RATINGS:
- Instruments should be rated CATEGORY III OR IV to enable use on all parts of the equipment
- Must be checked to prove functioning correctly IMMEDIATELY BEFORE AND AFTER a test

PROXIMITY VOLTAGE TESTER PROHIBITION:
- A proximity voltage tester MUST NOT be used as the only tool to prove de-energisation — not reliable

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using a proximity voltage tester as the only tool to prove de-energisation = unsafe and non-compliant
- Test instrument without visible category rating (CAT III or CAT IV) = questionable suitability
- Worker not checking test instrument before and after use = procedure failure

CHUNK 10: PPE and Clothing Requirements
Section: CoP s7.4; ES Regulation s22
Topic: Mandatory PPE specifications and prohibited materials

PPE REQUIREMENTS — must be suitable for the work, properly tested, and maintained in good working order:
- Clothing: Use NON-SYNTHETIC, FLAME RESISTANT clothing of non-fusible material
- Clothing containing METAL THREADS MUST NOT BE WORN
- Metallic, wire reinforced ladders MUST be avoided near electrical hazards
- Conductive tools MUST NOT be used near live parts

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Metallic or wire-reinforced ladder in use near electrical switchboards = dangerous/to be avoided
- Worker wearing synthetic clothing (e.g. polyester shirt) during live electrical work = PPE failure
- Worker wearing clothing with visible metallic threads near live parts = non-compliant
- Conductive tools in use near exposed energised parts = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: Record Retention Periods
Section: CoP s7.5; ES Regulation s22, s23
Topic: Minimum retention periods for electrical work documentation (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

RECORD RETENTION:
- Risk assessment: Keep until at least 28 DAYS AFTER THE WORK is completed
- SWMS: Keep until THE WORK IS COMPLETED
- Serious incidents: If a serious incident occurs, keep assessment and SWMS for at least 2 YEARS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No records of risk assessment available for recent live electrical work = non-compliant
- SWMS discarded before work completion = non-compliant
- Records of serious incident work discarded within 2 years = regulatory breach
