CHUNK 1: Facility Maintenance — Visible Condition
Section: s1.3; WHS Act s19
Topic: Observable maintenance standards for facilities and consumables

MANDATORY MAINTENANCE:
- Work environment MUST BE MAINTAINED in a clean and safe condition (s1.3)
- Broken or damaged furniture, fixtures and fittings (chairs, plumbing, lighting) must be REPLACED OR REPAIRED PROMPTLY
- Consumable items including SOAP AND TOILET PAPER must be replenished regularly

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No soap or toilet paper in facilities = maintenance failure
- Broken plumbing, flickering lights, damaged chairs left unrepaired = non-compliant
- Unclean amenities with visible dirt or waste = maintenance duty breach
- Facilities locked or inaccessible during work hours = access breach

CHUNK 2: Aisles, Walkways and Route Marking — Exact Dimensions
Section: s2.1, s2.2; WHS Regulation s40
Topic: Minimum aisle widths and marking specifications (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

AISLES AND WALKWAYS:
- Minimum width: AT LEAST 600mm WIDE (s2.1)
- Must be kept FREE OF OBSTRUCTIONS at all times

ROUTE MARKING:
- Where routes require definition, boundaries must be marked by a PERMANENT LINE
- Line colour: white, yellow, or other contrasting colour
- Minimum line width: AT LEAST 50mm WIDE (s2.2)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Aisles measured at less than 600mm wide = non-compliant
- Marked walkways using lines narrower than 50mm = non-compliant
- Trailing cables, loose carpet tiles, or debris in walkways = obstruction breach
- Aisles blocked by stored materials or equipment = non-compliant
- No route marking where needed to define safe paths = marking failure

CHUNK 3: Floor Surfaces and Anti-Fatigue Matting
Section: s2.4
Topic: Floor design and protection for static standing work

FLOORS:
- Must be designed, installed and maintained to allow work to be carried out safely (s2.4)
- Workers doing STATIC STANDING WORK must be protected from direct contact with concrete or steel floors

PROTECTION METHODS:
- Carpet, ANTI-FATIGUE MATTING, or duckboards must be provided

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers standing on bare concrete for prolonged periods without matting = non-compliant
- No anti-fatigue matting at static workstations on concrete or steel floors = ergonomic breach
- Damaged or worn matting with trip hazards = maintenance failure
- Matting not covering full standing work area = inadequate protection

CHUNK 4: Lighting Levels — Exact Lux Requirements by Task
Section: s2.5, Table 1
Topic: Minimum illumination levels for different work tasks (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY ILLUMINATION LEVELS (Table 1) — minimum lux by task type:
- Movement and orientation (corridors, tunnels, storerooms): 40 lux
- Rough intermittent work (locker rooms, loading bays, plant rooms): 80 lux
- Simple tasks (waiting rooms, canteens, rough bench work): 160 lux
- Ordinary/moderately easy tasks (continuously occupied interiors, general offices): 240 lux
- Difficult tasks (drawing boards, fine painting, inspection benches): 600 lux
- Very difficult tasks (fine manufacture, colour matching, intricate inspection): 800 lux

EMERGENCY LIGHTING MUST BE PROVIDED for safe evacuation (s2.5).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lighting levels visibly inadequate for the task being performed = insufficient illumination
- Significant glare or reflections on work surfaces = lighting design failure
- Emergency lighting not visible in corridors or near exits = non-compliant
- Burnt-out lights in work areas left unreplaced = maintenance failure
- Detail work (inspection, drawing) performed in poorly lit areas = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Ventilation Openings and Air Movement — Exact Percentages and Speeds
Section: s2.7, s2.8
Topic: Natural ventilation area and air speed requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

NATURAL VENTILATION:
- Permanent openings must total AT LEAST 5 PER CENT OF THE FLOOR AREA of the room (s2.7)

AIR MOVEMENT:
- Enclosed workplaces must be supplied with air movement rates BETWEEN 0.1 m/s AND 0.2 m/s (s2.7)

THERMAL COMFORT:
- Optimum comfort for SEDENTARY WORK: BETWEEN 20 AND 26 DEGREES CELSIUS (s2.8)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ventilation openings totalling less than 5% of floor area = natural ventilation gap
- Workplace temperature consistently above 26°C for sedentary tasks = thermal comfort breach
- No mechanical ventilation in enclosed workspace without adequate natural ventilation = non-compliant
- Workers visibly overheated or wearing excessive clothing indoors = thermal comfort indicator
- Air-conditioning units visibly not maintained or serviced = ventilation system gap

CHUNK 6: Drinking Water Temperature
Section: s3.2; WHS Regulation s41
Topic: Maximum drinking water temperature (KEY MEASUREMENT)

DRINKING WATER:
- MUST be provided FREE OF CHARGE at all times (s3.2)
- Temperature must be AT OR BELOW 24 DEGREES CELSIUS (s3.2)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Drinking water temperature above 24°C = supply standard failure
- No drinking water available at remote or outdoor work sites = non-compliant
- Water dispensers empty or not functioning = access failure
- No cups or drinking vessels available = provision gap

CHUNK 7: Toilet Provision Ratios — Exact Numbers
Section: s3.3; WHS Regulation s41
Topic: Mandatory toilet-to-worker ratios by gender (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

SEPARATE MALE TOILETS:
- 1 CLOSET PAN PER 20 MALES (s3.3)
- 1 URINAL PER 25 MALES (s3.3)

SEPARATE FEMALE TOILETS:
- 1 CLOSET PAN PER 15 FEMALES (s3.3)

UNISEX TOILETS:
- Allowed ONLY if total workforce is 10 OR FEWER and there are TWO OR FEWER WORKERS OF ONE GENDER (s3.3)

LOCATION:
- In multistorey buildings, toilets must be located on AT LEAST EVERY SECOND FLOOR (s3.3)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Multi-storey building with toilets only on ground floor = accessibility breach
- Workplace with mixed-gender workforce of more than 10 using single unisex toilet = ratio failure
- Insufficient toilets for workforce size based on ratios above = non-compliant
- Toilets locked or inaccessible during work hours = access breach

CHUNK 8: Washing Facilities and Dining Space — Exact Ratios and Areas
Section: s3.4, s3.5
Topic: Hand basin ratios and dining area space per person (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

HAND WASHING:
- Minimum ratio: ONE WASH BASIN FOR EVERY 30 WORKERS (male and female calculated separately) (s3.4)
- Hand washing facilities must NOT be used for work processes (e.g. washing paint brushes or tools) (s3.4)

DINING AREAS:
- Must provide 1m² OF CLEAR SPACE for each person likely to use the area at any one time (s3.5)
- The 1m² is calculated FREE OF FURNITURE AND OBSTRUCTIONS (s3.5)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Dining room for 20 people measuring less than 20m² of clear space (excluding furniture) = space deficiency
- Hand washing basins being used to wash tools, paint brushes, or work equipment = sanitation breach
- No hand washing facilities near toilets = hygiene failure
- Dining area without refrigeration for food storage = hygiene gap
- Workers eating in chemical handling or dusty areas without separate clean dining space = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Change Rooms and Locker Spacing — Exact Dimensions
Section: s3.7
Topic: Minimum space per worker and locker row spacing (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

CHANGE ROOMS:
- Must allow CLEAR SPACE OF NO LESS THAN 0.5m² for each worker changing at any one time (s3.7)

LOCKER SPACING:
- Row spacing: AT LEAST 1800mm BETWEEN ROWS FACING EACH OTHER (s3.7)
- AT LEAST 900mm between lockers and a seat or wall (s3.7)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Locker rows separated by less than 1800mm = non-compliant layout
- Less than 900mm between lockers and seat or wall = spacing breach
- Change room with less than 0.5m² clear space per worker = space deficiency
- Lockers blocking access to exits or facilities = obstruction breach

CHUNK 10: Shower Facilities — Exact Ratios and Dimensions
Section: s3.8
Topic: Shower provision for dirty or hazardous work (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

SHOWER REQUIREMENT:
- Required for dirty, hot, or hazardous work (e.g. welding, foundry, asbestos, lead risk) (s3.8)

SHOWER RATIOS AND DIMENSIONS:
- Ratio: ONE SHOWER CUBICLE FOR EVERY 10 WORKERS who may need to shower (s3.8)
- Floor area: Each shower must have A FLOOR AREA OF NOT LESS THAN 1.8m² (s3.8)
- Partitions: AT LEAST 1650mm HIGH and NO MORE THAN 300mm ABOVE THE FLOOR (s3.8)
- A separate dressing area with a seat must be provided for each shower (s3.8)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Shower floor area less than 1.8m² = dimension failure
- Shower partitions less than 1650mm high = privacy and design breach
- Partitions more than 300mm above floor = non-compliant
- No showers on site where workers regularly do hazardous or dirty work = non-compliant
- Shower ratio inadequate for number of workers requiring decontamination = non-compliant
- No separate dressing area or seat provided = facility breach

CHUNK 11: Emergency Evacuation Drills — Frequency Requirement
Section: s5.1; WHS Regulation s43
Topic: Mandatory evacuation drill interval (KEY MEASUREMENT)

EVACUATION DRILLS:
- Must be carried out AT LEAST EVERY 12 MONTHS (s5.1)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Training records showing last evacuation drill was more than 12 months ago = drill frequency breach
- Emergency exits or paths to assembly points blocked by stock or equipment = life-safety breach
- Fire extinguishers with expired inspection tags = maintenance failure
- Assembly point not clearly signed or visible from exit points = non-compliant
- Emergency exit signs not illuminated = non-compliant
- No documented site plan showing fire equipment and exits displayed = information provision failure
