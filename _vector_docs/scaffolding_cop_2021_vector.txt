CHUNK 1: Scaffolding Height Thresholds and Licence Requirements
Section: CoP s1.1; WHS Regulation s289
Topic: Height thresholds triggering high risk work licences and SWMS requirements

MANDATORY:
- Fall risk MORE THAN 4 METRES from scaffold platform or structure = high risk work licence (basic, intermediate or advanced) MANDATORY for all scaffolders
- Fall risk MORE THAN 2 METRES = high risk construction work; SWMS MANDATORY before work starts
- Scaffold plan MANDATORY for scaffolding work ABOVE 4 METRES (measured from top working platform to ground)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding where workers or loads could fall more than 4m with no licensed scaffolder = serious non-compliance
- Scaffolding work with fall risk exceeding 2m with no SWMS on site = serious non-compliance
- Scaffold above 4m TWP with no scaffold plan visible on site = non-compliance
- Scaffold in use with no handover certificate available = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Design Registration and Engineer Requirements
Section: CoP s2.2; WHS Regulation s243; Table 1
Topic: Design registration for prefabricated scaffolding and mandatory engineer verification thresholds

DESIGN REGISTRATION MANDATORY:
- PREFABRICATED SCAFFOLDING (modular, tower, cantilever, hung, suspended) MUST be design registered
- Design registration number must be kept READILY ACCESSIBLE on site

ENGINEER VERIFICATION MANDATORY for:
- Tube and coupler scaffold GREATER THAN 33m TWP or outside AS/NZS 1576.6 scope
- Cantilevered or spurred scaffold GREATER THAN 6m HIGH
- Bridging beams/truss/ladder beams GREATER THAN 4.8m SPAN or 6m of scaffold above
- Hung or drop scaffolds
- Independent free-standing or guyed scaffold towers GREATER THAN 4m TWP
- Crane lifted scaffold
- Access birdcages with cladding OR MORE THAN 20m TWP
- Mobile scaffold GREATER THAN 4m TWP (excluding standard aluminium mobile)
- Stair tower independent or attached MORE THAN 20m HIGH
- Gantry or overhead protection structures
- Loading bays GREATER THAN 9m TWP or 2 TONNES
- Suspended scaffold (swing-stage and supports)
- Perimeter demolition scaffold MORE THAN 9m HIGH
- Public access structures requiring BCA compliance

ENGINEER DESIGN VERIFICATION AND INITIAL INSPECTION (both required):
- Loading bays >9m TWP or 2T
- Suspended scaffold (swing-stage and supports)
- Perimeter demolition scaffold >9m high
- Public access structures requiring BCA compliance

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Prefabricated scaffold with no design registration number available = non-compliance
- Tube and coupler scaffold exceeding 33m with no engineer verification = serious non-compliance
- Suspended scaffold, demolition scaffold >9m, or loading bay >9m/2T with no engineer sign-off = serious non-compliance
- Scaffold constructed outside manufacturer's documented parameters without engineer certification = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Foundations — Soleboards and Baseplates
Section: CoP s2.3.3
Topic: Soleboard and baseplate specifications and placement requirements

SOLEBOARD MINIMUM SPECIFICATIONS (for soft ground):
- Minimum length: 500mm
- Minimum width: 225mm
- Minimum thickness: 30mm hardwood timber
- Must be LEVEL
- No part of baseplate or adjustable base may PROTRUDE OVER THE SIDE of soleboard

SOLEBOARDS GENERALLY NOT REQUIRED on firm surfaces such as concrete.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold erected on soft or suspect ground without soleboards = non-compliance
- Soleboards smaller than 500mm long x 225mm wide x 30mm thick timber (or uncertified substitute) = non-compliance
- Baseplate protrusion over edge of soleboard = non-compliance
- Scaffold erected on awnings, suspended floors, parapets without engineer certification = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Tying Requirements and Spacing
Section: CoP s2.4.1; AS/NZS 1576.1
Topic: Scaffold tie installation requirements and spacing rules

TIE INSTALLATION TRIGGER:
- Tie MUST BE INSTALLED when unclad scaffold reaches THREE TIMES THE MINIMUM BASE WIDTH (unless design documentation specifies otherwise)
- Cladded scaffolds may require ties at LOWER HEIGHT

TIE CONFIGURATION:
- Ties must INTERCONNECT WITH BOTH INNER AND OUTER SCAFFOLD STANDARDS (unless engineer or manufacturer specifies otherwise)
- Single leg ties must have SAFETY COUPLER on tie behind standard
- Tie tubes connected via bracket must have SAFETY COUPLER at bracket connection point

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold reaching or exceeding 3x base width with no ties installed = serious non-compliance
- Ties removed or loosened by other trades without authorisation = non-compliance; flag immediately
- Cladded scaffold with ties at same spacing as uncladded version = flag (likely insufficient)
- Tie spacing not matching scaffold plan = non-compliance
- Single leg ties without safety coupler behind standard = non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Post-Installed Anchor Specifications
Section: CoP s2.4.1
Topic: Acceptable anchor types, working load limits and verification requirements

ACCEPTABLE ANCHOR TYPES ONLY:
- Chemical anchors
- Screw bolts
- Coil expansion anchors
- Torque-controlled expansion anchors

DO NOT USE:
- Drop-in (impact setting) anchors
- Deformation-controlled expansion anchors

WORKING LOAD LIMIT:
- HORIZONTAL TIE ANCHORS: ≥ 6kN
- ALL OTHER ANCHORS: ≥ three times maximum calculated applied load

VERIFICATION MANDATORY:
- ALL post-installed anchors must be installed using TORQUE WRENCH set to appropriate torque (unless anchor has inbuilt torque indicator)
- DOCUMENTED VERIFICATION must be kept on site stating: anchor setting torque or installation procedure; date of installation; location; name of competent person installing
- INDIVIDUAL PROOF TESTING (100% of design load) of ALL chemical anchors must be undertaken and documented

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Drop-in or deformation-controlled expansion anchors used for ties = serious non-compliance
- No documentation of torque wrench settings or proof testing for chemical anchors = non-compliance
- Anchors installed without torque wrench = non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Perimeter Containment Screening — Mesh and Gap Specifications
Section: CoP s2.5; WHS Regulation s315E, s315F, s315G
Topic: Mesh opening sizes, prescribed lining requirements and maximum gap dimensions

MESH MAXIMUM OPENINGS:

SQUARE OR RECTANGLE MESH:
- WITH prescribed lining: maximum 50mm x 50mm openings; minimum 2.5mm gauge
- WITHOUT prescribed lining: maximum 25mm x 25mm openings; minimum 2.5mm gauge
- WITHOUT prescribed lining (rectangular): maximum 25mm x 50mm openings; minimum 2.5mm gauge

NOT SQUARE OR RECTANGLE MESH:
- WITH prescribed lining: maximum 50mm in any direction; minimum 2.5mm gauge
- WITHOUT prescribed lining: maximum 25mm in any direction; minimum 2.5mm gauge

PRESCRIBED LINING:
- Intact shade cloth or lining with mean bursting pressure AT LEAST 1000kPa (tested per AS 2001.2.4)
- Must be attached to INSIDE of mesh

MAXIMUM GAP DIMENSIONS (MUST NOT EXCEED 25mm):
- Gap measured HORIZONTALLY between screens immediately beside each other
- Gap measured VERTICALLY between screen and screen immediately above it
- Gap measured VERTICALLY OR HORIZONTALLY between screen and framework supporting it

INSTALLATION RULE:
- Containment sheeting installed NO HIGHER THAN UPPERMOST TIE (unless engineer certified otherwise)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Mesh openings larger than specified maximum for lining type present = non-compliance
- Gaps between screens or between screen and framework exceeding 25mm = non-compliance
- Containment sheeting installed above uppermost tie without engineer certification = non-compliance
- Missing or torn prescribed lining on mesh requiring it = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Working Platform Planks — Gaps, Lapping and Securing
Section: CoP s2.6; AS 1577; AS/NZS 1576.1
Topic: Scaffold plank gap limits, lapping rules and securing requirements

PLANK GAP LIMITS:
- NO SINGLE GAP between planks may exceed 25mm
- TOTAL GAP BETWEEN ALL PLANKS must not exceed 50mm

LAPPING RULES:
- Planks MUST NOT be lapped on STRAIGHT RUNS of modular and tube and coupler scaffolding
- Planks MAY be lapped on HANGING BRACKET scaffolds

LAPPED PLANKS (corners) may be unsecured ONLY if ALL of the following are met:
- TIMBER lapped over METAL planks
- Planks are 1.2 METRES LONG OR GREATER
- Plank overlap past edge of plank underneath is 300mm OR GREATER
- STANDARDS prevent planks from moving sideways

PLYWOOD SHEETS (to cover gaps):
- Minimum 17mm THICK
- Only for gaps LESS THAN 500mm WIDE (unless engineer approved)
- Must be SECURED

METAL PLANKS LAPPED ON METAL PLANKS:
- Must be secured using PROPRIETARY FIXING or STEEL STRAPPING
- Other non-structurally rated systems MUST NOT be used

PLASTIC ZIP TIES:
- ONLY for NON-LOAD BEARING situations
- MUST NOT be used in structural scaffolding applications

PLANK OVERHANG ON PUTLOGS:
- GREATER THAN 150mm but LESS THAN 250mm

CYCLONE-PRONE AREAS (north of Bundaberg):
- All planks must be SECURED AGAINST UPLIFT during cyclone season

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Single gap between planks exceeding 25mm = non-compliance
- Total gap between all planks exceeding 50mm = non-compliance
- Planks lapped on straight runs of modular or tube and coupler scaffold = non-compliance
- Metal planks lapped on metal planks without proprietary fixing or steel strapping = non-compliance
- Zip ties used in load-bearing structural positions = non-compliance
- Plank overhang on putlogs outside 150mm–250mm range = non-compliance
- Planks in cyclone-prone area not secured against uplift = flag

CHUNK 8: Hop-Up Brackets and Tie Bars
Section: CoP s2.6
Topic: Hop-up bracket tie bar requirements and plank support rules

HOP-UP BRACKET TIE BARS:
- All hop-up brackets (except SINGLE BOARD type) must be provided with TIE BARS to stop brackets spreading apart (unless manufacturer specifies otherwise)
- No single inadvertent force should dislodge tie bar pin or any part of tie bar

HOP-UP PLANK SUPPORT:
- Where hop-up planks supported by bracket on ONE END ONLY: opposite side support method must be APPROVED BY SCAFFOLD DESIGNER
- Where scaffold components altered to fix hop-up bracket: ENGINEER must approve alterations
- All alternative support methods must ensure plank remains CAPTIVE in hop-up bracket

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hop-up brackets without tie bars (unless single board type or manufacturer specified) = non-compliance
- Hop-up plank supported on one end only without designer-approved opposite support = non-compliance

CHUNK 9: Access and Egress — Stairway Specifications
Section: CoP s2.7.1, s2.7.1.1; WHS Regulation s40; AS/NZS 1576.1
Topic: Temporary stairway dimensional requirements and step height limits

TEMPORARY STAIRWAY DIMENSIONS:
- Rise range: 150mm ≤ R ≤ 225mm
- Going range: 175mm ≤ G ≤ 355mm
- Combination: 540 ≤ 2R + G ≤ 700
- Minimum tread depth: 175mm
- Maximum tread gap: 30mm

STRETCHER ACCESS STAIRWAY (additional requirements):
- Slope to horizontal: 45° or less
- Treads: at least 1000mm WIDE
- Landing length in direction of travel: at least 1200mm; width not less than stairway width
- Upper surface of external handrail or guardrail: at least 1400mm above landing or top of each stair tread

STEP HEIGHT CHANGE AT ACCESS/EGRESS POINT:
- Any DIFFERENCE IN STEP HEIGHT from scaffold stair to access/egress point must be NO MORE THAN 300mm
- Note: Applies to STAND-ALONE SCAFFOLDING where erection commences 12 months after 1 July 2021

SECOND MEANS OF ACCESS AND EGRESS REQUIRED when ALL apply:
- External perimeter scaffold with TWP MORE THAN 8m HIGH (generally four lifts from lowest ledger), AND
- Length MORE THAN 60 LINEAL METRES
- At least ONE means must be SUITABLE FOR EMERGENCY STRETCHER ACCESS
- Exception: does NOT include detached dwellings

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Stair rise outside 150mm–225mm range = non-compliance
- Stair going outside 175mm–355mm range = non-compliance
- Step height change at access/egress point exceeding 300mm (stand-alone scaffold post July 2022) = non-compliance
- External perimeter scaffold >8m TWP and >60 lineal metres with only one means of access/egress = non-compliance
- No stretcher-suitable access for scaffold meeting >8m and >60m criteria = non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Ladder Access Requirements
Section: CoP s2.7.1.2
Topic: Ladder access bay configuration and trap door requirements

LADDER ACCESS BAY:
- Ladders should be within SEPARATE LADDER ACCESS BAY wherever space permits
- If access bay is part of working platform: TRAP DOOR must be provided (except during erection/dismantling or where other controls prevent inadvertent falls)
- Trap door must remain CLOSED while working from platform
- SWING ARM OR GATE SYSTEM should be installed to provide safe access and stop person falling back through access bay

LADDER SETUP:
- Must be set up on FIRM, LEVEL SURFACE
- MUST NOT be used on scaffold bays to gain extra height

WORKERS MUST NOT climb over or under components to gain access (except on mobile scaffolds or where TWP is 2.5m or less).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ladder trap door not present on working platform access = non-compliance
- Trap door left open while workers on platform = non-compliance
- Ladder set up on scaffold bays to gain height = non-compliance
- Workers climbing over or under components to access platform (TWP >2.5m, non-mobile) = non-compliance

CHUNK 11: Overhead Electric Lines — Exclusion Zones
Section: CoP s3.1; Electrical Safety Regulation 2013 s68, s69
Topic: Exclusion zone requirements for scaffolding near energised overhead electric lines

PRIORITY CONTROL:
- DE-ENERGISING or RE-ROUTING electricity supply = most effective control; MUST BE CONSIDERED FIRST
- Obtain WRITTEN CONFIRMATION from person in control of electric line BEFORE work if lines de-energised

EXCLUSION ZONE (if de-energising not reasonably practicable):
- PCBU MUST ENSURE people, scaffold parts, equipment and materials DO NOT ENTER exclusion zone around overhead electric line
- Distance specified in Schedule 2 of ES Regulation (electricity entity may specify GREATER distance)
- Allow for SWAY OR SAG of electric lines (wind causes sway; temperature affects sag)

SAFETY OBSERVER ('SPOTTER'):
- MANDATORY if possibility exists that person or scaffold part could enter exclusion zone
- Must observe scaffolding work continuously

TIGER TAILS OR LINE MARKERS:
- DO NOT INSULATE WIRES
- Exclusion zone must still be maintained

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding being erected within exclusion zone of energised overhead electric line = IMMEDIATELY STOP WORK; serious non-compliance
- No spotter present where scaffold parts could potentially enter exclusion zone = serious non-compliance
- Overhead electric lines not de-energised and no exclusion zone controls in place = serious non-compliance
- Tiger tails present but being treated as insulation (exclusion zone not maintained) = flag

CHUNK 12: Mobile Plant and Traffic Hazards
Section: CoP s3.2
Topic: Control measures for mobile plant and vehicular traffic near scaffolding

CONTROL MEASURES:
- RE-ROUTE motor vehicles and mobile plant away from scaffold location (e.g. using traffic controllers)
- Use BARRICADES, SIGNS, POSTS, BUFFER RAILS, GUARDS, or concrete/timber kerbs to prevent contact with scaffold
- Ensure scaffolding has NO UNNECESSARY PROTRUSIONS (e.g. over-length transoms, putlogs, tie tubes, over-height standards)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No barricades or buffer rails between vehicular traffic and scaffold = flag
- Over-length transoms or putlogs creating protrusion hazard in traffic areas = flag

CHUNK 13: Mixing Scaffold Components
Section: CoP s3.4; AS/NZS 1576.1 s2.4; AS/NZS 4576
Topic: Restrictions on mixing scaffold components from different manufacturers

DO NOT MIX scaffolding from different manufacturers UNLESS engineer approves that:
- Components are of compatible SIZE AND STRENGTH
- Components have compatible DEFLECTION CHARACTERISTICS
- Fixing devices are COMPATIBLE
- Mixing does not LESSEN strength, stability, rigidity or suitability

DO NOT MIX scaffolding COUPLERS AND TUBING of DIFFERENT OUTER DIAMETERS AND STRENGTHS unless:
- Designed specifically for task by engineer, OR
- Coupler manufacturer designed couplers for this purpose

ALUMINIUM AND STEEL:
- DO NOT MIX aluminium and steel components
- Steel clamps may CRUSH aluminium tubing reducing tube strength

STAIR MODULE GAP TO TRANSOM:
- Gap between stair module and vertical edge of transom must NOT BE GREATER THAN 15mm anywhere along transom length (where no manufacturer instruction on maximum gap provided)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold components from different manufacturers mixed without engineer approval = serious non-compliance
- Aluminium tubing used with steel clamps = non-compliance; flag immediately
- Stair module gap to transom exceeding 15mm (where no manufacturer specification exists) = non-compliance

CHUNK 14: Fall Risk Thresholds During Erection and Dismantling
Section: CoP s3.5.1, s3.5.2, s3.5.3; WHS Regulation s306P, s306Q
Topic: Fall height thresholds triggering mandatory fall prevention or fall arrest during erection and dismantling

FALL RISK DURING ERECTION (WHS Regulation s306P):
- PCBU MUST NOT erect or allow erection if risk person could fall:
  - 3 METRES for HOUSING CONSTRUCTION WORK, OR
  - 2 METRES for ALL OTHER CONSTRUCTION WORK
UNLESS:
  - Control measure PREVENTS person from falling, OR
  - FALL ARREST SYSTEM is used, OR
  - PCBU otherwise complies with WHS Regulation s306P(4)

FALL RISK DURING DISMANTLING (WHS Regulation s306Q):
- PCBU MUST NOT dismantle or allow dismantling if risk person could fall:
  - 3 METRES for HOUSING CONSTRUCTION WORK, OR
  - 2 METRES for ALL OTHER CONSTRUCTION WORK
UNLESS:
  - Control measure PREVENTS person from falling, OR
  - FALL ARREST SYSTEM is used, OR
  - PCBU otherwise complies with WHS Regulation s306Q(3)(b)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding erection or dismantling at >2m height (non-housing) or >3m (housing) with no fall prevention or fall arrest = serious non-compliance
- Workers erecting scaffold on incomplete platforms without edge protection = non-compliance

CHUNK 15: Fall Arresting Platform Specifications
Section: CoP s3.5.4; WHS Regulation s78, s79
Topic: Fall arresting platform dimensional and positioning requirements

INSTALLATION POSITION based on slope:
- Slope NOT OVER 26°: install platform NO MORE THAN 1 METRE LOWER than surface
- Slope OVER 26°: install platform NO MORE THAN 300mm LOWER than surface

PLATFORM SPECIFICATIONS:
- Must be UNOBSTRUCTED and at least 675mm WIDE for FULL LENGTH
- Must be able to WITHSTAND IMPACT OF A FALL onto it
- Must have EDGE PROTECTION erected along:
  - OUTER EDGE of length of platform
  - EDGES OF EACH END of fall arresting platform

INTERNAL GAP RULE:
- If gap between INNER EDGE of platform length and building face immediately beside platform EXCEEDS 225mm: implement control measure to control fall risk

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Fall arresting platform installed more than 1m below work surface on slopes ≤26° = non-compliance
- Fall arresting platform installed more than 300mm below work surface on slopes >26° = non-compliance
- Fall arresting platform narrower than 675mm = non-compliance
- Fall arresting platform without edge protection on outer or end edges = non-compliance
- Internal gap between platform and building face exceeding 225mm without additional control = non-compliance

CHUNK 16: Fall-Arrest Systems — When Appropriate and Anchorage Requirements
Section: CoP s3.5.6; AS/NZS 1891
Topic: Appropriate use of fall-arrest systems, anchorage specifications and rescue requirements

FALL-ARREST SYSTEMS SHOULD BE USED for:
- Erecting or dismantling 'DROP' or 'HUNG' scaffolds (constructed top to bottom, allowing clear fall zone)
- Fixing and removal of TROLLEY TRACKS on suspension rigs
- Erecting or dismantling CANTILEVERED NEEDLES and decking between them; also during erection of first lift where workers stand on deck between needles
- Erection and dismantling of CANTILEVERED SCAFFOLDS prior to or when removing initial platform
- Attachment and removal of SPURS projecting from supporting structure

ANCHORAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Anchorage points must be capable of supporting load of 15kN

RESCUE PROCEDURES MANDATORY:
- WRITTEN PROCEDURES must exist for safely retrieving fallen person
- Suspended person must be retrieved IMMEDIATELY (risk of permanent injury from suspension trauma)
- Fall-arrest system MUST NOT be used unless AT LEAST ONE OTHER PERSON (or TWO if user is heavy or may be unconscious) is on site and ABLE TO RESCUE the user
- If EWP used for rescue: must be READILY AVAILABLE and able to reach position of fall-arrest system user AT ALL TIMES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Fall-arrest system used as sole fall control for general scaffold erection (not hung/drop type) = flag; likely inappropriate
- No rescue procedure documented where fall-arrest system in use = serious non-compliance
- No second person on site when fall-arrest system in use = non-compliance
- Fall-arrest anchorage not rated for 15kN = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Falling Objects — Gap Controls and Exclusion Zones
Section: CoP s3.6, s3.6.1, s3.6.2; WHS Regulation s315E, s315F, s315G; AS/NZS 1576.1
Topic: Maximum allowable gaps and exclusion zone requirements to prevent falling objects

MATERIALS MUST NOT BE DROPPED from scaffold. When passing scaffolding between levels: done INTERNALLY within scaffold, unless suitable exclusion zones maintained.

ALLOWABLE INTERNAL GAPS (AS/NZS 1576.1):
- Horizontal gap from scaffold working deck to building structure: 100mm – 225mm for working face (100mm if not working face)
- Vertical gap from top of floor level: 300mm maximum
- Vertical gap from underside of soffit level: 300mm maximum

CONTROL MEASURES:
- Establish EXCLUSION ZONES around scaffolding and adjoining areas
- Use PERIMETER CONTAINMENT SCREENING, scaffold fans, hoardings or gantries
- Use MECHANICAL HOISTS to move materials
- Attach DANGER TAGS and WARNING SIGNS ('Keep Out – Falling Objects'; 'Danger – Incomplete Scaffolding')

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Exclusion zone not established around active scaffolding in public areas = non-compliance
- Materials being dropped from scaffold height rather than passed internally or via hoist = non-compliance
- No falling objects warning signs on incomplete scaffold = non-compliance
- Internal gap between scaffold working deck and building face exceeding 225mm without control measure = non-compliance
- Vertical gap from floor level or soffit exceeding 300mm without control measure = non-compliance

CHUNK 18: Housekeeping and Bird Droppings
Section: CoP s2.7.3
Topic: Housekeeping obligations and bird droppings health risks

HOUSEKEEPING MANDATORY:
- PCBU using scaffold must leave area clean from: falling objects; spills; waste; other leftover materials (food scraps, nails, hazardous substances)

BIRD DROPPINGS RISK:
- Workers disturbing accumulations of DRIED BIRD DROPPINGS may be at risk of infectious diseases: psittacosis, cryptococcosis, histoplasmosis
- Risk from small amounts of fresh droppings very low
- Risk INCREASES significantly if large accumulations of concentrated, dried droppings disturbed
- Where scaffold in place for LONG PERIOD at location where birds roosting or nesting: risk from disturbing dried droppings must be considered

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Materials, tools, debris or food scraps left on scaffold platforms = non-compliance
- Large accumulations of dried bird droppings on scaffold with workers not wearing respiratory protection = flag

CHUNK 19: Scaffold Alteration — Visible Unauthorised Changes
Section: CoP s3.8; WHS Regulation s203, s205
Topic: Unauthorised access, use or alteration of scaffolding

MANDATORY:
- Scaffold >4m fall potential: unauthorised access must be prevented while incomplete or unattended
- Alterations to scaffold >4m: must be recorded on handover certificate (what altered, by who, when)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold ties removed by workers without authorisation = serious non-compliance
- Planks moved or removed without authorisation = serious non-compliance
- Incomplete scaffold (>4m fall potential) not secured against access = non-compliance
- Scaffold alteration not recorded on handover certificate = non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Scaffold Component Corrosion and Condition
Section: CoP s3.9
Topic: Scaffold quality, corrosion and component inspection requirements

CORROSION RISKS:
- Painted steel scaffolding more susceptible to corrosion than galvanised or aluminium
- Scaffolding in humid/coastal locations: corrosion can occur to critical parts (support nodes, platforms)
- Platforms can collapse without warning from corrosion
- Visual inspection may not identify internal corrosion (e.g. water-filled standards)

MANDATORY INSPECTION:
- Competent person must inspect all components prior to erection: overall condition, surface coatings, welding/fabrication issues
- Painted scaffolding: may need periodic paint removal from sample to verify structural integrity

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Visible external corrosion on scaffold in coastal/humid location = flag; competent person inspection required
- Painted scaffolding in long-term use without paint removal inspection = flag
- Scaffold components with visible rust beyond surface rust = non-compliance
- Bent, cracked welds, or significant rust on components = remove from service

CHUNK 21: Fire Risk from Perimeter Containment Screening
Section: CoP s3.12
Topic: Fire and explosion risks from flammable containment screening materials

FIRE HAZARDS:
- Some perimeter containment screening materials are flammable or combustible
- Hot works near combustible screening without protection = fire risk

MANDATORY:
- Hot works near perimeter containment screening must use shields or fire suppression

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hot works (welding, grinding) performed near perimeter containment screening without shields = non-compliance
- No flammability information available for screening materials = flag

CHUNK 22: Scaffold Erection Sequence — Platform and Edge Protection
Section: CoP s4.1; WHS Regulation s306P
Topic: Safe erection sequence including platform installation and edge protection timing

ERECTION SEQUENCE PROTOCOL:
After enough components erected to support scaffold, IMMEDIATELY INSTALL:
1. Platform at least 450mm wide along full length of section
2. Edge protection across space between standards at current level
3. Access (stairs or ladder) to current level

BEFORE NEXT LEVEL ERECTED:
- Install platform not more than 2 metres below position of next level
- Platform may be removed after work starts 2 levels above

INTERNAL GAPS during erection:
- If internal gap >225mm: install edge protection on inside edge OR additional planks to minimise gap

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold being erected without access to current level = non-compliance
- Edge protection not installed at current scaffold level during erection = non-compliance
- No platform within 2m below level being erected = non-compliance
- Workers climbing on guardrails to gain height = non-compliance
- Internal gap >225mm without controls during erection = non-compliance

CHUNK 23: Scaffold Dismantling — Platform and Component Handling
Section: CoP s4.3; WHS Regulation s306Q
Topic: Safe dismantling practices and housekeeping

DISMANTLING REQUIREMENTS:
- Edge protection and access removed at last possible stage
- Platform at least 450mm wide at level being dismantled where practicable
- Full set of planks within 2 metres vertically below worker at all times
- Do NOT drop scaffolding components from heights

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Components being dropped from height during dismantling = serious non-compliance
- No platform within 2m below workers during dismantling = non-compliance
- Debris and objects left on scaffold during dismantling = flag; falling object risk

CHUNK 24: Birdcage Scaffold — Bracing and Configuration
Section: CoP s5.1.1; AS/NZS 1576.1
Topic: Birdcage scaffold control measures for tube and coupler type

BIRDCAGE SCAFFOLD:
- Independent scaffold with more than 2 rows of standards in both directions
- Mainly used for single level work (e.g. ceilings)

CONTROL MEASURES (tube and coupler):
- Lengthwise bracing at each outer longitudinal row of standards
- Longitudinal bracing or tied face at every third longitudinal row of standards
- Outside row on each face and each third row internally braced with longitudinal bracing
- Transverse bracing at every fourth bay on scaffold ends

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Birdcage scaffold without lengthwise bracing on outer rows = non-compliance
- No transverse bracing at fourth bay intervals on ends = non-compliance
- Birdcage scaffold supporting formwork without specific design = serious non-compliance

---

CHUNK 25: Tower Scaffold — Base Support and Height Ratios
Section: CoP s5.1.2
Topic: Tower scaffold control measures including base support and environmental factors

TOWER SCAFFOLD:
- Independent scaffold with four vertical members connected longitudinally and transversely

MANDATORY:
- Tower must rest on firm level ground with wheels or feet properly supported
- Do NOT use bricks or building blocks to support any part of tower
- Working height must not exceed manufacturer's or designer's specified maximum

- Sheeted or exposed to strong winds
- Loaded with heavy equipment or materials
- Used to hoist heavy materials or support rubbish chutes
- Used for operations with heavy/awkward equipment (grit blasting, water-jetting)
- Supporting a ladder

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tower scaffold resting on bricks or building blocks = serious non-compliance; stop work
- Tower scaffold sheeted without alternative height-to-base ratio = flag

CHUNK 26: Mobile Scaffold — Movement and Castor Requirements

CHUNK 29: Suspended Scaffold — Counterweight Calculation and Fixing
Section: CoP s5.3.2, s5.3.3
Topic: Counterweight calculation formula and needle fixing methods

COUNTERWEIGHT CALCULATION:
Formula: Number of counterweights = 3 × (rope tension in kg) × (outboard in mm) ÷ (inboard in mm) ÷ (mass of each counterweight in kg)

Example (25kg counterweights): Max rope tension = 723kg, Outboard = 900mm, Inboard = 3600mm
→ 3 × 723 × 900 ÷ 3600 ÷ 25 = 21.69 (22 counterweights per needle)

FIXING NEEDLES:
- Method must be shown on detailed drawing certified by engineer
- Needle fixed to floor: must be positively restrained — chemical and friction anchors must NOT be used
- Scaffold couplers must NOT secure counterweight or anchorage to needle — use positive connection (bolted or pinned)
- Where props used: install to top of needle and underside of floor above; must be fixed to prevent dislodgement
- Counterweights: must be secured by keyed lock and placed preferably on top of needle

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffold couplers used to secure counterweights to needle = serious non-compliance
- No engineer drawing for needle fixing method = serious non-compliance
- Timber or single-skin brick parapet used to support suspension rig = serious non-compliance
- Counterweights not secured with keyed lock = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 48: Swing-Stage Load Testing — Procedures and Requirements
Section: CoP Appendix 5; Verification Forms
Topic: Load testing procedures for swing-stage initial setup and relocation

LOAD TEST — two options:

OPTION 1 — BOTH NEEDLES:
- Test load = 100% of WLL with 100% of load spread evenly across full length of cradle
- Test weights clearly marked with weight in kilograms
- Raise cradle 1 metre above landing; hold and check cradle does not move
- Persons operating hoists stand to side of cradles

OPTION 2 — ONE NEEDLE AT A TIME:
- Test load = 60% of WLL per needle applied within 1 metre of end of cradle
- Load one end; raise and check; lower and move weights to opposite end within 1 metre; repeat

MANDATORY:
- Load test required at initial set-up and after each relocation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Load test not performed at initial set-up or after relocation = serious non-compliance
- Person conducting load test standing directly beside or over cradle (not to side) = serious safety risk
- Test weights not clearly marked with weight in kilograms = flag
