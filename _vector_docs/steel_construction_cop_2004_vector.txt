CHUNK 1: Steel Member Mass Marking and Identification
Section: CoP s2.7, s4.3.3
Topic: Mandatory marking of steel components for safe handling and erection

MANDATORY MARKING BY FABRICATOR:
- MASS of steel members must be MARKED on steel (especially where difficult to estimate) — includes protective coatings
- LOCATING NUMBERS clearly marked on steel components for erection sequence identification
- Pipe with covered ends: weight must be marked
- Enables erector to select correctly designed lifting gear and crane of appropriate capacity

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Steel members without mass markings where mass is difficult to estimate = non-compliance
- Steel delivered without location numbers for erection sequence = flag
- No mass marking on pipe with covered ends = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Designer Obligations — Maximum Wind Speed and Temporary Bracing
Section: CoP s2.5
Topic: Designer must specify maximum permissible wind speed and temporary bracing instructions

DESIGNER MUST SPECIFY:
- MAXIMUM PERMISSIBLE WIND SPEED for erecting the steel structure — must be detailed on drawings
- Clear INSTRUCTIONS FOR TEMPORARY BRACING — must be detailed on drawings
- LIFTING POINTS identified on drawings for heavy sub-assemblies
- TEMPORARY BRACING/GUYING requirements highlighted on all documentation at pre-contract stage

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No design documentation specifying maximum permissible wind speed for erection = non-compliance
- Lifting points not identified on drawings for heavy sub-assemblies = flag
- No temporary bracing instructions provided to erector by designer = non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Rigger's Post System — Structural Verification Requirement
Section: CoP s3.7.1, Appendix 7
Topic: Mandatory engineer verification for rigger's post fall-arrest systems (KEY REQUIREMENT)

RIGGER'S POST SYSTEM DEFINITION:
Posts used as anchorages for a horizontal fall-arrest line (static line), often set up along top of portal frame steel structures.

MANDATORY VERIFICATION:
- Must be VERIFIED AS 'FIT FOR PURPOSE' by BUILDING DESIGNER
- BUILDING DESIGNER must be consulted to confirm structure is strong enough to support the system during erection
- Erector must inform designer of proposed post locations and installation stages
- Fall-arrest loadings on end anchorages can be EXTREMELY HIGH due to shock loading and triangulation effects

VERIFICATION MUST INCLUDE:
- Design of rigger's post and method of attaching static line to post
- Method of attaching posts to supporting structure
- Minimum strength of supporting structure (may include minimum steel section size)
- Specification of static line — material type, size, pre-tension
- Maximum SPAN OF LINE between posts
- Minimum VERTICAL CLEARANCE required underneath the line (may be MORE THAN 8 METRES)
- Maximum NUMBER OF PERSONS using the line at any one time
- Verified against AS/NZS 1891.2

ENGINEER CERTIFICATION:
- Must be prepared by REGISTERED PROFESSIONAL ENGINEER OF QUEENSLAND (RPEQ) or equivalent
- Must specify: maximum permissible wind speed during erection; maximum number of workers attached to static line; minimum number and type of anchors per column
- Certification must be AVAILABLE ON SITE — WHS inspectors may require it

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Rigger's post system installed without written structural verification by building designer = serious non-compliance
- No RPEQ (or equivalent) certification available on site = serious non-compliance
- Rigger's post system in use without documented verification = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Fabricated Working Platforms — Minimum Dimensions
Section: CoP s3.5.1
Topic: Specifications for fabricated working platforms used in steel construction (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be SECURED AGAINST UPLIFT OR DISPLACEMENT to structure
- Must be installed WITH EDGE PROTECTION SYSTEMS
- Must be DESIGNED BY AN ENGINEER
- Must be NOT LESS THAN 450 MM IN WIDTH OR LENGTH
- Must have area of sufficient SIZE AND STRENGTH to carry tools, materials and persons

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Fabricated working platform less than 450mm width or length = non-compliance
- Platform not secured against uplift or displacement = serious non-compliance
- No edge protection on fabricated platform = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: EWP Operator Licensing and Ground-Level Supervision
Section: CoP s3.5.2; WHS Regulation
Topic: Licensing requirements for EWP operators and ground-level supervision (KEY REQUIREMENTS)

LICENSING:
- Boom-type EWPs with boom length of 11 METRES OR MORE require a LICENSED OPERATOR (or direct supervision by licence holder)
- All operators must be COMPETENT AND ADEQUATELY TRAINED

GROUND-LEVEL SUPERVISION:
- A COMPETENT PERSON must be available at GROUND LEVEL to lower the platform in case of malfunction

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- EWP operator without licence for boom-type unit with 11m+ boom = serious non-compliance
- No person at ground level able to lower EWP in case of malfunction = non-compliance

CHUNK 6: EWP Access to Roof/Structure — Gap and Guardrail Requirements
Section: CoP s3.5.2
Topic: EWP platform-to-landing gap and guardrail specifications when used for roof/structure access (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS WHEN EWP USED FOR ROOF/STRUCTURE ACCESS:
- Gap between EWP platform and landing must NOT EXCEED 100 MM
- Platform must be SECURED AGAINST SIDEWAYS MOVEMENT when adjacent to roof/structure edge
- Safe access/egress by either:
  (a) Guardrail at roof/structure level extending AT LEAST 1.5 METRES EITHER SIDE of gate, OR
  (b) Safety harness system

EWP MUST NOT:
- Be used for any other purpose or driven away from building while persons are using it as access
- Be used near LIVE ELECTRICAL POWER LINES

AREA AROUND EWP:
- Must be FREE OF VEHICULAR TRAFFIC
- Ground conditions must be SUITABLE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gap between EWP platform and landing exceeding 100mm = non-compliance
- No guardrail extending 1.5m either side of gate at roof/structure level (and no harness system) = non-compliance
- EWP platform not secured against sideways movement at roof edge = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: EWP Harness Requirement — Boom-Type Units
Section: CoP s3.5.2
Topic: Mandatory harness and lanyard for all persons in boom-type EWPs

MANDATORY PPE IN BOOM-TYPE EWPs:
- ALL PERSONS in boom-type EWPs must wear FULL BODY HARNESS with ENERGY ABSORBER TYPE LANYARD
- Lanyard must be attached to an anchorage point IN THE BASKET

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Persons in boom-type EWP without full body harness attached to basket anchor = non-compliance

CHUNK 8: EWP Lifting of Steelwork — Prohibited Actions
Section: CoP s3.5.2
Topic: Requirements and prohibitions when using EWPs to lift steelwork

ONLY WHERE MANUFACTURER PERMITS:
- Total load (workers + materials) must NOT EXCEED RATED CAPACITY
- Steelwork must NOT be loaded onto EWP GUARDRAILS — use PURPOSE-BUILT CRADLE
- EWP must NEVER be used to FORCE STEELWORK INTO PLACE
- Working surface must be LEVEL, FIRM, with CLEAR ACCESS and no step up/down on floor slab
- EWPs for steel erection must be RATED FOR OUTDOOR USE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Steelwork being loaded onto EWP guardrails = non-compliance
- EWP being used to force steelwork into place = serious non-compliance
- Total load in EWP exceeding rated capacity = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 9: Work Box Design — Dimensions and Safety Features
Section: CoP s3.5.4–s3.5.7; AS 1418.17
Topic: Design specifications for personnel-carrying work boxes suspended from cranes (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

WORK BOX DEFINITION:
A personnel-carrying device designed to be suspended from a crane.

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
- Slings permanently attached by LOCKED SHACKLES or HAMMER LOCK device
- Factor of safety: AT LEAST 8 FOR CHAINS; AT LEAST 10 FOR WIRE ROPE
- Marked with SWL, TARE MASS and DESIGN REGISTRATION NUMBER (e.g. on data plate)
- Sides at least 1 METRE IN HEIGHT
- Door (if provided) must be INWARD OPENING, SELF-CLOSING with latch preventing accidental opening
- Used ONLY to lift persons and their equipment

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work box sides less than 1 metre high = non-compliance
- No SWL, tare mass or design registration number marked on work box = non-compliance
- Work box door not inward opening or self-closing = non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Work Box Crane Requirements and Operator Duties
Section: CoP s3.5.4–s3.5.7
Topic: Mandatory crane features and operator requirements when using work boxes (KEY REQUIREMENTS)

CRANE REQUIREMENTS FOR WORK BOXES:
- Hook with SPRING LOADED OPERABLE LATCH to prevent inadvertent release
- Functioning OVER-HOISTING DEVICE (anti-two-block)
- DEAD-MAN CONTROL on power lowering for self-centring and automatic brake engagement
- LOCKOUT CONTROL on crane free fall function to prevent free fall
- Minimum RATED CAPACITY of 1000 kg at maximum radius
- Minimum rated capacity of AT LEAST TWICE THE TOTAL LOAD of workbox and contents at maximum radius

CRANE OPERATOR DUTIES:
- Must REMAIN AT CONTROLS AT ALL TIMES while work box is occupied
- Work box and contents must be moved UNDER POWERED CONDITIONS AT ALL TIMES

PERSONS IN WORK BOXES:
- Must be attached AT ALL TIMES by FULL BODY SAFETY HARNESS, LANYARD AND ENERGY ABSORBER to a suitable anchorage point within workbox or to the main sling ring above workers' heads
- Fall arrest attachments MUST NOT be placed in the THROAT OF THE LIFT HOOK
- At least ONE PERSON in work box must be COMPETENT IN CRANE SIGNALS (e.g. a dogger)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane operator not at controls while work box is occupied = serious non-compliance
- Persons in work box without full body harness attached to anchorage = serious non-compliance
- Crane hook without spring loaded latch when lifting work box = non-compliance
- No over-hoisting device on crane lifting work box = non-compliance

CHUNK 11: Fall-Arrest Harness — Lanyard Length and Anchorage Capacity
Section: CoP s3.7
Topic: Fall-arrest harness lanyard specifications and anchorage point capacity (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

FALL-ARREST IS NOT PREFERRED:
Where possible and practicable, select a method that REDUCES THE RISK OF A PERSON FALLING (e.g. perimeter guardrail, EWPs). Fall-arrest harnesses do NOT prevent a fall.

REQUIREMENTS IF FALL-ARREST MUST BE USED:
- Lanyard assembly must be AS SHORT AS POSSIBLE — working slack NOT MORE THAN 2 METRES to minimise pendulum effect
- Individual anchorage capacity: AT LEAST 15 kN (equivalent to suspending 1.5 tonne)
- Static lines require anchorages with HIGHER STRENGTHS than individual anchorages
- Anchorage point must allow lanyard to be attached BEFORE user moves into risk position

EMERGENCY RECOVERY:
- A system MUST be in place to quickly retrieve fallen workers
- Persons can lose consciousness or suffer modified cardiac rhythm in 2 TO 12 MINUTES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lanyard working slack greater than 2 metres = non-compliance
- Individual anchorage points with capacity less than 15 kN = serious non-compliance
- Workers using fall-arrest system in isolation = non-compliance
- Workers suspended in harnesses with no emergency rescue system in place = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 12: Edge Protection — Guardrail Specifications
Section: CoP s3.9
Topic: Guardrail height and spacing specifications for edge protection (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

EDGE PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be designed to WITHSTAND THE FORCES if a person fell against it

TOP RAIL:
- Must be at AT LEAST 900 MM ABOVE the platform or other surface

MID-RAILS:
- Placed between top rail and platform so NO MORE THAN 450 MM exists between adjacent rails

BOTTOM RAIL / TOEBOARD:
- BOTTOM RAIL: fitted NO MORE THAN 250 MM AND NO LESS THAN 150 MM above the platform
OR
- TOEBOARD: at least 150 MM HIGH

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Top rail lower than 900mm above platform level = non-compliance
- Gap between adjacent rails greater than 450mm = non-compliance
- Bottom rail more than 250mm above platform = non-compliance
- Bottom rail less than 150mm above platform = non-compliance
- Toeboard less than 150mm high = non-compliance

CHUNK 13: Fall Protection Covers — Holes and Openings
Section: CoP s3.10
Topic: Requirements for covers over holes and openings to prevent falls

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:
All holes and openings, OTHER THAN lift shafts and stairwells, must be protected to prevent persons falling.

COVER REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be capable of SUPPORTING THE IMPACT OF A PERSON FALLING ONTO IT
- Must be SECURED AGAINST MOVEMENT
- Must NOT be used as a WORKING PLATFORM

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Holes or openings in floor surfaces uncovered and unguarded (other than lift shafts and stairwells) = serious non-compliance
- Cover not secured against movement = non-compliance
- Cover being used as a working platform = non-compliance

CHUNK 14: Tool Lanyards — Length Specification
Section: CoP s4.1
Topic: Tool lanyard length requirements to prevent falling object injuries (KEY MEASUREMENT)

TOOL LANYARDS:
- Short rope or webbing to secure tools/equipment to an anchorage point
- Material: synthetic fibre, natural fibre, steel rope or webbing — must maintain strength and abrasion resistance
- Lanyard attached at the WRIST must be NO LONGER THAN 300 MM — ensures dropped tool cannot hit person below
- Length must be KEPT TO A MINIMUM to reduce risk of snagging

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tool lanyards at wrist exceeding 300mm in length = non-compliance
- Workers at heights without tool lanyards on hand tools = flag

CHUNK 15: Lift Boxes and Toeboards — Falling Object Controls
Section: CoP s4.2, s4.5
Topic: Specifications for lift boxes and toeboards to prevent falling objects (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

LIFT BOXES:
- Container suspended from crane or hoist to transport plant and/or materials
- Must be FULLY SHEETED and ENCLOSE THE LOAD

TOEBOARDS:
- Vertical barrier to prevent fall of tools or materials
- Must be SECURELY FIXED adjacent to work surface
- Must extend MINIMUM 150 MM ABOVE THE WORK SURFACE
- Height must increase as size and height of materials/equipment stored near edge increases
- GAP BETWEEN TOEBOARD AND WORK SURFACE must NOT BE GREATER THAN 10 MM

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lift boxes not fully sheeted and enclosing load = non-compliance
- Toeboard less than 150mm above work surface = non-compliance
- Gap between toeboard and work surface greater than 10mm = non-compliance

CHUNK 16: Exclusion Zones — Falling Object Protection
Section: CoP s4.6
Topic: Exclusion zone requirements below steel erection work

EXCLUSION ZONES:
- Bunting or barricades with suitable signage erected around perimeter of exclusion zone
- Prevents personnel from being in the area under which riggers are erecting steelwork

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No exclusion zones below areas where riggers are erecting = non-compliance
- Exclusion zone without bunting, barricades or signage = non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Lifting Slings — Inspection Tags and Condition
Section: CoP s4.3.1
Topic: Mandatory inspection tags and condition requirements for lifting equipment

INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Chains and auxiliary fittings must be THOROUGHLY CLEANED and PERIODICALLY INSPECTED
- ALL LIFTING SLINGS must have SWL (Safe Working Load) displayed
- ALL LIFTING SLINGS must have CURRENT INSPECTION TAGS

DO NOT USE chain, ring, shackle, swivel, wire rope or similar lifting gear if:
- WEAR on any part EXCEEDS manufacturer's specified amount
- Any part is DEFORMED, NICKED, CRACKED, SPLIT or OTHERWISE DAMAGED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lifting chains or slings with visible deformation, nicks, cracks or damage = serious non-compliance
- Lifting gear without current inspection tags = non-compliance
- Lifting slings without SWL displayed = non-compliance

CHUNK 18: Steel Storage and Handling — Stability Requirements
Section: CoP s4.3.2
Topic: Requirements for storing and handling steel members on site

STORAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Steelwork must be stored so it CANNOT FALL ON WORKERS or cause damage to buildings or plant
- Where steelwork is STACKED: stacks must be STABLE and SAFE ACCESS must be available when workers sling a load
- Consider: effect of WIND and potential for MOBILE PLANT TO CONTACT stored steelwork

STRAPPED BUNDLES:
- When releasing strapping: CAUTION must be exercised — strapping may WHIP BACK and cause injury OR the bundle can fall apart and injure workers

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Steelwork stacked unstably or with no safe access for slinging = non-compliance
- No consideration of wind effects on stored steelwork = flag
- Workers releasing strapping on bundles without appropriate precautions = flag

CHUNK 19: Lifting Steelwork — Tag Lines and Lifting Beams
Section: CoP s4.3.3
Topic: Tag line and lifting beam requirements for steel member lifting

BEFORE LIFTING:
- Rigger/dogger must SLING THE LOAD correctly
- FIX TAG LINES (where appropriate) to ends of load
- When transferring from HORIZONTAL TO VERTICAL: care must be taken to avoid unrestrained movement of the lower end
- LIFTING BEAMS may be necessary for some members to ensure stability

LIFTING BUNDLES:
Lifting more than one steel member or bundles simultaneously should only occur where:
- Lifting slings are DESIGNED TO AVOID STEEL MEMBERS BECOMING ENTANGLED OR DISLODGED
OR
- CRADLES for bundles of steel or decking are used

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tag lines not used on loads where load control is needed = non-compliance
- Bundles lifted without cradles or designed slings to prevent entanglement = non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Column Erection — Base Plate Anchoring and Stabilisation
Section: CoP s4.3.5, s5.3
Topic: Requirements for anchoring and stabilising columns during erection

COLUMNS:
- Free-standing single columns or column assemblies must be secured by BOLTING THE BASE PLATE ONTO THE FOOTING
- Once anchored and in near-perpendicular position and STABILISED AGAINST OVERTURNING: lifting gear can be released
- Where possible: lifting sling released FROM FLOOR LEVEL using LONG SLINGS, REMOTE RELEASE SHACKLES or other suitable devices; or by using EWP

COLUMN SPLICES:
- Must be CAPABLE OF SUPPORTING THE STANDING COLUMN until tied together
- OR column must have TEMPORARY GUYS ATTACHED to ensure stability at height

BASE PLATE SETTING:
- Use TIGHTLY FITTED STEEL PACKERS or STEEL WEDGES DRIVEN UNDER EDGES of column base plate for added stability
- JACKING NUTS: columns may be levelled on cast-in fixings using jacking nuts on threads; top nuts, washers etc. installed to secure column

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Columns released from lifting gear before anchored and stabilised = serious non-compliance
- Columns released from lifting gear before splices are capable of supporting standing column = serious non-compliance
- No temporary guys for column without splice capable of self-support = non-compliance
- Column base plates set without steel packers/wedges for stability = flag

CHUNK 21: Rafter Erection — Single Lift vs Multiple Lift Methods
Section: CoP s4.3.6
Topic: Preferred methods for rafter erection and use of lifting brackets

RAFTERS:
- Should ideally be erected as ONE LENGTH with ONE CRANE and SINGLE LIFT to minimise collapse risk
- SINGLE LIFT: bolt rafter together on ground at ridge or intermediate joint first; then bolt to portal columns and end wall columns using riggers from EWP
- MULTIPLE LIFT: rafter-to-column connections made by rigger from EWP at apex or intermediate position
- Consider use of LIFTING BRACKETS (designed and certified by an engineer) in preference to chains or slings around steel

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Rafters erected in multiple lifts without riggers working from EWPs = flag
- Lifting brackets not engineer-designed/certified on rafter lifts = flag

---

CHUNK 22: Purlin Installation — Carrying Direction and Fall-Arrest
Section: CoP s4.3.7
Topic: Safe methods for purlin installation including carrying direction and fall protection

PURLINS:
- PREFERRED METHOD: riggers working from EWPs
- Where not practicable and riggers must work from rafter steelwork: must use a RECOGNISED, CORRECTLY INSTALLED AND TESTED FALL-ARREST SYSTEM (e.g. rigger's post system)
- Where carrying purlins along rafter: ALWAYS CARRY UP THE SLOPE — safer and easier than carrying down
- Carrying purlins normally involves TWO RIGGERS, each attached to a fall-arrest system, working from ADJACENT RAFTERS
- Alternative: use CRANE to raise and place purlins at each cleat location — no need to carry individual purlins along rafter tops; riggers work from EWP or purlin cradle

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Purlins being carried DOWN the rafter slope = non-compliance
- Riggers working from rafter steelwork without fall-arrest system = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 23: Girt Installation — Mechanical Lifting Requirement
Section: CoP s4.3.8
Topic: Mandatory mechanical lifting and EWP/cradle use for girt installation

GIRTS:
- Must be erected by rigger working from EWP or COMBINED ACCESS AND LIFTING CRADLE
- Individual girts must NOT BE CARRIED BY HAND — must be LIFTED INTO POSITION BY MECHANICAL EQUIPMENT
- To prevent girts falling: use MOBILE SCAFFOLD TOWERS or COMBINED ACCESS AND LIFTING CRADLE

CRANE OR HOIST FOR COMBINED ACCESS AND LIFTING CRADLE:
- Fitted with hook with SPRING LOADED OPERABLE LATCH
- Fitted with FUNCTIONING OVER-HOISTING DEVICE (motion cut-out anti-two-block)
- Equipped with DEAD-MAN CONTROL on power lowering
- Equipped with LOCKOUT CONTROL on crane free-fall function

WORKING LOAD LIMITS:
- Must be CLEARLY MARKED at each end of the cradle (number of workers + total mass of girts + total WLL in kg)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Individual girts carried by hand instead of using mechanical equipment = non-compliance
- Girts being installed without a rigger working from EWP or cradle = non-compliance
- Crane for girt cradle without spring loaded latch on hook = non-compliance
- No over-hoisting device on crane lifting girt cradle = non-compliance

CHUNK 25: Bolt Alignment — Prohibited Actions
Section: CoP s5.5
Topic: Prohibited actions when aligning bolts and holes in steel connections

PROHIBITED ACTIONS:
- BENDING BOLTS to line up with holes — bent bolts more likely to FAIL and cause uneven load distribution
- ENLARGING HOLES in steelwork to align with bolts — reduces ability to transfer loads and can cause TEARING

IF HOLES DO NOT LINE UP WITH BOLTS:
- The BUILDING DESIGN ENGINEER MUST BE CONSULTED
- Written VERIFICATION FROM THE ENGINEER must be provided before changes or alterations are made

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Bolts bent to align with holes = serious structural non-compliance
- Holes in steelwork enlarged beyond design tolerances = serious structural non-compliance
- Misaligned holes corrected without written engineer verification = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 26: Post-Installed Anchors — Use and Installation
Section: CoP s5.6
Topic: Requirements for post-installed anchors in concrete slabs

POST-INSTALLED ANCHORS:
Post-installed anchors (chemical, expansion or 'screw bolt' type) may be used to secure structural steel to concrete slab, but are DISCOURAGED.

WHERE POST-INSTALLED ANCHORS ARE USED:
- Must be installed IN ACCORDANCE WITH MANUFACTURER'S RECOMMENDATIONS
- Erector must ensure CONCRETE HAS REACHED SPECIFIED STRENGTH before anchor installation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Post-installed anchors used without following manufacturer's recommendations = non-compliance
- Anchors installed in concrete that has not reached specified strength = serious non-compliance
- No verification of correct setting torque for expansion anchors = non-compliance

CHUNK 27: Temporary Guys and Bracing — Identification and Barriers
Section: CoP s5
Topic: Requirements for temporary guys and bracing including identification and barriers

TEMPORARY GUYS AND BRACING:
- Must be SECURELY ANCHORED
- Anchor points must resist any force likely to be imposed on them
- Movement of an anchor must be REPORTED TO THE ERECTOR IMMEDIATELY and action taken
- Guys must be CLEARLY IDENTIFIED by COLOURED BUNTING or similar to avoid accidents
- In areas of plant and vehicle movement: ADEQUATE VISUAL BARRIERS must be located between guys and movement areas

STABILITY VERIFICATION — must occur:
- AT THE END OF THE WORKDAY or during temporary cessations
- Effectiveness of temporary guys, bracing and supports must be inspected at BEGINNING OF EACH SHIFT
- When FASTENINGS MAY BE INCOMPLETE (e.g. during lining up and adjustment)
- During HIGH WINDS or when high winds are forecast
- When structure may be subject to CONSTRUCTION LOADS (e.g. stacking of parts, lifting)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Temporary guys not identified with coloured bunting or similar = non-compliance
- No stability verification at end of each workday = non-compliance
- No inspection of temporary supports at beginning of each shift = non-compliance
- Guys running through plant and vehicle movement areas without visual barriers = non-compliance

CHUNK 28: Braced Bay Erection Sequence — Portal Frame Buildings
Section: CoP s5.2; Appendix 2
Topic: Required erection sequence for braced bays in portal frame buildings

BRACED BAY REQUIREMENT:
- Where required by design, erection must START IN A NOMINATED BRACED BAY so the structure can be plumbed and made self-supporting before further steelwork is added
- Braced bays must be NO MORE THAN SEVEN BAYS APART

PORTAL FRAMES:
- Portal frames must be LIFTED IN ONE PIECE (where this procedure applies)

IF PORTAL IS UNSTABLE WHEN FREE-STANDING:
- Frame must be GUYED FROM THE APEX IN OPPOSITE DIRECTIONS before detaching crane hook(s)
- Expected for FRAME SPANS OF 30 METRES AND OVER

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Erection not starting in braced bay without alternative temporary support plan = non-compliance
- Braced bays more than seven bays apart = non-compliance
- Portal frames of 30m+ span not guyed at apex before crane detaches = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 29: Stabilising Purlins — Number Required at Bracing Strut Locations
Section: CoP Appendix 2, Table 1
Topic: Number of purlins required at bracing strut locations based on strut spacing (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

STABILISING PURLINS (Table 1):
Number of purlins required at bracing strut locations:

- Bracing strut spacing up to 6000mm: One at eaves, One at strut locations

- Bracing strut spacing 9000–12000mm: Two at eaves, Three at strut locations

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Insufficient number of purlins at bracing strut locations for the strut spacing = non-compliance

CHUNK 30: Roof and Wall Sheeting — Loading and Installation Sequence
Section: CoP Appendix 2
Topic: Requirements for loading roof sheeting packs and installing wall sheeting

ROOF SHEETING PACKS:
- May ONLY be loaded onto structure when purlins and bridging are completed to end of building OR to second braced bay
- Must NOT be greater than ONE BAY COVERAGE in size
- Must be placed at each PORTAL RAFTER or END FRAME

WALL SHEETING:
- Can ONLY be attached after the WHOLE STRUCTURE or a section containing TWO BRACED BAYS has been completed including roof purlins, purlin fly bracing and bridging

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Roof sheeting packs loaded before purlins and bridging completed to full extent = non-compliance
- Wall sheeting installed before two braced bays and roof purlins/fly bracing/bridging completed = non-compliance
- Roof sheeting packs greater than one bay coverage in size = non-compliance

CHUNK 31: Crane Outrigger Packing — Configuration and Dimensions
Section: CoP s6.1
Topic: Outrigger packing configuration and minimum timber dimensions (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

PACKING REQUIREMENTS:
- Must always be laid in 'PIGSTY' CONFIGURATION (not parallel)
- Hardwood timber: AT LEAST 200 MM × 75 MM; length calculated by required area
- Outrigger extensions must be LOCKED OR PINNED IN POSITION as recommended by manufacturer
- Footpads/hydraulic pads must be placed on ADEQUATE FOOTING; crane must be LEVELLED
- Footing must be CHECKED FREQUENTLY during operation

BEFORE SETUP:
- Principal contractor must supply erector all information on location of TRENCHES AND BACKFILLED TRENCHES/SERVICES
- FULL EVALUATION of overhead, underground and jobsite hazards
- Visually inspect GROUND CONDITIONS to determine type and amount of packing required
- Before setting up on structures or suspended floors: confirm structure can support the loading

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Outrigger packing not in pigsty configuration = non-compliance
- Hardwood packing less than 200mm × 75mm = non-compliance
- Outrigger extensions not locked or pinned per manufacturer = non-compliance

---

CHUNK 32: Crane Hoist Rope Alignment and Load Control
Section: CoP s6.3
Topic: Requirements for hoist rope vertical alignment and load control during lifting

BEFORE HOISTING:
- Crane operator or dogger must confirm HOIST ROPE HANGS VERTICALLY over the load
- Avoid swinging the load when lift is taken
- When load is suspended: ensure load is UNDER CONTROL at all times

WHEN HANDLING MAXIMUM OR NEAR MAXIMUM LOADS — after lifting a few centimetres:
- Test hoist brakes
- Check weight recorded on load weight indicator
- Recheck load chart

EXCEPT IN EMERGENCY:
- Do NOT LEAVE CABIN OR CONTROL ROOM while a load is suspended

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No pre-lift check of hoist rope vertical alignment = non-compliance
- Load being swung or hoisted with rope not vertical = non-compliance
- Crane cabin vacated while load is suspended = serious non-compliance

---

CHUNK 33: Crane Lifting Near Persons — Prohibited Actions
Section: CoP s6.4
Topic: Prohibition on carrying loads over persons and warning requirements

LIFTING LOADS NEAR PERSONS:
- AVOID CARRYING LOADS OVER PERSONS
- NEVER do this when using devices functioning by friction, fluid pressure, partial vacuum, magnets or local indentation of surfaces
- Crane operators must give AUDIBLE WARNING before lifting loads near persons

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Loads carried over persons = serious non-compliance
- No audible warning before lifting near persons = non-compliance

---

CHUNK 34: Crane Travelling with Load — Safety Observer and Dogger Requirements
Section: CoP s6.6
Topic: Requirements when travelling with a load including safety observer and dogger positioning

TRAVELLING WITH A LOAD:
- Post a SAFETY OBSERVER to warn of potential collision with adjacent structures and overhead electrical power cables in congested areas
- Always use a DOGGER when travelling with load
- Keep loads CLOSE TO THE GROUND
- In some cases: rope off swinging loads to control swing
- Travel with load FACING UP THE SLOPE
- Dogger must remain IN SIGHT OF CRANE OPERATOR and must NOT WALK IN PATH OF CRANE

MOBILE CRANE OPERATOR MUST ENSURE:
- SLEW BRAKE applied at all times other than when slew motion is being used
- Awareness of UNEVEN ROAD SURFACES — undulations may move crane into zone of instability
- SLEWING BRAKE OR LOCK applied when travelling with a load

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Dogger walking in path of crane during pick-and-carry operations = serious non-compliance
- No safety observer when travelling with load in congested areas = non-compliance
- Load not kept close to ground when travelling = flag

---

CHUNK 35: Multiple Crane Lifts — Controller and Capacity Requirements
Section: CoP s6.7
Topic: Requirements for multiple crane lifts including controller designation and minimum capacity (KEY REQUIREMENTS)

MULTIPLE CRANE LIFTS:
- AVOID multiple crane lifts if possible
- If necessary:
  (a) ONE PERSON designated as OVERALL CONTROLLER of the lift
  (b) Accurate assessment of: share of load per crane; how load sharing is proportioned; how proportioning is maintained
  (c) Instructions clear; rehearsal wherever possible
  (d) Cranes of EQUAL CAPACITY AND SIMILAR CHARACTERISTICS must be used
  (e) Tractor type cranes in 'pick and carry': both cranes aligned in SAME DIRECTION
  (f) LUFFING UP preferred over luffing down

MINIMUM CAPACITY REQUIREMENTS — where multiple lifts NOT designed by engineer:
- TWO CRANES: each must have capacity 20% GREATER than calculated share of load
- THREE CRANES: each must have capacity 33% GREATER than calculated share
- FOUR CRANES: each must have capacity 50% GREATER than calculated share

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Multiple crane lifts without one designated overall controller = serious non-compliance
- Two-crane lift without each crane having 20% excess capacity over calculated share = non-compliance
- Three-crane lift without each crane having 33% excess capacity = non-compliance
- Four-crane lift without each crane having 50% excess capacity = non-compliance

---

CHUNK 36: Lattice Boom Cranes — Boom Angle and Joint Pin Requirements
Section: CoP s6.8
Topic: Prohibited boom positions and joint pin requirements for lattice boom cranes

LATTICE BOOM CRANES:
- Top of extended boom must NOT be lowered below HORIZONTAL PLANE through base pivot pin — could cause boom to buckle before lifting
- When changing boom sections: INTERMEDIATE OR ADVANCED RIGGER must prevent boom collapse; adequate support under each section before removing joint pins/bolts
- Joint pins or bolts replaced must have PROPERLY FITTED SPLIT PINS or LOCKING DEVICE
- Joint pins must be removable while operator stands on GROUND ON OUTSIDE OF BOOM OR JIB
- Must NOT operate with boom at angle GREATER THAN SHOWN ON LOAD CHART or hard against boom backstop

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lattice boom lowered below horizontal plane of base pivot pin = non-compliance
- Crane operating beyond boom angle shown in load chart = serious non-compliance
- Joint pins without split pins or locking devices = non-compliance

---

CHUNK 37: Load Chart and Load Indicators — Display and Requirements
Section: CoP s6.11
Topic: Load chart display and load indicator requirements for mobile cranes

LOAD CHART AND INDICATORS:
- Each crane must have PERMANENT NOTICE indicating working load limit
- Additional notice listing condition, incidence and manner of application of load; must be EASILY READ BY CRANE OPERATOR and dogger
- All cranes must have LOAD CHART and RADIUS INDICATOR

LOAD INDICATORS REQUIRED FOR:
- All mobile cranes with RATED CAPACITY OF 15 TONNES AND GREATER
- Mobile cranes MANUFACTURED SINCE 1996 with RATED CAPACITY OF 5 TONNES OR GREATER

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Mobile crane of 15-tonne+ rated capacity without load indicator = non-compliance
- Load chart not clearly displayed and readable by crane operator = non-compliance

CHUNK 38: Licensing — Riggers, Crane Operators and Dual Lifts
Section: CoP s2.8, Appendix 6
Topic: Licensing requirements for riggers, crane operators and dual lift supervision

LICENSING REQUIREMENTS:
- Steel erection supervised by person with MINIMUM BASIC RIGGER'S QUALIFICATION
- Crane operator and riggers/doggers have APPROPRIATE LICENCES
- Dual lifts supervised by person with INTERMEDIATE RIGGER QUALIFICATION
- Licences for work platforms (EWP) or training evidence
- VISUAL VERIFICATION of all occupational licences required; photocopy kept on file

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Steel erection not supervised by person with minimum basic rigger's qualification = serious non-compliance
- Dual lift not supervised by person with intermediate rigger qualification = serious non-compliance
- No visual verification of licences on file = non-compliance

CHUNK 39: Pre-Erection Safety Checks — Crane and Rigging Equipment
Section: CoP Appendix 6
Topic: Mandatory pre-erection checks for crane and rigging equipment

PRE-ERECTION CHECKS:
- Crane CAPABLE OF LIFTING WEIGHTS; all checks and servicing done
- Rigging configuration meets load requirements for dual lifts
- All slings have SWL and CURRENT INSPECTION TAGS
- Ground conditions suitable (level, compacted, outriggers used for slewing cranes, no penetrations or pits in proximity)
- Chains and slings checked
- Crane radius checked
- Tag lines supplied
- Crane fitted with SAFETY HOOK, OVER-HOISTING DEVICE, DEAD-MAN CONTROL ON POWER LOWERING, LOCKOUT CONTROL to prevent free fall

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Slings without current inspection tags = non-compliance
- No exclusion zone installed around erection area = non-compliance
- Crane not fitted with safety hook or over-hoisting device = non-compliance

CHUNK 40: Erection Safety Checks — Prohibited Practices
Section: CoP Appendix 6
Topic: Prohibited practices during steel erection including suicide lifting and hand flame cutting

ERECTION CHECKLIST:
- No SUICIDE LIFTING (lifting such that if rigging fails, steel will strike crane and/or operator)
- Hand flame cutting PROHIBITED per AS 4100 unless specifically required with engineering detail
- Bolts pitched at least ONE THREAD CLEAR OF NUT connection
- STEEL PACKERS ONLY for column
- Work area isolated for safety

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Suicide lifting rigging configurations in use = serious non-compliance
- Hand flame cutting without engineering detail = non-compliance
- Bolts not pitched at least one thread clear of nut = non-compliance

CHUNK 41: Daily Inspections and Toolbox Talks
Section: CoP Appendix 6
Topic: Daily inspection requirements and toolbox talk obligations

ONGOING REQUIREMENTS:
- Daily inspections and DOCUMENTED WEEKLY CHECKLIST on steel and temporary support systems
- COMPETENT PERSON to sign off at end of each day's erection sequence
- TOOLBOX TALK with all relevant parties PRIOR TO WORK COMMENCING EACH DAY

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No toolbox talk conducted before each day's erection work begins = non-compliance
- No documented weekly checklist for steel and temporary supports = non-compliance
- No competent person sign-off at end of each day's erection = non-compliance

CHUNK 42: SWMS Sign-Off and Worker Consultation
Section: CoP Appendix 6
Topic: SWMS sign-off requirements before workers commence steel erection

SUBCONTRACTOR SAFETY DOCUMENTATION:
- SWMS approved, discussed with and SIGNED OFF BY WORKERS before commencing
- Risk assessments/SWMS completed for ALL tasks

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers not signed off SWMS before commencing = non-compliance
- No SWMS for high risk construction work = serious non-compliance
