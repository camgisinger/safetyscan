CHUNK 1: High Risk Construction Work — Definition
Section: s291
Topic: What counts as high risk construction work requiring a SWMS

High risk construction work means construction work that:
(a) Involves a risk of a person FALLING MORE THAN 2m
(b) Is carried out on a telecommunication tower
(c) Involves demolition of a load-bearing element or element related to structural integrity
(d) Involves, or is likely to involve, disturbance of ASBESTOS
(e) Involves structural alterations or repairs that require TEMPORARY SUPPORT to prevent collapse
(f) Is carried out in or near a CONFINED SPACE
(g) Is carried out in or near a SHAFT OR TRENCH with an excavated depth GREATER THAN 1.5m, or a tunnel
(h) Involves the use of EXPLOSIVES
(i) Is carried out on or near PRESSURISED GAS DISTRIBUTION MAINS or piping
(j) Is carried out on or near CHEMICAL, FUEL OR REFRIGERANT LINES
(k) Is carried out on or near ENERGISED ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS OR SERVICES
(l) Is carried out in an area that may have a CONTAMINATED OR FLAMMABLE ATMOSPHERE
(m) Involves TILT-UP OR PRECAST CONCRETE
(n) Is carried out on, in or adjacent to a ROAD, RAILWAY, SHIPPING LANE OR TRAFFIC CORRIDOR in use by traffic
(o) Is carried out in an area with MOVEMENT OF POWERED MOBILE PLANT
(p) Is carried out in ARTIFICIAL EXTREMES OF TEMPERATURE
(q) Is carried out in or near WATER OR LIQUID involving a risk of drowning
(r) Involves DIVING WORK

A CONSTRUCTION PROJECT means construction work where the cost is $250,000 or more.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any work at height >2m without edge protection or fall arrest = non-compliant high risk work
- Excavation >1.5m deep without barriers = high risk work requiring SWMS
- Work near energised electrical services without isolation evidence = high risk work
- Asbestos work without licensed removalist signage = potential non-compliance
- Mobile plant operating in area without exclusion zones = high risk work issue

CHUNK 2: Safe Work Method Statements (SWMS)
Section: s299, s300, s301, s302, s303
Topic: SWMS requirements for high risk construction work

REQUIREMENT: A SWMS must be prepared BEFORE high risk construction work commences.

A SWMS must:
(a) Identify the work that is high risk construction work
(b) State hazards and risks associated with those hazards
(c) Describe the measures to be implemented to control the risks
(d) Describe how control measures are to be implemented, monitored and reviewed
(e) Be readily accessible and understandable to the persons who use it

IMPORTANT: If the SWMS for work involving a fall risk >2m states that ONLY administrative controls or PPE will be used, the SWMS must describe ALL control measures considered and why physical controls were ruled out.

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS:
- High risk work must be carried out IN ACCORDANCE with the SWMS — if not, work must stop immediately
- A copy of the SWMS must be given to the principal contractor before work commences
- The SWMS must be kept until the high risk construction work is completed
- If a notifiable incident occurs, the SWMS must be kept for at least 2 years after the incident

VISUAL INDICATORS OF COMPLIANCE:
- SWMS should be visible/accessible on site for workers doing high risk work
- Work being carried out differently from any visible SWMS = non-compliance
- No SWMS available for high risk work = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 3: WHS Management Plan — Principal Contractor Duties
Section: s308, s309, s310
Topic: Principal contractor obligations for construction projects ($250,000+)

PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR SIGNAGE (s308) — MANDATORY:
Signs must be installed that:
(a) Show the principal contractor's NAME and TELEPHONE CONTACT NUMBERS (including after-hours number)
(b) Show the LOCATION OF THE SITE OFFICE (if any)
(c) Are CLEARLY VISIBLE FROM OUTSIDE the workplace

This is a hard legal requirement — if the sign is missing, it is a direct offence.

WHS MANAGEMENT PLAN (s309) must include:
(a) Names, positions and health and safety responsibilities of all persons with specific WHS roles
(b) Arrangements for consultation, cooperation and coordination between PCBUs
(c) Arrangements for managing WHS incidents
(d) Site-specific health and safety rules and how all persons are informed of them
(e) Arrangements for collecting, assessing, monitoring and reviewing SWMS

VISUAL INDICATORS OF COMPLIANCE:
- Principal contractor signage clearly visible from outside site = compliant
- Missing PC sign = direct offence under s308
- No site office or contact number visible = non-compliant
- Safety notice board visible on site = positive indicator of WHS management plan

CHUNK 4: Falls from Height — General Requirements
Section: s78, s79, s80
Topic: Managing the risk of falls at any workplace

GENERAL DUTY (s78):
A PCBU must manage risks associated with a fall by a person from one level to another that is reasonably likely to cause injury. This includes falls:
- In or on an elevated workplace
- In the vicinity of an opening through which a person could fall
- In the vicinity of an edge over which a person could fall
- On a surface through which a person could fall

PREFERRED APPROACH — work on the ground or solid construction (s78(3)).

"SOLID CONSTRUCTION" means an area that has:
- A surface structurally capable of supporting all persons and things on it
- BARRIERS around its perimeter and any openings to prevent a fall
- An even and readily negotiable surface and gradient
- A safe means of entry and exit

HIERARCHY FOR FALL RISK CONTROL (s79) — in order of preference:
1. FALL PREVENTION DEVICE (first choice, if reasonably practicable):
   - Secure fence
   - Edge protection
   - Working platforms
   - Covers over openings
2. WORK POSITIONING SYSTEM (if fall prevention not practicable)
3. FALL ARREST SYSTEM (last resort — harness, safety nets)

If a fall arrest system is used, EMERGENCY AND RESCUE PROCEDURES must be established and tested (s80).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work at height without any fall prevention = serious non-compliance
- Edge protection present = compliant (subject to specification checks below)
- Harness worn without visible anchor point = potentially non-compliant
- Opening in floor/roof without cover or barrier = non-compliant
- Ladder as only means of access to elevated work area = check if appropriate

CHUNK 5: Falls — Construction Work Specific Requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)
Section: s306C, s306D, s306E
Topic: Specific fall control requirements with exact measurements

TWO THRESHOLD LEVELS:

LOWER THRESHOLD — s306C applies when:
- Housing construction work with fall risk LESS THAN 3m; OR
- Other construction work with fall risk LESS THAN 2m; OR
- Construction work on a roof with slope NOT OVER 26°

At this lower threshold: hazards must be IDENTIFIED and risks ASSESSED before work starts. Control measures must be used.

HIGHER THRESHOLD — s306D applies when:
- Housing construction work with fall risk AT LEAST 3m; OR
- Other construction work with fall risk AT LEAST 2m; OR
- Construction work on a roof with slope OVER 26°

At this higher threshold: MANDATORY control measures BEFORE work starts to either:
(a) PREVENT a person falling any distance (edge protection, fall protection cover, travel restraint system); OR
(b) If prevention not practicable — ARREST the fall (fall arresting platform, fall-arrest harness, industrial safety net)

EDGE PROTECTION SPECIFICATIONS (s306E) — EXACT MEASUREMENTS:
Top rail must be at least:
- 900mm higher than the work surface (where base is at least 1,200mm wide); OR
- 900mm higher than the work surface (in most other cases)

If edge protection has rails, it must have:
- Bottom rail: at least 150mm but NOT OVER 250mm above the base surface (OR a toe board at least 150mm high)
- Gap between rails: NOT OVER 450mm between any rail and the nearest rail
- For slopes over 26°: sturdy mesh/sheeting at least 900mm high from base

Edge protection must be designed to withstand the force of a person falling against it.

VISUAL CHECKLIST FOR EDGE PROTECTION:
✓ Top rail at least 900mm above working surface
✓ No gap greater than 450mm between rails
✓ Bottom rail/toe board between 150-250mm from base
✓ No visible damage or movement
✓ Installed for full length of exposed edge

CHUNK 6: Ladders in Construction
Section: s306K, s306L, s306M
Topic: Ladder requirements and restrictions in construction work

WHAT WORK CAN BE DONE FROM A SINGLE/EXTENSION LADDER (s306K):
Ladders may only be used for construction work (not just access) if the work is "permitted work" — generally only short-duration light duty tasks.

WHEN WORKING FROM A LADDER AT HEIGHT ≥2m (or ≥3m housing) (s306L):
Person must have at least 3 limbs on the ladder at all times (two hands and one foot, or two feet and one hand), OR be prevented from falling by a control measure (e.g. pole strap or fall arrest harness not attached to the ladder).

The ladder must be SECURED:
- At or near the TOP to prevent movement (tied to wall plate, clamped to steel); OR
- At or near the BOTTOM to prevent movement (tied to pegs, held by another person)

LADDER SPECIFICATIONS (s306M):
- Load rating: at least 120kg
- Manufactured for INDUSTRIAL USE (must be marked by manufacturer)
- Maximum lengths:
  - Single ladder: 6.1m
  - Extension ladder (electrical work): 9.2m
  - Other extension ladder: 7.5m
- Must not be used for purposes beyond its design

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Domestic/household ladder on a construction site = non-compliant (must be industrial rated)
- Ladder not secured at top or bottom = non-compliant
- Person on ladder with only 2 limbs in contact = non-compliant
- Ladder longer than 7.5m (non-electrical) = non-compliant
- Ladder used as primary access without fixed ladder or stair alternative (for regular access) = flag

CHUNK 7: Scaffolding Requirements
Section: s306P, s306Q
Topic: Erecting and dismantling scaffolding

ERECTING SCAFFOLDING (s306P):
A person must NOT erect scaffolding where a person could fall ≥2m (other construction) or ≥3m (housing) UNLESS:
- The person erecting is prevented from falling by a control measure; OR
- The person is using a fall arrest harness system; OR
- The following progressive platform system is used:

PROGRESSIVE PLATFORM REQUIREMENTS during erection:
(a) As each level is reached, install IMMEDIATELY:
   - A platform at least 450mm wide along the full length of the section
   - Edge protection across the outer frame at that level
   - Means of access (stairs or ladder) to that level
(b) Before erecting the next level: a platform must be installed below at a distance NOT MORE THAN:
   - 3m below (housing construction); OR
   - 2m below (other construction)

DISMANTLING SCAFFOLDING (s306Q):
Same fall risk thresholds apply. Edge protection and access must be maintained while practicable. Platform at level being dismantled must be at least 450mm wide. Lower platform must be within 2m (3m for housing) of dismantling level.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding being erected/dismantled without fall protection = serious non-compliance
- Missing platforms during erection = non-compliant
- Scaffold platform width visibly less than 450mm = flag
- No edge protection at scaffold perimeter = non-compliant
- Scaffold planks not covering full platform width = non-compliant

HIGH RISK WORK LICENCES REQUIRED FOR SCAFFOLDING:
- Basic scaffolding: prefabricated modular/mobile scaffolding, suspended scaffolding
- Intermediate scaffolding: includes tube and coupler, mast climbing work platforms
- Advanced scaffolding: cantilevered hoists, hung scaffolds, suspended scaffolds
Workers must hold the appropriate licence for the type of scaffolding being erected.

CHUNK 8: Excavation Requirements
Section: s305, s306 (in Chapter 6)
Topic: Managing risks in excavation work

EXCAVATION WORK RISKS that must be managed:
(a) A person FALLING INTO an excavation
(b) A person being TRAPPED BY COLLAPSE of an excavation
(c) A person being STRUCK BY A FALLING THING while working in an excavation
(d) A person being EXPOSED TO AN AIRBORNE CONTAMINANT

TRENCH REQUIREMENTS (s306):
Any trench AT LEAST 1.5m DEEP:
(1) WORK AREA MUST BE SECURED FROM UNAUTHORISED ACCESS — barriers required to prevent inadvertent entry
(2) ALL SIDES MUST BE ADEQUATELY SUPPORTED to minimise collapse risk by one or more of:
   - Shoring (shielding or comparable means)
   - Benching
   - Battering (sloping the sides)

Exception: Shoring/battering/benching NOT required if a GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEER provides WRITTEN ADVICE that the sides are safe from collapse (advice must state the period it applies for).

KEY MEASUREMENTS:
- 1.5m depth = trigger for trench security and shoring requirements
- >1.5m = HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK requiring a SWMS

VISUAL INDICATORS OF NON-COMPLIANCE:
- Trench ≥1.5m deep without visible shoring, benching or battering = serious non-compliance
- No barriers around open trench in area accessible to workers/public = non-compliant
- Vertical walls in sandy/unstable soil without support = imminent collapse risk
- Workers in unsupported trench = life-threatening non-compliance
- No evidence of geotechnical clearance where unshored ≥1.5m trench = flag

CHUNK 9: First Aid Requirements
Section: s42
Topic: First aid obligations at workplaces

A PCBU MUST ensure:
(a) Provision of FIRST AID EQUIPMENT for the workplace
(b) Each worker has ACCESS TO the equipment
(c) Access to FACILITIES FOR ADMINISTRATION of first aid

A PCBU MUST also ensure:
(a) An ADEQUATE NUMBER OF WORKERS are trained to administer first aid; OR
(b) Workers have access to an adequate number of TRAINED FIRST AIDERS

What is "adequate" depends on:
- Nature of the work being carried out
- Nature of the hazards at the workplace
- SIZE AND LOCATION of the workplace
- Number and composition of workers

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aid kit visible and accessible on site = positive
- First aid kit locked away or inaccessible = non-compliant
- No first aid kit visible on site = non-compliant
- First aid sign/location marker visible = positive
- Remote site or high-hazard work with no trained first aider identified = flag

CHUNK 10: Emergency Plans
Section: s43
Topic: Emergency plan requirements

A PCBU MUST prepare an emergency plan for the workplace that provides for:
(a) Emergency procedures including:
   - Effective response to an emergency
   - EVACUATION PROCEDURES
   - Notifying emergency services at the earliest opportunity
   - Medical treatment and assistance
   - Effective communication between the emergency coordinator and all persons
(b) TESTING of emergency procedures including frequency of testing
(c) Information, training and instruction to relevant workers in implementing the procedures

The emergency plan must be MAINTAINED so it remains effective, and IMPLEMENTED in the event of an emergency.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Emergency evacuation plan/muster point sign visible on site = positive
- Emergency assembly point marked = positive
- No emergency information visible on site = flag
- Site with no visible emergency egress signage = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Section: s44
Topic: PPE requirements

PPE is the LAST LINE OF DEFENCE in the hierarchy of controls — it must be used when risks cannot be eliminated or sufficiently controlled by other means.

PCBU MUST ensure PPE is:
(a) SELECTED to minimise risk:
   - Suitable for the nature of the work and hazards
   - A suitable SIZE AND FIT for the worker
   - Reasonably comfortable
(b) MAINTAINED — clean, hygienic, and in good working order
(c) USED correctly by the worker

PPE must be PROVIDED to workers by the PCBU (not sourced by the worker at their own cost).

Workers MUST:
- Wear/use PPE as instructed
- Not misuse or damage PPE
- Report damage or defects

COMMON PPE FOR CONSTRUCTION SITES (visual indicators):
- Hard hat: required on all construction sites
- High-visibility vest: required in traffic management zones, around plant
- Safety footwear (steel-capped boots): required on construction sites
- Safety glasses/goggles: for cutting, grinding, concrete work
- Hearing protection: near loud plant/equipment
- Respiratory protection (P1/P2 mask): for dust, silica, asbestos
- Gloves: for handling materials, chemicals
- Harness: for work at height using fall arrest system

NON-COMPLIANCE INDICATORS:
- Workers without hard hats on construction site = non-compliant
- Workers without hi-vis in traffic/plant zones = non-compliant
- Workers without safety footwear = non-compliant
- Workers doing grinding/cutting without eye protection = non-compliant
- Workers near loud plant without hearing protection = flag
- Damaged or clearly inadequate PPE being used = non-compliant

CHUNK 12: General Construction Induction Training (White Card)
Section: s316, s317
Topic: Construction induction card requirements

EVERY WORKER must have a General Construction Induction Training Card (White Card) before carrying out construction work.

A PCBU MUST NOT direct or allow a worker to carry out construction work unless:
(a) The worker has SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETED general construction induction training; AND
(b) If the training was completed MORE THAN 2 YEARS AGO — the worker has carried out construction work in the preceding 2 years

Workers must hold EITHER:
- A general construction induction training CARD (White Card); OR
- A general construction induction training CERTIFICATION issued within the preceding 60 days (while awaiting the card)

The White Card must be available for inspection on site.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers with White Cards visible on person or available for inspection = compliant
- Worker unable to produce White Card = non-compliant
- New workers on site without White Card = non-compliant for PCBU that allowed this

CHUNK 13: High Risk Work Licences
Section: s81, Schedule 3
Topic: Who needs a licence and for what work

A person MUST NOT carry out high risk work unless they hold a HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE for that class of work.

CLASSES OF HIGH RISK WORK REQUIRING A LICENCE (Schedule 3):

SCAFFOLDING:
- Basic Scaffolding: prefabricated modular/mobile/spur scaffolding, elevated work platforms, boatswain chairs
- Intermediate Scaffolding: includes all Basic + tube and coupler scaffolds, mast climbing work platforms, perimeter safety screens
- Advanced Scaffolding: includes all Intermediate + cantilevered hoists, hung scaffolds, suspended scaffolds

RIGGING:
- Dogging: application of slinging techniques and directing crane operators
- Basic Rigging: structural steel erection, hoists, precast concrete, safety nets
- Intermediate Rigging: includes Basic + hoists with jibs, cranes, tilt slabs, demolition
- Advanced Rigging: includes Intermediate + gin poles, flying foxes, guyed derricks

CRANES:
- Tower crane
- Self-erecting tower crane
- Non-slewing mobile crane (capacity >3t)
- Slewing mobile crane (up to 20t, 60t, unlimited)
- Vehicle loading crane (≥10 tonne-metres capacity)
- Bridge and gantry crane

ELEVATING WORK PLATFORMS:
- Boom-type EWP: use of a boom-type elevated work platform (e.g. cherry picker, knuckle boom)

FORKLIFTS:
- Forklift truck: use of any powered industrial truck with load-lifting attachment

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Person operating a crane without licence card = serious non-compliance
- Scaffolding being erected by unlicensed worker = non-compliance
- EWP operator unable to produce licence = non-compliant
- Forklift being operated without licence = non-compliant
- Licence cards should be available for inspection on site

CHUNK 14: Confined Space Entry
Section: s67 (Chapter 4, Part 4.3, Division 3)
Topic: Confined space entry requirements

A confined space is an enclosed or partially enclosed space that:
- Is not designed or intended for human occupancy
- Is at risk of having an atmosphere that could cause injury or death
- Could have restricted entry or exit

BEFORE ENTRY, the PCBU must:
(a) Carry out an atmosphere test of the confined space
(b) Issue a CONFINED SPACE ENTRY PERMIT
(c) Ensure the confined space is isolated from energy sources
(d) Provide appropriate respiratory equipment, rescue equipment
(e) Ensure a standby person is outside the confined space during entry
(f) Establish rescue procedures

NO PERSON may enter a confined space without a valid entry permit.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- "Confined Space — Do Not Enter" signage on tank/vessel/pit = correct
- Worker entering confined space without visible permit = non-compliant
- No standby person visible outside confined space during entry = non-compliant
- No atmosphere testing equipment visible before entry = flag

CHUNK 15: Noise Exposure
Section: s56, s57
Topic: Managing risk of hearing loss from noise

EXPOSURE STANDARD FOR NOISE:
- LAeq,8h of 85 dB(A) (equivalent continuous sound level over 8 hours), OR
- LC,peak of 140 dB(C) (peak noise level)

If noise levels are at or above the exposure standard, a PCBU must:
(a) Eliminate the risk of hearing loss, so far as is reasonably practicable
(b) If not practicable, minimise the risk using the hierarchy of controls
(c) Provide hearing protectors where noise cannot be adequately reduced

AUDIOMETRIC TESTING must be provided to workers who are frequently required to use hearing protectors.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Loud plant/equipment operating without hearing protection for nearby workers = flag
- Workers wearing hearing protection in noisy areas = positive
- No hearing protection available in high-noise zones = non-compliant

CHUNK 16: Plant Registration and Inspection
Section: s240, s241 (Chapter 5, Part 5.2)
Topic: Plant that must be registered and inspected

PLANT THAT MUST BE REGISTERED (registered plant):
Certain plant must be registered with the regulator before use, including:
- Tower cranes
- Self-erecting tower cranes
- Vehicle loading cranes (≥10 tonne-metres)
- Slewing mobile cranes (≥10t)
- Non-slewing mobile cranes (>3t)
- Hoists (personnel and materials)
- Elevating work platforms (boom type, ≥11m boom)
- Pressure vessels above certain thresholds

PCBU MUST ensure:
- Plant is registered with the regulator
- Current inspection records are maintained
- Plant is not used if it fails inspection or is damaged
- Log books are kept and current

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Registration/compliance plate visible on crane/EWP/hoist = positive
- Missing registration plate = flag — may be unregistered plant
- Plant with obvious damage being operated = non-compliant
- Log book not available for inspection = non-compliant
- Plant without current inspection markings = flag
