CHUNK 1: High Risk Construction Work — Fall and Excavation Thresholds
Section: s291(a), s291(g)
Topic: Visible fall and excavation depth triggers for high risk work classification

HIGH RISK WORK THRESHOLDS (KEY MEASUREMENTS):
- Fall risk: GREATER THAN 2m
- Shaft or trench: excavated depth GREATER THAN 1.5m

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work at height >2m without edge protection or fall arrest = high risk work
- Excavation >1.5m deep without barriers or shoring = high risk work
- Workers on scaffold/roof/slab edge >2m without visible fall controls = flag
- Open trench visibly deeper than 1.5m without support = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 2: High Risk Construction Work — Electrical and Plant Proximity
Section: s291(k), s291(o)
Topic: Work near energised services and mobile plant movement zones

HIGH RISK WORK includes:
- Work on or near ENERGISED ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS OR SERVICES
- Work in area with MOVEMENT OF POWERED MOBILE PLANT

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work near overhead powerlines without isolation evidence = high risk work
- Excavation near underground services without service location = flag
- Mobile plant (excavators, loaders, trucks) operating in area without exclusion zones = high risk work
- Workers on foot in mobile plant operating zone = serious non-compliance risk

---

CHUNK 3: High Risk Construction Work — Asbestos and Demolition
Section: s291(c), s291(d)
Topic: Demolition of structural elements and asbestos disturbance

HIGH RISK WORK includes:
- Demolition of LOAD-BEARING ELEMENT or element related to structural integrity
- Work involving or likely to involve disturbance of ASBESTOS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of structural walls/columns/beams without engineering plan = high risk work
- Asbestos work without licensed removalist signage = potential non-compliance
- Friable asbestos removal without Class A licence signage = serious non-compliance
- Asbestos barrier/exclusion zone signage visible = positive indicator

CHUNK 4: Principal Contractor Signage — Mandatory Display
Section: s308
Topic: Required signage at construction projects $250,000+

MANDATORY SIGNAGE must show:
- Principal contractor's NAME
- TELEPHONE CONTACT NUMBERS (including after-hours number)
- LOCATION OF SITE OFFICE (if any)
- Must be CLEARLY VISIBLE FROM OUTSIDE the workplace

This is a direct legal requirement — missing sign = offence.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Principal contractor sign clearly visible from street/public area = compliant
- No PC sign visible from outside site = direct non-compliance under s308
- Sign present but illegible or faded = non-compliant
- Sign showing only company name without contact numbers = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Falls — Construction Work Thresholds (KEY MEASUREMENTS)
Section: s306C, s306D
Topic: Two-tier fall risk thresholds triggering different control requirements

LOWER THRESHOLD (s306C) — hazard ID and risk assessment required:
- Housing construction: fall risk LESS THAN 3m
- Other construction: fall risk LESS THAN 2m
- Roof work: slope NOT OVER 26°

HIGHER THRESHOLD (s306D) — MANDATORY fall prevention or arrest controls:
- Housing construction: fall risk AT LEAST 3m
- Other construction: fall risk AT LEAST 2m
- Roof work: slope OVER 26°

At higher threshold, MUST use EITHER:
(a) Fall PREVENTION (edge protection, covers, travel restraint); OR
(b) Fall ARREST (harness, safety net, arresting platform)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work at ≥2m (or ≥3m housing) without any fall control = serious non-compliance
- Edge protection present at perimeter = compliant (check measurements below)
- Harness worn without visible anchor point = potentially non-compliant
- Roof slope visibly steep (>26°) without fall controls = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Edge Protection Specifications (KEY MEASUREMENTS)
Section: s306E
Topic: Exact dimensions for compliant edge protection

TOP RAIL HEIGHT:
- At least 900mm above work surface

BOTTOM RAIL OR TOE BOARD:
- At least 150mm but NOT OVER 250mm above base surface; OR
- Toe board at least 150mm high

GAP BETWEEN RAILS:
- NOT OVER 450mm between any rail and nearest rail

SLOPES OVER 26°:
- Sturdy mesh or sheeting at least 900mm high from base

Edge protection must withstand force of person falling against it.

VISUAL CHECKLIST:
- Top rail at least 900mm above working surface
- No gap greater than 450mm between rails
- Bottom rail/toe board between 150–250mm from base
- No visible damage, movement or missing sections
- Installed for full length of exposed edge

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Top rail visibly lower than 900mm = non-compliant
- Gap between rails visibly wider than 450mm = non-compliant
- No bottom rail or toe board = non-compliant
- Edge protection damaged, loose or incomplete = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Ladder Requirements — Securing and Limb Contact
Section: s306L, s306M
Topic: Ladder securing methods and three-point contact rule (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

WHEN WORKING FROM LADDER AT HEIGHT ≥2m (or ≥3m housing):
- Person must have AT LEAST 3 LIMBS on ladder at all times (two hands + one foot, or two feet + one hand); OR
- Be prevented from falling by other control (not harness attached to ladder)

LADDER MUST BE SECURED:
- At or near TOP (tied to wall plate, clamped to steel); OR
- At or near BOTTOM (tied to pegs, held by another person)

LADDER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Load rating: at least 120kg
- Manufactured for INDUSTRIAL USE (must be marked)
- Maximum lengths:
  - Single ladder: 6.1m
  - Extension ladder (electrical): 9.2m
  - Other extension ladder: 7.5m

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Domestic/household ladder on construction site = non-compliant
- Ladder not secured at top or bottom = non-compliant
- Person on ladder with only 2 limbs in contact = non-compliant
- Ladder visibly longer than 7.5m (non-electrical) = non-compliant
- Ladder leaning unsecured against wall = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Scaffolding Erection — Progressive Platform System
Section: s306P
Topic: Platform width and spacing during scaffold erection (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

PROGRESSIVE PLATFORM REQUIREMENTS during erection where fall risk ≥2m (or ≥3m housing):

AS EACH LEVEL IS REACHED, install IMMEDIATELY:
- Platform at least 450mm wide along full length of section
- Edge protection across outer frame at that level
- Means of access (stairs or ladder) to that level

BEFORE ERECTING NEXT LEVEL:
- Platform must be installed below at distance NOT MORE THAN:
  - 3m below (housing construction)
  - 2m below (other construction)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding being erected without fall protection = serious non-compliance
- Missing platforms during erection = non-compliant
- Scaffold platform width visibly less than 450mm = non-compliant
- Vertical gap between platforms exceeding 2m (or 3m housing) = non-compliant
- No edge protection at scaffold perimeter = non-compliant
- Scaffold planks not covering full platform width = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Excavation — Trench Depth Trigger and Support Requirements
Section: s306
Topic: Trench depth threshold and mandatory support methods (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

TRENCH AT LEAST 1.5m DEEP — MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:

(1) WORK AREA MUST BE SECURED from unauthorised access — barriers required

(2) ALL SIDES MUST BE ADEQUATELY SUPPORTED by one or more of:
   - Shoring (shielding or comparable means)
   - Benching
   - Battering (sloping the sides)

EXCEPTION: Shoring/battering/benching NOT required if GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEER provides WRITTEN ADVICE that sides are safe from collapse.

KEY MEASUREMENT:
- 1.5m depth = trigger for trench security and shoring requirements

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trench ≥1.5m deep without visible shoring, benching or battering = serious non-compliance
- No barriers around open trench in accessible area = non-compliant
- Vertical walls in sandy/unstable soil without support = imminent collapse risk
- Workers in unsupported trench ≥1.5m = life-threatening non-compliance
- Shoring/shielding visible in trench = compliant
- Battered (sloped) trench sides = compliant
- Benched (stepped) trench sides = compliant

CHUNK 10: PPE — Hard Hats, Hi-Vis and Safety Footwear
Section: s44
Topic: Mandatory PPE visible on construction workers

COMMON MANDATORY PPE FOR CONSTRUCTION (visual indicators):
- Hard hat: required on all construction sites
- High-visibility vest: required in traffic management zones, around mobile plant
- Safety footwear (steel-capped boots): required on construction sites
- Safety glasses/goggles: for cutting, grinding, concrete work
- Hearing protection: near loud plant/equipment
- Respiratory protection (P1/P2 mask): for dust, silica, asbestos
- Gloves: for handling materials, chemicals
- Harness: for work at height using fall arrest system

PPE must be:
- Suitable size and fit for worker
- Maintained clean and in good working order
- Used correctly by worker

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers without hard hats on construction site = non-compliant
- Workers without hi-vis in traffic/plant zones = non-compliant
- Workers without safety footwear = non-compliant
- Workers doing grinding/cutting without eye protection = non-compliant
- Workers near loud plant without hearing protection = flag
- Damaged or clearly inadequate PPE being used = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: White Card — Construction Induction Training
Section: s316, s317
Topic: General construction induction card requirement for all construction workers

EVERY WORKER must have White Card before carrying out construction work.

Workers must hold EITHER:
- General construction induction training CARD (White Card); OR
- General construction induction training CERTIFICATION issued within preceding 60 days (while awaiting card)

Card must be available for inspection on site.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers with White Cards visible on person or available for inspection = compliant
- Worker unable to produce White Card = non-compliant
- New workers on site without White Card = non-compliant for PCBU

CHUNK 12: High Risk Work Licences — Scaffolding Classes
Section: s81, Schedule 3
Topic: Scaffolding licence classes and what work each permits

SCAFFOLDING LICENCE CLASSES:

BASIC SCAFFOLDING:
- Prefabricated modular scaffolding
- Mobile scaffolding
- Spur scaffolding
- Elevated work platforms
- Boatswain chairs

INTERMEDIATE SCAFFOLDING (includes all Basic plus):
- Tube and coupler scaffolds
- Mast climbing work platforms
- Perimeter safety screens

ADVANCED SCAFFOLDING (includes all Intermediate plus):
- Cantilevered hoists
- Hung scaffolds
- Suspended scaffolds

Person must hold appropriate licence for type of scaffolding being erected.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding being erected by unlicensed worker = non-compliance
- Tube and coupler scaffold erected by Basic licence holder only = non-compliant
- Licence cards should be available for inspection on site
- Suspended scaffold erected by Intermediate licence holder = non-compliant (requires Advanced)

CHUNK 13: High Risk Work Licences — Cranes and EWPs
Section: s81, Schedule 3
Topic: Crane and elevating work platform licence requirements

CRANE LICENCES:
- Tower crane
- Self-erecting tower crane
- Non-slewing mobile crane (capacity >3t)
- Slewing mobile crane (up to 20t, 60t, unlimited)
- Vehicle loading crane (≥10 tonne-metres capacity)
- Bridge and gantry crane

ELEVATING WORK PLATFORM:
- Boom-type EWP (e.g. cherry picker, knuckle boom)

FORKLIFT:
- Forklift truck (any powered industrial truck with load-lifting attachment)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Person operating crane without licence card = serious non-compliance
- EWP operator unable to produce licence = non-compliant
- Forklift being operated without licence = non-compliant
- Licence cards should be available for inspection on site
- Crane operating without visible registration plate = flag (see plant registration)

CHUNK 14: Confined Space Entry — Permit and Standby Person
Section: s67
Topic: Confined space entry controls visible from outside

BEFORE ENTRY:
- Atmosphere test must be carried out
- CONFINED SPACE ENTRY PERMIT must be issued
- STANDBY PERSON must be outside confined space during entry

NO PERSON may enter without valid permit.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- "Confined Space — Do Not Enter" signage on tank/vessel/pit = correct
- Worker entering confined space without visible permit = non-compliant
- No standby person visible outside confined space during entry = non-compliant
- No atmosphere testing equipment visible before entry = flag
- Permit displayed at entry point = positive indicator

CHUNK 15: Plant Registration — Compliance Plates
Section: s240, s241
Topic: Registered plant identification and inspection markings

PLANT THAT MUST BE REGISTERED:
- Tower cranes
- Self-erecting tower cranes
- Vehicle loading cranes (≥10 tonne-metres)
- Slewing mobile cranes (≥10t)
- Non-slewing mobile cranes (>3t)
- Hoists (personnel and materials)
- Elevating work platforms (boom type, ≥11m boom)
- Pressure vessels above certain thresholds

Plant must have registration/compliance plate visible.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Registration/compliance plate visible on crane/EWP/hoist = positive
- Missing registration plate = flag — may be unregistered plant
- Plant with obvious damage being operated = non-compliant
- Log book not available for inspection = non-compliant
- Plant without current inspection markings = flag

Section: s42, s43

FIRST AID (s42):

- Workers must have access to equipment

CHUNK 17: Noise Exposure — Hearing Protection in High-Noise Zones
Section: s56, s57
Topic: Hearing protection use near loud plant and equipment

EXPOSURE STANDARD:
- LAeq,8h of 85 dB(A); OR
- LC,peak of 140 dB(C)

If noise at or above standard, hearing protectors must be provided where noise cannot be adequately reduced.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Loud plant/equipment operating without hearing protection for nearby workers = flag
- Workers wearing hearing protection (earmuffs/plugs) in noisy areas = positive
- No hearing protection available in high-noise zones = non-compliant
- Jackhammers, concrete saws, grinders operating without worker hearing protection = flag
