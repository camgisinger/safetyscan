CHUNK 1: Primary Duties and Application
Section: s1.3, s1.4, s2.2, s2.5
Topic: Legislative framework and who this CoP applies to

PRIMARY DUTY: A PCBU must ensure, as far as reasonably practicable, that workers and other persons are not exposed to health and safety risks arising from construction or maintenance work on or adjacent to a road.

APPLICATION — this CoP applies to:
- Construction work on or adjacent to a road: building roads, laying pipes, excavation
- Maintenance work on or adjacent to a road: mowing medians, tree trimming, utility service work, pothole repair

PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR:
- Required for construction projects costing MORE THAN $250,000
- Must prepare a WRITTEN WHS MANAGEMENT PLAN before work starts
- The plan must include or attach a TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT PLAN OR GUIDANCE SCHEME (per MUTCD Part 3)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers trimming trees on a median strip without any traffic management controls = application of this CoP; duty breach
- WHS management plan available on site for a road project costing >$250,000 = positive compliance
- Maintenance workers on a road-adjacent area without high-visibility garments = primary duty failure
- No traffic management plan on a construction project adjacent to a live road = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 2: High Risk Construction Work and SWMS
Section: s2.1, s2.3, s3.1
Topic: SWMS requirements for road-adjacent work

CLASSIFICATION: Construction work on or adjacent to a road is defined as HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK under the WHS Regulation.

SWMS REQUIREMENT: A PCBU must ensure a SAFE WORK METHOD STATEMENT IS PREPARED before work starts. The SWMS must:
- Identify the work that is high risk construction work
- Specify hazards and risks associated with the work
- Describe control measures (including compliance with MUTCD Part 3)
- Describe how control measures are to be implemented, monitored and reviewed
- Be SITE-SPECIFIC — generic SWMS without site-specific details (actual lane configuration, speed zone, device layout) are not acceptable

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work commencing on a road-adjacent area without a written SWMS on site = regulatory breach
- Generic SWMS not updated with site-specific details (actual lane configuration, speed environment) = process failure
- Workers performing tasks outside the work zone delineated in the SWMS = non-compliance
- SWMS not available for inspection on site = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Traffic Management Principles and MUTCD Compliance
Section: s4.2, s4.3, s4.4
Topic: Planning and placement of traffic control devices

TECHNICAL STANDARD: MUTCD Part 3 is the PRIMARY TECHNICAL STANDARD for temporary traffic control signs, devices, distances, and layouts. All traffic guidance schemes must comply.

BASIC MANDATORY PRINCIPLES:
- Signs and devices MUST BE ERECTED AND DISPLAYED BEFORE WORK COMMENCES — not after plant has started
- Devices MUST BE REGULARLY CHECKED AND MAINTAINED throughout the shift
- Signs MUST BE REMOVED AS SOON AS PRACTICABLE after work is complete
- Appropriate warning signs SHOULD REMAIN in place until related work (loose stone removal, line marking) is also completed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Roadwork signs found face-down, damaged, or blown over during active work = maintenance failure
- Worker setting up traffic cones AFTER heavy plant has already begun excavating = timing breach — devices must precede work
- "Workers Ahead" or "Road Works" signs present when no workers are on site (e.g. during lunch, after hours) = planning failure — must be removed or covered
- Traffic guidance scheme devices not matching the layout in the site SWMS or traffic management plan = non-compliant implementation

CHUNK 4: Traffic Controller Roles and Prohibitions
Section: s5.1, s5.2, s5.3
Topic: Mandatory standards for accredited traffic controllers

SPEED LIMIT RESTRICTION: A traffic controller MUST NOT DIRECT TRAFFIC through a worksite unless the APPROACH SPEED IS 60 km/hr OR LESS.
- Work requiring traffic control in 80 km/hr or higher zones requires additional engineering controls — a traffic controller alone is not sufficient

ALCOHOL: A traffic controller MUST MAINTAIN A ZERO PER CENT BLOOD ALCOHOL CONCENTRATION while performing functions.

MOBILE PHONES: MUST NOT be used in any circumstances while directing traffic.

MANDATORY SIGNAGE: The "TRAFFIC CONTROLLER AHEAD / PREPARE TO STOP" sign MUST BE:
- Erected correctly at the START OF A SHIFT
- REMOVED when work is suspended or completed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic controller directing traffic in an 80 km/hr or higher zone = serious safety breach
- Traffic controller using a mobile phone while holding a Stop/Slow bat = prohibited activity
- "Traffic Controller Ahead" sign missing while a controller is active on the road = mandatory signage failure
- Traffic controller visibly impaired or showing signs of alcohol or drug use = zero-tolerance breach
- Traffic controller wearing non-high-visibility clothing = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Training, Accreditation, and Certifications
Section: s5.3, s6.2, s6.3
Topic: Mandatory certifications for traffic controllers and site workers

TRAFFIC CONTROLLER ACCREDITATION:
- PCBUs must use ONLY ACCREDITED TRAFFIC CONTROLLERS to perform controller duties
- Controllers must CARRY THEIR IDENTITY CARD AT ALL TIMES while working
- Must present accreditation card on request

GENERAL SITE INDUCTION: All workers must have SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETED a general construction safety induction (White Card) before working on site.

MUTCD TRAINING LEVELS — three tiers:
1. WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC (Parts 1 and 2): For workers on or adjacent to a road who are not controlling traffic
2. TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT IMPLEMENT: For persons implementing (setting up) a traffic management plan or guidance scheme
3. TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT DESIGN: For persons designing traffic management plans and guidance schemes

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker on site unable to produce a White Card or equivalent induction evidence = training breach
- Traffic controller refusing to present accreditation ID card = compliance failure
- Complex traffic plan designed by a person without Traffic Management Design qualification = accreditation breach
- Worker installing devices without Traffic Management Implement qualification = non-compliant
- No evidence of any MUTCD training for road-adjacent workers = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Site Routines and Record Keeping
Section: s7.1, s7.4, s7.5, s7.6
Topic: Mandatory inspections and daily documentation

REQUIRED RECORDS: Daily records of the TRAFFIC GUIDANCE SCHEME (including regulatory signs and speed restrictions) MUST BE KEPT in a site diary or work sheet.

DAILY ROUTINES:

BEFORE WORK:
- Inspect all signs and devices
- Check water-ballasted barriers for damage and water level
- Conduct an INSPECTION DRIVE-THROUGH from a road-user perspective before opening to traffic

DURING WORK:
- Periodically drive through to check signs are satisfactory from a road-user perspective
- Move personnel clear of traffic hazard zones during breaks

END OF DAY:
- Carry out pre-closedown inspection
- REMOVE "Traffic Controller Ahead" signs
- Affix and light lamps on advance signs if work zone will be left overnight

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Site diary with no record of sign arrangement, changes, or inspection times = record-keeping failure
- Water-ballasted barrier modules that are empty or visibly damaged = maintenance failure
- No evidence of morning drive-through inspection = routine breach
- Work zone left overnight without lamps on advance signs = non-compliant after-hours setup
- Site diary not available for inspection = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Night Work and Low-Light Conditions
Section: s8.2, s8.3
Topic: Additional requirements when visibility is reduced

NIGHT WORK DEFINITION: Conditions from sunset to sunrise OR any time when there is INSUFFICIENT LIGHT TO SEE A PERSON OR VEHICLE AT 150 METRES.

NIGHT WORK REQUIREMENTS:
- At least TWO WORKERS at any time during night work
- Workers must wear RETROREFLECTIVE CLOTHING (vests AND gaiters)
- Traffic cones must have RETROREFLECTIVE BANDS — plain cones are non-compliant at night

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Night works in progress with only ONE WORKER on site = safety breach
- Traffic cones used at night WITHOUT RETROREFLECTIVE BANDS = visibility failure
- Workers wearing high-vis vests but not retroreflective gaiters during night work = incomplete PPE
- Work area at night with no additional lighting beyond standard traffic devices = flag

CHUNK 8: Environmental Hazards
Section: s8.4, s8.7, s8.8, s8.9
Topic: Heat, lightning, wet weather, and manual tasks

HEAT AND UV:
- UV radiation risk is highest BETWEEN 9am AND 3pm
- Workers should be provided shade, sun protection, and hydration

LIGHTNING:
- Metal objects (including metal Stop/Go bats) can act as conductors during electrical storms
- Workers MUST BE PROTECTED from lightning strikes during thunderstorms
- Work should cease and workers shelter during active lightning

WET WEATHER:
- Increases risk of SLIPPERY SURFACES for workers and vehicles
- Significantly increases risk of EXCAVATIONS CAVING IN — trench shoring requirements become more critical
- Electric cables must not be left lying on wet ground — must be raised

RECYCLED WATER:
- Non-potable water used for dust control or barrier filling is a "substance" under the WHS Act
- PCBUs must prevent injury or illness from its use (bacterial contamination risk)
- Warning signs required for public if recycled water is used for dust suppression

MANUAL TASKS — sign and device handling involves grasping, carrying, and moving loads:
- Bend at the knees
- Use team lifting or obtain assistance for heavy items
- Use mechanical aids where available

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic controller continuing to hold a METAL STOP/GO BAT during an active electrical storm = critical life-safety risk
- Single worker attempting to manually lift a large concrete barrier module = hazardous manual task
- Electric cables lying directly on wet ground during rain = non-compliant weather control
- Workers using recycled water for dust suppression without public warning signs = health risk management failure
- Workers with no shade, sun protection, or water during peak UV hours = heat management failure
