CHUNK 1: Traffic Controller Speed Limit and Signage
Section: s5.1, s5.2
Topic: Maximum approach speed for traffic controller operations and mandatory signage (KEY MEASUREMENT)

MAXIMUM APPROACH SPEED:
- Traffic controller MUST NOT direct traffic if approach speed exceeds 60 km/hr
- Work in 80 km/hr or higher zones requires additional engineering controls — controller alone is insufficient

MANDATORY SIGNAGE:
- "TRAFFIC CONTROLLER AHEAD / PREPARE TO STOP" sign MUST be erected at start of shift
- Sign MUST be removed when work suspended or completed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic controller directing traffic in 80 km/hr or higher zone = serious non-compliance
- "Traffic Controller Ahead" sign missing while controller active = mandatory signage failure
- Sign still displayed when no controller present = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 2: Traffic Controller Prohibitions and Accreditation
Section: s5.2, s5.3
Topic: Prohibited activities and mandatory accreditation requirements

PROHIBITED:
- Mobile phone use while directing traffic — ANY USE
- Blood alcohol concentration above ZERO while performing functions

ACCREDITATION:
- Only ACCREDITED traffic controllers may perform controller duties
- Must CARRY identity card at all times while working
- Must present card on request

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic controller using mobile phone while holding Stop/Slow bat = prohibited activity
- Controller unable to produce accreditation card = compliance failure
- Controller showing signs of impairment = zero-tolerance breach

---

CHUNK 3: Night Work Staffing and Equipment Requirements
Section: s8.2, s8.3
Topic: Minimum staffing and mandatory equipment for night operations (KEY MEASUREMENT)

NIGHT WORK DEFINITION:
- Sunset to sunrise OR insufficient light to see person/vehicle at 150 METRES

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum TWO WORKERS on site at all times during night work
- Workers must wear RETROREFLECTIVE clothing — vests AND gaiters
- Traffic cones must have RETROREFLECTIVE BANDS — plain cones non-compliant

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Night work with only ONE worker visible = safety breach
- Traffic cones without retroreflective bands at night = visibility failure
- Workers wearing hi-vis vests but no retroreflective gaiters at night = incomplete PPE

---

CHUNK 4: Device Timing and Maintenance
Section: s4.2, s4.3, s4.4
Topic: Mandatory timing for sign/device placement and ongoing maintenance

TIMING REQUIREMENTS:
- Signs and devices MUST be erected BEFORE work commences — not after plant starts
- Signs MUST be removed as soon as practicable after work complete
- Warning signs should remain until related work (loose stone removal, line marking) also complete

MAINTENANCE:
- Devices MUST be regularly checked and maintained throughout shift

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker setting up cones AFTER excavator already operating = timing breach
- "Road Works" signs present when no workers on site (lunch, after hours) = planning failure
- Signs face-down, damaged, or blown over during active work = maintenance failure

---

CHUNK 5: High Visibility Garments and PPE
Section: s8.2, s8.3; AS/NZS 4602
Topic: Mandatory high-visibility clothing requirements

DAY WORK:
- High visibility safety vest mandatory for all workers on or adjacent to road

NIGHT WORK:
- RETROREFLECTIVE clothing mandatory — vests AND gaiters
- Standard high-vis vests alone insufficient at night

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers on road-adjacent area without high-vis vests = primary duty failure
- Night workers wearing only standard hi-vis vest without gaiters = incomplete night PPE
- Workers in non-high-visibility clothing = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 6: Lightning and Metal Objects
Section: s8.7
Topic: Lightning strike protection and metal conductor hazards

LIGHTNING HAZARD:
- Metal objects (including metal Stop/Go bats) act as conductors during electrical storms
- Workers MUST be protected from lightning strikes during thunderstorms
- Work should cease and workers shelter during active lightning

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic controller holding METAL Stop/Go bat during active electrical storm = critical life-safety risk
- Workers continuing road work during visible lightning without shelter = non-compliant weather control

---

CHUNK 7: Wet Weather Controls
Section: s8.8
Topic: Additional hazards and controls during wet conditions

WET WEATHER HAZARDS:
- Slippery surfaces for workers and vehicles
- Significantly increased excavation cave-in risk — shoring becomes more critical
- Electric cables MUST NOT lie on wet ground — must be raised

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Electric cables lying directly on wet ground during rain = non-compliant
- Excavation without additional shoring during heavy rain = elevated cave-in risk

---

CHUNK 8: Overnight Work Zone Requirements
Section: s7.6
Topic: Mandatory controls when work zone left overnight

END OF DAY REQUIREMENTS:
- "Traffic Controller Ahead" signs MUST be removed
- Lamps MUST be affixed and lit on advance signs if work zone left overnight

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work zone left overnight without lamps on advance signs = non-compliant after-hours setup
- "Traffic Controller Ahead" sign still displayed overnight with no controller = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 9: Water-Ballasted Barriers
Section: s7.4
Topic: Daily inspection requirements for water-filled barrier modules

DAILY INSPECTION REQUIRED:
- Check for damage
- Check water level

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Water-ballasted barrier modules visibly empty = maintenance failure
- Barriers with visible damage (cracks, splits, deformation) = non-compliant condition

---

CHUNK 10: Recycled Water Warning Signs
Section: s8.9
Topic: Public warning requirements when using non-potable water

RECYCLED WATER USE:
- Non-potable water used for dust control or barrier filling is a "substance" under WHS Act
- Warning signs REQUIRED for public if recycled water used for dust suppression

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers using recycled water for dust suppression without public warning signs = health risk management failure
- Visible recycled water use (purple-tinted water, tanker markings) without signage = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 11: Manual Handling of Traffic Devices
Section: s8.9
Topic: Safe lifting and handling requirements for signs and barriers

MANUAL TASK CONTROLS:
- Bend at knees when lifting
- Use team lifting or obtain assistance for heavy items
- Use mechanical aids where available

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Single worker attempting to manually lift large concrete barrier module = hazardous manual task
- Worker lifting heavy sign with bent back/straight legs = poor manual handling technique

---

CHUNK 12: Heat and UV Protection Timing
Section: s8.4
Topic: Peak UV radiation period and worker protection (KEY MEASUREMENT)

UV RADIATION RISK:
- Highest BETWEEN 9am AND 3pm
- Workers should be provided shade, sun protection, and hydration

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers with no shade, sun protection, or water during peak UV hours (9am–3pm) = heat management failure
- No water supply visible on site during hot conditions = inadequate hydration provision

---

CHUNK 13: MUTCD Compliance and Device Layout
Section: s4.2, s4.3
Topic: Technical standard compliance for traffic control devices

TECHNICAL STANDARD:
- MUTCD Part 3 is PRIMARY TECHNICAL STANDARD for temporary traffic control signs, devices, distances, and layouts
- All traffic guidance schemes must comply

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic guidance scheme devices not matching MUTCD Part 3 specifications = non-compliant implementation
- Sign placement distances not matching MUTCD Part 3 requirements = technical breach
- Device layout not matching site SWMS or traffic management plan = non-compliant implementation

---

CHUNK 14: Construction Project Value Threshold
Section: s2.5
Topic: Written WHS management plan requirement trigger (KEY MEASUREMENT)

PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR REQUIREMENT:
- Required for construction projects costing MORE THAN $250,000
- Must prepare WRITTEN WHS MANAGEMENT PLAN before work starts
- Plan must include or attach TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT PLAN OR GUIDANCE SCHEME (per MUTCD Part 3)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- WHS management plan available on site for road project costing >$250,000 = positive compliance
- No traffic management plan on construction project adjacent to live road = serious non-compliance
- Generic plan without site-specific details (actual lane configuration, speed zone) = process failure

---

CHUNK 15: White Card and MUTCD Training Evidence
Section: s5.3, s6.2, s6.3
Topic: Mandatory certifications and training levels

GENERAL INDUCTION:
- All workers must have completed general construction safety induction (White Card) before working on site

MUTCD TRAINING LEVELS:
1. WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC (Parts 1 and 2): Workers on/adjacent to road not controlling traffic
2. TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT IMPLEMENT: Persons setting up traffic management plan/guidance scheme
3. TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT DESIGN: Persons designing traffic management plans

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker unable to produce White Card or equivalent = training breach
- Worker installing devices without Traffic Management Implement qualification = non-compliant
- No evidence of MUTCD training for road-adjacent workers = non-compliant

CHUNK 16: Site Diary and Daily Records
Section: s7.1, s7.5
Topic: Mandatory documentation and inspection records

REQUIRED RECORDS:
- Daily records of TRAFFIC GUIDANCE SCHEME (including regulatory signs and speed restrictions) MUST be kept in site diary or worksheet

DAILY INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:

BEFORE WORK:
- Inspect all signs and devices
- Conduct INSPECTION DRIVE-THROUGH from road-user perspective before opening to traffic

DURING WORK:
- Periodically drive through to check signs satisfactory from road-user perspective

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Site diary with no record of sign arrangement, changes, or inspection times = record-keeping failure
- No evidence of morning drive-through inspection = routine breach
- Site diary not available for inspection = non-compliant
