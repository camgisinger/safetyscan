CHUNK 1: High Risk Construction Work Triggers — SWMS Mandatory
Section: CoP s1.1; WHS Regulation s289, s299
Topic: Depth and activity triggers requiring Safe Work Method Statements for excavation work

SWMS MANDATORY for excavation work involving:
- Trench or shaft DEEPER THAN 1.5 METRES
- Any tunnel work
- Fall risk GREATER THAN 2 METRES
- Asbestos disturbance
- Work near energised electrical installations
- Work near pressurised gas distribution mains or piping
- Work near chemical, fuel or refrigerant lines
- Use of explosives
- Powered mobile plant that may collide with pedestrians or other plant

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trench/shaft >1.5m deep with no SWMS on site = serious non-compliance
- Workers entering excavations >1.5m deep without ground support installed = serious non-compliance
- Any tunnel work without SWMS = serious non-compliance
- Excavation work with powered mobile plant near workers without SWMS = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Trench Depth Trigger — 1.5 Metres for Ground Support and Securing
Section: CoP s3.7, s5.1; WHS Regulation s306
Topic: Mandatory requirements when trench depth reaches or exceeds 1.5 metres

TRENCH ≥1.5 METRES DEEP — MANDATORY:
- Work area MUST be secured from unauthorised access including inadvertent entry
- ALL SIDES of trench must be adequately supported by ONE OR MORE of:
  - SHORING (shielding or comparable means)
  - BENCHING
  - BATTERING

EXCEPTION — ground support not required IF:
- Written advice from GEOTECHNICAL ENGINEER states all sides are safe from collapse
- Advice must state the period of time it applies to
- May be subject to conditions

ENGULFMENT RISK:
- Where risk of engulfment exists when worker enters trench: implement controls REGARDLESS OF DEPTH

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trench ≥1.5m deep with no shoring, benching or battering = serious non-compliance
- Workers inside trench ≥1.5m deep without ground support = serious non-compliance; stop work
- Trench ≥1.5m deep with no perimeter fencing or barriers = serious non-compliance
- No geotechnical written advice where ground support omitted = non-compliance
- Trench of any depth in sandy/wet soil with workers inside and no support = implement controls immediately

CHUNK 3: Benching and Battering — Maximum Angle 45 Degrees
Section: CoP s6.2
Topic: Angle limits and design requirements for benching and battering as ground collapse controls

MAXIMUM ANGLE OF REPOSE:
- 45 DEGREES SHOULD NOT BE EXCEEDED
- Exception: unless designed by competent person and certified in writing

BENCHING:
- Series of horizontal steps with vertical surfaces between levels
- Benches must be wide enough to stabilise slopes and prevent material from top falling to working area
- Benches should be sloped to reduce water scouring

BATTERING:
- Wall of excavation sloped back to predetermined angle
- Should start from BOTTOM of excavation
- Safe slope = ground is stable, does not flatten over time, no material movement down slope, toe remains in same place

DESIGN REQUIREMENT:
- Control measures should be designed by competent person (geotechnical engineer)
- Relative to: soil type, moisture content, planned height of excavated face, surcharge loads

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation walls sloped at more than 45 degrees without geotechnical certification = non-compliance
- Benching or battering where slope is flattening or material is moving = imminent collapse risk; immediate action required
- No re-assessment of battered/benched excavation after heavy rain = flag
- Benches too narrow to stabilise material from top levels = flag

CHUNK 4: Spoil and Load Clearance — Minimum 1000mm from Edge
Section: CoP s4.1; Figure 2, Figure 15
Topic: Minimum clearance distances for excavated material, vehicles and loads near excavations

MINIMUM CLEARANCE:
- 1000mm (1 METRE) from edge of excavation for vehicles and spoil heaps
- Applies to both shoring configurations

ZONE OF INFLUENCE:
- Mechanical plant, vehicles and storage of materials (including excavated material or heavy loads) SHOULD NOT be located in zone of influence UNLESS ground support system designed by competent person to carry such loads

SPOIL PLACEMENT CONSIDERATIONS:
- Placing material on LOWER SIDE reduces effective height and reduces risk of material falling or being washed in
- TOEBOARD required where material placed on uphill side
- Material on high side must not increase risk of ground collapse or flooding
- Excavated material should channel rainwater AWAY from excavation

OLD SERVICE LINES:
- Place excavated material on side OPPOSITE old service line to prevent excessive loading on previously weakened ground

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Spoil heaps or plant positioned less than 1000mm from edge of unsupported excavation = non-compliance
- Vehicles operating within zone of influence without shoring designed to carry those loads = serious hazard
- Excavated material placed on uphill side with no toeboard = flag
- Spoil heap positioned to channel water toward excavation = flag

CHUNK 5: Ground Collapse Warning Signs — Immediate Hazards
Section: CoP s6.1
Topic: Visual indicators of imminent ground collapse requiring immediate action

SIGNS OF IMMINENT COLLAPSE:
- Water seeping into excavation
- TENSION CRACKS on surface near trench edge
- Building side walls showing distortion
- Seepage along slipping plane
- Areas of tension as wall starts to collapse

CRITICAL WEIGHT REFERENCE:
- Sand wedge over 1 METRE length of 2-METRE-deep trench weighs approximately 3 TONNES

FACTORS INCREASING SOIL STRESS:
- Deep cuts and steep slopes
- Loads on ground surface near top of face
- Shock and vibration (pile-driving, blasting, passing loads, vibration-producing plant)
- Water pressure from groundwater flow
- Soil saturation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tension cracks visible on surface adjacent to open trench = IMMEDIATE hazard; work must stop
- Water seeping into trench walls = imminent collapse risk; flag immediately
- Hard compact soil excavation with no shoring or benching = serious risk — "looks safe" is not safe
- Vibrating plant operating adjacent to open unsupported excavation = serious hazard
- Prolonged inactivity at excavation site with no re-evaluation of soil conditions = flag

CHUNK 6: Shoring — Timber Soldier Set Specifications
Section: CoP s6.3; Figure 15
Topic: Exact measurements for timber soldier set installations

TIMBER SOLDIER SET SPECIFICATIONS:
- Soldiers protrude 500mm ABOVE TOP of trench
- Top tom: no lower than 300mm from trench top
- Maximum spacing of toms: 750mm
- Maximum spacing between soldier sets: 1.5 METRES
- Soldier minimum size: 150mm × 38mm
- Tom minimum size: 150mm × 38mm
- Space between bottom tom and trench floor: normally no more than 1000mm
- Spoil heap: at least 1000mm clear of excavation

USE LIMITATION:
- Open soldier sets ONLY suitable for STABLE SOIL TYPES (rock, stiff clays)
- Only provide ground support at regular intervals — NOT positive ground support to whole excavated face

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Open soldier sets installed in unstable or wet/sandy soil = serious non-compliance
- Soldier set spacing exceeding 1.5m = non-compliance
- Tom spacing exceeding 750mm = non-compliance
- Soldiers not protruding 500mm above trench top = non-compliance
- Top tom lower than 300mm from trench top = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Shoring — Steel Sheet Piling Minimum Height
Section: CoP s6.3
Topic: Minimum protrusion height for steel sheet piling above ground surface

STEEL SHEET PILING:
- MINIMUM HEIGHT ABOVE SURFACE: 300mm
- Generally used for major excavations (large building foundations, large embankments)
- Used where excavation in close proximity to adjoining buildings
- Can be installed PRIOR TO excavation work commencing
- Must be installed BEFORE excavation starts when ground so unstable that sidewall collapse likely during excavation (loose and running sand)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Sheet piling less than 300mm above surface level = non-compliance
- Sheet piling not installed before excavation in loose/running sand = serious hazard

CHUNK 8: Hydraulic Shoring — Inspection and Maintenance Requirements
Section: CoP s6.3
Topic: Inspection frequency and defect identification for hydraulic shoring systems

HYDRAULIC SHORING:
- Must be designed by competent person in consultation with geotechnical engineer
- Hydraulic capacity must resist expected ground pressures and potential for collapse
- FREQUENT INSPECTIONS of pressure hoses and rams required to detect: abrasion, fatigue, damage, bent or notched rams
- When trench fully supported: hydraulic supports should be dismantled to prevent costly damage
- Must be inspected, repaired if necessary and carefully stored prior to re-use

Standard: AS 5047–2005: Hydraulic shoring and trench lining equipment

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hydraulic shoring with damaged hoses = non-compliance; remove from service
- Bent or notched rams on hydraulic shoring = non-compliance; remove from service
- Hydraulic shoring with visible leaks = flag; remove from service
- No documented inspection records for hydraulic shoring = flag

CHUNK 9: Shoring Removal — Sequence and Worker Protection
Section: CoP s6.4
Topic: Safe removal sequence and worker protection during shoring dismantling

SHORING REMOVAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be removed in REVERSE ORDER TO ITS INSTALLATION
- Persons performing work SHOULD NOT work OUTSIDE THE PROTECTION of ground support system during removal
- NO PART of ground support system should be removed UNTIL TRENCH IS READY FOR FINAL BACKFILL AND COMPACTION
- Temporary structural members may need to be installed before removal begins

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Shoring being removed before trench ready for final backfill = non-compliance
- Workers inside trench during shoring removal without remaining protection = serious hazard
- Shoring being removed in order other than reverse of installation sequence = flag
- No temporary structural members where needed before removal of permanent shoring = flag

CHUNK 10: Trench Shields and Boxes — Design and Maintenance
Section: CoP s6.5
Topic: Requirements for trench shields as worker protection devices (not collapse prevention)

KEY DISTINCTION:
- Shields and boxes protect workers IF collapse occurs — do NOT prevent collapse
- Shoring prevents collapse

DESIGN AND USE:
- Must be designed by competent person (engineer)
- Pre-manufactured to job-specific dimensions
- Must NOT be subjected to loads exceeding design capacity
- Incorporate specific lifting points for installation and removal

MAINTENANCE:
- Must be regularly inspected for damage
- Only altered or modified with approval of competent person
- Manufacturer's instructions for installation, use, removal and maintenance must be followed
- Stored and transported per manufacturer's instructions

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trench box visibly damaged, bent or with welds compromised = remove from service immediately
- Trench box not pre-engineered for actual trench depth and ground conditions = flag
- Workers inside trench box being overloaded by vehicles or heavy loads not accounted for in design = serious hazard

CHUNK 11: Underground Services — Dial Before You Dig Mandatory
Section: CoP s3.6; WHS Regulation s304
Topic: Mandatory pre-excavation service location requirements and contact obligations

PRE-EXCAVATION MANDATORY STEPS:
- Take ALL REASONABLE STEPS to get CURRENT INFORMATION about underground essential services in all areas where excavation will be carried out AND adjacent areas
- DIAL BEFORE YOU DIG: Phone 1100 | Website: www.1100.com.au
- MUST contact before excavating in public place to identify all electrical cables

ADDITIONAL CONTACTS:
- Electricity supply authorities
- Communication companies
- Local government authorities
- Water authorities
- On PRIVATE PROPERTY: contact owner or occupier about buried cables

POTHOLING:
- Required when exact location cannot be determined
- Dig with hand tools to pre-determined depth to verify if assets exist
- Use insulated hand digging tools suitable for voltage concerned, OR vacuum pumping

INFORMATION RETENTION:
- Underground services information must be made available to ANY WORKER, PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR and SUBCONTRACTORS
- Retained until excavation work COMPLETED or, if notifiable incident: for TWO YEARS after incident

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation work commenced with no Dial Before You Dig reference or services location records = serious non-compliance
- Mechanical excavation within clear zone of marked underground services without potholing = serious hazard
- Underground services information not available to workers or subcontractors = non-compliance
- Information not retained post-incident for two years = non-compliance

CHUNK 12: Powered Mobile Plant — Traffic Management and High-Visibility Clothing
Section: CoP s4.3; WHS Regulation s215
Topic: Mandatory traffic management, high-visibility clothing and communication requirements

TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT:
- TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT ARRANGEMENTS MUST be implemented when powered mobile plant used for excavation work to PREVENT COLLISION with pedestrians or other mobile plant
- SWMS MUST be prepared for excavation work involving powered mobile plant

HIGH-VISIBILITY CLOTHING:
- Mobile plant operators AND ground workers MUST wear high-visibility clothing

COMMUNICATION SYSTEM:
- EFFECTIVE SYSTEM based on TWO-WAY ACKNOWLEDGEMENT must be established between mobile plant operators and ground workers
- System must STOP ground workers from approaching plant UNTIL operator has agreed to request
- System must STOP operators from moving plant closer than set distance from ground workers until operator advised by ground workers they are aware of proposed movement
- Relevant workers must be trained in procedures BEFORE work commences

WARNING DEVICES:
- Plant operating near ground personnel or other plant MUST have warning device (reversing alarms, revolving lights)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ground workers approaching powered mobile plant without operator's acknowledgement = serious hazard
- No reversing alarms or warning lights on plant operating near workers = non-compliance
- Ground workers without high-visibility vests near mobile plant = non-compliance
- No documented traffic management plan where mobile plant and pedestrians share space = non-compliance

CHUNK 13: Powered Mobile Plant — Operator Protection and ROPS/FOPS
Section: CoP s4.3
Topic: Mandatory operator protective structures for earthmoving machinery

OPERATOR PROTECTION MANDATORY:
- Powered mobile plant MUST be equipped with combination of operator protection devices to PREVENT EJECTION of operator or being struck by falling objects
- Devices must be maintained and used
- Passengers must have EQUIVALENT PROTECTION to operator

ROPS/FOPS REQUIREMENT:
- Earthmoving machinery weighing MORE THAN 1500kg (not including attachments) designed to have seated operator MUST have:
  - ROPS (Roll-Over Protective Structures), and/or
  - FOPS (Falling Object Protective Structures) — depending on application

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Powered mobile plant without ROPS or FOPS for earthmoving machinery >1500kg = serious non-compliance
- ROPS or FOPS visibly damaged or removed = serious non-compliance
- Operator not using seatbelt in enclosed cabin = flag

CHUNK 14: Powered Mobile Plant — Proximity to Excavations
Section: CoP s4.3
Topic: Critical rules for plant operation near excavation edges and trenches

CRITICAL RULES:
- Large earthmoving machinery (bulldozers) SHOULD NOT operate close to overhang or deep excavation — weight may collapse sides
- Equipment should approach embankments or trenches FROM ACROSS THE LINE OF A TRENCH, NOT parallel to it
- Powered mobile plant should NOT operate or travel near edge of excavation UNLESS ground support system designed by competent person to carry such loads
- PHYSICAL BARRIERS (wheel stoppers) should be used to restrict plant movement near excavation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Powered mobile plant operating parallel to trench edge = serious hazard
- Plant operating near excavation edge without wheel stoppers or barriers = flag
- Heavy plant near excavation without ground support designed to carry those loads = serious hazard

CHUNK 15: Powered Mobile Plant — Blind Spots and Operator Visibility
Section: CoP s4.3; Figure 4
Topic: Blind spot awareness and visibility restrictions for earthmoving machinery

OPERATOR BLIND SPOTS:
- Operators can have SEVERELY RESTRICTED VISIBILITY of ground workers or nearby pedestrians, particularly those close to plant
- Plant types with documented blind spots: Dump trucks, Tractor scrapers, Bulldozers, Articulated rollers, Articulated loaders, Excavators, Graders
- Mobile plant operators and ground workers must be made familiar with blind spots of particular items of plant being used

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ground workers in documented blind spot zones without operator acknowledgement = serious hazard
- No documented blind spot training or awareness for plant operators and ground workers = flag

CHUNK 16: Atmospheric Hazards — Combustion Engines in Confined Excavations
Section: CoP s4.6; WHS Regulation s50
Topic: Prohibition on combustion engines in confined excavations and atmospheric monitoring

CRITICAL RULE — COMBUSTION ENGINES:
- Plant using COMBUSTION ENGINE (air compressors, electrical generators) MUST NEVER be used in CONFINED EXCAVATION (trench) if WORKERS ARE IN THE TRENCH
- Build-up of exhaust gases, particularly CARBON MONOXIDE, can cause DEATH

GASES HEAVIER THAN AIR that collect in tunnels and excavations:
- Gas (general)
- Sulphur dioxide
- Engine fumes
- Carbon monoxide
- Carbon dioxide
- Leakage from gas bottles, fuel tanks, sewers, drains, gas pipes, LPG tanks

AIR MONITORING MANDATORY when:
- NOT CERTAIN whether airborne concentration of substance exceeds relevant exposure standards
- Monitoring NECESSARY TO DETERMINE whether there is risk to health

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Air compressors or generators running inside trench or confined excavation with workers present = serious non-compliance; immediate stop
- No ventilation provided in enclosed or confined excavations = flag
- No gas monitors in use in excavations near drains, sewers or gas pipes = serious hazard
- Workers in tunnels or deep shafts without personal atmospheric monitors = flag
- No buddy/observer system for workers in confined excavations = flag

CHUNK 17: Asbestos — Identification and Licensing Requirements
Section: CoP s3.4; WHS Regulation s422, s426, s448, s449, s450, s451, s458
Topic: Asbestos identification, removal licensing thresholds and disposal requirements

ASBESTOS IN EXCAVATION WORK — may be encountered in:
- Underground water pipes
- Telecommunications pits
- Naturally occurring asbestos in excavated material

IDENTIFICATION MANDATORY:
- ALL asbestos or ACM at workplace, or ASSUMED PRESENT, must be identified by COMPETENT PERSON
- All asbestos likely to be disturbed must be identified and, so far as reasonably practicable, REMOVED BEFORE excavation starts
- If part of structure inaccessible or competent person uncertain: asbestos MUST BE ASSUMED present
- Construction work involving disturbance of asbestos = HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK; SWMS mandatory

LICENCE REQUIREMENTS:
- Class A Licence: any amount of friable asbestos or ACM; any amount of ACD; any amount of non-friable asbestos or ACM
- Class B Licence: any amount of non-friable asbestos or ACM; ACD associated with removal of non-friable asbestos or ACM
- NO LICENCE required: UP TO 10m² of non-friable asbestos or ACM; ACD associated with removal of LESS THAN 10m² non-friable ACM (where not also associated with friable asbestos)

DISPOSAL:
- Can only be disposed of at EPA-LICENSED SITE
- MUST NEVER be disposed of in general waste system

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation near or through underground pipes/pits with no asbestos assessment = non-compliance
- ACM disturbed during excavation without licensed removalist present = serious non-compliance
- Asbestos waste mixed with general construction waste = serious non-compliance
- Workers disturbing suspected ACM without respiratory protection = serious non-compliance
- No asbestos register or competent person inspection record = flag

CHUNK 18: Adjacent Buildings and Structures — Excavation Below Footings
Section: CoP s3.5
Topic: Requirements when excavating below footing level of adjacent structures

EXCAVATION BELOW FOOTING LEVEL:
- Excavation BELOW THE LEVEL OF THE FOOTING of structure (including retaining walls) that could affect structural stability:
  - Must be ASSESSED BY COMPETENT PERSON
  - Must be SECURED BY GROUND SUPPORT SYSTEM designed by competent person
  - Suitable STRUCTURAL BRACING may also be required; identified by competent person

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Vibration and concussion: other buildings in and around excavation site must not be adversely affected
- Special precautions required near hospitals and buildings containing equipment sensitive to shock and vibration
- Flooding or water penetration to adjacent buildings must be prevented

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation below footing level of adjacent structures without geotechnical assessment = serious non-compliance
- No structural support or monitoring for retaining walls adjacent to excavation = flag
- Hospital, heritage building or sensitive facility nearby with no vibration assessment = flag
- Water diversion from excavation pointing toward adjacent structures = flag

CHUNK 19: Explosives Use — Licensing and Notification
Section: CoP s4.5; WHS Act
Topic: Requirements for using explosives in excavation work

CLASSIFICATION:
- Construction work involving use of explosives = HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK
- SWMS MUST be prepared before this work starts

REQUIREMENTS:
- Competent person experienced in controlled application of explosives for excavation MUST be consulted before deciding whether explosives may be used
- Explosives MUST ONLY be used by COMPETENT PERSON who is LICENSED in use of explosives and has EXPERIENCE in work to be carried out
- Licensed competent person must develop and have authorised BLAST MANAGEMENT PLAN before blasting starts
- NOTIFICATION MUST BE MADE TO REGULATOR if explosives planned to be used

Key Standard: AS 2187.2: Explosives – Storage and use – Use of Explosives

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Explosives on excavation site without licensed explosives user present = serious non-compliance
- No blast management plan prepared before blasting commences = serious non-compliance
- No regulator notification where explosives to be used = non-compliance
- SWMS not prepared before explosive excavation work commences = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Tunnelling — Design Obligations and Ground Support
Section: CoP s5.2; WHS Regulation s295
Topic: Designer obligations and mandatory ground support for tunnel construction

DESIGNER OBLIGATIONS:
- Safe tunnel construction depends on adequate PRE-CONSTRUCTION ENGINEERING INVESTIGATION of ground and site
- Designer must give commissioning PCBU WRITTEN REPORT specifying hazards relating to design that create risk to health or safety of persons carrying out construction work on tunnel

TUNNEL DESIGN MUST INCLUDE:
- Tunnel dimensions and allowable excavation tolerances
- Temporary and FINAL SUPPORT AND LINING requirements for each location within tunnel
- Details of expected tunnel drive lengths and shaft location
- Information on excavation methods and ground conditions considered in design

CRITICAL RULE:
- All excavation for tunnelling MUST be supported
- Using ground support designed for unique circumstances of work is ESSENTIAL to control risk of collapse or tunnel support failure

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tunnel excavation proceeding without adequate ground support = serious non-compliance; immediate risk
- Workers in tunnel without gas/oxygen monitoring = serious hazard
- No ventilation system in tunnel during construction = serious non-compliance
- Combustion engine plant operating in tunnel with workers present without catalytic converter = flag

CHUNK 21: Shafts — Access Control and Covers
Section: CoP s5.3
Topic: Requirements for securing shaft openings and controlling access

ACCESS CONTROL:
- Access to shaft openings should be controlled by using SECURE COVER that is LOCKABLE and accessible ONLY BY DESIGNATED PERSON; OR
- Suitable GUARDRAIL and TOE BOARD with GATE for access, with sides supported by steel frames or timber sets

DESIGN REQUIREMENT:
- Shafts can have special features — design and construction advice should be obtained from COMPETENT PERSON (engineer) before excavation and installation
- Special VENTILATION FACILITIES may be required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Shaft opening without lockable cover or guardrail = serious non-compliance
- Workers in shaft without hoisting/rescue equipment = flag
- No ventilation in shaft where workers are working = flag
- Shaft access not restricted to designated persons = non-compliance

CHUNK 22: PPE Requirements for Excavation Workers
Section: CoP s1.4, s4.6, s4.7; WHS Act s28; WHS Regulation s46
Topic: Mandatory and typical PPE for excavation work

MANDATORY PPE:
- HARD HAT (safety helmet) — mandatory on all construction sites
- HIGH-VISIBILITY VEST — mandatory when near powered mobile plant or in construction zones; both mobile plant operators AND ground workers must wear
- SAFETY BOOTS — mandatory on construction sites

TYPICAL PPE FOR SPECIFIC HAZARDS:
- EYE PROTECTION — near falling debris, during drilling/blasting, power tools
- HEARING PROTECTION — when noise at or near exposure standard; particularly during steel trench sheeting driving with pneumatic hammers
- RESPIRATORY PROTECTION — when exposed to dust (crystalline silica, asbestos dust), chemical vapours or fumes; mandatory for asbestos work
- GLOVES — for manual work, hazardous chemical handling, asbestos work
- FALL PROTECTION EQUIPMENT (harness, lanyard) — when fall arrest system required and other controls not reasonably practicable
- PERSONAL ATMOSPHERIC MONITORS — when working in or near confined excavations, tunnels or areas with risk of gas or oxygen deficiency

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers on excavation site without hard hat = serious non-compliance
- Ground workers or plant operators without high-visibility vests = non-compliance
- Workers operating jackhammers or pneumatic hammers without hearing protection = non-compliance
- Workers in dusty excavation conditions without respiratory protection = flag
- Asbestos removal workers without appropriate respiratory protection = serious non-compliance
- Workers in enclosed excavations or tunnels without personal gas monitors = serious hazard

CHUNK 23: Principal Contractor — WHS Management Plan for Projects $250,000+
Section: CoP s3.1; WHS Regulation s292, s293, s308–s315
Topic: Principal contractor appointment and WHS Management Plan requirements

CONSTRUCTION PROJECT THRESHOLD:
- $250,000 OR MORE = principal contractor and WHS Management Plan required
- Only ONE principal contractor per construction project

PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR MUST:
- Prepare and review WHS Management Plan
- Prepare, or take all reasonable steps to obtain, SWMS before high risk construction work starts
- Put in place arrangements to manage work environment including falls, facilities, first aid, emergency plan and traffic management
- Install signs showing principal contractor's name, contact details and location of site office
- Secure the construction workplace

EXCAVATION CONTRACTOR AS PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR:
- Where significant excavation work carried out before building works start: excavation contractor may be appointed as principal contractor for site preparation phase

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No site signage showing principal contractor details = non-compliance
- WHS Management Plan not available for inspection on site for project $250,000+ = non-compliance
- Different contractors working without clear principal contractor coordination structure = flag
- SWMS not obtained before high risk excavation work commences = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 24: Inspection of Excavations — Frequency and Competent Person
Section: CoP s6.7
Topic: Mandatory inspection obligations for excavations and ground support systems

INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- SOIL CONDITION and STATE OF SHORING, BATTERING and TRENCH WALLS must be FREQUENTLY CHECKED by COMPETENT PERSON for signs of:
  - Earth FRETTING
  - SLIPPING
  - SLUMPING
  - Ground SWELLING

SOIL CONDITION CHANGES can happen quickly due to:
- Soil drying out
- Changes in water table
- Water saturation of soil

ACTION REQUIRED:
- Where necessary: REPAIR EXCAVATION or STRENGTHEN SHORING SYSTEM FROM ABOVE before allowing work below ground to continue

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No documented inspection records for shoring or trench walls = non-compliance
- Shoring showing signs of bowing, movement or distortion = immediate action required; stop work
- Trench walls showing fretting, slipping or slumping = immediate action required
- Inspection being conducted by person who is not competent person = flag
- No inspection after rain, water table change or prolonged inactivity = non-compliance

CHUNK 25: Emergency Plans — Excavation-Specific Requirements
Section: CoP s3.8; WHS Regulation s43
Topic: Emergency plan content and testing obligations for excavation sites

EMERGENCY PLAN MANDATORY:
- PCBU at workplace must ensure there is EMERGENCY PLAN for workplace
- Must be WRITTEN SET OF INSTRUCTIONS

EXCAVATION-SPECIFIC EMERGENCY SCENARIOS:
- Ground slip
- Engulfment
- Flooding
- Gas leaks
- RESCUING WORKERS FROM AN EXCAVATION

EMERGENCY PLAN MUST PROVIDE FOR:
- Emergency procedures including effective response to emergency
- Evacuation procedures
- Notifying emergency service organisations AT EARLIEST OPPORTUNITY
- Medical treatment and assistance
- Effective communication between person authorised to coordinate emergency response and ALL people at workplace
- Testing of emergency procedures — including FREQUENCY OF TESTING
- Information, training and instruction to relevant workers in relation to implementing emergency procedures

REVIEW TRIGGERS:
- Changes to workplace
- Changes in number or composition of staff
- New activities introduced
- After plan has been TESTED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No written emergency plan on excavation site = non-compliance
- Emergency plan does not address engulfment or ground collapse rescue procedures = flag for excavation sites
- No evidence emergency procedures have been tested = flag
- Workers unable to identify emergency response coordinator = flag
- Site in remote location with no documented access arrangements for emergency services = flag

CHUNK 26: Key Measurements Summary — All Numeric Thresholds
Section: CoP throughout; WHS Regulation s306, s299, s78
Topic: Consolidated numeric thresholds and measurements for excavation work

DEPTH THRESHOLDS:
- 1.5 METRES: Trench/shaft depth trigger for high risk construction work (SWMS mandatory); mandatory securing from unauthorised entry; mandatory ground support (shoring, benching or battering)
- 2 METRES: Fall risk trigger for high risk construction work (SWMS mandatory)

GROUND SUPPORT MEASUREMENTS:
- Sheet piling minimum protrusion above surface: 300mm
- Timber soldier set — soldier protrusion above trench top: 500mm
- Timber soldier set — top tom: no lower than 300mm from trench top
- Timber soldier set — maximum tom spacing: 750mm
- Timber soldier set — maximum soldier set spacing: 1.5 METRES
- Timber soldier set — soldier minimum size: 150mm × 38mm
- Timber soldier set — tom minimum size: 150mm × 38mm
- Timber soldier set — bottom tom to trench floor: normally no more than 1000mm
