CHUNK 1: Asbestos Removal Licence Classes and Thresholds
Section: WHS Regulation s458, Part 8.10 Div 1; COP s1.2, Table 1
Topic: Mandatory licence requirements and quantity thresholds

LICENCE CLASSES:
- CLASS A LICENCE: Required for removal of ANY AMOUNT OF FRIABLE ASBESTOS or ACM, and any amount of asbestos-contaminated dust (ACD)
- CLASS B LICENCE: Required for removal of ANY AMOUNT OF NON-FRIABLE ASBESTOS or ACM, and ACD associated with the removal of non-friable asbestos

THRESHOLD FOR UNLICENSED REMOVAL — a person without a licence may only remove:
- UP TO 10m² of non-friable asbestos or ACM
- ACD associated with removing LESS THAN 10m² of non-friable asbestos, or ACD that is a MINOR CONTAMINATION not associated with friable or non-friable removal

NOTE: 10m² is approximately the size of a small bathroom. Any removal exceeding this requires a Class B or higher licence.

METHODS DURING UNLICENSED REMOVAL:
- Must use methods that MINIMISE BREAKAGE of ACM
- Must not use hammers, angle grinders, or high-pressure water on ACM

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Removal of friable lagging or insulation from pipes by a contractor without a Class A licence displayed = serious regulatory breach
- Non-friable sheet removal area exceeding 10m² without a Class B licence = non-compliant
- Worker using a hammer to break ACM sheets during unlicensed removal = illegal
- No licence displayed at an active asbestos removal site = flag — request to see licence

CHUNK 2: Notification to the Regulator
Section: WHS Regulation s466; COP s3.6
Topic: Mandatory timeframes and information for notifying WorkSafe QLD

STANDARD NOTIFICATION: A licensed removalist MUST notify the regulator IN WRITING AT LEAST FIVE DAYS BEFORE licensed asbestos removal work commences.

EMERGENCY EXCEPTIONS — work may start immediately if there is:
- A SUDDEN AND UNEXPECTED EVENT (e.g. burst lagged pipe, forklift crash into AC wall)
- UNEXPECTED BREAKDOWN OF AN ESSENTIAL SERVICE (gas, water, sewerage, telecommunications)

In emergency cases the removalist MUST:
- Notify the regulator IMMEDIATELY BY TELEPHONE; AND
- Provide written notification NO MORE THAN 24 HOURS after the phone call

REQUIRED NOTIFICATION INFORMATION must include:
- Business details and licence number
- Supervisor name
- Client details and workplace address
- Start date and duration
- Asbestos type (friable or non-friable)
- Quantity of asbestos to be removed
- Worker competencies

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Licensed removal starting on a Monday with notification dated the previous Friday = non-compliant (less than 5 business days)
- Removalists on site without a copy of the notification form available for inspection = non-compliant documentation
- Emergency removal without evidence of telephone notification to regulator = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Supervisor Presence and Availability
Section: WHS Regulation s459, s529; COP s3.1
Topic: Mandatory oversight requirements during licensed removal

CLASS A REMOVAL (friable):
- The licence holder MUST ensure an asbestos removal supervisor is PHYSICALLY PRESENT AT THE ASBESTOS REMOVAL AREA when the work is being carried out
- The supervisor cannot be off-site for any reason while friable removal is in progress

CLASS B REMOVAL (non-friable):
- The licence holder MUST ensure a supervisor is READILY AVAILABLE to workers
- "Readily available" means the supervisor is:
  - CONTACTABLE BY PHONE; AND
  - ABLE TO ARRIVE AT THE WORKPLACE WITHIN 20 MINUTES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Friable removal in progress but the named supervisor is off-site = serious Class A breach
- Workers carrying out Class B removal unable to reach their supervisor by phone = non-compliant availability
- Self-employed person working alone on Class B work without holding the required VET certification = licensing breach
- No supervisor contact details visible at Class B removal site = non-compliant

CHUNK 4: Asbestos Removal Control Plan (ARCP)
Section: WHS Regulation s464, s465; COP s3.5; Appendix B
Topic: Mandatory written plan before removal commences

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT: A licensed asbestos removalist MUST prepare an ARCP for any licensed removal work BEFORE WORK COMMENCES.

ARCP MUST INCLUDE:
- Method of removal
- Tools and equipment to be used
- PPE requirements
- Location, type, and condition of the asbestos to be removed
- Site-specific hazards and controls (must not be generic)

ACCESS REQUIREMENTS:
- A copy MUST be kept at the workplace until completion
- Must be READILY ACCESSIBLE ON SITE for PCBUs, workers, and occupants (for residential premises)
- If a notifiable incident occurs: must be kept for AT LEAST 2 YEARS post-incident

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ARCP available on site but containing only generic procedures not addressing site-specific hazards = non-compliant
- PCBU unable to produce the ARCP during active removal work = documentation failure
- ARCP missing from site where a notifiable incident occurred = record-keeping breach
- No ARCP on site at all during licensed removal work = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Air Monitoring and Action Levels
Section: WHS Regulation s475, s476, s477; COP s3.11, Table 2
Topic: Required fibre counts and mandatory stop-work triggers

MANDATORY MONITORING: Required for ALL CLASS A (FRIABLE) REMOVAL.
- Not mandatory for non-friable removal (>10m²) but should be considered if near public locations or schools

EXPOSURE STANDARD: 0.1 fibres per mL (f/mL) over an 8-hour TWA — this must never be exceeded.

ACTION LEVELS (Table 2) — three-tiered response:

LEVEL 1 — LESS THAN 0.01 f/mL:
- Continue work with existing controls

LEVEL 2 — 0.01 TO 0.02 f/mL:
- MUST IMMEDIATELY REVIEW control measures
- Investigate the cause of elevated levels
- Implement changes to prevent further fibre release

LEVEL 3 — GREATER THAN 0.02 f/mL:
- MUST IMMEDIATELY STOP ALL REMOVAL WORK
- MUST NOTIFY THE REGULATOR BY PHONE
- Must NOT recommence until levels fall BELOW 0.01 f/mL

CLEARANCE AIR MONITORING THRESHOLD: Respirable fibre levels must be BELOW 0.01 f/mL before a clearance certificate can be issued.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Monitoring report showing 0.015 f/mL with no record of a control measure review = non-compliant
- Removalists continuing work when results exceed 0.02 f/mL = life-safety breach
- Air intake for a compressor located near the exhaust discharge of a negative pressure unit = dangerous setup
- No air monitoring records for Class A removal = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Clearance Inspections and Certificates
Section: WHS Regulation s473, s474; COP s3.10
Topic: Mandatory verification before re-occupancy after removal

MANDATORY CLEARANCE: A clearance certificate MUST be issued IN WRITING before the workplace can be re-occupied for normal use after licensed removal.

WHO CAN ISSUE:
- CLASS A WORK: Must be an INDEPENDENT LICENSED ASBESTOS ASSESSOR
- CLASS B WORK: Must be an INDEPENDENT COMPETENT PERSON

INDEPENDENCE RULE: The person issuing the certificate MUST NOT be involved in the removal work for that specific job — cannot be the same company or a related party.

CERTIFICATION CRITERIA:
- Area must be FREE FROM VISIBLE ASBESTOS CONTAMINATION
- If air monitoring conducted: results must show levels BELOW 0.01 f/mL

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trades entering a former removal area before a clearance certificate is posted at the entrance = access breach
- Clearance certificate signed by the removalist's own company or a business associate = lack of independence; non-compliant
- Warning signs and barricades removed before the final clearance certificate was issued = regulatory breach
- No clearance certificate visible at the entrance to a recently completed removal area = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Engineering Controls — Enclosures and Negative Pressure
Section: COP s5.1, s5.3, s3.12
Topic: Physical containment for friable removal

ENCLOSURE CONSTRUCTION:
- MUST be constructed of HEAVY-DUTY 200 µm (MINIMUM) POLYETHYLENE SHEETING
- Recycled sheeting MUST NOT be used
- Gaps between screens must NOT EXCEED 25mm in multi-storey screen setups

NEGATIVE PRESSURE:
- REQUIRED for all friable removal (except glove bag work)
- Must create a negative air pressure of APPROXIMATELY 12 Pa (water gauge)
- The unit MUST OPERATE 24 HOURS A DAY until decontamination is complete and a clearance certificate is issued

SMOKE TESTING:
- MANDATORY for enclosures used in friable removal
- An INDEPENDENT LICENSED ASBESTOS ASSESSOR should conduct smoke testing to confirm no leaks BEFORE WORK STARTS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Visible dead air pockets inside an enclosure (smoke not moving toward exhaust) = ventilation failure
- Enclosure sheeting attached with duct tape only = potential seal failure (cloth tape preferred)
- Gaps between screens exceeding 25mm = containment breach
- Negative pressure unit switched off outside working hours = non-compliant (must run 24 hours)
- Enclosure not smoke tested before friable removal commences = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Removal Methods
Section: COP s4.3, s5.4; Appendix G
Topic: Preferred techniques for suppressing airborne fibres

WET SPRAY METHOD — PREFERRED:
- Requires a constant LOW-PRESSURE water supply (fine spray or mist — not coarse spray)
- Detergent (wetting agent) or PVA emulsion may be added to improve penetration
- Both the front AND BACK of removed sheets must be wetted

SATURATION AND WATER INJECTION:
- Used for THICK FRIABLE ASBESTOS where surface spraying is insufficient
- Requires injecting water directly into the material

DRY METHOD — NOT PREFERRED:
- May ONLY be used if wet methods are not suitable (e.g. live electrical conductors, risk of damage to major equipment)
- Requirements if dry method used:
  - Full enclosure under AT LEAST 12 Pa NEGATIVE PRESSURE
  - Workers in FULL-FACE POSITIVE-PRESSURE AIR-LINE RESPIRATORS
  - Cannot be used simply for speed or convenience

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Coarse water spray hitting ACM sheets and splashing debris = improper wet technique (must be a mist)
- Dry removal being performed for convenience or speed = serious non-compliance (illegal)
- Workers wetting only the front of a sheet but not the back after removal = fibre release risk
- No water supply or wetting agent visible at removal site = flag — check if dry method is being used without justification

CHUNK 9: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Section: COP s4.5, s7.7; Appendix C; AS/NZS 1716
Topic: Mandatory gear specifications by work type

COVERALLS:
- DISPOSABLE — must never be taken home (laundering spreads asbestos contamination)
- Rating: TYPE 5, CATEGORY 3 (EN ISO 13982-1) or equivalent
- Design: Must have hood and cuffs; NO EXTERNAL POCKETS OR VELCRO FASTENINGS (difficult to decontaminate)
- Hood must be OVER (not under) respirator straps for correct facial seal

FOOTWEAR:
- GUM BOOTS (laceless) are required
- LACED BOOTS MUST NOT BE WORN — laces trap and retain asbestos dust

RESPIRATORY PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT by work type:
- Non-friable removal: P1 or P2 disposable or half-face respirator
- Friable removal (small scale or maintenance): P3 full-face cartridge respirator
- Dry stripping or friable enclosure work: Full-face powered air-purifying respirator (PAPR) or positive-pressure air-line respirator

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing coveralls with the hood tucked under respirator straps = incorrect facial seal; non-compliant
- Laced boots being worn in an asbestos removal area = non-compliant
- Respirator filter appearing clogged, saturated, or damaged = must be replaced immediately
- Worker wearing a P2 disposable mask for friable removal = grossly inadequate protection
- Disposable coveralls being placed in a bag to take home = non-compliant; serious contamination risk

CHUNK 10: Waste Containment and Disposal
Section: WHS Regulation s472; COP s4.8, s3.9
Topic: Secure handling and disposal of asbestos-contaminated materials

WASTE BAGS SPECIFICATION:
- Material: NEW heavy-duty POLYETHYLENE, 200 µm THICKNESS (recycled bags not acceptable)
- Maximum dimensions: NO MORE THAN 1200mm LONG AND 900mm WIDE
- Filling: Must NOT be filled more than HALF FULL
- Sealing method: Bags must be TWISTED TIGHTLY, neck folded over, and taped — known as GOOSE-NECKING

LARGE SHEETS: Large ACM sheets that do not fit in bags must be DOUBLE WRAPPED in 200 µm polyethylene sheeting.

MANDATORY LABELLING on every waste bag/wrapped item:
- "DANGER ASBESTOS — DO NOT OPEN OR DAMAGE BAG. DO NOT INHALE DUST"

DISPOSAL: Asbestos waste must only be disposed of at a LICENSED ASBESTOS WASTE DISPOSAL FACILITY — never in a general waste skip.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Asbestos waste bags thrown into a general waste skip = illegal; serious non-compliance
- Recycled plastic sheeting being used for waste wrapping = regulatory breach
- Large ACM sheets in a skip without double wrapping in 200 µm polyethylene = non-compliant containment
- Overfilled bags (more than half full) = non-compliant — risk of tearing
- Waste bags without the mandatory danger label = non-compliant
- Asbestos waste bags sealed with duct tape only (not cloth adhesive tape) = substandard practice
