CHUNK 1: Licence Classes and Removal Thresholds
Section: WHS Regulation s458; CoP s1.2, Table 1
Topic: Mandatory licence requirements and quantity thresholds (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

LICENCE CLASSES:
- CLASS A: Required for ANY AMOUNT of friable asbestos or ACM
- CLASS B: Required for ANY AMOUNT of non-friable asbestos or ACM

UNLICENSED REMOVAL THRESHOLD:
- Maximum 10m² of non-friable asbestos or ACM (approximately one small bathroom)
- Must use methods that MINIMISE BREAKAGE
- MUST NOT use hammers, angle grinders, or high-pressure water on ACM

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Friable lagging removal without Class A licence displayed = serious breach
- Non-friable sheet removal area exceeding 10m² without Class B licence = non-compliant
- Worker using hammer or angle grinder to break ACM sheets = illegal method
- No licence displayed at active removal site = flag

CHUNK 2: Notification Timeframes
Section: WHS Regulation s466; CoP s3.6
Topic: Mandatory notification periods to regulator

STANDARD NOTIFICATION:
- Must notify regulator IN WRITING AT LEAST 5 DAYS before licensed removal commences

EMERGENCY EXCEPTIONS (immediate start permitted):
- Sudden unexpected event (burst lagged pipe, vehicle impact into AC wall)
- Unexpected breakdown of essential service (gas, water, sewerage, telecommunications)
- Must notify regulator IMMEDIATELY BY TELEPHONE
- Written notification required WITHIN 24 HOURS of phone call

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Licensed removal starting Monday with notification dated previous Friday = non-compliant (less than 5 days)
- Emergency removal without evidence of telephone notification = non-compliant
- No notification form available for inspection on site = documentation failure

CHUNK 3: Supervisor Presence Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s459, s529; CoP s3.1
Topic: Mandatory oversight during licensed removal (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

CLASS A (FRIABLE):
- Supervisor MUST be PHYSICALLY PRESENT at removal area during all work
- Cannot be off-site while friable removal in progress

CLASS B (NON-FRIABLE):
- Supervisor must be READILY AVAILABLE:
  - Contactable by phone; AND
  - Able to arrive within 20 MINUTES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Friable removal in progress with named supervisor off-site = serious Class A breach
- Workers unable to reach supervisor by phone during Class B work = non-compliant
- No supervisor contact details visible at Class B site = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 4: Asbestos Removal Control Plan (ARCP)
Section: WHS Regulation s464, s465; CoP s3.5
Topic: Mandatory written plan before removal

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:
- ARCP MUST be prepared BEFORE licensed removal work commences
- Must include: removal method, tools, PPE, asbestos location/type/condition, site-specific hazards and controls
- Copy MUST be kept at workplace and READILY ACCESSIBLE until completion
- If notifiable incident occurs: must be kept for AT LEAST 2 YEARS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ARCP containing only generic procedures without site-specific hazards = non-compliant
- PCBU unable to produce ARCP during active removal = documentation failure
- No ARCP on site during licensed removal = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Air Monitoring Action Levels
Section: WHS Regulation s475–s477; CoP s3.11, Table 2
Topic: Mandatory fibre counts and stop-work triggers (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

EXPOSURE STANDARD: 0.1 fibres per mL (f/mL) — must never be exceeded

ACTION LEVELS:
- LEVEL 1 (LESS THAN 0.01 f/mL): Continue work
- LEVEL 2 (0.01 TO 0.02 f/mL): MUST IMMEDIATELY REVIEW controls and investigate cause
- LEVEL 3 (GREATER THAN 0.02 f/mL): MUST IMMEDIATELY STOP WORK and NOTIFY REGULATOR BY PHONE; cannot recommence until BELOW 0.01 f/mL

CLEARANCE THRESHOLD: Must be BELOW 0.01 f/mL before clearance certificate issued

MANDATORY MONITORING: Required for ALL CLASS A (friable) removal

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Monitoring report showing 0.015 f/mL without control measure review record = non-compliant
- Removalists continuing work when results exceed 0.02 f/mL = life-safety breach
- No air monitoring records for Class A removal = serious non-compliance
- Air intake for compressor near exhaust of negative pressure unit = dangerous setup

CHUNK 6: Clearance Certificates and Independence
Section: WHS Regulation s473, s474; CoP s3.10
Topic: Mandatory verification before re-occupancy (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY CLEARANCE: Certificate MUST be issued IN WRITING before workplace re-occupied after licensed removal

WHO CAN ISSUE:
- CLASS A: INDEPENDENT licensed asbestos assessor
- CLASS B: INDEPENDENT competent person
- Must NOT be involved in the removal work (cannot be same company or related party)

CERTIFICATION CRITERIA:
- Area FREE FROM VISIBLE asbestos contamination
- If air monitoring conducted: BELOW 0.01 f/mL

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trades entering former removal area before clearance certificate posted = access breach
- Clearance certificate signed by removalist's own company = lack of independence; non-compliant
- Warning signs removed before clearance certificate issued = regulatory breach
- No clearance certificate visible at entrance to completed removal area = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Enclosure Construction and Negative Pressure
Section: CoP s5.1, s5.3, s3.12
Topic: Physical containment specifications for friable removal (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

ENCLOSURE CONSTRUCTION:
- Material: Heavy-duty polyethylene sheeting MINIMUM 200 µm thickness
- Recycled sheeting MUST NOT be used
- Maximum gap between screens: 25mm (multi-storey setups)

NEGATIVE PRESSURE:
- REQUIRED for all friable removal (except glove bag work)
- Pressure: APPROXIMATELY 12 Pa (water gauge)
- Must operate 24 HOURS A DAY until decontamination complete and clearance certificate issued

SMOKE TESTING:
- MANDATORY for friable removal enclosures
- Independent licensed asbestos assessor should conduct BEFORE WORK STARTS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Visible dead air pockets inside enclosure (smoke not moving toward exhaust) = ventilation failure
- Gaps between screens exceeding 25mm = containment breach
- Negative pressure unit switched off outside working hours = non-compliant (must run 24 hours)
- Enclosure not smoke tested before friable removal = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Wet and Dry Removal Methods
Section: CoP s4.3, s5.4
Topic: Fibre suppression techniques and restrictions

WET SPRAY METHOD (PREFERRED):
- Constant LOW-PRESSURE water supply (fine mist — not coarse spray)
- BOTH front AND back of removed sheets must be wetted
- Detergent or PVA emulsion may be added

DRY METHOD (NOT PREFERRED):
- May ONLY be used if wet methods unsuitable (live electrical conductors, risk to major equipment)
- Requirements if dry method used:
  - Full enclosure under AT LEAST 12 Pa negative pressure
  - Workers in full-face positive-pressure air-line respirators
- Cannot be used for speed or convenience

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Coarse water spray splashing debris = improper wet technique (must be mist)
- Dry removal for convenience or speed = serious non-compliance (illegal)
- Workers wetting only front of sheet, not back after removal = fibre release risk
- No water supply visible at removal site = flag — check if dry method used without justification

CHUNK 9: PPE Specifications
Section: CoP s4.5, s7.7; AS/NZS 1716
Topic: Mandatory protective equipment by work type (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

COVERALLS:
- DISPOSABLE — must never be taken home
- Rating: TYPE 5, CATEGORY 3 (EN ISO 13982-1)
- Design: Hood and cuffs; NO EXTERNAL POCKETS OR VELCRO
- Hood OVER (not under) respirator straps for correct seal

FOOTWEAR:
- GUM BOOTS (laceless) required
- LACED BOOTS MUST NOT BE WORN

RESPIRATORY PROTECTION by work type:
- Non-friable removal: P1 or P2 disposable or half-face
- Friable removal (small scale/maintenance): P3 full-face cartridge
- Dry stripping or friable enclosure: Full-face PAPR or positive-pressure air-line

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hood tucked under respirator straps = incorrect seal; non-compliant
- Laced boots in removal area = non-compliant
- Respirator filter clogged, saturated, or damaged = must replace immediately
- P2 mask for friable removal = grossly inadequate protection
- Disposable coveralls in bag to take home = serious contamination risk

CHUNK 10: Waste Containment Specifications
Section: WHS Regulation s472; CoP s4.8, s3.9
Topic: Secure handling and disposal requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

WASTE BAGS:
- Material: NEW heavy-duty polyethylene, 200 µm THICKNESS (no recycled bags)
- Maximum dimensions: NO MORE THAN 1200mm LONG AND 900mm WIDE
- Filling: Must NOT be filled more than HALF FULL
- Sealing: GOOSE-NECKING — twisted tightly, neck folded over, taped

LARGE SHEETS: Must be DOUBLE WRAPPED in 200 µm polyethylene sheeting

MANDATORY LABEL on every bag/wrapped item:
- "DANGER ASBESTOS — DO NOT OPEN OR DAMAGE BAG. DO NOT INHALE DUST"

DISPOSAL: Only at LICENSED asbestos waste disposal facility — never general waste skip

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Asbestos waste in general waste skip = illegal; serious non-compliance
- Recycled plastic sheeting for waste wrapping = regulatory breach
- Large ACM sheets in skip without double wrapping in 200 µm polyethylene = non-compliant
- Overfilled bags (more than half full) = risk of tearing; non-compliant
- Waste bags without mandatory danger label = non-compliant
