CHUNK 1: Prohibitions and Exposure Standard
Section: WHS Regulation s419, s420; COP s1.1, s1.2
Topic: Mandatory bans and exposure limits

GENERAL PROHIBITION: A PCBU must not carry out or direct or allow a worker to carry out work involving asbestos if that work involves manufacturing, supplying, transporting, storing, removing, using, installing, handling, treating, disposing of or disturbing asbestos or ACM, except in prescribed circumstances.

EXPOSURE STANDARD: A PCBU must ensure the exposure standard for asbestos is not exceeded.
- Standard: 0.1 FIBRES PER MILLILITRE OF AIR (0.1 f/mL)
- Measured in the person's breathing zone
- Expressed as a TIME WEIGHTED AVERAGE over an EIGHT-HOUR WORKING DAY

STRICT PROHIBITIONS — the following are absolutely prohibited:
- High-pressure water spray or compressed air used on ACM (e.g. fibro roof) = STRICTLY PROHIBITED
- Installation of any new ACM products after 31 December 2003 = ILLEGAL
- Dry sweeping of dust in any area where ACM is being worked on = PROHIBITED
- Use of household vacuum cleaners near asbestos = PROHIBITED (must use H-Class HEPA vacuum)
- High-speed power tools used on ACM without LEV dust control hood or shadow vacuuming = PROHIBITED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- High-pressure water spray being used on a fibro or corrugated cement roof = critical non-compliance
- Worker using a household vacuum cleaner near asbestos dust = serious non-compliance
- Newly installed corrugated cement or fibro sheeting on a building = illegal installation post-2003

CHUNK 2: Identifying Asbestos and Competency
Section: WHS Regulation s422; COP s2.2
Topic: Requirements for identifying ACM at the workplace

MANDATORY IDENTIFICATION: A person with management or control of a workplace must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that ALL asbestos or ACM at the workplace is identified by a COMPETENT PERSON.

COMPETENT PERSON for identification must:
- Be trained to handle and take samples
- Be familiar with building practices to determine where asbestos is likely to be located
- Be able to determine if material is friable or non-friable and evaluate its condition

HIGH PROBABILITY INDICATORS — asbestos likely to be present in:
- Buildings constructed BEFORE 1990: cement sheets, roofs, wet areas (bathrooms, laundries), eaves, fencing
- Plant installed BEFORE 31 DECEMBER 2003: gaskets, insulation, friction brake products
- Corrugated cement roofing or "fibro" wall cladding
- Vinyl floor tiles (particularly pre-1990)
- Textured ceiling coatings (e.g. "popcorn" ceilings)
- Pipe lagging and insulation on old boilers and hot water systems

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Building constructed before 1990 with unidentified cement sheet cladding = assume ACM until tested
- Corrugated cement or fibro roofing, walls or fencing = likely ACM
- Old pipe lagging on boilers or hot water systems = assume ACM until proven otherwise
- Fire doors without compliance tags in pre-1990 buildings = presumed ACM core

CHUNK 3: Mandatory Assumptions
Section: WHS Regulation s422, s423; COP s2.3, s2.4
Topic: Treatment of unidentified or inaccessible materials

A person with management or control of a workplace MUST:
- ASSUME material is asbestos or ACM if it cannot be identified but a competent person reasonably believes it may be asbestos or ACM
- ASSUME asbestos is present if part of the workplace is INACCESSIBLE and it is likely to contain asbestos or ACM

INACCESSIBLE AREAS include:
- Completely enclosed cavities
- Inner linings of old boilers
- Vinyl tiles covered by multiple layers of non-ACM flooring

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unlabelled material that looks like asbestos cement but has not been tested = must be presumed ACM
- Locked rooms or crawl spaces not listed in the asbestos register = non-compliant identification
- Enclosed ceiling or wall cavities in pre-1990 buildings = must be assumed to contain ACM

CHUNK 4: Indicating Presence — Signs and Labels
Section: WHS Regulation s424; COP s2.5; Appendix C
Topic: Mandatory marking of ACM locations

REQUIRED MARKINGS: A person with management or control of a workplace must ensure the presence and location of ALL identified or assumed asbestos/ACM is clearly indicated. If reasonably practicable, this MUST be indicated by a LABEL.

WARNING SIGNS must:
- Be weatherproof and adequately secured
- Be placed at ALL MAIN ENTRANCES to work areas where asbestos is present
- Comply with AS 1319

CORRECT SIGN TEXT: "DANGER: ASBESTOS — CANCER AND LUNG DISEASE HAZARD"

ASBESTOS WASTE BAGS must be labelled: "Danger Asbestos — Do not open or damage bag. Do not inhale dust"

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- "DANGER: ASBESTOS" sign at entrance to a plant room = compliant marking
- ACM present in an area without any warning sign or label = non-compliant
- Asbestos waste bags without correct danger labelling = regulatory breach
- Unmarked double-bagged material in a skip = flag — may be unlabelled asbestos waste

CHUNK 5: The Asbestos Register
Section: WHS Regulation s425, s426, s427, s428; COP s3.1, s3.2, s3.3
Topic: Mandatory documentation and record keeping

PREPARATION: A person with management or control of a workplace MUST ensure an asbestos register is PREPARED AND KEPT AT THE WORKPLACE.

EXEMPTION: Not required ONLY if the building was constructed AFTER 31 DECEMBER 1989, no asbestos has been identified, and no asbestos is likely to be present.

REVIEW TRIGGERS — register MUST be reviewed and revised if:
- Further asbestos is identified
- Asbestos is removed, disturbed, sealed, or enclosed

MINIMUM REVIEW FREQUENCY: At least ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS

ACCESS: Workers carrying out work that may disturb ACM MUST be provided access to the register before commencing work.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- PCBU unable to produce an asbestos register for a building built before 1990 = serious regulatory breach
- Asbestos register dated more than 5 years ago without a review record = non-compliant
- Worker performing maintenance on pipes or ceiling without having been provided the register = access violation
- Register does not include inaccessible areas or assumed ACM locations = incomplete register

CHUNK 6: The Asbestos Management Plan (AMP)
Section: WHS Regulation s429, s430; COP s4.1, s4.2
Topic: Written procedures for managing identified asbestos risks

MANDATORY PLAN: A person with management or control of a workplace MUST ensure a written asbestos management plan is prepared if asbestos has been identified or assumed present.

PLAN MUST INCLUDE:
- Identification of asbestos (linked to the register)
- Decisions and reasons for decisions about management (e.g. safe work procedures, encapsulation vs removal)
- Procedures for accidents, incidents, and emergencies involving asbestos
- Details of workers carrying out asbestos work (consultation, training, supervision)

REVIEW: The plan MUST be reviewed and, if necessary, revised AT LEAST ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workplace with identified asbestos that has a register but no written AMP = regulatory breach
- AMP not containing procedures for emergencies or responsible persons = incomplete plan
- AMP not reviewed in over 5 years = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Asbestos Removal Licence Classes and Thresholds
Section: WHS Regulation s458; COP s5.2
Topic: When a licensed removalist is required and which licence class

LICENCE CLASSES:
- CLASS A LICENCE: Required for removal of FRIABLE ASBESTOS (any quantity)
- CLASS B LICENCE: Required for removal of NON-FRIABLE ASBESTOS above the threshold

LICENSING THRESHOLDS FOR NON-FRIABLE ACM:
- MORE THAN 10m² of non-friable ACM: MUST be removed by a LICENSED ASBESTOS REMOVALIST (Class B or higher)
- LESS THAN 10m² of non-friable ACM: Does not need to be undertaken by a licensed removalist, but still must be carried out safely with appropriate controls

FRIABLE vs NON-FRIABLE:
- FRIABLE: Can be crumbled, pulverised or reduced to powder by hand pressure when dry (e.g. pipe lagging, sprayed asbestos, insulation) — highest risk
- NON-FRIABLE: Cannot be crumbled by hand when dry (e.g. asbestos cement sheets, floor tiles) — lower risk but becomes friable when damaged or drilled

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers removing large sections of fibro sheeting (>10m²) without licensed removalist signage = licensing breach
- Any removal of friable asbestos (pipe lagging, sprayed insulation) by unlicensed workers = serious non-compliance
- Licensed removalist sign/registration not displayed at removal worksite = flag
- Workers removing ACM in street clothes without coveralls or respirators = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Notification Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s453, s457; COP s5.3
Topic: When WorkSafe Queensland must be notified

EMERGENCY DEMOLITION NOTIFICATION:
- If an emergency requires demolition of a structure containing asbestos (e.g. structurally unsound, imminent collapse), the person with management or control MUST provide the REGULATOR WITH WRITTEN NOTICE:
  - IMMEDIATELY after becoming aware of the emergency; AND
  - BEFORE the demolition starts

GENERAL REMOVAL NOTIFICATION:
- Standard notification requirements (days' notice, forms) are specified in the How to Safely Remove Asbestos Code of Practice

PRE-DEMOLITION AND REFURBISHMENT DUTIES:
- Prior to any demolition or refurbishment, a person with management or control MUST:
  - Review the asbestos register (revise if inadequate)
  - Provide a copy of the register to the PCBU carrying out the work
  - Ensure asbestos likely to be disturbed is identified and, so far as is reasonably practicable, REMOVED BEFORE WORK COMMENCES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of a pre-1990 building commencing without a pre-demolition asbestos survey = serious non-compliance
- Refurbishment work disturbing walls or ceilings in pre-1990 building without asbestos survey = non-compliant
- No notification records visible at a licensed removal site = flag

CHUNK 9: Safe Work Practices and Controls
Section: WHS Regulation s419; COP s5.4, s7.1; Appendix G
Topic: Controls during asbestos-related work and maintenance

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS for asbestos-related work:
- The area MUST be signed and barricaded
- Facilities MUST be provided to allow for decontamination
- Anything removed MUST be decontaminated or sealed in a container or bag
- WETTING METHODS or thickened substances (pastes or gels) should be used to suppress fibres

PLASTIC SHEETING: At a minimum, HEAVY-DUTY 200 µm THICK PLASTIC SHEETING should be used to cover surfaces.

SEGREGATION ZONE: Vehicle brake repairs and similar work must be conducted with a 3-METRE SEGREGATION ZONE around the work area.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker drilling ACM using a hand drill with a cup of shaving cream enshrouding the drill bit = positive safe work practice
- Vehicle brake repairs done without a 3-metre segregation zone = non-compliant asbestos-related work
- No decontamination facilities (e.g. wet wipes, water, waste bags) at the asbestos work area = non-compliant
- Asbestos work area not signed or barricaded = non-compliant
- Wetting not being used during ACM removal = flag

CHUNK 10: Air Monitoring and Clearance Certificates
Section: WHS Regulation s445; COP s6.2
Topic: When air monitoring is required and clearance before re-occupancy

AIR MONITORING is required if the PCBU is uncertain on reasonable grounds as to whether the airborne concentration of asbestos exceeds the exposure standard (0.1 f/mL).

WHO MUST CARRY OUT MONITORING:
- A COMPETENT PERSON which may include:
  - A licensed asbestos assessor
  - A person who has undertaken the endorsed VET unit of competency for licensed asbestos assessors
  - An occupational hygienist with experience in asbestos exposure monitoring

CLEARANCE AIR MONITORING: Required to ensure the work area is FREE OF ASBESTOS FIBRES prior to being certified for reoccupation after removal.

CLEARANCE CERTIFICATE:
- MANDATORY before the work area can be reoccupied after licensed removal work
- Must be issued by a COMPETENT PERSON holding:
  - A certification in relation to the specified VET course for asbestos assessor work; OR
  - A tertiary qualification in occupational health and safety, occupational hygiene, science, building, construction, or environmental health

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Licensed asbestos removal site re-occupied without a clearance certificate visible = serious non-compliance
- No air monitoring records available during or after licensed removal work = non-compliant
- Work area barriers removed before clearance certificate issued = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: Health Monitoring and Training
Section: WHS Regulation s39, s445; COP s6.2, s6.3
Topic: Medical tracking and competency records

HEALTH MONITORING — must be provided to workers at risk of exposure for:
- Licensed removal work
- Unlicensed ongoing removal work
- Asbestos-related work

MONITORING INTERVAL: At regular intervals, AT LEAST ONCE EVERY TWO YEARS

RECORD RETENTION:
- Health monitoring reports: MUST be kept as a CONFIDENTIAL RECORD for AT LEAST 40 YEARS
- Training records: MUST be kept while the worker is carrying out the work AND FOR FIVE YEARS AFTER they stop

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker performing regular ACM maintenance who has not had a health monitoring examination in more than 2 years = non-compliant
- PCBU unable to produce training records for a worker who left the company 3 years ago = regulatory breach (must keep for 5 years)

CHUNK 12: PPE and Equipment Standards
Section: COP s7.5, s7.7; AS/NZS 60335.2.69
Topic: Mandatory gear and tool specifications

VACUUM CLEANERS:
- Must use HEPA FILTERED H-CLASS INDUSTRIAL VACUUM CLEANERS only
- Should undergo ANNUAL FILTRATION EFFICIENCY TESTING
- HOUSEHOLD VACUUM CLEANERS MUST NEVER BE USED near asbestos

COVERALLS:
- DISPOSABLE COVERALLS should be worn
- Rated TYPE 5, CATEGORY 3 (EN ISO 13982-1) or equivalent
- Must NOT have open pockets or velcro fastenings (difficult to decontaminate)

FOOTWEAR:
- GUM BOOTS preferred — easier to clean and decontaminate
- LACED BOOTS must NOT be worn in asbestos work areas

DISPOSAL BAGS:
- HEAVY DUTY PLASTIC (200 µm POLYETHYLENE)
- DOUBLE-BAGGED
- Sealed with ADHESIVE CLOTH TAPE (preferred over duct tape for durability)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing coveralls with open pockets or velcro fastenings = non-compliant
- Laced boots worn in an asbestos work area = non-compliant
- Single plastic bag used for asbestos waste = non-compliant (must be double-bagged)
- Waste sealed with duct tape instead of adhesive cloth tape = substandard practice
- Non-H-Class vacuum cleaner in use near asbestos = serious non-compliance
