CHUNK 1: Strict Prohibitions — Visible Work Methods
Section: WHS Regulation s419, s420; COP s1.1, s1.2
Topic: Absolutely prohibited work methods and exposure standard

EXPOSURE STANDARD:
- 0.1 FIBRES PER MILLILITRE OF AIR (0.1 f/mL) measured in breathing zone over 8-hour working day

STRICTLY PROHIBITED METHODS:
- High-pressure water spray on ACM (fibro roof, cement sheeting)
- Compressed air used on ACM
- Dry sweeping of dust where ACM is being worked on
- Household vacuum cleaners near asbestos
- High-speed power tools on ACM without LEV dust control hood or shadow vacuuming
- Installation of any new ACM products after 31 December 2003

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- High-pressure water spray on fibro or corrugated cement roof = critical non-compliance
- Worker using household vacuum cleaner near asbestos dust = serious non-compliance
- Newly installed corrugated cement or fibro sheeting = illegal installation post-2003
- Dry sweeping visible in asbestos work area = prohibited
- Angle grinder or circular saw cutting ACM without dust extraction hood = prohibited

CHUNK 2: High Probability ACM Locations
Section: WHS Regulation s422; COP s2.2
Topic: Visual indicators of likely asbestos presence

HIGH PROBABILITY INDICATORS — asbestos likely present in:
- Buildings constructed BEFORE 1990: cement sheets, roofs, wet areas (bathrooms, laundries), eaves, fencing
- Plant installed BEFORE 31 DECEMBER 2003: gaskets, insulation, friction brake products
- Corrugated cement roofing or "fibro" wall cladding
- Vinyl floor tiles (particularly pre-1990)
- Textured ceiling coatings ("popcorn" ceilings)
- Pipe lagging and insulation on old boilers and hot water systems

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Building constructed before 1990 with unidentified cement sheet cladding = assume ACM until tested
- Corrugated cement or fibro roofing, walls or fencing = likely ACM
- Old pipe lagging on boilers or hot water systems = assume ACM until proven otherwise
- Fire doors without compliance tags in pre-1990 buildings = presumed ACM core
- Textured ceiling coating in older building = likely ACM
- Vinyl floor tiles in pre-1990 building = assume ACM

CHUNK 3: Mandatory Assumptions for Unidentified Materials
Section: WHS Regulation s422, s423; COP s2.3, s2.4
Topic: Treatment of unidentified or inaccessible materials

MANDATORY ASSUMPTIONS:
- Material MUST be assumed to be ACM if it cannot be identified but may reasonably be asbestos
- Asbestos MUST be assumed present if area is INACCESSIBLE and likely to contain ACM

INACCESSIBLE AREAS include:
- Completely enclosed cavities
- Inner linings of old boilers
- Vinyl tiles covered by multiple layers of flooring

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unlabelled material that looks like asbestos cement without test results = must be presumed ACM
- Locked rooms or crawl spaces not listed in asbestos register = non-compliant identification
- Enclosed ceiling or wall cavities in pre-1990 buildings = must be assumed to contain ACM
- Sealed plant rooms or equipment housings in older buildings = assume ACM present

CHUNK 4: Warning Signs and Labels — Exact Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s424; COP s2.5; Appendix C; AS 1319
Topic: Mandatory marking of ACM locations

REQUIRED MARKINGS:
- Presence and location of ALL identified or assumed ACM must be clearly indicated
- If reasonably practicable, MUST be indicated by a LABEL

WARNING SIGNS must:
- Be weatherproof and adequately secured
- Be placed at ALL MAIN ENTRANCES to work areas where asbestos is present
- Comply with AS 1319

CORRECT SIGN TEXT: "DANGER: ASBESTOS — CANCER AND LUNG DISEASE HAZARD"

ASBESTOS WASTE BAGS must be labelled: "Danger Asbestos — Do not open or damage bag. Do not inhale dust"

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- "DANGER: ASBESTOS" sign at entrance to plant room = compliant marking
- ACM present in area without warning sign or label = non-compliant
- Asbestos waste bags without correct danger labelling = regulatory breach
- Unmarked double-bagged material in skip = flag — may be unlabelled asbestos waste
- Sign text not matching required wording = non-compliant signage

CHUNK 5: Asbestos Register Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s425, s426, s427, s428; COP s3.1, s3.2, s3.3
Topic: Mandatory documentation and review intervals

MANDATORY REGISTER:
- Must be PREPARED AND KEPT AT THE WORKPLACE
- EXEMPTION: Only if building constructed AFTER 31 DECEMBER 1989, no asbestos identified, and none likely present

REVIEW TRIGGERS — register MUST be reviewed and revised if:
- Further asbestos is identified
- Asbestos is removed, disturbed, sealed, or enclosed

MINIMUM REVIEW FREQUENCY: AT LEAST ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS

ACCESS: Workers carrying out work that may disturb ACM MUST be provided access to register before commencing work

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- PCBU unable to produce asbestos register for building built before 1990 = serious regulatory breach
- Asbestos register dated more than 5 years ago without review record = non-compliant
- Worker performing maintenance on pipes or ceiling without having been provided register = access violation
- Register does not include inaccessible areas or assumed ACM locations = incomplete register

CHUNK 6: Asbestos Management Plan (AMP)
Section: WHS Regulation s429, s430; COP s4.1, s4.2
Topic: Written procedures for managing identified asbestos risks

MANDATORY PLAN: Written asbestos management plan MUST be prepared if asbestos has been identified or assumed present

PLAN MUST INCLUDE:
- Identification of asbestos (linked to register)
- Decisions and reasons for management approach (safe work procedures, encapsulation vs removal)
- Procedures for accidents, incidents, and emergencies involving asbestos
- Details of workers carrying out asbestos work (consultation, training, supervision)

REVIEW: Plan MUST be reviewed and, if necessary, revised AT LEAST ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workplace with identified asbestos that has register but no written AMP = regulatory breach
- AMP not containing procedures for emergencies or responsible persons = incomplete plan
- AMP not reviewed in over 5 years = non-compliant
- AMP not accessible to workers at site = access violation

CHUNK 7: Licence Classes and Removal Thresholds
Section: WHS Regulation s458; COP s5.2
Topic: When licensed removalist is required and which licence class

LICENCE CLASSES:
- CLASS A LICENCE: Required for removal of FRIABLE ASBESTOS (any quantity)
- CLASS B LICENCE: Required for removal of NON-FRIABLE ASBESTOS above threshold

LICENSING THRESHOLDS FOR NON-FRIABLE ACM:
- MORE THAN 10m² of non-friable ACM: MUST be removed by LICENSED ASBESTOS REMOVALIST (Class B or higher)
- LESS THAN 10m² of non-friable ACM: Does not need licensed removalist, but must be carried out safely with appropriate controls

FRIABLE vs NON-FRIABLE:
- FRIABLE: Can be crumbled, pulverised or reduced to powder by hand pressure when dry (pipe lagging, sprayed asbestos, insulation) — highest risk
- NON-FRIABLE: Cannot be crumbled by hand when dry (asbestos cement sheets, floor tiles) — becomes friable when damaged or drilled

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers removing large sections of fibro sheeting (>10m²) without licensed removalist signage = licensing breach
- Any removal of friable asbestos (pipe lagging, sprayed insulation) by unlicensed workers = serious non-compliance
- Licensed removalist sign/registration not displayed at removal worksite = flag
- Workers removing ACM in street clothes without coveralls or respirators = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Emergency Demolition and Pre-Work Duties
Section: WHS Regulation s453, s457; COP s5.3
Topic: Notification requirements and pre-demolition/refurbishment duties

EMERGENCY DEMOLITION NOTIFICATION:
- If emergency requires demolition of structure containing asbestos (structurally unsound, imminent collapse), person with management or control MUST provide regulator with WRITTEN NOTICE:
  - IMMEDIATELY after becoming aware of emergency; AND
  - BEFORE demolition starts

PRE-DEMOLITION AND REFURBISHMENT DUTIES:
- Prior to any demolition or refurbishment, person with management or control MUST:
  - Review asbestos register (revise if inadequate)
  - Provide copy of register to PCBU carrying out work
  - Ensure asbestos likely to be disturbed is identified and, so far as reasonably practicable, REMOVED BEFORE WORK COMMENCES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of pre-1990 building commencing without pre-demolition asbestos survey = serious non-compliance
- Refurbishment work disturbing walls or ceilings in pre-1990 building without asbestos survey = non-compliant
- No notification records visible at licensed removal site = flag
- Demolition equipment on site with ACM still visible in structure = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Safe Work Practices — Physical Controls and Measurements
Section: WHS Regulation s419; COP s5.4, s7.1; Appendix G
Topic: Controls during asbestos-related work and maintenance

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS for asbestos-related work:
- Area MUST be signed and barricaded
- Facilities MUST be provided for decontamination
- Anything removed MUST be decontaminated or sealed in container or bag
- WETTING METHODS or thickened substances (pastes or gels) should be used to suppress fibres

PLASTIC SHEETING: At minimum, HEAVY-DUTY 200 µm THICK PLASTIC SHEETING should be used to cover surfaces

SEGREGATION ZONE: Vehicle brake repairs and similar work must be conducted with 3-METRE SEGREGATION ZONE around work area

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker drilling ACM using hand drill with cup of shaving cream enshrouding drill bit = positive safe work practice
- Vehicle brake repairs done without 3-metre segregation zone = non-compliant asbestos-related work
- No decontamination facilities (wet wipes, water, waste bags) at asbestos work area = non-compliant
- Asbestos work area not signed or barricaded = non-compliant
- Wetting not being used during ACM removal = flag
- Plastic sheeting visibly thinner than heavy-duty grade = substandard control

CHUNK 10: Clearance Certificates and Air Monitoring
Section: WHS Regulation s445; COP s6.2
Topic: When air monitoring is required and clearance before re-occupancy

AIR MONITORING TRIGGER:
- Required if PCBU is uncertain on reasonable grounds whether airborne concentration of asbestos exceeds exposure standard (0.1 f/mL)

CLEARANCE AIR MONITORING:
- Required to ensure work area is FREE OF ASBESTOS FIBRES prior to certification for reoccupation after removal

CLEARANCE CERTIFICATE:
- MANDATORY before work area can be reoccupied after licensed removal work
- Must be issued by COMPETENT PERSON holding:
  - Certification in relation to specified VET course for asbestos assessor work; OR
  - Tertiary qualification in occupational health and safety, occupational hygiene, science, building, construction, or environmental health

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Licensed asbestos removal site re-occupied without clearance certificate visible = serious non-compliance
- No air monitoring records available during or after licensed removal work = non-compliant
- Work area barriers removed before clearance certificate issued = non-compliant
- Workers re-entering area without visible clearance certificate posted = flag

CHUNK 11: Health Monitoring Intervals and Record Retention
Section: WHS Regulation s39, s445; COP s6.2, s6.3
Topic: Medical tracking intervals and record keeping periods

HEALTH MONITORING — must be provided to workers at risk of exposure for:
- Licensed removal work
- Unlicensed ongoing removal work
- Asbestos-related work

MONITORING INTERVAL: At regular intervals, AT LEAST ONCE EVERY TWO YEARS

RECORD RETENTION:
- Health monitoring reports: MUST be kept as CONFIDENTIAL RECORD for AT LEAST 40 YEARS
- Training records: MUST be kept while worker is carrying out work AND FOR FIVE YEARS AFTER they stop

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker performing regular ACM maintenance who has not had health monitoring examination in more than 2 years = non-compliant
- PCBU unable to produce training records for worker who left company 3 years ago = regulatory breach (must keep for 5 years)

CHUNK 12: PPE and Equipment Specifications — Exact Standards
Section: COP s7.5, s7.7; AS/NZS 60335.2.69; EN ISO 13982-1
Topic: Mandatory gear and tool specifications

VACUUM CLEANERS:
- Must use HEPA FILTERED H-CLASS INDUSTRIAL VACUUM CLEANERS only
- Should undergo ANNUAL FILTRATION EFFICIENCY TESTING
- HOUSEHOLD VACUUM CLEANERS MUST NEVER BE USED near asbestos

COVERALLS:
- DISPOSABLE COVERALLS should be worn
- Rated TYPE 5, CATEGORY 3 (EN ISO 13982-1) or equivalent
- Must NOT have open pockets or velcro fastenings (difficult to decontaminate)

FOOTWEAR:
- GUM BOOTS preferred — easier to clean and decontaminate
- LACED BOOTS must NOT be worn in asbestos work areas

DISPOSAL BAGS:
- HEAVY DUTY PLASTIC (200 µm POLYETHYLENE)
- DOUBLE-BAGGED
- Sealed with ADHESIVE CLOTH TAPE (preferred over duct tape for durability)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing coveralls with open pockets or velcro fastenings = non-compliant
- Laced boots worn in asbestos work area = non-compliant
- Single plastic bag used for asbestos waste = non-compliant (must be double-bagged)
- Waste sealed with duct tape instead of adhesive cloth tape = substandard practice
- Non-H-Class vacuum cleaner in use near asbestos = serious non-compliance
- Household vacuum cleaner visible in asbestos work area = prohibited equipment
