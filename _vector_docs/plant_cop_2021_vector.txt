CHUNK 1: Plant Registration — Design and Item Registration Numbers
Section: CoP s2.5, s6.1; WHS Regulation Schedule 5, s244
Topic: Registrable plant requiring design and item registration; registration number display requirements

DESIGN REGISTRATION NUMBER DISPLAY (WHS Regulation s244):
- Person with management or control must ensure design registration number is KEPT READILY ACCESSIBLE IN THE VICINITY OF THE PLANT AT ALL TIMES
- RECOMMENDED: permanently mark the design registration number ON THE PLANT

ITEM REGISTRATION:
- Registrable items must be ITEM REGISTERED PRIOR TO COMMISSIONING FOR USE
- ANNUAL RENEWAL required
- Registration holder must apply BEFORE REGISTRATION EXPIRES

PLANT DESIGNS REQUIRING REGISTRATION (Schedule 5 Part 1):
- TOWER CRANES including self-erecting tower cranes
- LIFTS, ESCALATORS AND MOVING WALKWAYS
- Building maintenance units
- Hoists with platform movement EXCEEDING 2.4 METRES (designed to lift people)
- Work boxes designed to be suspended from cranes
- Concrete placing booms
- PREFABRICATED SCAFFOLDING
- BOOM-TYPE ELEVATING WORK PLATFORMS
- GANTRY CRANES with SWL > 5 TONNES or BRIDGE CRANES with SWL > 10 TONNES
- Vehicle hoists
- Mast climbing work platforms
- MOBILE CRANES with rated capacity > 10 TONNES
- Pressure equipment (hazard level A, B, C or D per AS 4343)
- Gas cylinders (AS 2030.1)
- Amusement devices (AS 3533.1 — except class 1, playground, water slides, wave generators, certain inflatables)

ITEMS OF PLANT REQUIRING REGISTRATION (Schedule 5 Part 2):
- BOILERS (hazard level A, B or C per AS 4343)
- PRESSURE VESSELS (hazard level A, B or C — except gas cylinders, LP Gas automotive, serially produced vessels)
- TOWER CRANES including self-erecting tower cranes
- LIFTS, ESCALATORS AND MOVING WALKWAYS
- Building maintenance units
- Amusement devices (AS 3533.1 — except class 1, playground, water slides, wave generators, certain inflatables)
- Concrete placing booms
- MOBILE CRANES with rated capacity > 10 TONNES

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tower crane, lift, escalator, mobile crane (>10t), boom-type EWP, mast climber, concrete placing boom, prefabricated scaffold, vehicle hoist, boiler or pressure vessel on site without visible registration number or registration documentation = serious non-compliance
- Registration expired (past annual renewal date) = serious non-compliance
- No design registration number displayed on or near registrable plant = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Guarding — Physical Barriers and Interlocks
Section: CoP s5.1; WHS Regulation s208
Topic: Mandatory guarding hierarchy and physical barrier requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

GUARDING HIERARCHY (WHS Regulation s208) — in priority order:
1. PERMANENTLY FIXED BARRIER — if access NOT NECESSARY during operation, maintenance or cleaning
2. INTERLOCKED PHYSICAL BARRIER — if access IS NECESSARY; must ALLOW ACCESS when area does not present risk AND PREVENT ACCESS at any other time
3. PHYSICAL BARRIER FIXED IN POSITION (tool-required removal) — if above not reasonably practicable; can only be altered or removed using A TOOL
4. PRESENCE-SENSING SAFEGUARDING SYSTEM — if physical barrier not reasonably practicable; must ELIMINATE RISK when person or part of person is in guarded area

GUARD CONSTRUCTION:
- SOLID CONSTRUCTION and SECURELY MOUNTED to resist impact or shock
- Make BYPASSING OR DISABLING as difficult as reasonably practicable
- Must NOT CREATE A RISK in itself (no pinch points, rough or sharp edges)
- Must be PROPERLY MAINTAINED
- Must be able to be REMOVED when plant not in normal operation for maintenance — and when removed, plant CANNOT BE RESTARTED unless guard replaced (so far as reasonably practicable)

INTERLOCKED BARRIERS:
- Connected to operational controls so plant PREVENTED FROM OPERATING until guard is closed
- Guard options: REMAIN LOCKED while plant operates (with delay before opening if dangerous parts take time to stop); or NOT LOCKED but STOP MACHINE when opened
- If moving parts do not stop immediately: guard DESIGNED TO DELAY RELEASE until moving parts have stopped

PHYSICAL BARRIERS FIXED IN POSITION:
- Easy to remove and replace but ONLY WITH A SPECIAL TOOL (e.g. spanner, Allen key)
- Should only be opened when machine NOT IN OPERATION
- Special tool MUST NOT be same as any tool issued to operator during normal work
- DEVICES THAT CAN BE OPERATED USING FINGERS (e.g. wing nuts, wedge inserts) MUST NOT BE USED

COLOUR CODING (best practice):
- All guards painted SAME HIGH VISIBILITY COLOUR (e.g. YELLOW OR RED — different from plant's colour)
- Paint surfaces BEHIND GUARD a CONTRASTING OR BRIGHT COLOUR so removal makes exposed surface clearly visible

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Moving parts accessible to workers without any guarding = serious non-compliance (WHS Regulation s208)
- Guards with wing nuts or finger-operated fasteners = non-compliance
- Interlocked guard defeated or bypassed = serious non-compliance
- Guards not replaced before plant restart after maintenance = serious non-compliance
- Guards painted same colour as surrounding machinery (not distinguishable) = flag

CHUNK 3: Presence-Sensing Safeguarding Systems
Section: CoP s5.1; WHS Regulation s208, s226
Topic: Presence-sensing systems as guarding; record keeping requirements

PRESENCE-SENSING SYSTEMS (WHS Regulation s208):
- Used when PHYSICAL GUARDS NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE
- Types: PHOTOELECTRIC LIGHT BEAMS, LASER SCANNERS, FOOT PRESSURE MATS
- Detect when person or part of body enters defined area and STOP MACHINE before person reaches danger zone
- Rely on SENSITIVE TRIP MECHANISMS and machine being able to STOP QUICKLY (may require brake)
- Require: selecting APPROPRIATE TRIP DEVICE for the work, and CORRECT LOCATION OF BEAMS accounting for speed of entry and machine stopping time

MANDATORY RECORDS (WHS Regulation s226):
Person with management or control must KEEP A RECORD of:
- SAFETY INTEGRITY TESTS
- INSPECTIONS
- MAINTENANCE
- COMMISSIONING
- DECOMMISSIONING
- DISMANTLING
- ALTERATIONS

RETENTION PERIOD:
- FIVE YEARS, OR
- LIFE OF THE PLANT or until person relinquishes control — if plant is REGISTERED PLANT or HAS BEEN ALTERED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Presence-sensing system with no maintenance or safety integrity test records = non-compliance (WHS Regulation s226)
- Presence-sensing system with no records dating back at least 5 years = non-compliance
- Light beams or sensors visibly misaligned or obstructed = non-compliance
- Machine with presence-sensing system that does not stop when beam interrupted = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Operational Controls and Emergency Stops
Section: CoP s5.2, s5.3; WHS Regulation s210, s211
Topic: Operator control requirements and emergency stop specifications (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

OPERATIONAL CONTROL REQUIREMENTS (WHS Regulation s210):
- IDENTIFIED ON THE PLANT to indicate nature, function and direction of operation
- LOCATED SO THEY CAN BE READILY AND CONVENIENTLY OPERATED by each person using plant
- LOCATED OR GUARDED TO PREVENT UNINTENTIONAL ACTIVATION
- ABLE TO BE LOCKED INTO 'OFF' POSITION to enable disconnection from energy sources

EMERGENCY STOP REQUIREMENTS (WHS Regulation s211):
If design includes emergency stop control, person with management or control must ensure:
- Stop control is PROMINENT, CLEARLY AND DURABLY MARKED and IMMEDIATELY ACCESSIBLE to each operator
- Any HANDLE, BAR OR PUSH BUTTON associated with stop control is coloured RED
- Stop control CANNOT BE ADVERSELY AFFECTED by electrical or electronic circuit malfunction

MULTI-OPERATOR PLANT:
- Where plant operated by MORE THAN ONE PERSON and MORE THAN ONE EMERGENCY STOP fitted: controls must be 'STOP AND LOCK-OFF' TYPE
- Plant CANNOT BE RESTARTED after emergency stop used UNLESS EACH ACTIVATED STOP CONTROL IS RESET

EMERGENCY STOP OPERATION:
- Once engaged, emergency stop controls REMAIN ENGAGED until deliberately disengaged
- Can ONLY BE DISENGAGED by DELIBERATE ACTION
- DISENGAGING emergency stop should NOT RESTART PLANT — should only ALLOW NORMAL STARTING SEQUENCE to be activated

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Controls not labelled with function or direction = non-compliance (WHS Regulation s210)
- Controls that can be accidentally activated without guarding = non-compliance
- Controls unable to be locked in 'off' position = non-compliance
- Emergency stop button NOT coloured red = non-compliance (WHS Regulation s211)
- Emergency stop not immediately accessible from each operator position = non-compliance
- Emergency stop that restarts plant when disengaged = non-compliance
- Multi-operator plant emergency stops allowing restart without each activated control being reset = non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Warning Devices for Plant
Section: CoP s5.4; WHS Regulation s212, s215
Topic: Warning device types and requirements for collision warning

WARNING DEVICE OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s212, s215):
- If plant design includes emergency warning device OR necessary to include one to minimise risk: device must be POSITIONED TO WORK TO BEST EFFECT
- If possibility of plant COLLIDING WITH PEDESTRIANS OR OTHER POWERED MOBILE PLANT: plant must have WARNING DEVICE that will warn persons at risk from movement

AUTOMATIC AUDIBLE ALARMS:
- Emit INTERMITTENT SOUND activated when gear or drive lever engaged
- Sound must be DISTINCT AND CLEARLY AUDIBLE ONLY IN THE HAZARD AREA
- More effective to COMBINE AUDIBLE ALARMS WITH OTHER WARNING DEVICES (e.g. flashing lights)

MOTION SENSORS:
- Activate alarm when plant MOVES SUDDENLY IN ANY DIRECTION (e.g. rollers, bulldozers, excavators, boom lifts, scissor lifts)
- Alarms usually DEACTIVATE AFTER SHORT TIME — MUST NOT deactivate if operator has RESTRICTED VISION when reversing

LIGHTS:
- Usually warn of FORWARD AND REVERSING MOVEMENT
- Wired to operate continuously or in HAZARD MODE BY FLASHING (usually when reversing)
- Choose INTENSITY AND COLOUR appropriate to workplace (orange light suitable inside warehouse but MAY NOT BE SEEN IN SUNLIGHT)

FLASHING/ROTARY LIGHTS:
- Coloured revolving lights mounted prominently (e.g. top of vehicle cabin)
- Suitable for any plant moving in workplace (forklifts, skid steer loaders)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Powered mobile plant operating in areas with pedestrian traffic without any audible or visual warning device = serious non-compliance
- Warning device present but not audible in hazard area (tested against ambient noise) = non-compliance
- Reversing alarm automatically deactivating when operator has restricted rearward vision = non-compliance
- Orange warning lights used on outdoor plant in daylight without assessment of visibility = flag
- Warning device absent on fixed plant with hazardous start-up or overpressure risk = non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Isolation — Lockout/Tagout Procedures
Section: CoP s5.5; WHS Regulation (general)
Topic: Lockout/tagout process steps and device requirements

LOCKOUT PROCESS STEPS (in order):
1. SHUT DOWN machinery and equipment
2. IDENTIFY ALL ENERGY SOURCES and other hazards
3. IDENTIFY ALL ISOLATION POINTS
4. ISOLATE ALL ENERGY SOURCES
5. CONTROL OR DE-ENERGISE ALL STORED ENERGY
6. LOCK OUT ALL ISOLATION POINTS
7. TAG machinery controls, energy sources and other hazards
8. TEST by 'trying' to REACTIVATE PLANT without exposing tester or others to risk

ENERGY SOURCES REQUIRING ISOLATION:
- Electricity (mains)
- Battery or capacitor banks
- Solar panels
- Fuels
- Heat
- Steam
- Fluids or gases under pressure (water, air, steam, hydraulic oil)
- Stored energy (compressed springs)
- Gravity
- Radiation

MULTIPLE WORKERS:
- Use lock allowing ONE OR MORE PADLOCKS to be fitted
- If more than one person working on plant simultaneously: EACH WORKER must attach PADLOCK to device
- Alternative: ONE PADLOCK on isolation point, KEYS LOCKED IN BOX separately locked by each worker

KEY MANAGEMENT:
- Each worker should have LOCK, TAG AND KEY for EACH ISOLATION POINT
- NO DUPLICATE KEY for any lock, except MASTER KEY kept in SECURE LOCATION and used ONLY IN EMERGENCY
- If MORE THAN ONE ENERGY SOURCE must be isolated: SINGLE KEY to each lock-out device held by SAME PERSON

TAGS:
- Tags should ONLY be used as means of PROVIDING INFORMATION to others
- TAG MUST NOT BE USED ON ITS OWN as isolation device — ONLY A LOCK is effective
- Tags and locks should ONLY BE REMOVED by PERSON WHO APPLIED THEM or by SUPERVISOR after consultation with tag signatory

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Maintenance being performed without isolation (lockout/tagout) = serious non-compliance
- Tags used alone without locks as isolation devices = non-compliance
- Locks without tags and/or tags without locks in isolation systems = flag
- Multiple workers sharing single padlock on isolation point = non-compliance
- Workers performing stored energy tasks (hydraulic lines, springs, suspended loads) without additional release steps beyond lockout = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Inspection and Maintenance Obligations
Section: CoP s4.5, s4.6; WHS Regulation s213, s210
Topic: Inspection frequency, maintenance requirements, isolation during maintenance

INSPECTION OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s213):
Person with management or control must ensure MAINTENANCE, INSPECTION AND, IF NECESSARY, TESTING carried out by COMPETENT PERSON.

INSPECTION FREQUENCY — must be carried out:
- In accordance with MANUFACTURER'S RECOMMENDATIONS (if any)
- If no manufacturer's recommendations: in accordance with RECOMMENDATIONS OF COMPETENT PERSON
- If not reasonably practicable to comply with above: ANNUALLY AT MINIMUM

ISOLATION BEFORE MAINTENANCE OR CLEANING:
- Plant should USUALLY BE ISOLATED before maintenance or cleaning starts
- Methods to PREVENT ACCIDENTAL OPERATION must be implemented

OPERATIONAL CONTROLS DURING MAINTENANCE (WHS Regulation s210):
If plant needs to be operated while being maintained or cleaned:
- Controls PERMIT OPERATION while person undertaking maintenance or cleaning
- Controls EITHER: CANNOT BE OPERATED by person other than person carrying out maintenance/cleaning, OR can ONLY BE OPERATED by AUTHORISED person
- Allow plant to be operated in way that ELIMINATES RISKS or, if not reasonably practicable, MINIMISES RISKS

POST-MAINTENANCE:
- All GUARDING must be replaced before plant used after maintenance or cleaning
- Plant with damage posing risk must be WITHDRAWN FROM SERVICE until risks controlled

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No inspection records for plant on site = non-compliance
- Plant in use beyond manufacturer's or competent person's recommended inspection interval = non-compliance
- Guards removed and not replaced after inspection or maintenance = serious non-compliance
- Plant undergoing maintenance without isolation = serious non-compliance
- Plant operated during maintenance without controlled operational procedure = serious non-compliance
- Hand-held powered plant with visibly worn or damaged parts in use = non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Plant Storage and Unattended Powered Mobile Plant
Section: CoP s4.7; WHS Regulation s207, s214, s215
Topic: Storage requirements and powered mobile plant when unattended

STORAGE OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s207):
Person with management or control must ensure plant NOT IN USE is left in state that does NOT CREATE RISK to health or safety.

STORAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Implement controls to ELIMINATE OR MINIMISE risks during storage (e.g. RELEASE STORED ENERGY, LOWER MOVING PARTS that lower under gravity, PROVIDE SUPPORT to prevent toppling)
- Plant stored for extended period should be RECOMMISSIONED by carrying out SAME LEVEL OF TESTING AND INSPECTION as when first commissioned

POWERED MOBILE PLANT WHEN UNATTENDED:
- Must be parked on FIRM, LEVEL SURFACE
- HANDBRAKE APPLIED
- MOTOR SWITCHED OFF and rendered INOPERABLE (e.g. key removed)

POWERED MOBILE PLANT — RISK MANAGEMENT (WHS Regulation s214):
Person with management or control must manage risks associated with:
- Plant OVERTURNING
- Things FALLING ON OPERATOR
- Operator being EJECTED from plant
- Plant COLLIDING with any person or thing
- MECHANICAL FAILURE of pressurised elements releasing fluids that pose risk

OPERATOR PROTECTIVE DEVICES (WHS Regulation s215(2)):
- SUITABLE COMBINATION of operator protective devices must be PROVIDED, MAINTAINED AND USED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Plant not in use left in unstable position, unsupported, or with energy sources not released = serious non-compliance
- Forklift or industrial truck left unattended without handbrake applied and key removed = non-compliance
- Plant removed from service after extended storage being returned to use without recommissioning = flag
- Powered mobile plant without appropriate rollover or operator protection = non-compliance

CHUNK 9: Lifting and Suspending Loads
Section: CoP s3.1, s4.3; WHS Regulation s219, s220
Topic: Lifting operations requirements, load limits, suspending persons (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

LIFTING AND SUSPENDING OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s219):
Person with management or control must ensure:
- Plant used for lifting/suspending is SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED TO LIFT OR SUSPEND THE LOAD (so far as reasonably practicable)
- Lifting carried out WITH LIFTING ATTACHMENTS SUITABLE FOR LOAD
- Lifting carried out WITHIN SAFE WORKING LIMITS OF PLANT

GENERAL LIFTING RULES:
- NO LOADS SUSPENDED OR TRAVEL OVER A PERSON unless plant SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED for that purpose
- Loads REMAIN UNDER CONTROL during activity
- NO LOAD LIFTED SIMULTANEOUSLY BY MORE THAN ONE ITEM OF PLANT unless method ensures load on each item does not exceed DESIGN CAPACITY

LIFTING PERSONS — EXCEPTIONS (WHS Regulation s220):
If not reasonably practicable to use purpose-designed plant for lifting persons:
- Plant must not cause GREATER RISK than purpose-designed plant
- Persons lifted in WORK BOX SECURELY ATTACHED to plant
- Persons REMAIN SUBSTANTIALLY WITHIN work box while lifted or suspended
- SAFETY HARNESS worn if risk of falling from height
- MEANS PROVIDED for persons to SAFELY EXIT from plant in event of failure in normal operation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Loads suspended or travelling over workers without purpose-designed plant = serious non-compliance
- Personnel being lifted in improvised platform rather than certified work box = serious non-compliance
- Lifting operation where two items of plant simultaneously lifting same load without documented safe lifting method = non-compliance
- Plant lifting loads in excess of marked safe working load = serious non-compliance
- No safety harness on persons being elevated in work box where fall risk exists = non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Working Near Overhead and Underground Electric Lines
Section: CoP s3.3; Electrical Safety Regulation Part 5
Topic: Electrical line safe working distances and exclusion zones (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

PCBU OBLIGATIONS (Electrical Safety Regulation Part 5):
- Must ensure, so far as reasonably practicable, NO PERSON, PLANT OR THING at workplace comes within UNSAFE DISTANCE of overhead or underground electric line
- If not reasonably practicable to ensure safe distance: must ensure RISK ASSESSMENT conducted and control measures CONSISTENT WITH RISK ASSESSMENT and requirements of relevant electricity supply entity

MINIMUM EXCLUSION ZONE:
- AT LEAST 3 METRES from any overhead powerline for voltages UP TO 132kV
- Greater distances apply above 132kV
- Applies to ANY PART of boom AND drop hose — not just truck body

PROHIBITED OPERATIONS:
- Plant MUST NOT be operated OVER THE TOP of energised powerlines
- Pipes must be kept PARALLEL TO GROUND when setting up or unloading near powerlines

SPOTTER REQUIREMENT:
- SPOTTER REQUIRED when risk of plant entering exclusion zone — must remain in clear communication with operator

NOTE:
- Tiger tails/line markers DO NOT insulate powerlines — exclusion zone applies regardless

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any part of plant within 3m of overhead powerline = serious non-compliance
- Plant operating over or across energised powerlines = serious non-compliance
- No spotter present when plant operating near powerlines = non-compliance
- No exclusion zone markers or signage near underground electric line routes in excavation or plant movement zones = flag
- Plant operating within striking distance of overhead power lines without documented risk assessment or clearance from electricity authority = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 11: High Risk Work Licences
Section: CoP s4.3; WHS Regulation s85, Schedule 3, Schedule 4
Topic: High risk work licence requirements for plant operators

HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s85):
- PCBU MUST NOT DIRECT OR ALLOW worker to carry out HIGH RISK WORK requiring licence unless PCBU has seen WRITTEN EVIDENCE that worker holds relevant HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE

HIGH RISK WORK REQUIRING LICENCES includes:
- OPERATING INDUSTRIAL TRUCKS (forklifts)
- OPERATING SOME CRANES
- SCAFFOLD AND RIGGING WORK

LICENCE CLASSES:
- Refer to WHS Regulation Schedule 3 for classes of high-risk work licences and types of plant involved
- WHS Regulation Schedule 4 sets out competency requirements

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker operating crane, forklift or other licensed plant without sight of valid high risk work licence = serious non-compliance
- Operator of industrial truck, crane or other licensed plant unable to produce high risk work licence = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 12: Plant Modifications and Altered Use
Section: CoP s4.4; WHS Regulation s244, s282, s205, s206
Topic: Risk management for plant modifications and changed use; altered design registration

WHEN CHANGES ARE PLANNED:
If person with management or control intends to:
- ALTER DESIGN of plant
- CHANGE WAY PLANT IS USED, or
- CHANGE SYSTEM OF WORK associated with plant
Then person MUST CARRY OUT RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS AGAIN

ALTERNATIVE USE (WHS Regulation s206):
- If plant to be used in DIFFERENT WAY or for PURPOSE IT WAS NOT DESIGNED FOR: risks of new use must be ASSESSED BY COMPETENT PERSON
- If competent person decides plant NOT SUITABLE for proposed task = MUST NOT BE USED FOR THAT TASK

MAKING ALTERATIONS:
- Before altering, consult with DESIGNER AND MANUFACTURER
- If person with management or control makes alterations = person becomes DESIGNER OR MANUFACTURER and those obligations apply
- If original designer/manufacturer cannot be contacted: alterations must be carried out by COMPETENT PERSON in accordance with relevant TECHNICAL STANDARDS
- Plant MUST BE ISOLATED from power sources and UNABLE TO BE SWITCHED ON ACCIDENTALLY before and during alterations

ALTERED DESIGN REGISTRATION (WHS Regulation s244):
- If registered plant design is altered AND altered design MAY AFFECT HEALTH AND SAFETY = ALTERED DESIGN MUST BE REGISTERED
- Application for registration of altered design must be made in JURISDICTION THAT REGISTERED ORIGINAL DESIGN

BEFORE RETURNING ALTERED PLANT TO SERVICE:
- CONTROL MEASURES in place to eliminate or minimise risks created by alteration
- INSPECT AND TEST plant having regard to altered design specifications and relevant technical standards

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Plant visibly modified from original configuration without documentation, without risk assessment, or without notification to regulator = serious non-compliance
- Plant clearly being used for task outside design intent without competent person assessment = non-compliance
- Plant in use post-modification without visible test records or training records = flag
- Altered registrable plant without updated registration = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 13: Installation and Commissioning — Positioning and Clearances
Section: CoP s4.2; WHS Regulation s204
Topic: Installation requirements, positioning clearances, commissioning obligations (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

COMMISSIONING OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s204):
Person with management or control must:
- NOT COMMISSION plant unless established that plant is, so far as reasonably practicable, WITHOUT RISKS to health and safety
- Ensure persons installing, assembling, constructing, commissioning are COMPETENT PERSONS, provided with all information necessary to minimise risks
- Ensure processes INCLUDE INSPECTIONS to monitor risks during these activities

POSITIONING PLANT — CLEARANCES:
- SUFFICIENT SPACE for safe access: SUGGESTED 600mm — minimum width of walkway — for operation, cleaning, maintenance, inspection and emergency evacuation
- Plant must NOT OBSTRUCT DOORWAYS AND EMERGENCY EXITS
- Plant rests on SUITABLE FOUNDATION ensuring it is STABLE AND SECURE

COMMISSIONING:
- Commission plant by performing necessary ADJUSTMENTS, TESTS AND INSPECTIONS to ensure plant in full working order to specified requirements before use
- Commissioning sequence must be in accordance with DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS
- Tests must check plant will perform within DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Minimum 600mm walkway clearance around plant not maintained = non-compliance for access, maintenance and evacuation
- Plant installed without commissioning documentation or test records = flag
- Electrical installations not compliant with AS/NZS 3000 = non-compliance
- Plant commissioned without records of inspection by competent person = non-compliance
- Plant positioned obstructing emergency exits or doorways = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 14: Mandatory Record Keeping for Plant
Section: CoP Chapter 7; WHS Regulation s237, s226
Topic: Mandatory record keeping requirements for registered plant and presence-sensing systems

MANDATORY RECORDS — REGISTERED PLANT (WHS Regulation s237):
Person with management or control of plant REQUIRED TO BE REGISTERED must keep record of:
- ALL TESTS
- INSPECTIONS
- MAINTENANCE
- COMMISSIONING
- DECOMMISSIONING
- DISMANTLING
- ALTERATIONS

RETENTION PERIOD — registered plant:
- For PERIOD THAT PLANT IS USED or until person RELINQUISHES CONTROL of plant

MANDATORY RECORDS — PRESENCE-SENSING SAFEGUARDING SYSTEM (WHS Regulation s226):
Person with management or control must keep record of:
- SAFETY INTEGRITY TESTS
- INSPECTIONS
- MAINTENANCE
- COMMISSIONING
- DECOMMISSIONING
- DISMANTLING
- ALTERATIONS

RETENTION PERIOD — presence-sensing systems:
- FIVE YEARS, OR
- LIFE OF PLANT or until person relinquishes control — if plant is REGISTERED PLANT or HAS BEEN ALTERED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Registered plant in operation with no test, inspection or maintenance records available = serious non-compliance (WHS Regulation s237)
- Presence-sensing safeguarding system with no safety integrity test records dating back at least 5 years = non-compliance (WHS Regulation s226)
- No operator training or competency records for plant on site = non-compliance

CHUNK 15: Second-Hand Plant — Supplier Duties
Section: CoP s4.1; WHS Regulation s199, s200
Topic: Second-hand plant supplier obligations and scrap/spare parts requirements

SECOND-HAND PLANT — SUPPLIER DUTIES (WHS Regulation s199):
Supplier must:
- Take all reasonable steps to obtain information required from manufacturer
- Ensure person to whom plant supplied is GIVEN INFORMATION OBTAINED
- Ensure, so far as reasonably practicable, any FAULTS IN PLANT ARE IDENTIFIED
- Provide WRITTEN NOTICE before supply stating:
  - CONDITION OF PLANT
  - ANY FAULTS IDENTIFIED
  - If appropriate: that plant SHOULD NOT BE USED until faults rectified
- Take all reasonable steps to obtain information about HOW TO USE PLANT CORRECTLY AND SAFELY from manufacturer or original supplier
- Provide buyer: all obtained information and ALL AVAILABLE RECORDS kept by previous owner (may include data sheets, test certificates, operations and service manuals, reports, safety manual)

SECOND-HAND PLANT — OUTDATED OR MISSING SAFETY FEATURES:
- Supplier must consider whether reasonably practicable to RETROFIT OR MODIFY plant to improve safety
- Must ensure plant is SAFE AND HAS ALL REQUIRED SAFETY FEATURES before bringing into service

SCRAP AND SPARE PARTS (WHS Regulation s200):
- Plant sold for scrap or spare parts does not need to be made safe or supplied with instructions for use
- Supplier MUST INFORM prospective buyers IN WRITING or by MARKING THE ITEM that it is supplied for scrap or spare parts only and CANNOT BE USED SAFELY in current form for other purposes

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Second-hand plant arriving on site without written notice of condition and identified faults from supplier = non-compliance
- Plant purchased or hired without evidence of manufacturer's specifications, service manuals or maintenance records = flag
- Scrapped plant without written notice of spare/scrap status = non-compliance if re-used

CHUNK 16: Specific Plant — Industrial Robots, Lasers, Pressure Equipment
Section: CoP s3.3; WHS Regulation s207 (various)
Topic: Specific control requirements for industrial robots, lasers, and pressure equipment

INDUSTRIAL ROBOTS:
- Typical risks: robot moving unexpectedly; entrapment in robot's range of movement
- Controls typically include: PERIMETER GUARDING with interlocked access; PRESENCE-SENSING SYSTEMS; clear EXCLUSION ZONES; ISOLATION during maintenance
- Robot taken off-line but still functional = must be isolated from power supply, with LOCK-OUT AND TAG-OUT and PHYSICAL STOPS to prevent movement if accidentally powered (WHS Regulation s207)

LASERS:
- Relevant standards: AS/NZS IEC 60825.1 (Equipment classification and requirements); AS/NZS IEC 60825.14 (User's guide); AS 2397 (Safe use of lasers in building and construction)
- Classification determines level of risk control required

PRESSURE EQUIPMENT:
- Relevant standards: AS/NZS 1200; AS 4343 (Hazard levels); AS/NZS 3788 (In-service inspection); AS 3873 (Operation and maintenance)
- Design registration required for pressure equipment categorised as HAZARD LEVEL A, B, C OR D (per AS 4343–2005 s2.1)
- Item registration required for BOILERS (hazard level A, B or C) and PRESSURE VESSELS (hazard level A, B or C — except gas cylinders, LP Gas automotive, serially produced vessels)
- ANNUAL RENEWAL of item registration required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Industrial robot operating without perimeter guarding or presence-sensing system = serious non-compliance
- Robot taken off-line but left unsecured and energised = non-compliance (WHS Regulation s207)
- Boiler or pressure vessel on site without current item registration and inspection records = serious non-compliance
- Laser equipment in use without classification assessment or appropriate controls for the class = non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Outrigger Timber and Pad Specifications for Mobile Plant
Section: CoP s4.2 (general plant positioning); AS 2550 (cranes safe use)
Topic: Minimum timber/pad dimensions and ground pressure considerations (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

TIMBER/PAD MINIMUM SPECIFICATIONS (general best practice):
- Minimum width: 200mm
- Minimum thickness: 75mm
- Timbers must be laid with NO GAPS and wider than outrigger foot

GROUND PRESSURE CONSIDERATIONS:
- Plant must rest on SUITABLE FOUNDATION ensuring it is STABLE AND SECURE
- Outrigger pads/timbers must be appropriate for visible ground type
- Plant must NOT be positioned over or adjacent to: previously disturbed or backfilled ground; excavations, trenches, holes, cellars, basements or pits; soft, wet or inadequately compacted ground

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Outrigger pads/timbers visibly undersized for visible ground type = flag
- Single small pad on soft or wet ground = non-compliant
- Timbers with visible gaps between them = non-compliant
- No pads used at all on soft ground = serious non-compliance
- Ground visibly soft, wet or muddy under or near outrigger pads = flag
- Outrigger pads sinking or tilting = immediate non-compliance
- Mobile plant adjacent to open excavation without visible distance checking = flag

CHUNK 18: PPE Requirements for Plant Operations
Section: CoP s2.4; WHS Act s19; WHS Regulation s39
Topic: PPE as lowest hierarchy control; training and instruction obligations

PPE IN HIERARCHY OF CONTROLS:
- PPE is LOWEST in hierarchy of control measures
- Does NOT control hazard at SOURCE
- RELIES ON HUMAN BEHAVIOUR and supervision
- Used alone = LEAST EFFECTIVE in minimising risks
- Must be supplemented with higher-order controls wherever reasonably practicable

TRAINING OBLIGATIONS (WHS Act s19; WHS Regulation s39):
- PCBU must ensure provision of information, training, instruction or supervision NECESSARY TO PROTECT ALL PERSONS from risks arising from work
- Information, training and instruction must be SUITABLE AND ADEQUATE having regard to: NATURE OF WORK carried out; NATURE OF RISKS associated with work at time of training; CONTROL MEASURES implemented
- Must ensure information, training and instruction provided in way that is READILY UNDERSTANDABLE to recipient
- Workers must be TRAINED and have APPROPRIATE SKILLS to carry out task safely
- Training must be provided by COMPETENT PERSON

SAFE WORK PROCEDURES SHOULD INCLUDE:
- Proper USE, WEARING, STORAGE AND MAINTENANCE OF PPE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Only administrative controls and/or PPE in place with no higher-order controls = non-compliant hierarchy; flag for review
- Workers operating plant without evidence of training or demonstrated competency = non-compliance
- Workers using plant in way clearly inconsistent with manufacturer's instructions or safe work procedures = flag

CHUNK 19: Hopper Grate Specifications
Section: CoP (general machinery guarding); AS 4024 (safety of machinery)
Topic: Hopper grate bar spacing and clearance requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

HOPPER GRATE REQUIREMENTS (general best practice from concrete pumping and machinery safety standards):
- Grate bar spacing MUST NOT EXCEED 75mm
- Distance from top of grate to any moving part must be AT LEAST 100mm
- Grates that open must have interlock that de-activates moving parts when lifted

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hopper grate open or missing during operation = serious non-compliance
- Grate bar spacing exceeding 75mm = non-compliance
- Distance from grate to moving parts less than 100mm = non-compliance
- Grate without interlock allowing operation when open = non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Anchor Bracket and Pipe Clamp Specifications
Section: CoP (general plant installation); AS 4024 (safety of machinery)
Topic: Anchor bracket spacing and pipe clamp requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

ANCHOR BRACKETS (general best practice from concrete pumping standards):
- Maximum spacing: 3 METRES APART (unless engineer specifies otherwise)
- Cranes, hoist towers, scaffolding or formwork MUST NOT be used to secure lines

PIPE CLAMPS:
- Must sustain maximum pressure applied
- Locking pins must be used and engaged
- Damaged or deformed clamps must be immediately replaced
- Quick release clamps without locking pin provision must NOT be used
- Clamps must be marked with manufacturer, pressure rating and size

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Anchor brackets at intervals greater than 3m with no engineer spec = non-compliance
- Pipeline secured to scaffolding or crane tower = non-compliance
- Pipe clamps without visible locking pins = non-compliance

Section: CoP s5.4; WHS Regulation s212 (warning devices)
Topic: Traffic cone specifications and exclusion zone marking requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)
