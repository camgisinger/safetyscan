CHUNK 1: Definitions — Key Terms for Falls Risk Management
Section: CoP Appendix A (Glossary); WHS Regulation s78–s80
Topic: Legal definitions of falls, fall prevention device, work positioning system, fall arrest system, solid construction, competent person

FALL means:
- A fall by a person FROM ONE LEVEL TO ANOTHER that is reasonably likely to cause injury
- Includes falls at ground level INTO HOLES (e.g. trenches, service pits)
- Not limited to falls from height — ground-level openings are within scope

RISK OF A FALL means circumstances exposing a worker or other person to a risk of a fall reasonably likely to cause injury, including where the person is:
- In or on plant or a structure AT AN ELEVATED LEVEL
- In or on plant being used to GAIN ACCESS TO AN ELEVATED LEVEL
- In the vicinity of an OPENING through which a person could fall
- In the vicinity of an EDGE over which a person could fall
- On or in the vicinity of a SURFACE THROUGH WHICH a person could fall
- On or near a SLIPPERY, SLOPING OR UNSTABLE SURFACE

FREE FALL means:
- Any fall or part of a fall where the person is under the UNRESTRAINED INFLUENCE OF GRAVITY over any fall distance, whether vertically or on a slope where it is not possible to walk without assistance of a handrail or hand line

SOLID CONSTRUCTION means an area that has:
- A surface STRUCTURALLY CAPABLE of supporting all people and things on it
- BARRIERS around its perimeter and any openings to prevent a fall
- An EVEN AND READILY NEGOTIABLE surface and gradient
- A SAFE MEANS OF ENTRY AND EXIT

FALL PREVENTION DEVICE means:
- Material or equipment (or combination) designed to prevent a fall for TEMPORARY WORK AT HEIGHTS
- Once in place after initial installation, requires NO ONGOING ADJUSTMENT, ALTERATION OR OPERATION by any person to ensure its integrity
- Includes: secure fencing, edge protection, working platforms, covers

WORK POSITIONING SYSTEM means:
- Any plant or structure, OTHER THAN A TEMPORARY WORK PLATFORM, that enables a person to be POSITIONED AND SAFELY SUPPORTED at a location in such a way that a fall is prevented

FALL ARREST SYSTEM means:
- Plant or material designed to ARREST A FALL
- Includes: catch platforms, safety nets, individual fall arrest systems (harnesses, anchorage lines/rails)

TEMPORARY WORK PLATFORM means:
- A working platform, other than a permanently installed fixed platform, used to provide a working area for the duration of the work
- The design of the platform prevents workers from falling
- Includes: scaffolds, EWPs, mast climbers, workboxes, building maintenance units, portable/mobile fabricated platforms

COMPETENT PERSON means:
- A person who has acquired through TRAINING, QUALIFICATION OR EXPERIENCE the knowledge and skills to carry out the task

ENERGY ABSORBER means:
- A device that REDUCES DECELERATION FORCE when a fall is suddenly arrested
- Reduces loadings on the anchorage and the person's body
- May be a separate item or manufactured as part of the lanyard

TOTAL FALL DISTANCE means:
- The TOTAL DISTANCE a person is likely to fall during both the free and restrained parts of a fall
- Includes the MAXIMUM DYNAMIC EXTENSION of all supporting components

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any unprotected edge, hole, opening or level change on a construction site = fall hazard requiring assessment and control
- Workers accessing elevated areas with no physical fall prevention in place = non-compliance
- Ground-level openings (trenches, service pits, penetrations) with no cover or barricading = non-compliance
- Surfaces visibly fragile, sloping, wet or unstable with workers present and no controls = flag

CHUNK 2: Duty Holders — Who Has Health and Safety Duties for Falls?
Section: CoP s1.1; WHS Act s19, s22–s29, s27, s28, s46, s47; WHS Regulation s78, s79
Topic: Legal duties of PCBUs, designers, manufacturers, officers, workers and other persons at the workplace

PRIMARY DUTY — PCBU (WHS Act s19):
- Must ELIMINATE the risk of falls in the workplace, OR if not reasonably practicable: MINIMISE those risks so far as is reasonably practicable
- Must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that any work involving risk of a fall is carried out ON THE GROUND OR ON A SOLID CONSTRUCTION
- Must provide SAFE MEANS OF ACCESS TO AND EXIT FROM the workplace and from areas from which a person could fall
- Must provide ADEQUATE PROTECTION against fall risk in the following priority order (WHS Regulation s79):
  1. Fall prevention device (if reasonably practicable)
  2. Work positioning system (if fall prevention device not reasonably practicable)
  3. Fall arrest system (if neither of the above is reasonably practicable)
- A COMBINATION of controls may be used if a single control is not sufficient

HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK — SWMS MANDATORY:
- Work involving a risk of a person FALLING MORE THAN 2 METRES = high risk construction work
- A SAFE WORK METHOD STATEMENT (SWMS) MUST be prepared BEFORE the work starts

DESIGNER / MANUFACTURER / IMPORTER / SUPPLIER DUTIES (WHS Act Part 2 Division 3):
- Must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that plant or structure is WITHOUT RISKS TO HEALTH AND SAFETY
- Must carry out testing and analysis
- Must provide specific information about the plant or structure
- Manufacturers must CONSULT WITH DESIGNERS of plant
- Importers must CONSULT WITH DESIGNERS AND MANUFACTURERS
- Person commissioning construction work must CONSULT WITH THE DESIGNER of the structure

OFFICER DUTIES (WHS Act s27):
- Must exercise DUE DILIGENCE to ensure the PCBU complies with the WHS Act and Regulation
- Must take reasonable steps to:
  - Gain an understanding of the HAZARDS AND RISKS associated with the operations
  - Ensure the business has and uses APPROPRIATE RESOURCES AND PROCESSES to eliminate or minimise fall risks

WORKER DUTIES (WHS Act s28):
- Must take REASONABLE CARE for own health and safety
- Must not adversely affect health and safety of other persons
- Must COMPLY WITH REASONABLE INSTRUCTIONS so far as reasonably able
- Must COOPERATE with health and safety policies and procedures notified to workers
- Must USE OR WEAR PPE in accordance with information, training and instruction provided

OTHER PERSONS AT THE WORKPLACE (WHS Act s29):
- Must take reasonable care for own health and safety
- Must comply, so far as reasonably able, with reasonable instructions given by the PCBU

CONSULTATION — WORKERS (WHS Act s47):
- PCBU MUST consult with workers who are directly affected by a health and safety matter
- Covers employees, contractors, subcontractors, on-hire workers, outworkers, apprentices, trainees, work experience students, volunteers
- Workers ENTITLED to participate and be represented by a health and safety representative

CONSULTATION — OTHER DUTY HOLDERS (WHS Act s46):
- PCBU MUST consult, cooperate and coordinate with ALL OTHER PERSONS who have a WHS duty in relation to the same matter

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No SWMS available for construction work with fall risk >2m = serious non-compliance
- No physical fall controls in place (reliance on signage/training only) = non-compliant hierarchy
- No evidence of worker consultation on fall hazards = flag
- Multiple PCBUs on site with no coordination arrangements for fall controls = flag

CHUNK 3: Risk Management Process — Identifying, Assessing and Controlling Fall Hazards
Section: CoP s1.2, s3.1–s3.4; WHS Regulation s34–s38
Topic: Four-step risk management process; hazard identification; risk assessment; hierarchy of controls for falls

RISK MANAGEMENT — FOUR STEPS:
1. IDENTIFY HAZARDS — find out what could cause harm
2. ASSESS RISKS (if necessary) — understand the nature and severity of harm and likelihood of it occurring
   - Note: If the risk is known with known controls, formal risk assessment may not be required
3. CONTROL RISKS — implement the most effective controls reasonably practicable, in accordance with the hierarchy
4. REVIEW CONTROL MEASURES — ensure they remain effective over time

HAZARD IDENTIFICATION — KEY THINGS TO LOOK FOR:
- EDGES — open edges of floors, working platforms, walkways, walls or roofs requiring protection
- HOLES, OPENINGS OR EXCAVATIONS — requiring guarding
- SURFACES — check for: stability, fragility or brittleness; strength/load capacity; slip potential (wet, polished or glazed surfaces); safe movement where surfaces change; slope of work surfaces
- LEVELS — where levels change and workers may be exposed to a fall from one level to another
- STRUCTURES — stability of temporary or permanent structures
- GROUND — evenness and stability for safe support of scaffolds or work platforms
- WORKING AREA — whether crowded or cluttered
- SAFE MEANS OF ACCESS TO AND EXIT FROM the workplace and working area
- HAND GRIP — places where hand grip may be lost

WORK TASKS REQUIRING SPECIAL ATTENTION:
- Near an UNPROTECTED OPEN EDGE (e.g. incomplete stairwells, leading formwork edges)
- Using EQUIPMENT TO WORK AT ELEVATED LEVEL (EWPs, portable ladders)
- On or alongside a FRAGILE SURFACE (cement sheeting, rusty metal, fibreglass sheeting, skylights)
- On a POTENTIALLY UNSTABLE SURFACE (risk of ground collapse)
- On a SLOPING OR SLIPPERY SURFACE
- Near a HOLE, SHAFT OR PIT (trenches, lift shafts, service pits, floor/column penetrations)

RISK ASSESSMENT — FACTORS TO CONSIDER:
- What could happen if a fall occurred and HOW LIKELY it is to happen
- The DESIGN AND LAYOUT of elevated work areas including DISTANCE OF POTENTIAL FALL
- Number and movement of people at the workplace
- PROXIMITY OF WORKERS to unsafe areas (loading docks, work above people, risk of falling objects)
- ADEQUACY of inspection and maintenance of plant (e.g. scaffolds)
- ADEQUACY OF LIGHTING
- Weather conditions (rain, wind, extreme heat/cold — slippery/unstable conditions)
- Suitability of FOOTWEAR AND CLOTHING
- Suitability and condition of LADDERS including where/how they are being used
- ADEQUACY OF KNOWLEDGE AND TRAINING (particularly young, new or inexperienced workers)
- Adequacy of EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

HIERARCHY OF CONTROLS FOR FALLS (WHS Regulation s78, s79) — mandatory priority order:
1. WORK ON THE GROUND OR SOLID CONSTRUCTION — eliminate the fall hazard (HIGHEST)
2. FALL PREVENTION DEVICE — e.g. edge protection, working platforms, covers, guardrails
3. WORK POSITIONING SYSTEM — e.g. restraint technique, industrial rope access
4. FALL ARREST SYSTEM — e.g. catch platforms, safety nets, individual fall arrest harness systems
5. ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS — no go areas, permit systems, work sequencing (LOWEST; must not be used exclusively unless higher controls not reasonably practicable)

MAINTAINING AND REVIEWING CONTROLS (WHS Regulation s37, s38):
- Control measures must be maintained so they remain FIT FOR PURPOSE, suitable for the NATURE AND DURATION of the work, and CORRECTLY INSTALLED, SET UP AND USED
- INSPECTION REGIME must include:
  - Equipment to be inspected including UNIQUE IDENTIFICATION
  - FREQUENCY AND TYPE of inspection (pre-use checks, detailed inspections)
  - ACTION to be taken on finding defective equipment
  - MEANS OF RECORDING inspections
  - Training of users
  - System of MONITORING the inspection regime to verify inspections are carried out appropriately
- REVIEW MUST OCCUR when:
  - Control measure does not control the risk so far as reasonably practicable
  - Before a CHANGE at the workplace likely to give rise to a new or different risk
  - A new relevant hazard or risk is identified
  - Results of CONSULTATION indicate review is necessary
  - A HSR requests a review

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No inspection records for scaffolds, EWPs or fall arrest equipment = non-compliance
- Defective fall arrest/restraint equipment in use = serious non-compliance
- Administrative controls (signs, barricades only) used as the sole fall control where physical controls are practicable = non-compliant hierarchy
- No documented risk assessment or SWMS for high-risk construction work with falls >2m = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Information, Training, Instruction and Supervision
Section: CoP s1.3; WHS Act s19; WHS Regulation s39, s317
Topic: Training obligations, content requirements, supervision, general construction induction

PCBU TRAINING OBLIGATIONS (WHS Act s19; WHS Regulation s39):
- Must ensure information, training and instruction is SUITABLE AND ADEQUATE having regard to:
  - The NATURE OF THE WORK carried out
  - The NATURE OF THE RISKS at the time of training
  - The CONTROL MEASURES implemented
- Must ensure information, training and instruction are provided in a way that is READILY UNDERSTANDABLE for the person receiving it
- Workers must be TRAINED AND HAVE APPROPRIATE SKILLS to carry out tasks safely
- Training must be provided by a COMPETENT PERSON

TRAINING CONTENT — MINIMUM FOR WORKERS EXPOSED TO FALL RISK AT ANY HEIGHT:
- Proper USE, WEARING, STORAGE AND MAINTENANCE of PPE
- HAZARDS AND RISKS associated with work at these heights
- How to follow HEALTH AND SAFETY PROCEDURES including contents of any SWMS
- REASONS fall protection measures have been put in place and HOW TO USE THEM PROPERLY
- PROCEDURES FOR EMERGENCY AND RESCUE
- Procedures for REPORTING fall hazards and incidents
- Correct SELECTION, FITTING, USE, CARE, INSPECTION, MAINTENANCE AND STORAGE of fall arrest and restraint equipment (where used)
- Correct use of TOOLS AND EQUIPMENT (e.g. using a tool belt instead of carrying tools)
- Control measures for OTHER POTENTIAL HAZARDS (e.g. electrical hazards, crushing/entanglement from EWPs)

SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS:
- Workers exposed to fall risk must be ADEQUATELY SUPERVISED by a competent person, especially when:
  - Undergoing training
  - Unfamiliar with the working environment
- PCBU must check that:
  - Only workers who have received training and instruction ARE AUTHORISED to carry out the work
  - Workers USE FALL CONTROL MEASURES in the correct manner

GENERAL CONSTRUCTION INDUCTION (WHS Regulation s317):
- PCBU MUST NOT direct or allow a worker to carry out CONSTRUCTION WORK unless the worker has SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETED GENERAL CONSTRUCTION INDUCTION TRAINING
- Worker must hold a GENERAL CONSTRUCTION INDUCTION TRAINING CARD (White Card) or certification

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers using fall arrest harnesses, EWPs or rope access without visible competency evidence = flag
- No White Card evidence for construction workers = non-compliance
- Workers new to site or task working at height without supervisor present = flag
- No tool belts or equivalent — workers carrying tools up ladders in hands = unsafe practice

CHUNK 5: Role of Designers — Eliminating Falls at the Design Stage
Section: CoP s2.1–s2.3; WHS Act Part 2 Division 3
Topic: Designer obligations; safer building design; plant design; building/structure design; planning for maintenance and site layout

DESIGNER OBLIGATIONS:
- Must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that workers involved with construction, USE OR SUBSEQUENT MAINTENANCE are not exposed to risks associated with work at height
- Must consider the WHOLE LIFE CYCLE of the plant or structure: manufacture, use through to demolition and disposal
- When risks remain, information MUST BE INCLUDED WITH THE DESIGN to alert others to the risks
- Consultation, cooperation and coordination MUST occur between the builder and other designers to ensure SAFE INTERACTION OF DIFFERENT DESIGN ASPECTS

SAFER BUILDING DESIGN — MEASURES TO ELIMINATE OR MINIMISE FALL RISK:
- LOW-LEVEL MOUNTING of roof vents
- Locating air conditioning and similar plant AT GROUND LEVEL
- Locating AC units and roof-mounted plant (satellite dishes) AWAY FROM EDGES
- Specifying NON-FRAGILE MATERIAL for the roof
- Using PERMANENT SAFETY MESH
- SAFER GUTTERS: large volume gutters/downpipes to minimise roof access; locate gutters at ground level or away from edges; smooth transition from roof to walls
- SAFE ENTRY TO AND EXIT from work areas including scaffolding
- Designing PERMANENT GUARDRAILS or edge protection (e.g. parapet walls) for permanent fall prevention on roofs
- Planning for FUTURE MAINTENANCE (AC units, sloping exteriors, windows)
- Specifying STRENGTH OF ROOF MEMBERS and anchor/guardrail fixing points
- Designing PREFABRICATION OF STRUCTURES ON THE GROUND before lifting into position
- Specific safety requirements for: antenna/satellite dish installers; AC service contractors; window and gutter cleaners

PLANT DESIGN — MEASURES TO ELIMINATE OR MINIMISE FALL RISK:
- Providing STEPS AND HANDRAILS on vehicles (e.g. dump trucks)
- Incorporating systems to PREVENT FALLS in silos and overhead conveyors
- Ensuring workers can SAFELY MAINTAIN, REPAIR OR CLEAN the plant
- Safe access to and exit from the plant
- Considering the SAFETY OF PASSENGERS

BUILDING/STRUCTURE DESIGN — CONSTRUCTION STAGE MEASURES:
- Reducing risk for those at height — e.g. installing GUARDRAILS TO PERIMETER STRUCTURAL MEMBERS BEFORE ERECTION
- Reducing TIME SPENT AT HEIGHTS by pre-fabricating modules on the ground before lifting
- SEQUENCING OF WORK to be carried out at heights
- Locating ACCESS ROADS to enable cranes to place materials in most accessible positions
- Preparing ground/floor below work area — must be COMPACTED AND LEVEL to support cranes and EWPs
- IDENTIFYING UNDERGROUND SERVICES including drainage (for safe crane set-up)
- Providing PERMANENT SAFETY MESH

PLANNING FOR BUILDING MAINTENANCE:
- Consider methods for maintenance, repair or cleaning AT DESIGN STAGE, e.g.:
  - Designing WINDOW CLEANING BAYS OR GANGWAYS integrated into structural frame
  - Designing PERMANENT ANCHORAGE AND HOISTING POINTS into structures

PLANNING SITE LAYOUT — FACTORS TO CONSIDER:
- FIRM, LEVEL SURFACES below work areas for EWPs and mobile scaffolds
- Condition of ACCESS ROADS so plant can place and retrieve materials, reducing manual handling at height
- SAFE ACCESS TO AND EXIT FROM work areas and amenities (stairways, ladders, catwalks, guardrails, barriers)
- Direct and unobstructed means of ESCAPE AND RESCUE in event of emergency

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Roof-mounted plant positioned at roof edges with no access/egress system = design failure requiring remediation
- Fragile roofing materials (fibreglass, corrugated sheeting, skylights) with no safety mesh or fall controls = high risk
- Roof structure with no permanent anchor points or edge protection = flag for sites with ongoing maintenance
- Building maintenance requiring roof access with no permanent guardrails, BMU or anchor system = design deficiency

CHUNK 6: Work on the Ground — Elimination of Fall Risk
Section: CoP s4.1; WHS Regulation s78
Topic: Eliminating the need to work at height; ground-level task examples

ELIMINATION — HIGHEST ORDER CONTROL:
- Eliminating the NEED TO WORK AT HEIGHT is the most effective way of protecting workers from the risk of falls
- A PCBU can only take steps to MINIMISE the risk of a fall if it is NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE TO ELIMINATE that risk

EXAMPLES OF TASKS THAT CAN BE PERFORMED ON THE GROUND:
- Prefabricating ROOFS AT GROUND LEVEL
- Prefabricating WALL FRAMES horizontally then standing them up
- Installing AIR-CONDITIONING UNITS at ground level
- Using MECHANICAL TARP SPREADERS to cover truck loads from the ground
- REDUCING SHELVING HEIGHTS so workers can access items from ground level
- Using TOOLS WITH EXTENDABLE HANDLES (e.g. paint rollers) — note: assess musculoskeletal disorder risk
- Lowering a CONCERT HALL CHANDELIER to repair it
- Fitting OUTLETS, INLETS AND CONTROLS of large tanks and silos near the ground

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers climbing to access items that could be repositioned at ground level = elimination not considered
- Roof trusses being assembled at height when ground-level prefabrication is practicable = flag
- Workers manually adjusting loads on truck trays by climbing on the load = elimination not considered

CHUNK 7: Work on a Solid Construction — Requirements and Specifications
Section: CoP s4.2; WHS Regulation s78
Topic: Definition and requirements for solid construction; structural strength; barriers; hole/penetration protection; surface/gradient standards; entry and exit

SOLID CONSTRUCTION — FOUR REQUIREMENTS (all must be met):
1. Surface STRUCTURALLY CAPABLE of supporting workers, materials and all other loads
2. BARRIERS around perimeter and around any openings from or through which a person could fall
3. EVEN AND READILY NEGOTIABLE surface and gradient
4. SAFE MEANS OF ENTRY AND EXIT

STRUCTURAL STRENGTH:
- Surface and supports must SAFELY CARRY THE EXPECTED LOADS — workers, materials, tools and equipment
- When in doubt: have a STRUCTURAL ENGINEER DETERMINE THE SAFE LOAD CAPACITY BEFORE USE

BARRIERS — WHERE REQUIRED:
- Perimeters of buildings or other structures
- Floors including MEZZANINE FLOORS
- OPENINGS IN FLOORS
- The open edge of a STAIR, LANDING, PLATFORM or SHAFT OPENING

BARRIER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Must be designed and constructed to WITHSTAND THE FORCE OF SOMEONE FALLING AGAINST IT
- Must consist of GUARDRAILS, SOLID BALUSTRADES or other structural components (e.g. wire mesh with posts and reinforced top edge)
- TOP OF GUARDRAIL OR COMPONENT: between 900 mm AND 1100 mm above the working surface
- Guardrail systems must also have: MID-RAILS AND TOE-BOARDS or wire mesh infill panels
- If access is required to equipment (e.g. a hoist): must be protected with GATES OR OTHER MEANS to prevent a person falling

PROTECTION AROUND HOLES, PENETRATIONS AND OPENINGS:
- A fall prevention device MUST be used to provide and maintain a safe system of work where persons work near holes, penetrations and openings through which a person could fall (if reasonably practicable to do so)
- Holes, penetrations and openings must be made safe IMMEDIATELY AFTER BEING FORMED, e.g. with covers, barricading or embedded mesh
- When MESH IS USED: an additional cover must be used to prevent things falling through the mesh

COVER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Must be made of material STRONG ENOUGH to prevent people or objects falling through the hole
- Must be SECURELY FIXED to prevent dislodgement or accidental removal
- Must be designed to safely withstand a POINT LOAD OF AT LEAST 2 KILONEWTONS (200 KG)
- PLYWOOD COVERS ALONE ARE NOT PREFERRED because:
  - May be INDISTINGUISHABLE from other plywood pieces
  - Difficult to determine if properly secured
  - Can be unsecured to gain access and not re-secured
- MUST INCLUDE SIGNAGE indicating purpose — e.g. 'DANGER HOLE BENEATH'

SURFACE AND GRADIENT SPECIFICATIONS:
- Surfaces must be NON-SLIP and FREE FROM TRIP HAZARDS
- General maximum gradient: 7 DEGREES (1 in 8 gradient)
- CLEATED SURFACES (greater slip-resistance): must NOT BE STEEPER THAN 20 DEGREES (1 in 3 gradient)

GRID MESH OR CHECKER PLATE FLOORING — REQUIREMENTS:
- Flooring panels must be SECURELY FIXED and assembled to manufacturer's specifications
- Where possible: FITTED TO STRUCTURE BEFORE LIFTING into permanent position
- EACH PANEL MUST BE FIXED SECURELY before the next panel is placed
- During installation: secured by TACK WELDING, PANEL GRIPS or other means to prevent movement before permanent fixing
- If panels are REMOVED: edge protection must be provided and gaps protected

ENTRY AND EXIT — REQUIREMENTS:
- Must have SAFE MEANS for people to enter, exit and move around the work area
- Examples: permanently installed platforms, ramps, stairways or FIXED LADDERS
- Reference standard: AS 1657 Fixed platforms, walkways, stairways and ladders
- Safety considerations for entries/exits:
  - Exposure to WEATHER (rain → slippery surfaces; wind → loss of hand grip)
  - Providing NATURAL OR ARTIFICIAL LIGHTING to access ways
  - Clearing OBSTRUCTIONS so people can move easily to and from the workplace
- PORTABLE STEPLADDERS AND TRESTLE LADDERS must NOT be used for entries or exits
- SINGLE OR EXTENSION LADDERS: only used for entry/exit where use of safer systems is NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Guardrail top rail height outside 900–1100 mm range = non-compliance
- Guardrail system missing mid-rails or toe-boards = non-compliance
- Hole or penetration with no cover, or cover with no signage = non-compliance
- Plywood cover over penetration with no fixing and no signage = non-preferred / flag
- Working surface gradient exceeding 7 degrees with no cleating = slip hazard / non-compliance
- Grid mesh panels not fixed — loose underfoot = non-compliance
- Workers using stepladders as primary access to elevated work areas = flag
- No lighting at access ways = flag

CHUNK 8: Fall Prevention Devices — Scaffolds (General Requirements)
Section: CoP s5.1; WHS Regulation s225; AS/NZS 4576, AS/NZS 1576 (set)
Topic: Scaffold compliance requirements; inspection obligations; duty ratings; safety considerations; worker information requirements

SCAFFOLD INSPECTION — MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS (WHS Regulation s225):
- A person with management or control of a scaffold must NOT ALLOW USE of a scaffold from which a person or object could fall MORE THAN 4 METRES unless a COMPETENT PERSON, who has INSPECTED the scaffold, provides WRITTEN CONFIRMATION that the scaffold has been completed (HANDOVER CERTIFICATE)
- Scaffold and supporting structure must be inspected by a COMPETENT PERSON:
  - BEFORE USE IS RESUMED after an incident that may affect stability (severe storm, impact by mobile plant)
  - BEFORE USE IS RESUMED after any repairs
  - AT LEAST EVERY 30 DAYS
- If inspection indicates risk to health or safety: repairs, alterations and additions MUST be made, and scaffold MUST BE INSPECTED AGAIN by a competent person before use is resumed
- UNAUTHORISED ACCESS to incomplete scaffold left unattended must be PREVENTED (e.g. danger tags and warning signs at appropriate locations)

SCAFFOLD DUTY RATINGS — SAFE LOAD LIMITS (includes weight of people AND materials, tools, debris):
- LIGHT DUTY: up to 225 KG PER BAY — painting, electrical work, light carpentry; platform at least TWO PLANKS WIDE (approx. 450 mm)
- MEDIUM DUTY: up to 450 KG PER BAY — general trades work; platform at least FOUR PLANKS WIDE (approx. 900 mm)
- HEAVY DUTY: up to 675 KG PER BAY — bricklaying, concreting, demolition; platform at least FIVE PLANKS WIDE (approx. 1000 mm)
- SPECIAL DUTY: designated allowable load as designed

EXAMPLE SAFE LOAD CALCULATION (light duty):
- Mobile scaffold with light duty platform (225 kg per bay):
  - Can safely support ONE 80 kg worker + 145 kg of tools/materials, OR
  - TWO 80 kg workers + 65 kg of tools/materials

SCAFFOLD — SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS:
- Must conform to AS/NZS 4576 (Guidelines for Scaffolding) and AS/NZS 1576 (set)
- Must be erected, altered and dismantled by COMPETENT PEOPLE
- Scaffolds with fall potential >4 metres: must be erected, altered and dismantled by a person holding a SCAFFOLDING HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE, or a TRAINEE UNDER DIRECT SUPERVISION of a licensed scaffolder
- PREFABRICATED SCAFFOLDS: same type only — no mixing components unless APPROVED BY MANUFACTURER
- SAFE ACCESS TO AND EXIT FROM the scaffold must be provided
- EDGE PROTECTION (handrails, mid-rails and toe-boards) must be provided at EVERY OPEN EDGE of a work platform

WORKER INFORMATION, TRAINING AND INSTRUCTION — SCAFFOLD-SPECIFIC:
- Emergency response procedures
- What loads the scaffold can safely take (SAFE WORKING LOAD — SWL)
- Must not make UNAUTHORISED ALTERATIONS (removing guardrails, planks, ties, toe-boards, braces)
- Keep working platforms CLEAR OF DEBRIS and obstructions along their length
- Never access INCOMPLETE OR DEFECTIVE SCAFFOLDS
- IMMEDIATELY REPORT DEFECTS
- Isolate ELECTRICAL LEADS from metal scaffold frame with plastic lead hooks or insulated rubber material (where reasonably practicable)
- SWMS required for work at or above 2 metres
- Comply with electrical PERMIT TO WORK requirements for work within powerline 'no go' zone

MOBILE SCAFFOLD — ADDITIONAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS:
- Workers must be trained in use, including ensuring scaffold:
  - Remains LEVEL AND PLUMB
  - Kept WELL CLEAR OF POWERLINES, open floor edges and penetrations
  - Not accessed until CASTORS ARE LOCKED to prevent movement
  - NOT MOVED WHILE ANYONE IS ON IT
  - Accessed using an INTERNAL LADDER (except for low height platforms where not reasonably practicable)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No handover certificate displayed or available for scaffold where fall >4m is possible = serious non-compliance
- No 30-day inspection tag/record on scaffold = non-compliance
- Scaffold with missing handrails, mid-rails or toe-boards at open edges = non-compliance
- Materials overloaded on scaffold platform beyond rated SWL = non-compliance
- Mobile scaffold accessed from outside via climbing the frame rather than internal ladder = non-compliance
- Mobile scaffold with castors unlocked while workers are on it = serious safety risk
- Mixed prefabricated scaffold components from different manufacturers without approval = flag
- Danger tags and warning signs absent from incomplete scaffold = non-compliance

CHUNK 9: Fall Prevention Devices — Suspended (Swing Stage) Scaffolds

Topic: Types of suspended scaffold; licensing requirements; safety management

SUSPENDED SCAFFOLD — TYPES:
- SWING STAGES with cradles supported by a SINGLE ROW of suspension ropes
- DOUBLE ROPE SCAFFOLDS with cradles supported by TWO ROWS of suspension ropes
- WORK CAGES — small cradles supported by ONE SUSPENSION ROPE only

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Swing stage occupants not wearing fall arrest harnesses connected to the platform anchorage = serious non-compliance
- Swing stage platform tilting during movement (not remaining horizontal) = mechanical failure risk

CHUNK 10: Fall Prevention Devices — Elevating Work Platforms (EWPs)
Section: CoP s5.1; AS 2550.10
Topic: Types of EWP; hazards; risk controls; licensing requirements; harness obligations

EWP TYPES:
- BOOM-TYPE EWPs (including travel towers, cherry pickers)
- SCISSOR LIFTS
- Some designed for HARD FLAT SURFACES ONLY; others for ROUGH TERRAIN
- Some for INDOOR USE only — not suitable for windy conditions outdoors

MAIN HAZARDS WHEN USING EWPs:
- Contact with ELECTRIC LINES
- OVERTURNING the machine
- FALLING FROM THE WORK PLATFORM
- CRUSHING HAZARDS when elevating the platform or moving laterally

EWP RISK CONTROL MEASURES:
- Train and instruct workers in SAFE OPERATING PROCEDURES for the specific brand and type of EWP
- Train workers in SAFE WORK PROCEDURES to avoid crushing and electrical hazards
- Train workers in SAFE USE OF FALL ARREST EQUIPMENT and EMERGENCY RESCUE PROCEDURES
- EWP only used as WORKING PLATFORM — not as means of entering and exiting a work area unless conditions in AS 2550.10 are met
- Only used on SOLID LEVEL SURFACE unless designed for rough terrain
- CHECK SURFACE AREA for penetrations or obstructions that could cause uncontrolled movement or overturning

FALL ARREST HARNESS — EWP REQUIREMENTS:
- Workers in TRAVEL TOWERS, BOOM LIFTS or CHERRY PICKERS must wear a PROPERLY ANCHORED FALL ARREST HARNESS where not reasonably practicable to use fall prevention device or work positioning system
- Lanyard should be AS SHORT AS POSSIBLE
- Lanyard attached DIRECTLY TO THE DESIGNATED ANCHOR POINT ON THE EWP — NOT TO THE HANDRAIL (unless handrail is the manufacturer's specified anchor point)

LICENSING — BOOM-TYPE EWPs:
- Workers operating boom-type EWPs with boom length of 11 METRES OR MORE must EITHER:
  - Hold a BOOM-TYPE EWP HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE, OR
  - Be enrolled in a training course for that licence AND be SUPERVISED by the holder of a boom-type EWP high risk work licence

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- EWP operated on uneven or soft ground not rated for rough terrain = overturn risk
- Workers in boom lifts/cherry pickers with no fall arrest harness attached = serious non-compliance
- Lanyard attached to EWP handrail (not manufacturer's anchor point) = non-compliance
- Operator of boom EWP with boom ≥11m with no visible licence = non-compliance
- EWP positioned adjacent to powerlines without appropriate clearance = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 12: Fall Prevention Devices — Workboxes and Work Platforms
Section: CoP s5.1; AS 1418.17; AS 2550.1; AS 2359.1-2015; AS 2359.2
Topic: Types and use of workboxes and forklift work platforms; mandatory precautions; prohibitions

WORKBOXES AND WORK PLATFORMS — DESCRIPTION:
- Platform surrounded by EDGE PROTECTION, designed to provide an elevated work area
- WORKBOXES: supported FROM ABOVE by crane, hoist or other mechanical device
- WORK PLATFORMS: supported FROM UNDERNEATH OR SIDE by forklifts or other mechanical devices
- Where reasonably practicable: use EWPs or scaffolds as ALTERNATIVE to workboxes/work platforms

GENERAL SAFETY PRECAUTIONS:
- Workbox/work platform must be DESIGNED FOR THE TASK and securely attached to the crane, hoist, forklift or other mechanical device
- Must NOT BE SUSPENDED OVER PEOPLE
- Effective MEANS OF COMMUNICATION between person in workbox/platform and operator
- OPERATOR MUST REMAIN AT THE CONTROLS at all times
- LIFTING ATTACHMENTS AND RECORDS must be checked by a competent person before use

CRANE WORKBOXES — ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be DESIGN REGISTERED before use in the workplace (WHS Regulation)
- Workbox must be designed to AS 1418.17: Cranes — Part 17: Design and Construction of Workboxes
- Must be fitted with ANCHORAGE capable of withstanding fall forces specified in AS/NZS 1891.4
- Where risk of falling from height: person MUST BE ATTACHED TO ANCHORAGE by lanyard and harness UNLESS workbox is FULLY ENCLOSED
- Workers must REMAIN WITHIN THE WORKBOX while being lifted or suspended
- Except in an emergency: workers must NOT ENTER OR LEAVE THE WORKBOX WHEN ELEVATED unless conditions in AS 2550.1 s6.19.3 are met
- Directions to crane operator from workbox must be provided by a person holding a DOGGING OR RIGGING LICENCE
- Crane must be fitted with means to SAFELY LOWER WORKBOX in emergency or power failure
- Crane must be SUITABLY STABILISED while workbox is in use
- 'Drive up' and 'drive down' controls on both hoisting and luffing motions must be used — NO DECLUTCHING ALLOWING FREE FALL while workbox is in use

FORKLIFT WORK PLATFORMS — REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be ENGINEER-DESIGNED and constructed to AS 2359.1–2015: Powered Industrial Trucks — Part 1: General Requirements
- Must be SECURELY ATTACHED TO FORKLIFT CARRIAGE to prevent sliding off the forks (LOCKING BRACKET AND PIN)
- Workers only raised by forklift WHEN IN A COMPLIANT WORK PLATFORM
- Workers must REMAIN WITHIN THE WORK PLATFORM when elevated or being raised/lowered
- NO OTHER DEVICE (ladder, pallet) must be used to GAIN ADDITIONAL HEIGHT from the platform
- SAFETY GATE must be SELF-CLOSING and KEPT SHUT when in the elevated position
- Reference standard: AS 2359.2 Powered Industrial Trucks — Part 2: Operations

PROHIBITED PRACTICES — FORKLIFT:
- Using a forklift as a working platform by standing on the TYNES = UNACCEPTABLE PRACTICE
- Standing on a PALLET on the tynes = UNACCEPTABLE PRACTICE
- Placing a LADDER on the forklift to gain extra height = UNACCEPTABLE PRACTICE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker standing on forklift tynes or pallet (not in a compliant work platform) = serious non-compliance
- Ladder placed on forklift to gain additional height = serious non-compliance
- Crane workbox in use without harness worn by occupants (and workbox not fully enclosed) = serious non-compliance
- Safety gate on forklift work platform left open when elevated = non-compliance
- Crane operator not at controls while workbox is elevated = serious non-compliance
- Dogging/rigging licence holder not directing crane from workbox = non-compliance

BUILDING MAINTENANCE UNIT (BMU) — DESCRIPTION:

CHUNK 14: Fall Prevention Devices — Trestle Ladder Platforms
Section: CoP s5.1; WHS Regulation s306O(3)(a)
Topic: Trestle scaffold limitations; height thresholds; guardrail requirements; platform width; pin requirements

TRESTLE SCAFFOLD — LIMITATIONS:
- GENERALLY NOT SUITABLE for working at heights of 2 METRES OR ABOVE
- If used above 2 metres: GUARDRAILING AND TOE-BOARDS must be used to prevent people and material falling off open sides or ends of the work platform
- Suitable mostly for LIGHT DUTY work only (painting, rendering)
- Work should only be carried out BETWEEN THE TRESTLES

PLATFORM WIDTH:
- Working platforms on trestle scaffolds must have a width of at least 450 MM (TWO PLANKS) — except as described in WHS Regulation s306O(3)(a)

DISTANCE FROM PLATFORM EDGE TO WORKING FACE:
- Except where platform is at or below 2 metres in height, if there is NO GUARDRAIL OR MID-RAIL adjacent to the working face: distance between platform edge and working face must NOT BE GREATER THAN ONE PLANK WIDTH (225 mm)

PIN REQUIREMENTS:
- Only PURPOSE-DESIGNED PINS must be used when adjusting height of bricklayer's trestle scaffold
- DO NOT USE NAILS OR PIECES OF REINFORCING BAR
- Pins should be attached to the trestle by a CHAIN to prevent loss

ALTERNATIVES TO CONSIDER:
- EWPs (e.g. small scissor lifts)
- Light duty aluminium MOBILE SCAFFOLDS
- MODULAR SCAFFOLDING

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Trestle scaffold used above 2m with no guardrails or toe-boards = non-compliance
- Trestle scaffold platform narrower than 450mm (two planks) = non-compliance
- Nails or reinforcing bar used as height adjustment pins = non-compliance
- Workers working at the end of the trestles rather than between them = flag

CHUNK 15: Fall Prevention Devices — Perimeter Guardrails
Section: CoP s5.2; AS/NZS 4994 (set); WHS Regulation (edge protection in construction)
Topic: Where guardrails are required; specifications; load resistance; trenching applications

GUARDRAILS — WHERE REQUIRED:
- At the EDGES OF ROOFS
- At the edges of MEZZANINE FLOORS, WALKWAYS, STAIRWAYS, RAMPS and LANDINGS
- On top of PLANT AND STRUCTURES where access is required
- Around OPENINGS IN FLOOR AND ROOF STRUCTURES
- At the edges of SHAFTS, PITS and other EXCAVATIONS

GUARDRAIL SPECIFICATIONS:
- TOP RAIL: 900 mm TO 1100 mm above the working surface
- MID-RAIL: must be included
- TOE-BOARD: must be included
- The guardrail system, method of attachment and supporting structure must be capable of WITHSTANDING THE LOADS THAT WILL BE APPLIED
- Load resistance depends on the MOMENTUM OF A FALLING PERSON (increases with roof pitch)
- Reference standard: AS/NZS 4994 (set): Temporary Edge Protection

GUARDRAILS FOR TRENCHING WORK:
- Where trenching works present risk of a person falling into the trench:
  - GUARDRAILING including guardrails fitted to TOP EDGES OF TRENCH SHIELDS, OR
  - A BARRIER approximately 1.5 METRES BACK from the excavation to prevent persons approaching the trench

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Top rail outside 900–1100 mm height range = non-compliance
- Guardrail system missing mid-rail or toe-board = non-compliance
- Guardrail system at roof edge with no documentation of load capacity assessment = flag
- Trench with no guardrailing at top edges and no setback barrier = non-compliance

CHUNK 16: Fall Prevention Devices — Safety Mesh
Section: CoP s5.3; AS/NZS 4389
Topic: Purpose and limitations of safety mesh; wire specifications; installation requirements

SAFETY MESH — PURPOSE AND SCOPE:
- Designed to PREVENT INTERNAL FALLS THROUGH A ROOF
- Provides fall protection for ROOF INSTALLERS and long-term protection for maintenance/repair workers
- Does NOT prevent falls from the EDGE OF A ROOF or through HOLES IN A ROOF
- MUST ALWAYS BE USED IN CONJUNCTION WITH OTHER FALL PREVENTION DEVICES (e.g. edge protection/guardrails) or other control measures (e.g. fall arrest systems)

SAFETY MESH — COMPLIANCE STANDARD:
- Must comply with AS/NZS 4389: Roof Safety Mesh

WIRE SPECIFICATIONS:
- Formed from 2 MM DIAMETER WIRE, WELDED into a mesh
- LONGITUDINAL WIRES: not more than 150 mm APART
- CROSS WIRES: not more than 300 mm APART
- TRANSVERSE WIRES: minimum tensile strength of 450 MPa
- LONGITUDINAL WIRES: minimum tensile strength of 500 MPa

INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be installed by COMPETENT PEOPLE in accordance with manufacturer's instructions
- Workers installing roof safety mesh must be protected by control measures DURING INSTALLATION (e.g. scaffolding, EWPs, fall arrest systems — whichever is reasonably practicable)
- Mesh must be SECURELY CONNECTED TO THE STRUCTURE
- OVERLAP BETWEEN ADJACENT SECTIONS must be sufficient to generate the necessary strength to resist the force of a person falling onto it
- Safety mesh must be COVERED BY ROOF CLADDING as soon as reasonably practicable after installation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Roof safety mesh installed without edge protection at roof perimeter = non-compliance (mesh alone is insufficient)
- Mesh wires visibly wider apart than 150 mm (longitudinal) or 300 mm (cross) = may not meet AS/NZS 4389
- Safety mesh left uncovered by cladding for extended period = flag
- Workers installing mesh with no fall control measures in place during installation = non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Work Positioning Systems — Restraint Technique
Section: CoP s6.1; WHS Regulation
Topic: What restraint technique is; conditions for use; when it is NOT suitable; installation requirements

RESTRAINT TECHNIQUE — DESCRIPTION:
- Controls a person's movement by PHYSICALLY PREVENTING THE PERSON FROM REACHING a position at which there is a risk of a fall
- Consists of a HARNESS CONNECTED BY A LANYARD TO AN ANCHORAGE OR HORIZONTAL LIFELINE
- MUST BE SET UP TO PREVENT THE WEARER FROM REACHING AN UNPROTECTED EDGE

CONDITIONS FOR SUITABLE USE:
- User CAN MAINTAIN SECURE FOOTING without having to tension the restraint line and without the aid of another handhold or lateral support
- When assessing secure footing: consider slope of surface, supporting material type, texture of surface and whether it is likely to be wet, oily or otherwise slippery

WHEN RESTRAINT TECHNIQUE MUST NOT BE USED (situations where individual fall arrest system may be more appropriate):
- The user CAN REACH A POSITION WHERE A FALL IS POSSIBLE
- The user has a RESTRAINT LINE THAT CAN BE ADJUSTED IN LENGTH so that a free fall position can be reached
- There is danger the user may FALL THROUGH THE SURFACE (e.g. fragile roofing material)
- The slope is OVER 15 DEGREES
- Any other reasonably likely use or misuse of the system could lead to FREE FALL

RESTRAINT TECHNIQUE — MUST ONLY BE USED when it is not reasonably practicable to use:
1. Ground-level or solid construction work
2. A fall prevention device (e.g. guardrail)

INSTALLATION AND ANCHOR REQUIREMENTS:
- Must be installed by a COMPETENT PERSON in accordance with manufacturer's instructions
- Restraint anchorage should be designed for FALL ARREST LOADING
- A PURPOSE-DESIGNED ROOF ANCHOR, used in accordance with designer's/manufacturer's instructions, may be used as part of restraint technique on METAL DECK OR TILED ROOFS
- Restraint anchorage should be designed for FALL ARREST LOADING (not restraint loading only)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Restraint lanyard of sufficient length to allow worker to reach the roof edge = system misuse / non-compliance
- Restraint technique used on slope >15 degrees = not suitable — higher order control required
- Restraint system used over fragile roofing materials = not suitable
- Workers using work positioning systems without training evidence = flag

CHUNK 18: Work Positioning Systems — Industrial Rope Access
Section: CoP s6.2; AS/NZS 4488 (set); WHS Regulation (high risk construction work)
Topic: Rope access system description; when to use; risk assessment; supervision and operator requirements

INDUSTRIAL ROPE ACCESS — DESCRIPTION:
- Used for GAINING ACCESS TO AND WORKING AT A WORKFACE, usually by means of VERTICALLY SUSPENDED ROPES
- Fall arrest components are used in the system, but the main purpose is ACCESS, not backup fall protection
- OTHER METHODS (EWPs, BMUs) must be CONSIDERED BEFORE rope access

HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK:
- Rope access involving construction work with risk of a person FALLING MORE THAN 2 METRES = high risk construction work
- SWMS must be prepared BEFORE the work starts

PRE-WORK RISK ASSESSMENT — must consider:
- Location of work and SPECIAL FEATURES OF THE STRUCTURE
- ANCHOR POINTS
- EMERGENCY ACCESS
- WEATHER CONDITIONS

SUPERVISION AND OPERATOR REQUIREMENTS:
- Rope access work must be PROPERLY SUPERVISED
- Supervisors must have the necessary EXPERIENCE AND COMPETENCE to supervise the work AND any potential RESCUE for each project
- Operators must be COMPETENT in the technique
- Operators must possess a SUITABLE ATTITUDE FOR WORKING AT HEIGHT
- Operators must be FIT FOR WORK — not affected by alcohol, drugs or prescribed medication that may impair ability to work at height
- Operators must NOT WORK ALONE in case they require assistance in an emergency

PPE REQUIREMENTS:
- Appropriate PPE for tasks: HELMETS, GLOVES, HEARING PROTECTION, GOGGLES OR MASKS

OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Systems installed where PROMPT ASSISTANCE OR RESCUE can be provided if required
- Equipment CHECKED REGULARLY by a competent person
- FIXED ANCHORAGE POINTS checked by a competent person BEFORE ATTACHING ROPE ACCESS LINES
- BACK-UP SYSTEM for each person — use TWO INDEPENDENTLY ANCHORED ROPES per person
- Person WITHIN 3 METRES OF AN UNGUARDED EDGE must be adequately secured
- Operators wear FULL BODY OR WORK POSITIONING SIT HARNESS WITH SHOULDER STRAPS
- Supervisors can COMMUNICATE WITH WORKERS
- EXCLUSION ZONES established and enforced on access areas below working area and anchorage locations

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Rope access operator working alone with no other person available for rescue = serious non-compliance
- Single rope per person (no back-up rope) = serious non-compliance
- No exclusion zone below rope access working area = non-compliance
- No SWMS visible for rope access construction work with fall risk >2m = non-compliance
- Operator without full body harness = non-compliance

CHUNK 19: Fall Arrest Systems — Catch Platforms
Section: CoP s7.1; WHS Regulation (catch platforms in construction)
Topic: Catch platform construction requirements; licensing; deck, positioning and edge protection specifications

CATCH PLATFORMS — PURPOSE:
- Temporary platform located BELOW A WORK AREA to catch a worker in a fall
- Must be of ROBUST CONSTRUCTION and designed to withstand the MAXIMUM POTENTIAL IMPACT LOAD
- Scaffolding components may be used to construct fixed and mobile catch platforms

LICENSING — CANTILEVERED, SPUR OR TUBE AND COUPLER CATCH PLATFORMS:
- Must be installed by people who:
  - Hold a high risk work licence for INTERMEDIATE OR ADVANCED SCAFFOLDING, OR
  - Are enrolled in training to obtain that licence AND supervised by the holder of an intermediate or advanced scaffolding high risk work licence

LICENSING — PREFABRICATED SCAFFOLDING CATCH PLATFORMS (where fall >4m is possible):
- Must be installed by people who:
  - Hold a high risk work licence for BASIC SCAFFOLDING, OR
  - Are enrolled in training to obtain that licence AND supervised by the holder of a basic scaffolding high risk work licence

CATCH PLATFORM SPECIFICATIONS:
- Must incorporate a FULLY PLANKED-OUT DECK
- Deck must extend AT LEAST 2 METRES BEYOND UNPROTECTED EDGES of the work area (EXCEPT where extended guardrailing is fitted to the catch platform)
- Positioned AS CLOSE AS POSSIBLE TO THE UNDERSIDE OF THE WORK AREA — distance a person could fall before landing on the catch platform must be NO MORE THAN 1 METRE
- Must ALWAYS be used with EDGE PROTECTION

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Catch platform deck not extending 2m beyond unprotected edges = non-compliance
- Gap between work area underside and catch platform greater than 1m = non-compliance
- Catch platform installed without edge protection = non-compliance
- Catch platform erected by workers without required scaffold licence = non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Fall Arrest Systems — Safety Nets
Section: CoP s7.2; WHS Regulation
Topic: Safety net specifications; licensing; positioning; inspection; precautions

SAFETY NETS — PURPOSE AND LIMITATIONS:
- Provide satisfactory means of protection while allowing workers MAXIMUM FREEDOM OF MOVEMENT
- Must NOT BE USED to ENTER OR EXIT a work area or as a WORKING PLATFORM

LICENSING — SAFETY NET ERECTION AND SERVICING:
- Must be carried out by people who:
  - Hold a high risk work licence for BASIC RIGGING OR BASIC SCAFFOLDING, OR
  - Are enrolled in training to obtain that licence AND supervised by the holder of a basic rigging or basic scaffolding high risk work licence

SAFETY NET SPECIFICATIONS AND REQUIREMENTS:
- Constructed of material STRONG ENOUGH to catch a falling person or thing
- SECURELY ANCHORED before work starts
- HUNG AS CLOSE AS PRACTICABLE to the underside of the working area — but NO MORE THAN 2 METRES BELOW the working area
- SUFFICIENTLY TENSIONED and have clearance to prevent a falling person CONTACTING ANY SURFACE OR STRUCTURE BELOW THE NET
- INSPECTED, particularly after installation, relocation or repair

SAFETY NET STORAGE:
- Stored in DRY, SHADED AREAS with GOOD AIR CIRCULATION

PRECAUTIONS WHEN USING SAFETY NETS:
- NO HOT WORK (welding, oxy cutting or work that may damage the net) above safety nets
- Material and refuse must NOT BE ALLOWED TO ACCUMULATE in safety nets

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Safety net more than 2m below the working area = non-compliance
- Net not securely anchored before work commences = non-compliance
- Hot work (welding, grinding) being conducted directly above safety nets = serious risk / non-compliance
- Debris accumulation in safety nets = flag (impairs function; adds load)
- Workers using safety net to enter or exit a work area = non-compliance

CHUNK 21: Fall Arrest Systems — Individual Fall Arrest Systems (General)
Section: CoP s7.3; AS/NZS 1891 (set)
Topic: Components; worker obligations; head protection; component inspection; free fall distance limits; harness requirements; anchor point requirements

INDIVIDUAL FALL ARREST SYSTEMS — COMPONENTS:
- Anchorages including rail system
- Lifelines, lanyard, shock absorber and inertia reel
- Rope and wire grabs
- HARNESS
- Snap hooks and karabiners — DOUBLE OR TRIPLE ACTION to prevent roll-out
- Rescue equipment

MANDATORY CONDITIONS:
- Must only be used where it is NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE TO USE HIGHER LEVEL CONTROL MEASURES
- Workers must be TRAINED IN SAFE USE of the system
- Equipment must comply with AS/NZS 1891 (set): Industrial Fall-Arrest Systems and Devices
- Equipment must be PERMANENTLY MARKED OR LABELLED with relevant information to reduce misuse
- Workers using a fall arrest system must wear appropriate HEAD PROTECTION

IF EQUIPMENT HAS BEEN USED TO ARREST A FALL:
- Must NOT BE USED AGAIN until inspected and CERTIFIED BY A COMPETENT PERSON as safe to use

FALL ARREST LINES — EDGE CONTACT:
- Fall arrest lines that can come into contact with edges (concrete or steel beam edges) must be of a type that has been shown to NOT FAIL DURING SUCH USE

ANCHORAGE POINT REQUIREMENTS:
- Must comply with anchor strength specified in AS/NZS 1891.4:2009
- INSERT-TYPE ANCHORS (installed in partial depth holes — friction, chemical, screw type) must NOT be used for fall arrest anchors where potential loading is a DIRECT PULL-OUT LOAD
- Anchorages must be TESTED AND APPROVED by a competent person BEFORE USE
- For insert-type anchors: PULL-OUT TESTING REQUIRED — cannot be assessed by visual inspection alone
- Each anchorage point must be located so that lanyard CAN BE ATTACHED BEFORE the person moves into a position where they could fall

COMPONENT INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Each component must be inspected by a competent person:
  - AFTER INSTALLATION but BEFORE USE
  - AT REGULAR INTERVALS
  - IMMEDIATELY AFTER USE TO ARREST A FALL
- Inspection must be in accordance with MANUFACTURER'S SPECIFICATIONS and relevant standards
- Signs of excessive wear or defects = COMPONENTS MUST BE WITHDRAWN FROM USE

FREE FALL DISTANCE — LIMITING REQUIREMENTS:
- Maximum free fall distance before system takes effect: 2 METRES (lesser free fall distance is preferable)
- There must be sufficient distance between the work surface and surface below to enable the system (including shock absorber) to FULLY DEPLOY
- FACTORS TO CONSIDER FOR TOTAL FALL DISTANCE CALCULATION:
  - Worker's HEIGHT
  - Height and position of the ANCHORAGE POINT
  - Length of the LANYARD
  - Any SLACK IN THE HORIZONTAL LIFELINE
  - Any STRETCHING of the lanyard or lifeline when extended by a fall
  - Length of ENERGY ABSORBER when extended by a fall
- Do NOT use lanyards in conjunction with INERTIA REELS — can result in excessive free fall

HARNESS REQUIREMENTS:
- FULL BODY HARNESS must be worn and must be CORRECTLY FITTED
- Connect fall arrest line to attachment point (DORSAL or CHEST) providing best protection for the situation
- Consider: potential fall distance, impact with structure, body position after fall, need to interact with rope-grabs

MAINTAINING MINIMUM SLACK — LANYARD:
- Must maintain a MINIMUM OF SLACK in the fall arrest lanyard between user and attachment
- ANCHORAGE POINT should be AS HIGH AS EQUIPMENT PERMITS
- Work ABOVE the anchor point must be avoided — increases free fall distance, increases forces on body, increases risk of lanyard snagging

INERTIA REELS — CORRECT USE:
- May NOT BE EFFECTIVE on steeply pitched roofs (line may keep extending from reel without locking)
- Must NOT BE USED AS WORKING SUPPORTS by locking the system and allowing it to support the user
- CAN BE USED as a control measure in connection with work from bosun's chairs and ladders
- Only ONE PERSON may be attached to one inertia reel line

COMPATIBLE COMPONENTS — REQUIREMENT:
- Use of NON-COMPATIBLE COMPONENTS may lead to 'ROLL-OUT' — resulting in injury or death
- Hazard cannot always be avoided even with same-brand components — CHECK WITH MANUFACTURER
- SNAP HOOKS must be DOUBLE ACTION TYPE (at least two consecutive deliberate actions to open)
- Snap hooks must NOT be connected to each other (risk of roll-out)
- HEX NUT CONNECTORS may be appropriate for semi-permanent connections

RESCUE — MANDATORY PLANNING:
- PCBU implementing fall arrest system MUST establish EMERGENCY AND RESCUE PROCEDURES
- Procedures must be TESTED to ensure they are effective
- Workers must receive suitable and adequate INFORMATION, TRAINING AND INSTRUCTION in relation to emergency procedures
- Rescue of suspended worker should occur PROMPTLY to prevent suspension intolerance and treat injuries
- Worker must NOT use fall arrest system unless THERE IS AT LEAST ONE OTHER PERSON ON SITE who can rescue them if they fall

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker alone on site using fall arrest harness = non-compliance (rescue requirement not met)
- Fall arrest harness worn but not connected to any anchorage = non-compliance
- Anchorage point visibly below the worker's attachment point = unsafe (increases free fall)
- Snap hooks connected to each other = roll-out risk / non-compliance
- Non-compatible components combined in fall arrest system = flag
- Equipment used to arrest a previous fall still in service without competent person certification = serious non-compliance
- No head protection worn with fall arrest system = non-compliance
- Inertia reel combined with lanyard = non-compliance

CHUNK 22: Individual Fall Arrest Systems — Swing Down and Swing Back Hazards
Section: CoP s7.3 (Individual fall arrest system: hazards); AS/NZS 1891 (set)
Topic: Swing down and swing back hazard descriptions; prevention measures

SWING DOWN — DESCRIPTION:
- Occurs if the LANYARD SLIDES BACK ALONG THE PERIMETER EDGE OF THE ROOF until it is vertical
- Person may HIT THE GROUND, OR the LANYARD MAY BREAK from contact with the roof edge

MEASURES TO ADDRESS SWING DOWN RISK:
- Installation of GUARDRAILS
- Placing the ANCHORAGE POINT AT A RIGHT ANGLE to the position of the lanyard at the perimeter edge (e.g. using a MOBILE ANCHORAGE)
- Installation of a SECOND ANCHORAGE POINT AND BELAY DEVICES — intermediate anchorages

SWING BACK — DESCRIPTION:
- Person swings back INTO THE BUILDING OR STRUCTURE after an arrested fall
- Length of lanyard and positioning of anchor may allow the worker to HIT THE STRUCTURE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Single anchor point located well back from working position near roof edge with no intermediate anchorages = swing down risk
- Lanyard length allows worker to reach perimeter edge and swing vertically = swing down risk
- Anchor placement allowing worker to contact the structure face on a swing back = assess anchorage position

---

CHUNK 23: Fall Arrest Systems — Vertical Anchorage Lines or Rails
Section: CoP s7.4; AS/NZS 1891 (set)
Topic: Description; where used; safety precautions; post-fall inspection

VERTICAL ANCHORAGE LINES OR RAILS — DESCRIPTION:
- Temporary or permanent fall arrest systems for WORKERS USING LADDERS OR CLIMBING TOWERS
- Can be used on PLANT (tower cranes) and on BUILDINGS AND STRUCTURES
- Person has CONTINUOUS FALL PROTECTION by being attached to the anchorage line and harness

SAFETY PRECAUTIONS:
- Systems must comply with AS/NZS 1891 (set) — including suitable harness and check that USE BY DATE has not passed
- LOCKING DEVICE attached to the FRONTAL ATTACHMENT POINT of the harness
- LANYARD ASSEMBLY: maximum 300 mm length
- POINT OF CONNECTION onto anchorage system must be NEAR THE BASE OF THE LADDER to allow:
  - Connection to be made BEFORE beginning to ascend
  - Continuous connection to the DISCONNECTING POINT when at a safe higher level
- FREE FALL LIMITED TO A MAXIMUM OF 600 mm
- Permanent systems must be of WIRE OR RAIL CONSTRUCTION and installed according to manufacturer's instructions

POST-FALL:
- REMOVE THE SYSTEM FROM SERVICE and have inspected by a COMPETENT PERSON before use again

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker ascending fixed ladder with no fall arrest system in use = non-compliance (on ladder exceeding applicable height threshold)
- Free fall potential on vertical anchorage line exceeding 600mm = non-compliance
- Locking device attached to dorsal or side D-ring rather than frontal attachment point = non-compliance
- System not removed from service after a fall = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 24: Fall Arrest Systems — Double Lanyards
Section: CoP s7.5; AS/NZS 1891 (set)
Topic: Description; correct use; misuse prevention; limitations

DOUBLE LANYARDS — DESCRIPTION:
- Also known as TWIN TAIL or 'Y' LANYARD
- Allow person climbing to ALWAYS BE CONNECTED TO THE LADDER OR STRUCTURE
- Must include DOUBLE ACTION CONNECTORS to prevent unintentional opening during use

PROHIBITED PRACTICES WITH DOUBLE LANYARDS:
- NO BACK HOOKING
- Must NOT be wrapped around the body or passed BETWEEN THE LEGS
- CHEST CONNECTION must NEVER be higher than the HIGHEST ATTACHMENT POINT
- NOT SUITABLE FOR FREQUENT USE because of possible misuse or muscle injury
- The ladder or structure ATTACHMENT POINTS MUST BE CAPABLE OF ARRESTING FORCES generated by a fall with the double lanyard

SIDE LOADING NOTE:
- In applications where connectors will slide down to the lowest point on the rail: connectors are likely subject to SIDE LOADING
- Side loading controlled by: SOFT CONNECTIONS LIKE SLINGS or use of HOOKS RATED TO WITHSTAND SIDE LOADING

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Double lanyard used with back hooking = non-compliance
- Double lanyard wrapped around body or passed between legs = non-compliance
- Chest connection positioned higher than highest attachment point = non-compliance
- Standard hooks used where side loading is expected without assessment of side load rating = flag

CHUNK 25: Administrative Controls — No Go Areas, Permit Systems, Work Sequencing
Section: CoP s8.1–s8.3; WHS Regulation s79
Topic: When administrative controls are permissible; no go areas; permit systems; work sequencing

ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS — GENERAL RULE:
- May be used to SUPPORT OTHER CONTROL MEASURES
- Must NOT be used EXCLUSIVELY to minimise the risk of falls unless it is NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE to use a higher order control
- If relying on administrative controls: may be necessary to provide a HIGH LEVEL OF SUPERVISION to ensure they are being adhered to

NO GO AREAS:
- Effective method of ensuring people are not exposed to FALL HAZARDS or FALLING OBJECT RISKS
- Require CLEAR SIGNS warning people not to access the hazardous area
- Can highlight risks of entry where there is an UNGUARDED HAZARD, or where WORK IS CARRIED OUT OVERHEAD
- INFORMATION AND INSTRUCTION must be provided about no go areas with supervision to ensure NO UNAUTHORISED ENTRY
- BARRIERS must be used in conjunction with signs to cordon off areas — must be HIGHLY VISIBLE and SECURELY FIXED to prevent displacement

PERMIT SYSTEMS:
- Allow only COMPETENT, TRAINED PEOPLE to work in areas with hazards
- Examples:
  - TAGGING ACCESS POINTS to incomplete scaffold with 'ONLY LICENSED SCAFFOLDERS PERMITTED' to restrict unauthorised access during erection/dismantling
  - Requiring PERMITS for access to areas where restraint or fall arrest systems are to be used

ORGANISING AND SEQUENCING OF WORK:
- Sequence jobs so that DIFFERENT TRADES ARE NOT WORKING ABOVE OR BELOW EACH OTHER at the same time
- Plan so that work is NOT CARRIED OUT FOR EXTENDED PERIODS FROM A LADDER
- Plan so that WORK AT HEIGHT IS MINIMISED IN EXTREMELY HOT OR COLD WEATHER

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Barricades/no go area signs not securely fixed — can be displaced = non-compliance
- Workers entering no go area without authorisation and no supervisor present = non-compliance
- Administrative controls (signs only) used as the exclusive fall control where physical controls are practicable = non-compliant hierarchy
- Different trades working directly above and below each other simultaneously = flag

CHUNK 26: Ladders — General Rules and Preference Hierarchy
Section: CoP s9; WHS Regulation (construction)
Topic: When ladders should and should not be used; the preference hierarchy; types of portable ladders

LADDER PREFERENCE RULE:
- Falls from ladders have caused SIGNIFICANT SERIOUS AND FATAL INJURIES, even at LOW HEIGHTS
- Ladders are OFTEN INCORRECTLY CONSIDERED THE FIRST OPTION when working at heights
- Ladders must only be considered AFTER SAFER ALTERNATIVES (EWPs, scaffolding) have been considered first and found to be NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE

TYPES OF PORTABLE LADDERS:
- EXTENSION OR SINGLE LADDERS: should only be used as ACCESS TO OR EXIT from a work area, OR for SHORT DURATION LIGHT WORK that can be carried out safely from the ladder
- STEP PLATFORMS: provide improved level of fall protection over traditional step/single ladders — include a small WORKING PLATFORM and PARTIAL HANDRAIL

SELECTING LADDERS:
- Must be selected to SUIT THE WORK to be carried out
- Consider: DURATION OF WORK, PHYSICAL SURROUNDINGS and PREVAILING WEATHER CONDITIONS
- Must have a LOAD RATING OF AT LEAST 120 KG and be manufactured for INDUSTRIAL USE
- DOMESTIC or 'HOMEMADE' LADDERS must NOT be used for industrial use or construction sites

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Domestic grade ladder in use on a construction site = non-compliance
- Ladder selected as the primary access method without evidence that EWPs/scaffolding were first considered = flag
- Worker carrying out extended duration work from a single/extension ladder = flag

CHUNK 27: Ladders — Positioning, Setup, and Safe Use
Section: CoP s9.1; AS/NZS 1892 (set)
Topic: Pre-use inspection; setup requirements; slope; securing; three points of contact; prohibited uses

PRE-USE INSPECTION:
- Must inspect ladder for VISIBLE DAMAGE OR FAULTS before setup (e.g. broken rungs, stiles, footings)
- FAULTY OR DAMAGED LADDERS MUST BE REMOVED FROM SERVICE

SETUP — CHECKS REQUIRED:
- Ladder is the CORRECT HEIGHT for the work to avoid over-reaching or stretching
- LOCKING DEVICES are secure
- Ladder is NOT PLACED so that the weight is supported by the RUNGS

SETUP — SLIP PREVENTION FOR SINGLE AND EXTENSION LADDERS:
- Ensuring ladder has NON-SLIP FEET
- Placing ladder at a slope of 4:1 — distance between base and supporting structure should be about 1 METRE FOR EVERY 4 METRES OF WORKING LADDER HEIGHT
- SECURING LADDERS AT THE TOP OR BOTTOM, or at both ends if necessary

STEPLADDERS:
- Must be set up in the FULLY OPENED POSITION
- May require a second person to 'FOOT' the ladder for added stability

SAFE USE — THREE POINTS OF CONTACT (mandatory):
- When GOING UP OR DOWN: always have TWO FEET AND ONE HAND, or ONE FOOT AND TWO HANDS, on the ladder
- When WORKING FROM A LADDER: have TWO FEET AND ONE OTHER POINT OF CONTACT (e.g. hand or thighs against ladder)
- Use a TOOL BELT OR SIDE POUCH — do not carry tools or materials in hands while climbing
- Only LIGHT DUTY WORK carried out from ladder — tools must be operable safely with ONE HAND
- ENSURE NO-ONE WORKS UNDERNEATH the ladder
- DO NOT allow anyone else on the ladder AT THE SAME TIME
- DO NOT STRADDLE the ladder
- Wear SLIP-RESISTANT FOOTWEAR

ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED LADDER PRACTICES:
- Using METAL OR METAL-REINFORCED ladders when working on LIVE ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS
- Carrying out 'HOT WORK' (arc welding, oxy cutting) from ladders

PROHIBITED UNLESS ADDITIONAL FALL PROTECTION IS IN PLACE:
- Using a stepladder NEAR THE EDGE OF AN OPEN FLOOR, PENETRATION OR BESIDE A RAILING
- OVER-REACHING — centre of torso must remain WITHIN THE LADDER STILES throughout work
- Using POWER OR HAND TOOLS REQUIRING TWO HANDS (concrete cutting saws, circular saws)
- Using tools requiring HIGH DEGREE OF LEVERAGE FORCE that could cause over-balancing or fall (stillsons, pinch bars)
- FACING AWAY FROM THE LADDER when going up, going down, or when working from it
- Standing on a rung CLOSER THAN 900 mm TO THE TOP of a single or extension ladder
- Standing HIGHER THAN THE SECOND TREAD BELOW THE TOP PLATE of a stepladder (exception: three-rung stepladders, or when working through an overhead opening providing additional support)

ADDITIONAL PRECAUTIONS — SPECIAL SITUATIONS:
- In ACCESS AREAS OR DOORWAYS: erect a barrier or lock the door shut
- NEXT TO POWERLINES: only if worker is trained/authorised and correct ladder is used
- In VERY WET OR WINDY CONDITIONS: additional precautions required
- NEXT TO TRAFFIC AREAS: work area must be barricaded

USING LADDERS FOR ENTRY AND EXIT — WHERE USED:
- Firm, stable WORK PLATFORM to step onto from ladder
- Ladder must be SECURELY FIXED
- Ladder must extend AT LEAST 1 METRE ABOVE THE STEPPING-OFF POINT on the working platform
- FALL PROTECTION must be provided at the stepping-off point where people access the working platform
- Consider installing STAIRCASE AS SOON AS POSSIBLE as an alternative

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ladder slope clearly not at 4:1 (too steep or too shallow) = non-compliance
- Ladder not secured at top or bottom = non-compliance
- Worker carrying materials in hands (no tool belt) while climbing = unsafe practice
- Worker facing away from ladder while working from it = unsafe practice
- Worker standing on top tread/top plate of stepladder = non-compliance
- Worker using two-handed power tool from ladder without additional fall protection = non-compliance
- Ladder not extending at least 1m above stepping-off point = non-compliance
- Metal ladder in use near electrical installations = serious risk

CHUNK 28: Ladders — Special Situations and Entry/Exit Requirements
Section: CoP s9.1; AS/NZS 1892 (set)
Topic: Additional precautions for doorways, powerlines, wet/windy conditions, traffic areas; ladder as entry/exit requirements

SPECIAL SITUATIONS — LADDER USE:

IN ACCESS AREAS OR DOORWAYS:
- If necessary: ERECT A BARRIER or LOCK THE DOOR SHUT

NEXT TO POWERLINES:
- Only carried out if worker is TRAINED AND AUTHORISED and the CORRECT LADDER is being used

IN VERY WET OR WINDY CONDITIONS:
- Additional safety precautions in place

NEXT TO TRAFFIC AREAS:
- Working area must be BARRICADED

WHERE LADDERS USED FOR ENTRY AND EXIT — REQUIREMENTS:
- Firm, stable WORK PLATFORM free from obstructions to step onto from the ladder
- Ladder SECURELY FIXED
- Ladder extends at least 1 METRE ABOVE THE STEPPING-OFF POINT on the working platform
- FALL PROTECTION provided at the stepping-off point where people access the working platform
- Alternative: consider having the STAIRCASE INSTALLED AS SOON AS POSSIBLE in stairwells

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ladder as sole access to elevated work area in a stairwell when staircase installation is practicable = flag
- Ladder accessing a platform with no fall protection at stepping-off point = non-compliance
- Ladder not extending 1m above stepping-off point = non-compliance
- Ladder in doorway without barrier or locked door = flag

CHUNK 29: Ladders — Fixed Ladders
Section: CoP s9.2; AS 1657
Topic: Fixed ladder angle requirements; ladder cages; fall arrest systems for fixed ladders; rescue procedures

FIXED LADDERS — DESIGN REQUIREMENTS (AS 1657):
- Must be installed in accordance with AS 1657: Fixed Platforms, Walkways, Stairways and Ladders — Design, Construction and Installation
- Angle of slope: must NOT BE LESS THAN 70 DEGREES to the horizontal and NOT GREATER THAN 75 DEGREES to the horizontal
- In NO CASE should the ladder OVERHANG THE PERSON CLIMBING the ladder

LADDER CAGES:
- On vertical fixed ladders (>75 degrees to horizontal), ladder cages:
  - Do NOT stop a fall — they FUNNEL A FALL
  - In some cases, MORE INJURIES can occur from striking protective backguards on the way down
  - May HINDER RESCUES
- Therefore: fixed ladders with angles EXCEEDING 75 DEGREES to the horizontal should be fitted with a PERMANENT OR TEMPORARY FALL ARREST SYSTEM using anchorage lines or rails

FALL ARREST SYSTEM FOR FIXED LADDERS WHERE ANGLE >75 DEGREES:
- Must provide a SAFE SYSTEM OF WORK to prevent falls
- Options: PERMANENT FALL ARREST SYSTEM or FULL BODY HARNESS WITH DOUBLE LANYARD

RESCUE PROCEDURES:
- A SPECIFICALLY DESIGNED RESCUE PROCEDURE must be developed for use in LADDER CAGE SITUATIONS
- Training in rescue procedures must occur BEFORE USING THE FIXED LADDER

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Fixed ladder with angle >75 degrees relying only on ladder cage with no fall arrest system = non-compliance
- Fixed ladder with angle <70 degrees or >75 degrees = does not meet AS 1657 requirements
- No rescue procedure developed or training undertaken for ladder cage situations = non-compliance
- Fixed ladder overhanding the climber = does not meet AS 1657

CHUNK 30: Ladders — Maintenance and Inspection
Section: CoP s9.3
Topic: Inspection frequency; defects requiring removal from service; maintenance requirements

LADDER MAINTENANCE:
- Ladders must be REGULARLY INSPECTED by a COMPETENT PERSON in accordance with MANUFACTURER'S RECOMMENDATIONS

DEFECTS REQUIRING REMOVAL FROM SERVICE OR REPAIR:
- FIBREGLASS STILES: cracked, chipped or severely faded with fibres exposed
- TIMBER STILES: warped, splintered, cracked or bruised
- METAL STILES: twisted, bent, kinked, crushed or with cracked welds
- RUNGS, STEPS, TREADS OR TOP PLATES: missing, worn, damaged or loose
- TIE RODS: missing, broken or loose
- ROPES, BRACES OR BRACKETS: missing, broken or worn
- TIMBER MEMBERS: covered with OPAQUE PAINT or treatment that could DISGUISE FAULTS in the timber
- FASTENERS (rivets, bolts, pins): missing, loose, bent or worn
- FEET: worn or damaged, including non-slip material

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ladder with visible cracked, bent or damaged stiles in use = non-compliance — must be removed from service
- Timber ladder painted with opaque paint (defects obscured) = non-compliance
- Ladder missing rungs, treads or top plate = non-compliance — must be removed from service
- Ladder with worn or missing non-slip feet = non-compliance

CHUNK 31: Emergency Procedures for Falls — Requirements and Considerations
Section: CoP s10; WHS Regulation s80, s42
Topic: Mandatory emergency and rescue procedures; first aid obligations; suspension intolerance; rescue planning factors

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES — MANDATORY (WHS Regulation s80):
- PCBU who provides a FALL ARREST SYSTEM as a control measure MUST ESTABLISH EMERGENCY AND RESCUE PROCEDURES
- Procedures MUST BE TESTED to ensure they are effective
- Workers must be provided with SUITABLE AND ADEQUATE INFORMATION, INSTRUCTION AND TRAINING in relation to emergency procedures
- Emergency procedures must be incorporated into the EMERGENCY PLAN required for the workplace under WHS Regulation

FIRST AID OBLIGATIONS (WHS Regulation s42):
- PCBU must ensure workers have access to FIRST AID EQUIPMENT AND FACILITIES
- Workers must be trained to administer first aid OR workers have ACCESS TO PEOPLE TRAINED IN FIRST AID

TYPICAL INJURIES FROM FALLS:
- Unconsciousness
- Blocked airway
- Impalement
- Serious head or abdominal injuries
- Fractures
- SUSPENSION INTOLERANCE (from being suspended in harness after fall arrest)

EMERGENCY PROCEDURE PLANNING — KEY CONSIDERATIONS:
- LOCATION OF WORK AREA:
  - Is it remote or isolated?
  - How accessible in an emergency?
  - How far from medical facilities?
  - Can rescue after arrested fall be provided IMMEDIATELY without relying on emergency services?
- COMMUNICATIONS:
  - How can workers at height communicate in an emergency?
- RESCUE EQUIPMENT:
  - What emergencies may arise?
  - Provision of suitable rescue equipment depending on nature of work and control measures used
  - Equipment must include: emergency rapid response kit with man-made fibre rope per AS/NZS 4142.3
  - Rescue equipment must be kept IN CLOSE PROXIMITY to the work area so it can be used IMMEDIATELY
- CAPABILITIES OF RESCUERS:
  - Properly trained and sufficiently fit?
  - Capable of using equipment (breathing apparatus, lifelines, firefighting equipment)?
  - Have emergency procedures been tested?
- FIRST AID:
  - First aid available for fall injuries?
  - Trained first aid officers available?
- LOCAL EMERGENCY SERVICES (if to be relied on for rescue):
  - How will ambulance etc. be notified?
  - What is the likely RESPONSE TIME?

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No emergency and rescue procedure on site where fall arrest systems are in use = serious non-compliance
- Emergency rescue equipment not kept in close proximity to work area = non-compliance
- No first aid officer or trained first aid personnel accessible on site = non-compliance
- Workers using fall arrest systems with no other person on site capable of rescue = non-compliance
- No evidence that emergency procedures have been tested = non-compliance

CHUNK 32: Suspension Intolerance — Recognition and Prevention
Section: CoP s10.1–s10.2
Topic: What suspension intolerance is; physiological process; prevention; rescue training

SUSPENSION INTOLERANCE — DESCRIPTION:
- Can occur when a person has an ARRESTED FALL and is suspended in an UPRIGHT, VERTICAL POSITION in a harness
- The lower legs' capacity to store large amounts of blood REDUCES RETURN OF BLOOD TO THE HEART
- Slows the heart rate → person may FAINT
- Can lead to RENAL FAILURE and eventually DEATH, depending on person's susceptibility
- Worsened by HEAT AND DEHYDRATION

URGENCY OF RESCUE:
- QUICK RESCUE AS SOON AS POSSIBLE is vital
- Workers must be capable of conducting a rescue AND BE FAMILIAR with on-site RESCUE EQUIPMENT AND PROCEDURES
- Relevant workers MUST BE TRAINED in rescue procedures
- Workers must be able to RECOGNISE the risks of suspension intolerance and ACT QUICKLY

PREVENTING SUSPENSION INTOLERANCE:
- Workers must NEVER WORK ALONE when using a harness as fall protection
- Workers should use a harness that allows LEGS TO BE KEPT HORIZONTAL
- Where rescue is likely to take MORE THAN FIVE MINUTES: harness and connection point used should allow the suspended worker to RAISE LEGS TO NEAR HORIZONTAL, or worker should carry STRAPS TO PROVIDE FOOTHOLDS
- Workers trained to do the following when suspended after a fall (if physically possible):
  - MOVE THEIR LEGS IN THE HARNESS and push against any footholds
  - MOVE LEGS OR BODY TO A NEAR HORIZONTAL POSITION

TRAINING FOR RESCUES (CoP s10.2):
- Rescue process should START IMMEDIATELY
- TRAINING FREQUENCY should account for worker's competence and their ability to RETAIN COMPETENCE through regular exposure to equipment and skills
- Workers must NOT PUT THEMSELVES AT RISK during a rescue

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker in harness suspended after arrested fall with no rescue commenced promptly = life-threatening emergency
- Only one person on site with no rescue capability = non-compliance with fall arrest system requirements
- No harness providing the ability to raise legs to near horizontal for longer rescues = flag for high sites
- Workers not trained in rescue procedures or unfamiliar with rescue equipment on site = non-compliance

CHUNK 33: Inspection Checklist — Falls Risk Compliance Audit Reference
Section: CoP s3.2, s4.2, s5.1–s5.3, s7.3, s9.1–s9.3
Topic: Comprehensive site audit checklist for fall hazard identification and control compliance

SITE LAYOUT AND ENVIRONMENT:
- Are all floor/roof openings, penetrations and holes covered or barricaded?
- Are hole covers secure, clearly signed ('DANGER HOLE BENEATH') and capable of withstanding 2 kN (200 kg)?
- Are all open edges at perimeters, stairwells, mezzanines and roofs protected by compliant guardrails (900–1100 mm top rail, mid-rail, toe-board)?
- Are surfaces non-slip and free from trip hazards?
- Do work surface gradients comply with 7 degrees maximum (or 20 degrees for cleated surfaces)?
- Is grid mesh flooring securely fixed and assembled to manufacturer's specifications?
- Are no go area signs and barriers clearly visible, securely fixed and supervised?

SCAFFOLDS:
- Is a handover certificate available for any scaffold from which fall >4m is possible?
- Has the scaffold been inspected by a competent person within the last 30 days?
- Does edge protection (handrails, mid-rails, toe-boards) exist at every open edge of every working platform?
- Is the duty rating of each platform clearly known and not exceeded?
- Is the scaffold clear of debris, obstructions and unauthorised materials?
- Are mobile scaffold castors locked whenever workers are on the platform?
- Is access to mobile scaffold via internal ladder only?
- Are incomplete scaffolds tagged and protected from unauthorised access?
- Are prefabricated scaffold components all from the same manufacturer (or mixing approved)?

ELEVATING WORK PLATFORMS:
- Is the EWP on a solid, level surface appropriate for its design?
- Are workers in boom lifts/cherry pickers wearing a properly anchored fall arrest harness?
- Is the harness lanyard attached to the designated anchor point on the EWP (not the handrail)?
- Does the operator of a boom EWP with boom ≥11m hold the appropriate high risk work licence?
- Are surface penetrations and obstructions checked before EWP operation?
- Is appropriate powerline clearance maintained?

FORKLIFT WORK PLATFORMS AND CRANE WORKBOXES:
- Is the work platform engineer-designed and certified?
- Is the work platform secured to the forklift carriage with a locking bracket and pin?
- Are workers remaining within the platform when elevated?
- Is the safety gate self-closing and kept shut?
- Is the crane operator at the controls at all times when the workbox is elevated?
- Are workers in crane workboxes wearing a fall arrest harness and connected to the designated anchorage?

LADDERS:
- Is the ladder industrial grade with a load rating of at least 120 kg?
- Has the ladder been inspected and found to be free of defects?
- Is the ladder set up at a 4:1 slope (for single/extension ladders)?
- Is the ladder secured at top or bottom?
- Are workers maintaining three points of contact?
- Is the ladder extending at least 1m above the stepping-off point (where used for access)?
- Are workers using tool belts and not carrying tools in hands while climbing?
- Are workers facing the ladder at all times?

INDIVIDUAL FALL ARREST SYSTEMS:
- Is the equipment compliant with AS/NZS 1891 (set) and in date?
- Are all components compatible and approved for use together?
- Is the maximum free fall distance less than 2m?
- Is the anchorage point as high as possible?
- Is the harness correctly fitted and connected to the appropriate attachment point?
- Is there sufficient clearance below for the system (including shock absorber) to fully deploy?
- Has the equipment been inspected by a competent person before use?
- If previously used to arrest a fall — has equipment been re-certified?

ROPE ACCESS:
- Is a SWMS prepared for rope access construction work involving falls >2m?
- Are two independently anchored ropes in use for each person?
- Is supervision in place by a suitably experienced and competent supervisor?
- Is there an exclusion zone below the working area?
- Are workers not working alone?

SAFETY NETS AND CATCH PLATFORMS:
- Are safety nets within 2m of the underside of the working area?
- Are safety nets sufficiently tensioned and free of debris?
- Is no hot work occurring directly above safety nets?
- Are catch platforms planked out fully and within 1m below the work area?
- Do catch platforms extend at least 2m beyond unprotected edges?

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES:
- Are emergency and rescue procedures documented and tested?
- Is rescue equipment kept in close proximity to the work area?
- Are trained first aid officers accessible?
- Are workers trained in rescue procedures including recognition of suspension intolerance?
- Is at least one other person on site capable of rescue whenever a fall arrest system is in use?
