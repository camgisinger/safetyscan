CHUNK 1: Workplace Facilities and Access — Visible Compliance
Section: s19(3)(a), s19(3)(e), s20
Topic: Mandatory facilities and safe access/egress at workplaces

MANDATORY FACILITIES visible on site:
- Toilets accessible to workers
- Drinking water supply
- Hand-washing facilities
- Shade or shelter for rest breaks
- Clear, safe access and egress paths without trip hazards

WORKPLACE CONDITION REQUIREMENTS:
- Work environment without visible risks (no exposed hazards, unguarded edges, unstable surfaces)
- Plant and equipment visibly maintained, not damaged or makeshift
- Safe systems visible: barricades, exclusion zones, signage in place

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No toilets, drinking water or hand-washing visible on site = non-compliant
- Blocked or unsafe site access/egress paths = non-compliant
- No shade or shelter for workers = flag
- Damaged, unmaintained plant or equipment = non-compliant
- Makeshift or improvised equipment = serious non-compliance
- No barricades or exclusion zones around hazards = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: PPE and Supervision — Visible Worker Protection
Section: s19(3)(f)
Topic: PPE appropriate to task and visible supervision for high-risk work

MANDATORY PPE visible on workers:
- Hard hats in construction zones
- High-visibility vests in traffic areas or exclusion zones
- Eye protection for grinding, cutting, concrete work
- Safety boots
- Gloves appropriate to task
- Hearing protection in high-noise areas
- Respiratory protection for dust/fume exposure

SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS:
- Supervisor or competent person visible during high-risk tasks (working at heights, confined spaces, excavation, crane lifts)
- Workers appear to have received induction (cards/stickers visible)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers without hard hats in construction zone = non-compliant
- Workers in traffic areas without hi-vis vests = non-compliant
- Line hand or concrete worker without eye protection = non-compliant
- High-risk work (heights, excavation, crane lift) with no supervisor visible = flag
- Workers without appropriate PPE for visible task = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Notifiable Incidents — Dangerous Incident Categories Visible in Photos
Section: s35, s36(3)
Topic: Dangerous incidents requiring immediate notification to WorkSafe Queensland

DANGEROUS INCIDENT = incident exposing person to serious risk from:
(a) Uncontrolled escape, spillage or leakage of substance
(b) Uncontrolled implosion, explosion or fire
(c) Uncontrolled escape of gas or steam
(d) Uncontrolled escape of pressurised substance
(e) Electric shock
(f) Fall or release from height of plant, substance or thing
(g) Collapse, overturning, failure or malfunction of plant requiring authorisation (cranes, EWPs, forklifts, hoists)
(h) Collapse or partial collapse of structure
(i) Collapse or failure of excavation or shoring
(j) Inrush of water, mud or gas in underground excavation or tunnel
(k) Interruption of main ventilation in underground excavation or tunnel

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Collapsed scaffolding or structural elements = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Overturned crane, EWP, forklift or hoist = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Collapsed or failed excavation walls or shoring = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Fire or explosion damage visible = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Fallen powerlines or electric shock evidence (burn marks) = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Unsupported trench walls in sandy/unstable soil showing failure = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately
- Plant damage indicating malfunction or failure = dangerous incident — must be notified immediately

CHUNK 4: Incident Site Preservation — Visible Disturbance Restrictions
Section: s39
Topic: Site preservation requirements after notifiable incident

MANDATORY after notifiable incident:
- Site where incident occurred MUST NOT be disturbed until inspector arrives or directs otherwise
- Exception: disturbance allowed ONLY to:
  • Assist injured person
  • Remove deceased person
  • Make site safe or prevent further incidents
  • Comply with inspector direction

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Active incident site with equipment moved or cleaned up before inspector arrival = non-compliant (unless for safety)
- Incident site with no preservation barriers or tape = flag
- Evidence of cleanup or disturbance at recent incident site = potential non-compliance
- No signage or barriers preventing access to incident site = flag

CHUNK 5: Compliance Notices — Visible Stop Work and Improvement Notices
Section: s191, s195, s198
Topic: Inspector-issued notices visible on site or plant

IMPROVEMENT NOTICE (s191):
- Specifies contravention and time to remedy
- Must be displayed at workplace or on plant

PROHIBITION NOTICE (s195):
- Prohibits activity involving serious risk until notice complied with
- Must be displayed at workplace or on plant
- Activity MUST CEASE immediately

NON-DISTURBANCE NOTICE (s198):
- Preserves site for investigation
- Site MUST NOT be disturbed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Improvement Notice or Prohibition Notice displayed on site = active compliance issue
- Stop Work sign or tag on plant = Prohibition Notice likely in force — plant must not be used
- Red tag or stop work notice on equipment = non-compliant if equipment in use
- Non-Disturbance Notice visible = site must be preserved, no work permitted
- Work continuing despite visible Prohibition Notice = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Plant and Structure Installation — Visible Safe Installation
Section: s21, s22, s26
Topic: Plant, fixtures and structures must be installed without risk

MANDATORY:
- Plant must be installed, constructed or commissioned without risk to health and safety (s26)
- Fixtures, fittings and plant at workplace must be without risk (s21)
- Structures must be designed and installed without risk (s22)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding erected unsafely (missing ties, unstable base, overloaded) = non-compliant
- Temporary structures visibly unstable or inadequately braced = non-compliant
- Plant installed on unstable or inadequate foundations = non-compliant
- Formwork or falsework visibly inadequate or damaged = non-compliant
- Cranes or hoists on soft ground without adequate pads = non-compliant
- Electrical installations exposed or unsafe (no RCD, damaged cables) = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Hierarchy of Controls — Visible Control Measures
Section: s17, s18
Topic: Risk elimination and minimisation — hierarchy of controls visible on site

HIERARCHY (most to least preferred):
1. Elimination — hazard removed entirely
2. Substitution — less hazardous alternative used
3. Isolation — people separated from hazard (barriers, exclusion zones)
4. Engineering controls — guards, barriers, ventilation, interlocks
5. Administrative controls — signage, procedures, training
6. PPE — last resort, used WITH other controls

MANDATORY:
- Risks must be ELIMINATED if reasonably practicable (s17)
- If elimination not practicable, risks must be MINIMISED (s18)
- Physical controls (barriers, guards) must be in place BEFORE relying on signage or PPE alone

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- PPE only with no physical barriers or engineering controls = potential non-compliance
- Unguarded machinery or edges with only signage = non-compliant
- Exclusion zones marked by signage only without physical barriers = inadequate control
- Workers relying on PPE alone for fall protection without edge protection = non-compliant
- No physical separation between workers and moving plant = inadequate control

CHUNK 8: Serious Injury or Illness — Definitions Visible in Photos
Section: s36(1), s36(2)
Topic: Serious injuries requiring immediate notification to WorkSafe Queensland

SERIOUS INJURY OR ILLNESS = injury/illness requiring:
(a) Immediate treatment as hospital in-patient; OR
(b) Immediate treatment for:
  • Amputation
  • Serious head injury
  • Serious eye injury
  • Serious burn
  • Separation of skin from underlying tissue (degloving/scalping)
  • Spinal injury
  • Loss of bodily function
  • Serious lacerations
(c) Medical treatment within 48 hours of substance exposure

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker with visible serious injury (head trauma, burns, lacerations, eye injury) = serious injury — must be notified immediately
- Evidence of amputation incident = serious injury — must be notified immediately
- Worker being treated by paramedics or transported to hospital = potential serious injury — must be notified if meets criteria
- Incident involving substance exposure with worker receiving medical treatment = serious injury if within 48 hours

CHUNK 9: Officer Due Diligence — Visible Site Management Indicators
Section: s27
Topic: Visible indicators of officer due diligence and site management systems

OFFICERS must exercise due diligence including:
(a) Knowledge of WHS matters
(b) Understanding of operations, hazards and risks
(c) Appropriate resources and processes to eliminate/minimise risks
(d) Processes for receiving and responding to incident/hazard information
(e) Processes for complying with WHS duties
(f) Verification of resource and process provision and use

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Site managers/supervisors visibly present and engaged = positive indicator
- Safety notice boards with current information, toolbox talk records, incident reports = positive indicator
- No visible supervision or management presence = flag
- No safety signage, notice boards or communication systems visible = flag
- Hazards visible with no evidence of response or control = non-compliant
- No incident reporting system visible (forms, boards, procedures) = flag

CHUNK 10: Worker Consultation — Visible Consultation Systems
Section: s46, s47, s48
Topic: Visible evidence of worker consultation on WHS matters

CONSULTATION REQUIRED when:
(a) Identifying hazards and assessing risks
(b) Deciding on risk elimination/minimisation
(c) Deciding on adequacy of welfare facilities
(d) Proposing changes affecting worker health/safety
(e) Making decisions on consultation procedures, issue resolution, monitoring, information and training

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Safety notice boards with toolbox talk records, meeting minutes, consultation records = positive indicator
- Workers participating in safety meetings or toolbox talks = positive indicator
- Health and Safety Representative (HSR) contact details displayed = positive indicator
- No safety notice boards or consultation records visible = flag
- Workers unaware of hazards or controls (visible confusion, unsafe behavior) = potential consultation failure
- No evidence of worker involvement in safety decisions = flag

CHUNK 11: Penalty Categories — Visible Risk Levels
Section: s31, s32, s33, s34C
Topic: Penalty categories based on visible risk exposure

CATEGORY 1 (s31) — Reckless or negligent conduct exposing person to risk of death or serious injury:
Maximum: Individual 6,000 penalty units or 5 years imprisonment; Body corporate 30,000 penalty units

CATEGORY 2 (s32) — Failure exposing person to risk of death or serious injury:
Maximum: Individual 3,000 penalty units; Body corporate 15,000 penalty units

CATEGORY 3 (s33) — Failure to comply with health and safety duty:
Maximum: Individual 500 penalty units; Body corporate 3,000 penalty units

INDUSTRIAL MANSLAUGHTER (s34C) — Negligent conduct causing death:
Maximum: Individual 20 years imprisonment; Body corporate 100,000 penalty units

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers at unprotected heights without fall protection = Category 1 risk (death/serious injury)
- Unsupported excavation with workers inside = Category 1 risk (death/serious injury)
- Workers in crane lift exclusion zone = Category 1 risk (death/serious injury)
- Unguarded machinery in operation = Category 2 risk (serious injury)
- Missing PPE or inadequate facilities = Category 3 (duty failure)
- Any fatality on site = potential Industrial Manslaughter investigation
