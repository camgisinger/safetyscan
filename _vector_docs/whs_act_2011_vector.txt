CHUNK 1: Primary Duty of Care
Section: s19
Topic: Primary duty of care — what a PCBU must ensure

A person conducting a business or undertaking (PCBU) must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health and safety of workers engaged or caused to be engaged, and other persons, while at work.

Without limiting this, a PCBU must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable:
(a) The provision and maintenance of a work environment without risks to health and safety
(b) The provision and maintenance of safe plant and structures
(c) The provision and maintenance of safe systems of work
(d) The safe use, handling and storage of plant, structures and substances
(e) The provision of adequate facilities for the welfare of workers, including access to those facilities
(f) The provision of any information, training, instruction or supervision necessary to protect all persons from risks
(g) Monitoring the health of workers and conditions at the workplace to prevent illness or injury

WHAT THIS MEANS ON SITE (visual indicators of compliance):
- Workers have access to amenities: toilets, drinking water, hand-washing, shade/shelter
- Plant and equipment is visibly maintained, not damaged or makeshift
- Safe systems are in place (e.g. barricades, exclusion zones, signage)
- Workers appear to have received site induction (induction cards/stickers visible)
- PPE is being worn appropriate to the task
- Supervision is present for high-risk tasks

CHUNK 2: Management of Risks — Hierarchy of Controls
Section: s17, s18
Topic: How risks must be managed — hierarchy of controls

A duty to ensure health and safety requires the person to:
(a) ELIMINATE risks to health and safety, so far as is reasonably practicable; AND
(b) If elimination is not reasonably practicable, MINIMISE those risks so far as is reasonably practicable

"Reasonably practicable" means what is reasonably able to be done, taking into account:
- The likelihood of the hazard or risk occurring
- The degree of harm that might result
- What the person knows or ought to know about the hazard and ways to eliminate/minimise it
- The availability and suitability of ways to eliminate or minimise the risk
- The cost of available control measures (cost is not an excuse unless grossly disproportionate to the risk)

HIERARCHY OF CONTROLS (most to least preferred):
1. Elimination — remove the hazard entirely
2. Substitution — replace with something less hazardous
3. Isolation — separate people from the hazard
4. Engineering controls — physical controls (guards, barriers, ventilation)
5. Administrative controls — procedures, training, signage, supervision
6. PPE — last resort, used in conjunction with other controls

WHAT THIS MEANS ON SITE:
- If a risk can be eliminated (e.g. by design), it must be — not just controlled
- Physical barriers and engineering controls must be in place before relying on signage or PPE alone
- PPE alone without other controls visible is a potential compliance issue
- Evidence of risk assessment and control hierarchy should be visible in site setup

CHUNK 3: Notifiable Incidents — Definitions
Section: s35, s36, s37
Topic: What must be notified to the regulator (WorkSafe Queensland)

A NOTIFIABLE INCIDENT means:
(a) The death of a person; OR
(b) A serious injury or illness of a person; OR
(c) A dangerous incident

A SERIOUS INJURY OR ILLNESS means an injury or illness requiring:
(a) Immediate treatment as an in-patient in a hospital; OR
(b) Immediate treatment for:
    - Amputation of any part of the body
    - A serious head injury
    - A serious eye injury
    - A serious burn
    - Separation of skin from underlying tissue (degloving or scalping)
    - A spinal injury
    - Loss of a bodily function
    - Serious lacerations
(c) Medical treatment within 48 hours of exposure to a substance

A DANGEROUS INCIDENT means an incident at a workplace that exposes a worker or any person to a serious risk to health or safety from immediate or imminent exposure to:
(a) An uncontrolled escape, spillage or leakage of a substance
(b) An uncontrolled implosion, explosion or fire
(c) An uncontrolled escape of gas or steam
(d) An uncontrolled escape of a pressurised substance
(e) Electric shock
(f) The fall or release from a height of any plant, substance or thing
(g) The collapse, overturning, failure or malfunction of, or damage to, any plant required to be authorised for use under a regulation (e.g. cranes, EWPs, forklifts)
(h) The collapse or partial collapse of a structure
(i) The collapse or failure of an excavation or any shoring supporting an excavation
(j) The inrush of water, mud or gas in workings, in an underground excavation or tunnel
(k) The interruption of the main system of ventilation in an underground excavation or tunnel

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PHOTO ANALYSIS:
If a photo shows evidence of any of the above dangerous incident categories, this is a critical compliance issue requiring immediate reporting to WorkSafe Queensland. Examples visible in photos:
- Collapsed scaffolding or structural elements
- Unsupported excavation walls that have failed or are at risk
- Overturned plant or equipment
- Fire or explosion damage
- Evidence of electric shock incident (burn marks, fallen lines)
- Unsupported trenches in sandy/unstable soil
- Cranes or EWPs that appear damaged or malfunctioning

CHUNK 4: Duty to Notify and Preserve Incident Sites
Section: s38, s39
Topic: Obligations after a notifiable incident occurs

DUTY TO NOTIFY (s38):
- A PCBU must notify the regulator (WorkSafe Queensland) IMMEDIATELY after becoming aware a notifiable incident has occurred
- Notice must be given by the fastest possible means — by telephone or in writing
- A written record of each notifiable incident must be kept for at least 5 years

DUTY TO PRESERVE INCIDENT SITES (s39):
- The person with management or control of the workplace must ensure the site where the incident occurred is NOT disturbed until an inspector arrives or directs otherwise
- Exception: disturbance is allowed to the extent necessary to:
  - Assist an injured person
  - Remove a deceased person
  - Make the site safe or to prevent further notifiable incidents
  - Comply with a direction given by the regulator or an inspector

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PHOTO ANALYSIS:
If a photo shows an active incident site or aftermath of a dangerous incident, the PCBU must:
1. Have already notified WorkSafe Queensland immediately
2. Not disturb the site until an inspector arrives
3. Document the incident

CHUNK 5: Duties Relating to Workplaces and Plant
Section: s20, s21, s22, s23
Topic: Duties of persons managing or controlling workplaces and plant

MANAGEMENT OR CONTROL OF A WORKPLACE (s20):
A person with management or control of a workplace must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that the workplace, the means of entering and exiting the workplace, and anything arising from the workplace are without risks to health and safety.

MANAGEMENT OR CONTROL OF FIXTURES, FITTINGS OR PLANT (s21):
A person with management or control of fixtures, fittings or plant at a workplace must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that the fixtures, fittings and plant are without risks to health and safety.

DESIGN OF PLANT, SUBSTANCES OR STRUCTURES (s22):
A designer must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that plant, substance or structure is designed to be without risks to health and safety for persons who use, maintain, clean, repair or decommission it.

INSTALL, CONSTRUCT OR COMMISSION PLANT OR STRUCTURES (s26):
A person who installs, constructs or commissions plant or a structure must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that the way in which the plant or structure is installed, constructed or commissioned ensures it is without risks to health and safety.

WHAT THIS MEANS ON SITE:
- Scaffolding must be erected to be without risk — not just functional but safe
- All plant on site must be maintained and without risk
- Site access and egress must be safe (no trip hazards, adequate lighting, clear paths)
- Temporary structures must be designed and installed safely

CHUNK 6: Duty of Officers (Due Diligence)
Section: s27
Topic: Due diligence obligations of officers of a PCBU

An officer of a PCBU must exercise due diligence to ensure the PCBU complies with its duties. Due diligence includes:
(a) Acquiring and keeping up-to-date knowledge of work health and safety matters
(b) Gaining an understanding of the nature of the operations of the business or undertaking and generally of the hazards and risks associated with those operations
(c) Ensuring the PCBU has available for use, and uses, appropriate resources and processes to eliminate or minimise risks to health and safety
(d) Ensuring the PCBU has appropriate processes for receiving and considering information regarding incidents, hazards and risks, and for responding in a timely way to that information
(e) Ensuring the PCBU has, and implements, processes for complying with any duty or obligation under the WHS Act
(f) Verifying the provision and use of the resources and processes above

WHAT THIS MEANS ON SITE:
- Site managers and supervisors must be knowledgeable about WHS requirements
- There must be a system in place to identify and respond to hazards
- Incidents and near-misses must be recorded and acted upon

CHUNK 7: Consultation Requirements
Section: s46, s47, s48
Topic: When and how PCBUs must consult workers

A PCBU must consult, so far as is reasonably practicable, with workers who carry out work for the business or undertaking and who are, or are likely to be, directly affected by a matter relating to work health or safety.

Consultation is required when:
(a) Identifying hazards and assessing risks to health and safety arising from work
(b) Making decisions about ways to eliminate or minimise those risks
(c) Making decisions about the adequacy of facilities for the welfare of workers
(d) Proposing changes that may affect the health or safety of workers
(e) Making decisions about the procedures for consulting with workers, resolving work health and safety issues, monitoring the health and safety of workers, monitoring conditions at the workplace, providing information and training

WHAT THIS MEANS ON SITE:
- Workers should be involved in safety decisions — toolbox talks, safety meetings are indicators of compliance
- Workers should be aware of hazards and controls on site
- Safety notice boards with consultation records are a positive compliance indicator

CHUNK 8: Penalties for Non-Compliance
Section: s31, s32, s33
Topic: Penalties for failing to comply with health and safety duties

CATEGORY 1 (most serious) — s31:
Engaging in conduct that exposes a worker or other person to risk of death or serious injury or illness, with negligence or recklessness.
Maximum penalty: Individual — 6,000 penalty units or 5 years imprisonment; Body corporate — 30,000 penalty units

CATEGORY 2 — s32:
Failure to comply with a health and safety duty, where the failure exposes a worker or other person to risk of death or serious injury or illness.
Maximum penalty: Individual — 3,000 penalty units; Body corporate — 15,000 penalty units

CATEGORY 3 — s33:
Failure to comply with a health and safety duty.
Maximum penalty: Individual — 500 penalty units; Body corporate — 3,000 penalty units

INDUSTRIAL MANSLAUGHTER — s34C:
A PCBU who, through negligent conduct, causes the death of a worker or other person.
Maximum penalty: Individual — 20 years imprisonment; Body corporate — 100,000 penalty units

NOTE ON PENALTY UNITS (QLD):
As at 2026, 1 penalty unit = $143.75 (subject to annual adjustment).

CHUNK 9: Key Definitions
Section: s4, s5, s6, s7, s8
Topic: Important definitions under the WHS Act

PERSON CONDUCTING A BUSINESS OR UNDERTAKING (PCBU):
A person who conducts a business or undertaking, whether alone or with others, and whether for profit or not. Includes employers, self-employed persons, principals, head contractors, subcontractors. Does NOT include a worker who is engaged solely as a worker.

WORKER:
A person who carries out work in any capacity for a PCBU, including an employee, contractor, subcontractor, employee of a contractor or subcontractor, labour hire employee, outworker, apprentice or trainee, student on work experience, or volunteer.

WORKPLACE:
A place where work is carried out for a business or undertaking and includes any place where a worker goes, or is likely to be, while at work. This includes construction sites, mobile workplaces, and vehicles.

OFFICER:
A director, secretary, or person who makes, or participates in making, decisions that affect the whole, or a substantial part, of the business or undertaking. Includes partners in a partnership, and managers with significant influence over PCBU management.

HEALTH AND SAFETY REPRESENTATIVE (HSR):
A worker elected to represent a work group on health and safety matters. HSRs have specific powers including the power to issue Provisional Improvement Notices (PINs) and direct cessation of unsafe work.

REGULATOR:
In Queensland, the regulator is Workplace Health and Safety Queensland (WHSQ), a division of the Office of Industrial Relations.

CHUNK 10: Compliance Notices
Section: s191, s195, s198
Topic: Types of notices that can be issued by inspectors

IMPROVEMENT NOTICE (s191):
Issued by an inspector when they reasonably believe a person is contravening, or has contravened and is likely to contravene again, a provision of the WHS Act or Regulation. The notice specifies the contravention and a time to remedy it.

PROHIBITION NOTICE (s195):
Issued by an inspector when they reasonably believe an activity involves or will involve a serious risk to the health or safety of a person. The notice prohibits the carrying on of the activity until the notice is complied with.

NON-DISTURBANCE NOTICE (s198):
Issued by an inspector to preserve a site after an incident for investigation purposes.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR PHOTO ANALYSIS:
- An Improvement Notice or Prohibition Notice on display at a site is a significant compliance indicator
- Stop Work signs or tags on plant may indicate a Prohibition Notice is in force
- Site with active stop work notices should be flagged as non-compliant
