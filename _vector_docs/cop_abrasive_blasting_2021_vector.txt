CHUNK 1: Dead Man Control — Nozzle Cut-Off Device
Section: CoP s3.3
Topic: Mandatory fast-acting self-actuating cut-off device on all blast nozzles (KEY REQUIREMENT)

MANDATORY:
- Dead man control MUST be fitted to ALL blast nozzles
- Must be under direct control of the nozzle operator
- Must NEVER be taped down, bypassed or modified
- Pneumatic type: suitable up to 40m hose length
- Electric type: required over 40m for instant response

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Nozzle operator drops hose and flow continues = dead man control failure or tampering — serious non-compliance
- Dead man control taped down or wedged open = serious non-compliance
- No visible dead man control on nozzle = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 2: Prohibited Blast Media — Silica and Heavy Metals
Section: WHS Regulation s382, Schedule 10; CoP s3.1
Topic: Maximum permissible concentrations of hazardous substances in blast media (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

PROHIBITED CONCENTRATIONS — blast media MUST NOT contain:
- Crystalline Silicon Dioxide (Free Silica): >1%
- Lead: >0.1%
- Arsenic, Antimony, Beryllium, Cadmium, Cobalt, Nickel, Tin: >0.1%
- Chromium: >0.5% (except specified wet blasting)
- Asbestos: ANY use of high-pressure water or compressed air on asbestos-containing materials is STRICTLY PROHIBITED

WET BLASTING: Media MUST NOT contain chromate, nitrate, or nitrite.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Use of river sand, beach sand, or white sand = ILLEGAL (contains >1% free silica)
- Safety Data Sheet showing >0.1% lead = PROHIBITED media
- Blasting on suspected asbestos surface = high-risk non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Respiratory Protection — Airline Respirators
Section: CoP s3.2; WHS Regulation s44
Topic: Mandatory respiratory protection for blasting operators (KEY REQUIREMENT)

MANDATORY for manual blasting operators:
- Airline respirator: positive pressure hood or helmet type
- Must have inner bib and high-visibility shoulder cape
- Air quality must meet AS/NZS 1715
- Air-fed respirators should have carbon monoxide alarms

POT ATTENDANTS:
- Must wear at least an air-purifying respirator while blasting is in progress

PROHIBITED:
- Standard dust masks for manual blasting = LIFE-THREATENING NON-COMPLIANCE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using standard dust mask for manual blasting = serious non-compliance
- Helmet hung by air feed hose = potential equipment damage
- Pot attendant without respirator during active blasting = non-compliant
- No visible carbon monoxide alarm on air supply = flag

---

CHUNK 4: Protective Clothing and Gloves
Section: CoP s3.2; AS/NZS 2161
Topic: Body protection requirements for blasting operators

PROTECTIVE SUITS:
- Leather or elastic straps at wrists and ankles
- Overlapping flaps to prevent abrasive penetration
- Waterproof footwear that minimises particle penetration

GLOVES:
- Industrial safety gloves (canvas or leather) per AS/NZS 2161

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Damaged or taped-up holes in protective suits = non-compliant body protection
- No elastic straps or closures at wrists/ankles = non-compliant
- Worker in standard work clothes performing blasting = serious non-compliance
- Gloves visibly torn or worn through = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 5: Blast Hoses — Anti-Static and Whip Checks
Section: CoP s3.3
Topic: Hose specifications and coupling safety devices (KEY REQUIREMENTS)

BLAST HOSES:
- Must be anti-static OR fitted with an earth wire to prevent static shock/explosion
- Visible pin holes in hoses = imminent blow-out risk — must be replaced immediately
- Rated working pressure must not be exceeded

HOSE WHIP CHECKS:
- MUST be fitted to ALL couplings to prevent injury if a connection fails

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Blast hose with visible pin holes taped over = imminent blow-out risk — serious non-compliance
- Hose couplings without safety locks, pins or whip checks = non-compliant
- No visible earth wire on non-anti-static hose = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 6: Blasting Cabinets — Interlocks and Seals
Section: CoP s3.2, s3.4
Topic: Requirements for fully enclosed blasting cabinets for small objects

MANDATORY:
- Fully sealed cabinet; operator works from outside via sealed windows and gloves
- Doors must have interlocks to prevent operation while open
- Respiratory protection generally not required if cabinet maintained

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Dust leaking from cabinet door seals = maintenance failure — non-compliant
- Cabinet door open during blasting operation = interlock failure — serious non-compliance
- Operator opening doors immediately after blasting without extraction time = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 7: Blasting Chambers — Ventilation and Emergency Exit
Section: CoP s3.2, s3.4
Topic: Ventilation speeds, illumination and emergency exit requirements for large blasting chambers (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

VENTILATION MINIMUM SPEEDS:
- Down-draught: 0.3 m/s
- Cross-draught: 0.4 m/s

ILLUMINATION:
- At least 200 lux

EMERGENCY EXIT:
- Must be at the furthest position from the main entrance
- Must be signposted and backlit

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Operator opening chamber doors immediately after blasting = non-compliant (allow 5-minute extraction time)
- Chamber emergency exit blocked or not illuminated = serious safety breach
- Illumination visibly inadequate = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 8: Exclusion Zones and Warning Signs
Section: CoP s3.2
Topic: Exclusion zone marking and signage for open-air blasting

EXCLUSION ZONES:
- Size determined by risk assessment (consider wind direction)
- Must be marked with warning signs: "DANGER – ABRASIVE BLASTING IN PROGRESS"

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Warning signs "DANGER – ABRASIVE BLASTING IN PROGRESS" visible = compliant
- No exclusion zone marked or signed = non-compliant
- Abrasive blasting in windy conditions without extra containment = non-compliant
- Spent abrasive piling up on site = poor housekeeping; risk of drift

---

CHUNK 9: Temporary Enclosures — Height and Material
Section: CoP s3.2
Topic: Temporary enclosure specifications for fixed structures (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

TEMPORARY ENCLOSURES (for bridges, tanks, fixed structures):
- Screens should extend 2 METRES ABOVE the structure
- Porous materials (e.g. shade cloth) MUST NOT be used for toxic dusts like silica or lead

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Enclosure screens visibly less than 2m above structure = non-compliant
- Shade cloth or porous material used for silica/lead blasting = non-compliant
- Dust escaping from enclosure = ineffective containment

---

CHUNK 10: Noise Exposure Limits
Section: CoP s4.1; WHS Regulation s57
Topic: Noise levels and maximum unprotected exposure times (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

NOISE LEVELS:
- Blasting nozzles generate 112–119 dB(A)
- Exposure standard: 85 dB(A) over 8 hours
- Unprotected exposure at 101 dB(A) is limited to 12 MINUTES per shift

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Operator not wearing hearing protection inside a blast helmet = non-compliant
- Workers in blast area without hearing protection = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 11: Vibration Injury — White Finger
Section: CoP s4.3
Topic: Vibration-related injury symptoms and controls

VIBRATION RISK:
- Prolonged use causes Occupational Raynaud's disease (white finger)
- Controls: vibration-isolating handles and job rotation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Operator showing white or numb fingers = symptom of vibration injury
- No vibration-isolating handles on nozzle = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 12: Heat Strain Controls
Section: CoP s4.2
Topic: Managing heat stress from PPE and confined spaces

HEAT STRAIN RISK:
- PPE and confined/poorly ventilated spaces increase heat stress
- Controls: fit cooling devices to blast helmet air supplies; provide cool drinking water

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No cool drinking water provided on hot blasting site = non-compliant
- Operator showing signs of heat exhaustion = flag

---

CHUNK 13: Dust Containment — Visible Dust Clouds
Section: CoP s3.2
Topic: Effectiveness of containment and ventilation systems

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Visible clouds of dust escaping work area = ineffective containment/ventilation — non-compliant
- Workers eating or smoking in blast area = high risk of lead/silica ingestion — non-compliant
- Dry sweeping of spent abrasive = non-compliant (use vacuum or wet methods)
