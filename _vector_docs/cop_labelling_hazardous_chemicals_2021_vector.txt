TYPE: Approved Code of Practice
JURISDICTION: QLD
REGION: QLD

CHUNK 1: Exemptions — Chemicals Not Requiring Workplace Labels
Section: s1.2; WHS Regulation s335, s338, s341
Topic: Specific conditions where hazardous chemicals do not require workplace labels

NO LABEL REQUIRED IF:
- Chemicals in transit: Kept at workplace for NOT MORE THAN 5 CONSECUTIVE DAYS and not opened or used
- Consumer products: Labelled per Poisons Standard AND used in quantity CONSISTENT WITH HOUSEHOLD USE (e.g. 750mL bottle acceptable; 100L drum NOT exempt)
- Food/beverages: Packaged for consumption (small quantities only — large volumes require workplace labels)
- Therapeutic goods: TGA-labelled and intended for human consumption/administration

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- 100L drum of flammable liquid without GHS workplace label = non-compliant (exceeds consumer quantity)
- Unlabelled chemicals in loading dock for 6+ days = transit period exceeded; non-compliant
- Unlabelled container of powdered chemical in work area = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Mandatory Label Elements — Complete Requirements
Section: s2.1, s2.5; Schedule 9
Topic: All required information that must appear on every hazardous chemical label

EVERY LABEL MUST INCLUDE:
- Product identifier (unique name or number)
- Australian contact details (name, address, business phone) of manufacturer/importer
- Disclosure of ingredients (identity and proportion)
- Signal word: "Danger" OR "Warning" — ONLY ONE (if "Danger" applies, "Warning" must NOT appear)
- Hazard pictograms (GHS diamond symbols)
- Hazard statements (nature and degree of hazard)
- Precautionary statements (6–10 maximum recommended)
- Expiry date if applicable (e.g. chemicals forming explosive peroxides: ethers)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Label showing BOTH "Danger" AND "Warning" = precedence rule failure; non-compliant
- Ether container without expiry date = serious safety omission
- Label missing Australian contact details = non-compliant
- No hazard pictograms on known hazardous chemical = non-compliant
- Product identifier on label does not match SDS = compliance breach
- More than 10 precautionary statements = poor practice flag

CHUNK 3: Ingredient Disclosure — Approved Concentration Ranges
Section: s2.3; Schedule 8
Topic: Permitted proportion ranges for commercially confidential ingredients

APPROVED PROPORTION RANGES (where exact concentration is confidential):
- Less than 10%
- 10 to less than 30%
- 30 to 60%
- Greater than 60%
- Narrower ranges should normally be used (e.g. 30–40% instead of 30–60%)

GENERIC NAMES permitted ONLY if:
- Identity is genuinely commercially confidential
- Ingredient belongs to specific health hazard categories (e.g. Acute Toxicity Category 4, Skin Irritation Category 2)
- Ingredient does NOT have established Workplace Exposure Standard

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Label listing hazardous ingredient at exact "35%" while claiming confidentiality = non-compliant (must use approved brackets)
- Ingredient proportions not totalling approximately 100% = accuracy failure flag
- Generic name used for substance with published Workplace Exposure Standard = illegal

CHUNK 4: Small Containers — Minimum Label Requirements
Section: s3.1; Schedule 9
Topic: Reduced labelling for containers too small for full labels (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

SMALL CONTAINER MINIMUM (if too small for full label):
- Product identifier
- Australian contact details
- Hazard pictogram OR hazard statement

MINIMUM LABEL SIZES by container capacity:
- ≤500mL: Pictogram 15 x 15mm; Text 2.5mm minimum height
- >500mL to ≤5L: Pictogram 20 x 20mm; Text 3mm minimum height
- >5L to ≤25L: Pictogram 50 x 50mm; Text 5mm minimum height
- ≥25L: Pictogram 100 x 100mm; Text 7mm minimum height

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Small vial (10mL) missing manufacturer contact details = non-compliant if not meeting small container minimum
- 20L drum with pictograms smaller than 50 x 50mm = visibility non-compliance
- Text height on 30L drum less than 7mm = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Decanted Chemicals — Immediate Use Exemption
Section: s3.2; Schedule 9
Topic: Specific conditions where decanted containers do not require labels

NO LABEL REQUIRED ONLY if ALL of the following:
- Chemical is USED IMMEDIATELY
- Container NOT LEFT UNATTENDED by person who decanted it
- Used ONLY BY PERSON PRESENT at decanting
- Container RENDERED FREE FROM HAZARDOUS CHEMICAL immediately after use

OTHERWISE decanted container MUST include at minimum:
- Product identifier
- Hazard pictogram OR hazard statement

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Beaker of solvent left on lab bench without label while worker absent = non-compliant (left unattended)
- Unlabelled containers at workstations = non-compliant unless actively in use by person present
- Permanent decanting station with reused containers lacking re-labelling = procedural failure
- Multiple unlabelled beakers/containers in work area = systematic non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Research Chemicals and Unknown Properties
Section: s3.3; Schedule 9
Topic: Minimum labelling for research samples and chemicals with unknown hazards

RESEARCH CHEMICALS/SAMPLES minimum label:
- Product identifier
- Hazard pictogram OR hazard statement
- If properties unknown: label SHOULD include "Caution — Unknown Properties"

NANOMATERIALS (where hazards unknown):
- Label should include: "Caution: Hazards unknown" OR "Caution: Hazards not fully characterised"

UNLABELLED CONTAINERS (identifier not known):
- MUST be marked: "CAUTION — DO NOT USE — UNKNOWN SUBSTANCE"
- MUST NOT be used
- Must be STORED IN ISOLATION until identified or disposed of

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unlabelled container in workspace without "Caution — Do Not Use" tag and not isolated = serious safety risk
- Nanomaterial container without hazard characterisation statement = flag
- Research sample without product identifier = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 7: Hazardous Waste Container Labels
Section: s3.5; Schedule 9
Topic: Minimum labelling requirements for waste containers

HAZARDOUS WASTE labels MUST include at minimum:
- Product identifier (e.g. "chlorinated solvent waste")
- Manufacturer or importer contact details
- Hazard pictogram AND hazard statement (both required)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Waste drum labelled only "Chemicals" without hazard nature (e.g. "Flammable") = non-compliant
- Waste container without hazard pictogram = non-compliant
- Waste container missing contact details = non-compliant
- Multiple unlabelled waste drums in storage area = systematic failure

---

CHUNK 8: Pipe Work Identification
Section: s2.7; Schedule 9; AS 1345
Topic: Requirements for identifying hazardous chemicals in fixed pipe work

PIPE WORK IDENTIFICATION:
- Hazardous chemical in pipe work MUST be identified by label, sign or other method ON OR NEAR THE PIPE WORK
- Acceptable methods: signs adjacent to pipes, colour coding, prominently displayed schematic layouts

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hazardous chemical pipes running through facility without any identification markings = regulatory breach
- No labels, signs or schematics visible near pipe work carrying hazardous chemicals = non-compliant
- Pipe work identification faded or illegible = maintenance duty breach

CHUNK 9: Label Placement and Durability
Section: s4.2, s4.3
Topic: Physical placement and design requirements for label visibility

PLACEMENT REQUIREMENTS:
- Labels SHOULD be placed on BODY of container
- Labels SHOULD NOT be placed only on lid (unless unavoidable)

DESIGN REQUIREMENTS:
- Text colour must provide distinct contrast to background
- Labels must be durable and legible
- Pictograms must meet minimum size requirements (see Chunk 4)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Label attached only to removable screw-cap lid = high-risk placement; non-compliant
- Label text colour lacking contrast to background = design failure
- Pictograms faded, damaged or illegible = maintenance duty breach
- Label peeling off or partially detached = non-compliant; must be replaced
- Partially torn or illegible label = PCBU duty breach; must be replaced

---

CHUNK 10: Label Maintenance and SDS Review Intervals
Section: s5.1, s5.2
Topic: Required update frequency and handling of outdated information

MANDATORY REVIEW INTERVALS:
- SDSs MUST be updated at least ONCE EVERY 5 YEARS
- Labels should be reviewed when SDS is updated

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- SDS folder showing documents dated more than 5 years ago = compliance failure
- Chemical container with partially torn or illegible label = PCBU duty breach; must be replaced
- Labels not matching current SDS version = potential non-compliance
- Multiple containers with outdated or damaged labels = systematic maintenance failure
