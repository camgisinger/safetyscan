CHUNK 1: Primary Duties and Exemptions
Section: s1.1, s1.2; WHS Regulation s335, s338, s341
Topic: Responsibility for correct labelling and exemption conditions

PRIMARY DUTIES:
- Manufacturers/Importers: Must ensure the chemical is correctly labelled as soon as practicable after manufacturing or importing
- Suppliers: Must NOT supply a hazardous chemical if they know, or ought reasonably to know, it is not correctly labelled
- PCBUs: Must ensure any hazardous chemical used, handled, or stored at the workplace is correctly labelled

LABELLING EXEMPTIONS — a label is NOT required for:
- Chemicals in transit: Kept at the workplace for NOT MORE THAN FIVE CONSECUTIVE DAYS and not opened or used
- Consumer products: Labelled in accordance with the Poisons Standard and used in a way and quantity CONSISTENT WITH CONSUMER HOUSEHOLD USE
- Food and beverages: Packaged for consumption (e.g. a 750mL bottle of spirits) — NOTE: LARGE QUANTITIES (e.g. 100L) must be labelled to meet workplace requirements
- Therapeutic goods: Labelled in accordance with TGA requirements and intended for human consumption or administration

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A 100L drum of flammable liquid without a GHS workplace label = non-compliant (exceeds consumer use quantity)
- Hazardous chemicals sitting in a loading dock for 6 days without workplace labels = in-transit period exceeded; non-compliant
- An unlabelled container of powdered chemical in a manufacturing area = regulatory breach
- Hazardous chemical with no label at all = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Mandatory Label Elements
Section: s2.1, s2.5; Schedule 9
Topic: Required information on every hazardous chemical label

A hazardous chemical is correctly labelled if the container includes ALL of the following:
- PRODUCT IDENTIFIER (unique name or number)
- AUSTRALIAN CONTACT DETAILS (name, address, business phone number) of the manufacturer or importer
- DISCLOSURE OF INGREDIENTS (identity and proportion)
- SIGNAL WORD: "Danger" (severe hazard) OR "Warning" (less severe) — only ONE should appear; if "Danger" applies, "Warning" must NOT also appear
- HAZARD PICTOGRAMS (diamond-shaped GHS symbols)
- HAZARD STATEMENTS (nature and degree of hazard)
- PRECAUTIONARY STATEMENTS (measures to minimise or prevent adverse effects) — as a guide, a MAXIMUM OF BETWEEN SIX AND TEN should appear
- EXPIRY DATE if applicable (e.g. chemicals that can form explosive peroxides such as ethers)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A label displaying both "Danger" and "Warning" signal words = precedence rule failure; non-compliant
- A label for a chemical that can form explosive peroxides (e.g. ethers) missing an expiry date = serious safety omission
- Safety Data Sheet (SDS) and label product identifiers do not match = compliance breach
- Label missing Australian contact details = non-compliant
- No hazard pictograms on a known hazardous chemical container = non-compliant
- More than 10 precautionary statements on a label = poor practice

CHUNK 3: Disclosure of Ingredients and Generic Names
Section: s2.3; Schedule 8
Topic: Rules for identifying chemical constituents on labels

APPROVED PROPORTION RANGES — where exact concentrations are commercially confidential, these ranges can be disclosed:
- Less than 10%
- 10 to less than 30%
- 30 to 60%
- Greater than 60%
- NOTE: A narrower range should normally be used (e.g. 30–40% instead of 30–60%)

GENERIC NAMES: May be used ONLY if:
- The identity is genuinely commercially confidential
- The ingredient belongs to specific health hazard categories (e.g. Acute Toxicity Category 4, Skin Irritation Category 2)
- The ingredient does NOT have an established Workplace Exposure Standard

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A label listing a hazardous ingredient at "35%" while claiming confidentiality = non-compliant (must use approved brackets)
- Ingredient proportions on a label that do not indicate a total of approximately 100% = potential accuracy failure
- Use of a generic name for a substance that has a published Workplace Exposure Standard = illegal

CHUNK 4: Special Labelling Situations — Small, Research, and Decanted Containers
Section: s3.1, s3.2, s3.3; Schedule 9
Topic: Reduced labelling requirements for specific workplace tasks

SMALL CONTAINERS — if the container is too small for a full label, it MUST at a minimum include:
- Product identifier
- Australian contact details
- A hazard pictogram OR hazard statement

RESEARCH CHEMICALS AND SAMPLES — must at a minimum include:
- Product identifier
- A hazard pictogram or hazard statement
- If properties are unknown: label SHOULD include "Caution — Unknown Properties"

DECANTED CHEMICALS — a label is NOT required ONLY if the chemical is USED IMMEDIATELY, meaning ALL of the following:
- It is NOT LEFT UNATTENDED by the person who decanted it
- It is USED ONLY BY A PERSON PRESENT at the decanting process
- The container is RENDERED FREE FROM ANY HAZARDOUS CHEMICAL immediately after use

OTHERWISE, the decanted container MUST at a minimum include the product identifier and a hazard pictogram or hazard statement.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A beaker of solvent left on a lab bench without a label while the worker is absent = non-compliant (left unattended)
- A small vial (e.g. 10mL) missing manufacturer contact details = regulatory breach if not meeting small container minimum
- A permanent decanting station where the container is reused for different chemicals without re-labelling = procedural failure
- Unlabelled containers at workstations = non-compliant unless actively in use by the person present

CHUNK 5: Hazardous Waste and Pipe Work
Section: s3.5, s2.7; Schedule 9
Topic: Identifying waste substances and fixed installations

HAZARDOUS WASTE — labels on waste containers MUST at a minimum include:
- Product identifier (e.g. "chlorinated solvent waste")
- Manufacturer or importer contact details
- A hazard pictogram AND hazard statement

PIPE WORK: A PCBU must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that a hazardous chemical in pipe work is identified by a label, sign or another method ON OR NEAR THE PIPE WORK.
- Acceptable methods: signs adjacent to pipes, colour coding, prominently displayed schematic layouts

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A waste drum labelled simply "Chemicals" without indicating the nature of hazard (e.g. "Flammable") = non-compliant
- Hazardous chemical pipes running through a factory without any identification markings or nearby schematics = regulatory breach
- An unlabelled container found with no "Caution — Do Not Use" tag = safety risk
- Waste container without hazard pictogram = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Design, Layout, and Minimum Label Sizes
Section: s4.2, s4.3, Table 1
Topic: Physical requirements for label visibility and durability

PLACEMENT:
- Labels should be placed on the BODY of the container
- Labels should NOT be placed only on the lid — unless unavoidable

MINIMUM LABEL SIZES (Table 1) — pictogram size and minimum text height by container capacity:
- Capacity ≤ 500mL: Pictogram 15 x 15mm; Text 2.5mm minimum
- Capacity >500mL to ≤ 5L: Pictogram 20 x 20mm; Text 3mm minimum
- Capacity >5L to ≤ 25L: Pictogram 50 x 50mm; Text 5mm minimum
- Capacity ≥ 25L: Pictogram 100 x 100mm; Text 7mm minimum

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A 20L drum with hazard pictograms smaller than 50 x 50mm = visibility non-compliance
- Label text printed in a colour that does not provide distinct contrast to the background = design failure
- Workplace label attached only to a removable screw-cap lid = high-risk placement; non-compliant
- Pictograms faded, damaged or illegible = PCBU maintenance duty breach
- Label peeling off or partially detached = non-compliant — must be replaced

CHUNK 7: Maintenance, Review, and Unlabelled Containers
Section: s5.1, s5.2
Topic: Keeping labels current and handling unknown substances

SDS UPDATES: SDSs MUST be updated at least ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS. Labels should be reviewed at the same time.

UNLABELLED CONTAINERS: If an unlabelled chemical is found and the identifier is not known:
- It MUST be clearly marked: "CAUTION — DO NOT USE — UNKNOWN SUBSTANCE"
- It MUST NOT be used
- It must be STORED IN ISOLATION until identified or disposed of

NANOMATERIALS: For engineered or manufactured nanomaterials where hazards are unknown, the label should include: "Caution: Hazards unknown" or "Caution: Hazards not fully characterised"

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Chemical container with a partially torn or illegible label = PCBU duty breach — must be replaced
- SDS folder on site showing documents dated more than 5 years ago = compliance failure
- An unlabelled container in a workspace without a "Caution — Do Not Use" tag and not in isolation = serious safety risk
- Nanomaterial container without hazard characterisation statement = flag
- Multiple unlabelled containers in a storage area = systematic labelling failure
