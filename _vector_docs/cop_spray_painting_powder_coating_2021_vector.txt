CHUNK 1: Prohibited Chemicals — Banned Substances for Spray Painting
Section: s3.1; Schedule 10
Topic: Mandatory bans on specific hazardous substances (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

PROHIBITED FOR SPRAY PAINTING — MUST NOT use, handle or store:
- Arsenic and its compounds
- Benzene (benzol): if substance contains MORE THAN 1% BY VOLUME
- Carbon disulphide (carbon bisulphide)
- Lead carbonate
- Methanol (methyl alcohol): if substance contains MORE THAN 1% BY VOLUME
- Tetrachloroethane and Tetrachloromethane (carbon tetrachloride)
- Tributyl tin

ISOCYANATES (two-pack polyurethane paints):
- Exposure is HIGH RISK
- Full air-supplied respiratory protection MANDATORY when spraying in any enclosed booth (s3.10)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- SDS showing Benzene content >1% by volume = illegal/prohibited
- SDS showing Methanol content >1% by volume = prohibited
- Two-pack polyurethane spraying without air-supplied respirator = life-threatening non-compliance
- Sanding uncured polyurethane paint = high isocyanate exposure risk

CHUNK 2: Spray Booth Ventilation — Minimum Airflow Speeds
Section: s4.1; AS/NZS 4114
Topic: Mandatory air movement thresholds for spray booths (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MINIMUM VENTILATION SPEEDS (system must be capable of producing):
- Full DOWN-DRAUGHT BOOTH: 0.3 m/s
- ELECTROSTATIC SPRAYING: 0.4 m/s
- ANY OTHER BOOTH: 0.5 m/s

POST-PURGE CYCLE:
- MINIMUM FIVE MINUTES after spraying before personnel re-enter

PROHIBITIONS inside spray booths:
- Absence of air filter medium = PROHIBITED — booth must not be used
- Absorbent materials (paper, cardboard, wood) on booth floors = fire and maintenance breach

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No air filter medium visible inside spray booth = prohibited
- Booth ventilation system not running during spray operations = serious non-compliance
- Booth filters visibly clogged or saturated = reduced ventilation — must be replaced
- Worker positioned between spray gun and exhaust outlet = dangerous positioning
- Absorbent materials on booth floor = fire risk

CHUNK 3: Spraying Outside a Booth — Exclusion Zone Distances
Section: s4.1; Appendix C
Topic: Mandatory exclusion zone dimensions for open-air spraying (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

EXCLUSION ZONES when spraying outside a booth:

GENERAL RULE (low risk open area):
- AT LEAST 6 METRES HORIZONTAL (ensuring 3 metres distance in all directions from sprayer)
- AT LEAST 2 METRES VERTICAL CLEARANCE above and below point of application

OPEN SPRAY BOOTH (per AS 4114):
- AT LEAST 2 METRES extending in all directions from any opening or outlet

OUTDOOR SPRAYING (low risk):
- AT LEAST 3 METRES in all directions

ENTRY RESTRICTION:
- People other than sprayer MUST NOT ENTER zone unless wearing EQUIVALENT PPE to sprayer

MANDATORY SIGNAGE:
- "SPRAY PAINTING AREA — AUTHORISED PERSONNEL ONLY" must be displayed at exclusion boundary

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No exclusion zone signage at boundary = non-compliant
- Electrical equipment not certified for explosive atmospheres within exclusion zone = fire/explosion risk
- Bystanders within 3 metres of outdoor spraying without PPE = non-compliant
- No clear demarcation of exclusion zone boundary = non-compliant

CHUNK 4: Powder Coating — TGIC Hazard and Cleaning Controls
Section: s4.2; AS 3754
Topic: TGIC powder hazards and mandatory cleaning methods

TGIC (TRIGLYCIDYL ISOCYANURATE) HAZARD:
- Toxic by ingestion and inhalation
- Skin sensitiser
- Commonly found in powder coating formulations

CLEANING REQUIREMENTS:
- Use vacuum cleaner with HEPA FILTER only
- DO NOT use compressed air to clean powder dust
- DO NOT use dry sweeping

ENGINEERING CONTROLS:
- Interlocks: Power supply and powder feed lines should interlock with air extraction — if ventilation fails, supplies cut off automatically

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using compressed air to blow powder dust off clothing = serious health and explosion risk
- Dry sweeping of powder coating residue = non-compliant (must use HEPA vacuum)
- No interlock between power supply and ventilation system = engineering control gap
- Worker handling TGIC powder without gloves or respiratory protection = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Electrostatic Spray Equipment — Earthing Requirements
Section: s3.6, s4.2; AS/NZS 1020
Topic: High-voltage earthing and static electricity controls (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

ELECTROSTATIC VOLTAGE:
- Droplets charged to AT LEAST 60 KILOVOLTS

EARTHING REQUIREMENTS — ALL of the following within 3 METRES of charged spray gun head MUST BE EARTHED:
- All equipment and metal surfaces within 3 METRES of spray gun head
- FLOOR OF THE EXCLUSION ZONE (must be electrically conducting)
- METAL HOUSING AND HANDLE of spray gun

ANTI-STATIC FOOTWEAR — recommended resistance range:
- NOT LESS THAN 7.5 x 10⁴ OHMS
- NOT GREATER THAN 10 x 10⁷ OHMS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Dirty or paint-covered hooks suspending objects = earthing failure (insulating paint layer breaks earth connection)
- Worker wearing synthetic or untreated wool clothing near electrostatic equipment = static discharge risk
- Non-conducting floor surface in electrostatic spray area = earthing failure
- No anti-static footwear worn by operators = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Respiratory Protection — Isocyanate Spraying Requirements
Section: s3.10; Appendix C
Topic: Mandatory air-supplied respirators for isocyanate spray painting

RESPIRATORY PROTECTION:
- AIR-SUPPLIED RESPIRATOR: MANDATORY when spray painting using ISOCYANATES within any enclosed extraction booth
- Half-face P2 respirator is NOT SUFFICIENT and is life-threatening non-compliance
- Recommended for two-part polyurethane, two-part epoxy, and catalytic acrylic paints

PPE DECONTAMINATION:
- Disposable overalls MUST NOT be taken home — must be disposed of at workplace

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing only half-face respirator for isocyanate spraying in booth = life-threatening non-compliance
- Disposable overalls being taken home by worker = contamination breach
- No eye protection worn during spray painting = non-compliant
- Air-supplied respirator hose kinked or disconnected during spraying = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Paint Injection Injury Prevention — Gun Cleaning Prohibition
Section: s3.10
Topic: High-pressure paint gun cleaning safety

PAINT INJECTION INJURY PREVENTION:
- Pressurised paint pots and guns MUST HAVE PRESSURE RELEASED PRIOR TO CLEANING
- PROHIBITION: Gun MUST NEVER be cleaned by covering nozzle with cloth or material held in hand
- Risk: High-pressure paint can penetrate skin and cause serious internal damage

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker covering spray gun nozzle with hand or cloth while cleaning = injection injury risk
- Pressure gauge on paint pot showing pressure while worker attempts to clean gun = serious non-compliance
- Worker pointing pressurised gun at body or hand = injection injury risk

CHUNK 8: Fire and Explosion Controls — Flammable Liquid Handling
Section: s3.4; WHS Regulation s51–53
Topic: Static ignition prevention and flammable atmosphere limits (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

HAZARDOUS ATMOSPHERE DEFINITION:
- Concentration of flammable gas, vapour, mist or fume EXCEEDS FIVE PER CENT OF LOWER EXPLOSIVE LIMIT (LEL)

FLAMMABLE LIQUID TRANSFER:
- Before pouring: SET BOTH CONTAINERS DOWN ON EARTHED SURFACE and keep in contact while pouring (prevents static ignition)

WASTE MANAGEMENT:
- Solvent-soaked rags SHOULD BE STORED WET in closed metal container

IGNITION SOURCE PROHIBITION:
- No lit cigarettes, open flames, or ignition sources in or near spray painting areas
- Only intrinsically safe (explosion-proof) electrical equipment within hazardous zone

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Containers of flammable liquid in direct sunlight = fire hazard from thermal expansion
- Lids left off paint or solvent containers when not in use = vapour accumulation risk
- Lit cigarette or open flame in spray painting workshop = critical safety breach
- Solvent-soaked rags in open bin or on floor = fire risk
- Standard (non-intrinsically safe) electrical equipment within exclusion zone = explosion risk
- Pouring flammable liquids without earthing both containers = static ignition risk
