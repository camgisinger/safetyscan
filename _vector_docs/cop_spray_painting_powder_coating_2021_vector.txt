CHUNK 1: Definitions and Primary Duties
Section: s1.1, s1.2; WHS Act s19
Topic: Legislative scope and core safety obligations

SPRAY PAINTING means a process by which liquid paint is applied under pressure to an object, including conventional compressed air, airless spray painting, and electrostatic spray painting.

POWDER COATING means a process by which electrostatically charged powder is applied onto an earthed object.

PRIMARY DUTY: A PCBU must ELIMINATE risks arising from spray painting or powder coating, or if not reasonably practicable, MINIMISE the risks so far as is reasonably practicable.

DUTY HOLDERS:
- Designers/Manufacturers/Suppliers: Must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that plant or substances are without risks to health and safety, including carrying out testing and providing specific information
- Workers: Must take reasonable care for their own safety and comply with any reasonable instructions

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers performing spray painting without a designated booth or exclusion zone = duty breach
- Worker describing two-pack polyurethane paint application as "minor touch-up" to avoid controls = non-compliant risk assessment
- No SDS available for spray paint products being used = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: Prohibited and Restricted Chemicals
Section: s3.1; Schedule 10
Topic: Mandatory bans on specific hazardous substances for spray painting

PROHIBITED FOR SPRAY PAINTING — a PCBU MUST NOT use, handle or store these chemicals for spray painting:
- Arsenic and its compounds
- Benzene (benzol): if the substance contains MORE THAN 1 PER CENT BY VOLUME
- Carbon disulphide (carbon bisulphide)
- Lead carbonate
- Methanol (methyl alcohol): if the substance contains MORE THAN 1 PER CENT BY VOLUME
- Tetrachloroethane and Tetrachloromethane (carbon tetrachloride)
- Tributyl tin

ISOCYANATES: Commonly found in two-pack polyurethane paints. Exposure SHOULD BE CONSIDERED AS HIGH RISK. Full air-supplied respiratory protection is mandatory when spraying isocyanates in any enclosed booth.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- SDS for a spray paint showing Benzene content >1% by volume = illegal/prohibited
- SDS showing Methanol content >1% by volume = prohibited for spray painting
- Two-pack polyurethane spray painting without air-supplied respirator = life-threatening non-compliance
- Sanding of polyurethane paints that are not fully cured = high risk of isocyanate exposure via dust

CHUNK 3: Spray Booths — Design and Ventilation
Section: s4.1; AS/NZS 4114
Topic: Mandatory engineering controls and airflow thresholds

BOOTH REQUIREMENT: A spray booth SHOULD BE USED when spray painting with a hazardous chemical, except for items that cannot fit in a booth or for minor touch-ups.

VENTILATION SPEEDS — minimum air movement the system must be capable of producing:
- Full DOWN-DRAUGHT BOOTH: 0.3 m/s
- ELECTROSTATIC SPRAYING: 0.4 m/s
- ANY OTHER BOOTH: 0.5 m/s

POST-PURGE CYCLE: Control systems should operate a MINIMUM FIVE-MINUTE POST-PURGE PERIOD following spraying before personnel re-enter.

PROHIBITIONS inside spray booths:
- Absence of an air filter medium inside the booth = PROHIBITED
- Use of absorbent materials (paper, cardboard, wood) on booth floors = fire and maintenance breach

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No air filter medium visible inside spray booth = prohibited — booth must not be used
- Worker positioned between the spray gun and the exhaust air outlet = dangerous positioning (should be beside or behind gun)
- Absorbent materials on booth floor = fire risk
- Booth ventilation system not running during spray operations = serious non-compliance
- Booth filters visibly clogged or saturated = reduced ventilation — must be replaced

CHUNK 4: Spraying Outside a Booth — Exclusion Zones
Section: s4.1; Appendix C
Topic: Mandatory distances for open-air and outdoor spraying

EXCLUSION ZONES — when spraying outside a booth, a zone must be designated:

GENERAL RULE (low risk open area):
- AT LEAST 6 METRES HORIZONTAL (ensuring 3 metres distance in all directions from sprayer)
- AT LEAST 2 METRES VERTICAL CLEARANCE above and below the point of application

OPEN SPRAY BOOTH (per AS 4114):
- AT LEAST 2 METRES extending in all directions from any opening or outlet

OUTDOOR SPRAYING (low risk):
- AT LEAST 3 METRES in all directions

ENTRY RESTRICTION: People other than the sprayer MUST NOT ENTER the zone unless they wear EQUIVALENT PPE to the sprayer.

MANDATORY SIGNAGE: "SPRAY PAINTING AREA — AUTHORISED PERSONNEL ONLY" must be displayed at the exclusion boundary.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No exclusion zone signage at boundary of spray painting area = non-compliant
- Electrical equipment not certified for explosive atmospheres located within the exclusion zone = fire/explosion risk
- Bystanders within 3 metres of outdoor spraying without PPE = non-compliant
- No clear demarcation of exclusion zone boundary = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Powder Coating Controls
Section: s4.2; AS 3754
Topic: TGIC hazard, electrostatic risks, and cleaning requirements

TGIC (TRIGLYCIDYL ISOCYANURATE) HAZARD:
- Toxic by ingestion and inhalation
- A skin sensitiser
- Commonly found in powder coating formulations

ENGINEERING CONTROLS:
- Interlocks: PCBUs should interlock the power supply and powder coating feed lines with the air extraction system so that if ventilation fails, supplies are cut off automatically
- Cleaning: Use a vacuum cleaner with a HEPA FILTER only
- DO NOT use compressed air or dry sweeping to clean powder dust

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using compressed air to blow powder dust off clothing = serious health and explosion risk
- Dry sweeping of powder coating residue = non-compliant (must use HEPA vacuum)
- No interlock between power supply and ventilation system = engineering control gap
- Worker handling TGIC powder without gloves or respiratory protection = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Electrical Safety and Static Electricity
Section: s3.6, s4.2; AS/NZS 1020
Topic: Managing high-voltage and static ignition risks

ELECTROSTATIC VOLTAGE: Droplets are charged to a high voltage of AT LEAST 60 KILOVOLTS.

EARTHING REQUIREMENTS — ALL of the following within 3 metres of the charged head of the spray gun MUST BE EARTHED:
- All equipment and metal surfaces within 3 METRES of the spray gun head
- The FLOOR OF THE EXCLUSION ZONE (must be electrically conducting)
- The METAL HOUSING AND HANDLE of the spray gun

ANTI-STATIC FOOTWEAR — recommended resistance range:
- NOT LESS THAN 7.5 x 10⁴ OHMS
- NOT GREATER THAN 10 x 10⁷ OHMS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Dirty or paint-covered hooks used to suspend objects being coated = earthing failure (insulating layer of paint breaks earth connection)
- Worker wearing synthetic or untreated wool clothing near electrostatic equipment = static discharge risk
- Non-conducting floor surface in electrostatic spray area = earthing failure
- No anti-static footwear being worn by operators = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
Section: s3.10; Appendix C
Topic: Mandatory PPE specifications for hazardous paint systems

RESPIRATORY PROTECTION:
- AIR-SUPPLIED RESPIRATOR: MANDATORY when spray painting using ISOCYANATES within any enclosed extraction booth — a half-face P2 respirator is NOT SUFFICIENT and is life-threatening non-compliance
- Recommended for two-part polyurethane, two-part epoxy, and catalytic acrylic paints

PAINT INJECTION INJURY PREVENTION:
- Pressurised paint pots and guns MUST HAVE PRESSURE RELEASED PRIOR TO CLEANING
- PROHIBITION: The gun MUST NEVER be cleaned by covering the nozzle with a cloth or material held in the hand — risk of PAINT INJECTION INJURY (high-pressure paint can penetrate skin and cause serious internal damage)

PPE DECONTAMINATION:
- Disposable overalls must NOT be taken home — must be disposed of at the workplace

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker wearing only a half-face respirator for isocyanate spraying in a booth = life-threatening non-compliance
- Worker covering spray gun nozzle with hand or cloth while cleaning = injection injury risk
- Disposable overalls being taken home by a worker = contamination breach
- No eye protection worn during spray painting = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Fire and Explosion Management
Section: s3.4; WHS Regulation s51–53
Topic: Accumulation limits, ignition control, and flammable liquid handling

HAZARDOUS ATMOSPHERE DEFINITION: An atmosphere where the concentration of flammable gas, vapour, mist or fume EXCEEDS FIVE PER CENT OF THE LOWER EXPLOSIVE LIMIT (LEL).

FLAMMABLE LIQUID TRANSFER:
- Before pouring flammable liquids: SET BOTH CONTAINERS DOWN ON AN EARTHED SURFACE and keep them in contact while pouring (prevents static ignition)

WASTE MANAGEMENT:
- Solvent-soaked rags SHOULD BE STORED WET and safely contained after use in a closed metal container

IGNITION SOURCE PROHIBITION:
- No lit cigarettes, open flames, or ignition sources in or near spray painting areas
- Only intrinsically safe (explosion-proof) electrical equipment within the hazardous zone

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Containers of flammable liquid left in direct sunlight = fire hazard from thermal expansion and vapour generation
- Lids left off paint or solvent containers when not in use = vapour accumulation risk
- Lit cigarette or open flame in a spray painting workshop = critical safety breach
- Solvent-soaked rags in an open bin or on the floor = fire risk
- Standard (non-intrinsically safe) electrical equipment within the exclusion zone = explosion risk
- Pouring flammable liquids from one container to another without earthing both = static ignition risk
