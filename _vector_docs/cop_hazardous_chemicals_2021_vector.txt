CHUNK 1: Chemical Container Labelling — Decanted Chemicals
Section: WHS Regulation s335, s338, s344; CoP s2.2, s2.3
Topic: Label requirements for hazardous chemical containers and decanted substances

MANDATORY LABELLING:
- All hazardous chemical containers MUST be correctly labelled
- Pipe work containing hazardous chemicals MUST be labelled with signs, colour coding, or schematic layouts

DECANTED CHEMICAL EXEMPTION (label not required ONLY if ALL apply):
- Chemical used IMMEDIATELY
- NOT left unattended by person who decanted it
- Used ONLY by person present at decanting
- Container rendered FREE OF CHEMICALS immediately after use

UNLABELLED CONTAINERS:
- Must be marked "CAUTION — DO NOT USE: UNKNOWN SUBSTANCE"
- Must be stored in isolation until identified

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unlabelled container in active use = non-compliant
- Decanted chemical left unattended without label = non-compliant
- Hazardous chemical pipes without signs, colour coding, or schematic = non-compliant
- Container marked "CAUTION — DO NOT USE" not isolated from work area = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: Oxidising Agent Separation Distances
Section: WHS Regulation s355, s356; CoP s3.4, s4.2
Topic: Mandatory separation distances for oxidising chemicals and incompatible materials

MINIMUM SEPARATION DISTANCES:
- Oxidising agents: AT LEAST 5 METRES from combustible materials
- Solid pool chlorine: AT LEAST 10 METRES from ammonium salts
- Organic peroxides (cabinet/store): 5m safety zone opposite cabinet doors and blow-out panels
- Organic peroxides (ignition sources): eliminate ignition sources within 3m of storage area

INCOMPATIBLE STORAGE:
- Acids and alkalis MUST NOT share same bund
- Oxidising and flammable gases MUST NOT be stored together

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Oxidising agent within 5m of combustible materials (wood, paper, cardboard) = non-compliant
- Pool chlorine within 10m of fertiliser or ammonium products = non-compliant
- Weathered wooden pallets used to store oxidising chemicals = incompatible material
- Acids and alkalis in same spill bund = non-compliant containment
- Oxidising and flammable gas cylinders stored together = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Gas Cylinder Storage and Securing
Section: CoP s3.4, s4.2; AS 2030.1
Topic: Physical requirements for compressed gas cylinder storage and protection

MANDATORY CONFIGURATION:
- Cylinders MUST be stored UPRIGHT
- Cylinders MUST be SECURED (chained or strapped to wall/rack) to prevent dislodgement
- Empty or used cylinders treated with SAME PRECAUTIONS as full cylinders
- Cylinders MUST be protected from vehicle impact by bollards or barriers
- Cylinder valves MUST NOT be left open when not in use

LOCATION:
- Store OUTDOORS OR IN VERY WELL-VENTILATED AREAS
- NOT in unventilated rooms or enclosed spaces

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gas cylinders stored horizontally = non-compliant
- Cylinders not secured (no chains or straps visible) = non-compliant
- Cylinders stored near heat sources = non-compliant
- No bollards or barriers protecting cylinders from vehicle traffic = impact risk
- Cylinder valves left open when not in use = non-compliant
- Cylinders in enclosed room without ventilation = asphyxiation risk
- Visible gas leakage from cylinder connections = immediate hazard

CHUNK 4: Hot Work Permits Near Flammable Chemicals
Section: WHS Regulation s355, s356, s361; CoP s3.4, s4.2
Topic: Ignition control requirements in areas with flammable chemical hazards

MANDATORY CONTROLS:
- Hot work (welding, grinding, cutting) MUST be controlled via PERMIT SYSTEMS in areas where flammable chemicals are present
- Ignition sources MUST NOT be introduced into hazardous areas
- Only INTRINSICALLY SAFE electrical equipment permitted in flammable atmospheres

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Welding or grinding occurring near chemical storage without hot work permit = non-compliant
- Standard electrical equipment (fans, stirrers, switches) in flammable liquid storage areas = major fire risk
- Non-intrinsically safe equipment in areas with flammable vapours = non-compliant
- Explosion vents or blow-out panels blocked or obstructed = engineering control failure
- Open flames or spark-producing tools near flammable chemical storage = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Spill Containment Bunding
Section: WHS Regulation s36, s355, s357; CoP s4.1, s4.2
Topic: Physical spill containment system requirements

MANDATORY BUNDING:
- System MUST CONTAIN any spill or leak and resulting effluent WITHIN THE WORKPLACE
- Bunding MUST be compatible with chemicals stored within it
- Incompatible chemicals MUST NOT share same bund (e.g. acids and alkalis separate)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No bunding around chemical storage areas = spill containment failure
- Acids and alkalis stored in same bund = non-compliant
- Bund material visibly degraded or corroded by stored chemicals = incompatible material
- Bund drain valve left open = containment failure
- Cracks or gaps visible in bund walls = containment failure
- Bund filled with rainwater reducing containment capacity = maintenance failure

CHUNK 6: Local Exhaust Ventilation (LEV) Systems
Section: WHS Regulation s36, s355; CoP s4.1, s4.2
Topic: Engineering controls for chemical vapour and dust capture

LEV REQUIREMENTS:
- Must capture chemical vapours, fumes, or dusts at source
- Ductwork must be intact and connected
- System must be operating when chemicals in use

HIERARCHY VIOLATION:
- Reliance on respiratory PPE when LEV is practicable = non-compliant hierarchy application

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- LEV ductwork visibly damaged, disconnected, or with gaps = engineering control failure
- LEV system not operating during chemical use = control failure
- Workers wearing dust masks while LEV system sits idle = hierarchy violation
- Chemical vapours or fumes visibly escaping work area = inadequate capture
- LEV hood positioned too far from emission source to be effective = poor design/setup

CHUNK 7: Chemical Container Lids and Vapour Control
Section: WHS Regulation s355; CoP s4.2
Topic: Container closure requirements to prevent vapour release and spills

MANDATORY PRACTICE:
- Lids of chemical containers MUST be closed when not in active use
- Containers must not be left open unattended

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Chemical container lids left open while not in use = vapour and spill hazard
- Multiple open containers on work bench = non-compliant practice
- Solvent containers without lids in work area = vapour exposure risk
- Flammable liquid containers left uncapped = fire and vapour hazard

CHUNK 8: Eating, Drinking, Smoking in Chemical Areas
Section: WHS Act s19; CoP s1.2
Topic: Prohibited activities in hazardous chemical handling areas

PROHIBITED:
- Workers MUST NOT eat, drink, or smoke in areas where hazardous chemicals are used or stored

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers eating or drinking in chemical handling areas = non-compliant administrative control
- Food or drink containers on work benches with chemicals = contamination risk
- Smoking in areas with flammable chemicals = serious fire risk
- No signage prohibiting eating/drinking in chemical areas = control deficiency

---

CHUNK 9: Hand-Washing and Decontamination Facilities
Section: CoP s5.1
Topic: Hygiene facilities for workers handling hazardous chemicals

MANDATORY FACILITIES:
- Hand-washing facilities MUST be provided near chemical use areas
- Decontamination facilities required for high-risk chemical exposure

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No hand-washing facilities visible near chemical handling areas = hygiene control failure
- No emergency eyewash station near corrosive chemical use = inadequate emergency response
- Hand-washing facilities present but not accessible (blocked, locked) = access failure
- No soap or towels at hand-washing station = inadequate facility maintenance

CHUNK 10: Organic Peroxide Storage Safety Zones
Section: WHS Regulation s355, s356; CoP s3.4, s4.2
Topic: Clearance zones for organic peroxide storage cabinets and blow-out panels

MANDATORY SAFETY ZONES:
- 5 METRE safety zone opposite cabinet doors and blow-out panels
- Eliminate ignition sources within 3 METRES of storage area

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Equipment, materials, or work activities within 5m of organic peroxide cabinet doors = non-compliant clearance
- Ignition sources (electrical equipment, hot work) within 3m of organic peroxide storage = serious fire/explosion risk
- Blow-out panels obstructed by stored materials = engineering control failure
- No clear zone markings on floor = control deficiency

---

CHUNK 11: Cylinder Valve Protection and Damage
Section: AS 2030.1; CoP s3.4, s4.2
Topic: Physical protection requirements for gas cylinder valves and fittings

MANDATORY PROTECTION:
- Cylinder valves MUST be protected from impact damage
- Protective caps MUST be fitted when cylinders not in use
- Damaged cylinders MUST be removed from service

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gas cylinders without protective valve caps = non-compliant
- Visible damage to cylinder valves or fittings = immediate hazard
- Cylinders with dents, gouges, or corrosion = potential structural failure
- Cylinder valve threads visibly damaged = non-compliant equipment
- Leaking valve connections = immediate maintenance required

CHUNK 12: Intrinsically Safe Equipment Marking
Section: WHS Regulation s355, s361; CoP s4.2; AS/NZS 60079
Topic: Identification of explosion-proof electrical equipment in hazardous areas

REQUIRED MARKING:
- Intrinsically safe equipment MUST be marked with certification marking
- Equipment must display "Ex" marking and zone rating

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Electrical equipment in flammable atmosphere areas without "Ex" marking = non-compliant equipment
- Standard (non-certified) electrical switches, lights, or motors in hazardous zones = major explosion risk
- Certification plate missing or illegible on equipment in flammable areas = verification failure
- Extension cords or portable tools without intrinsic safety rating used in hazardous zones = serious non-compliance
