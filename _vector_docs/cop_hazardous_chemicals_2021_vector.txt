CHUNK 1: Primary Duties and Exclusions
Section: WHS Act s19, s20; WHS Regulation s328; COP s1.2
Topic: Legal framework and scope of chemical management duties

PRIMARY DUTY: A PCBU must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that the health and safety of workers and other persons are not put at risk from hazardous chemicals. This includes ensuring the SAFE USE, HANDLING AND STORAGE of substances. Must ELIMINATE risks, or if not reasonably practicable, MINIMISE the risks so far as is reasonably practicable.

EXCLUSIONS — WHS Regulation duties do NOT apply to:
- Dangerous goods or explosives being transported (regulated under transport laws)
- Batteries incorporated in plant
- Potable liquids that are consumer products at retail premises
- Food and beverages packaged for human consumption
- Tobacco or tobacco products
- Therapeutic goods at the point of intentional human intake
- Veterinary chemical products at the point of intentional administration to animals

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Toxic fumes generated by welding or metal grinding without capture systems = unmanaged process risk
- Workers eating, drinking, or smoking in areas where hazardous chemicals are used = non-compliant administrative control
- Standard household cleaning chemicals used in industrial quantities without an SDS = duty applies at industrial scale
- No hazard controls visible in chemical handling areas = PCBU duty breach

CHUNK 2: Chemical Register and Manifest
Section: WHS Regulation s346, s347, Schedule 11; COP s1.6
Topic: Mandatory documentation for chemical inventories

HAZARDOUS CHEMICALS REGISTER: A PCBU must ensure a register is PREPARED AND KEPT UP TO DATE containing:
- A list of all hazardous chemical product names
- The CURRENT SDS for each chemical

ACCESS: Must be READILY ACCESSIBLE to workers involved and anyone else likely to be affected.

MANIFEST OF SCHEDULE 11 CHEMICALS: Required when prescribed Schedule 11 MANIFEST QUANTITIES ARE EXCEEDED. Must include:
- Site plans showing storage locations
- Emergency contacts
- Detailed information on chemical quantity, classification, and location for emergency services

NOTIFICATION: If manifest quantities are exceeded, the PCBU MUST NOTIFY THE REGULATOR.

ILLUSTRATIVE THRESHOLD: Bulk storage of flammable liquids (Category 1-3) exceeding 10,000L may trigger manifest requirements (refer Schedule 11 for full threshold table).

SDS MAINTENANCE: SDS documents must be CURRENT — review and replace any SDS more than 5 years old.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- PCBU unable to produce a site plan identifying chemical storage locations = manifest deficiency
- Register containing SDS documents dated more than 5 years ago = non-compliant maintenance
- Chemical register not readily accessible to workers at the point of use = access failure
- No chemical register on site = serious non-compliance
- Chemicals stored on site not listed in the register = incomplete register

CHUNK 3: Safety Data Sheets (SDS) and Labelling
Section: WHS Regulation s335, s338, s344; COP s2.2, s2.3
Topic: Requirements for hazard information and access

SDS ACCESS: A PCBU must ensure the CURRENT SDS is readily accessible to:
- Workers using or likely to be exposed to the chemical
- Emergency service workers
- Anyone else likely to be affected
The SDS MUST be obtained BEFORE the chemical is first used at the workplace.

LABELLING: A PCBU must ensure hazardous chemicals, their containers, and pipe work are CORRECTLY LABELLED (refer Labelling of Workplace Hazardous Chemicals CoP 2021 for full label specifications).

DECANTED CHEMICALS: Labels not required ONLY if the chemical is used IMMEDIATELY meaning:
- NOT left unattended by the person who decanted it
- Used ONLY by a person present at the decanting
- Container rendered FREE OF CHEMICALS immediately after use

UNLABELLED CONTAINERS: Must be clearly marked "CAUTION — DO NOT USE: UNKNOWN SUBSTANCE" and stored in isolation until identified.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hazardous chemical pipes without signs, colour coding, or nearby schematic layouts = regulatory breach
- Electronic SDS database inaccessible (e.g. network failure) without hard-copy backup on site = access failure
- Unlabelled container in active use without a caution tag = life-safety hazard
- Decanted chemical left unattended on a bench without a label = non-compliant

CHUNK 4: Risk Assessment and Review Triggers
Section: WHS Regulation s38, s352; COP s3.1, s3.2, s5.2
Topic: Mandatory triggers for reassessing chemical hazards

MANDATORY RISK ASSESSMENTS required for:
- Entry into CONFINED SPACES
- DIVING WORK
- LIVE ELECTRICAL WORK
- Any work with carcinogens, reproductive hazards, or other high-risk chemicals

CONTROL MEASURES MUST BE REVIEWED AND REVISED when:
- The control measure is NOT EFFECTIVE
- BEFORE A CHANGE at the workplace that may give rise to new or different risks
- If an SDS OR REGISTER OF HAZARDOUS CHEMICALS IS CHANGED
- If a HEALTH MONITORING REPORT shows a worker has elevated chemical metabolites
- AT LEAST ONCE EVERY FIVE YEARS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Generic risk assessment used for high-risk chemicals such as carcinogens = improper assessment level
- Chemical spills or near-misses recurring without an updated risk assessment = review failure
- Workplace changes (new equipment, changed job roles) without revised controls = procedural breach
- Risk assessment not reviewed in over 5 years = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: Hierarchy of Control — Chemical-Specific Measures
Section: WHS Regulation s36, s355, s357; COP s4.1, s4.2
Topic: Priority order for managing hazardous chemicals

HIERARCHY OF CONTROL (most to least effective):
1. ELIMINATION: Remove the hazard entirely (e.g. use nails instead of adhesive, use pre-cut materials to avoid chemical processes)
2. SUBSTITUTION: Replace with a less hazardous chemical (e.g. aqueous paints instead of solvent-based, water-based cleaning agents instead of hydrocarbon solvents)
3. ISOLATION: Separate people from chemicals (e.g. glove boxes, enclosed process cabinets, remotely operated dispensing systems)
4. ENGINEERING CONTROLS: Physical devices including Local Exhaust Ventilation (LEV), enclosures, intrinsically safe electrical equipment, bunding
5. ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS: Procedures and rules (e.g. exclusion zones, written spill procedures, permit systems for hot work)
6. PPE: Last resort or interim protection only

SPILL CONTAINMENT: A PCBU must ensure provision is made for a system that CONTAINS ANY SPILL OR LEAK and resulting effluent WITHIN THE WORKPLACE. Bunding must:
- Be compatible with the chemicals stored within it
- Not contain incompatible chemicals together (e.g. acids and alkalis must not share the same bund)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Reliance on dust masks when engineering controls like LEV are practicable = hierarchy violation
- Spill bunding containing incompatible chemicals (e.g. acids and alkalis stored together) = containment failure
- Lids of chemical containers left open while not in use = vapour and spill hazard
- No bunding around chemical storage areas = spill containment failure
- LEV ductwork visibly damaged or disconnected = engineering control failure

CHUNK 6: Fire, Explosion, and Chemical Reaction Hazards
Section: WHS Regulation s355, s356, s361; COP s3.4, s4.2
Topic: Preventing catastrophic chemical events

IGNITION CONTROL: A PCBU must ensure an ignition source is NOT INTRODUCED into a hazardous area.
- HOT WORK (welding, grinding, cutting) MUST be controlled via PERMIT SYSTEMS in areas where flammable chemicals are present
- Only INTRINSICALLY SAFE electrical equipment may be used in flammable atmospheres

STABILITY: A PCBU must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that hazardous chemicals DO NOT BECOME UNSTABLE, DECOMPOSE, OR CHANGE so as to create a different hazard or significantly increase risk.

MANDATORY SEPARATION DISTANCES:
- Oxidising agents: At least 5m from combustible materials
- Solid (dry) pool chlorine: At least 10m from ammonium salts
- Organic peroxides (cabinet/store): Safety zone of 5m opposite cabinet doors and blow-out panels
- Organic peroxides (ignition sources): Eliminate ignition sources within 3m of storage area

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Non-intrinsically safe electrical equipment (standard fans, stirrers) in flammable liquid storage areas = major fire risk
- Weathered wooden pallets used to store oxidising chemicals = incompatible material (can ignite on contact)
- Explosion vents or blow-out panels on a chemical store blocked or obstructed = engineering control failure
- Hot work (welding/grinding) occurring near chemical storage without a hot work permit = non-compliant
- Oxidising agent stored within 5m of combustible materials = non-compliant separation distance
- Pool chlorine stored within 10m of fertiliser or ammonium-based products = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Compressed Gases and Asphyxiants
Section: COP s3.4, s4.2; AS 2030.1
Topic: Safe handling of pressurised and oxygen-displacing gases

GAS CYLINDER STORAGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Store in an UPRIGHT POSITION and SECURED to prevent dislodgement (chained or strapped to wall/rack)
- Store OUTDOORS OR IN VERY WELL-VENTILATED AREAS
- Empty or used cylinders MUST BE TREATED WITH THE SAME PRECAUTIONS as full cylinders
- Cylinders must be protected from vehicles by bollards or barriers
- Cylinder valves must NOT be left open when not in use

ASPHYXIATION HAZARD: Asphyxia can occur from:
- OXYGEN DEPLETION: From fuel burning, rusting, or biological processes consuming oxygen
- DISPLACEMENT: High concentration of inert gases (CO2, nitrogen, argon) pushing out oxygen

OXYGEN THRESHOLD: Avoid working in atmospheres where oxygen is UNDER 19% (the confined space CoP sets a more stringent standard of 19.5% — use the more stringent threshold).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gas cylinders stored near heat sources or without bollard protection from vehicle impact = impact risk
- Distinctive odours (e.g. "rotten eggs" for H2S) or visible gas leakage = immediate maintenance required
- Cylinder valves left open when not in use = regulatory breach
- Cylinders stored horizontally without blocking = non-compliant — must be upright and secured
- Cylinders stored in an unventilated room or enclosed space = asphyxiation risk
- Mixed storage of incompatible gases (e.g. oxidising and flammable gases) = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Health Monitoring and Record Keeping
Section: WHS Regulation s368, Schedule 14; COP s5.1
Topic: Medical surveillance and document retention timeframes

MANDATORY HEALTH MONITORING required for workers carrying out ongoing work with Schedule 14 chemicals where there is a significant risk, including:
- Acrylonitrile
- Arsenic
- Benzene
- Cadmium
- Chromium (certain compounds)
- Isocyanates
- Mercury
- MOCA (4,4'-Methylene bis(2-chloroaniline))
- Organophosphate pesticides
- Crystalline silica

RECORD RETENTION:
- Health monitoring reports: MUST be kept as confidential records for AT LEAST 30 YEARS (40 years for asbestos)
- Air monitoring results: MUST be kept for 30 YEARS
- Training records: Must document who was trained, when, and on what subject

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker performing spray-painting with isocyanates reporting light-headedness or skin rashes = monitoring trigger event — review required
- PCBU unable to produce medical records for a worker exposed to lead 20 years ago = record retention failure (30-year requirement)
- No hand-washing or decontamination facilities near high-risk chemical use areas = hygiene control failure
- No training records for workers handling Schedule 14 chemicals = non-compliant documentation
