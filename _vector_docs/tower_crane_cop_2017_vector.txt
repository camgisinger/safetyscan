CHUNK 1: Design Registration Plate and Item Registration
Section: CoP s2.1; WHS Regulation s243–s245
Topic: Mandatory design and item registration for tower cranes; registration plate requirements

MANDATORY:
- ALL tower cranes (luffing, hammerhead, self-erecting) require DESIGN REGISTRATION before supply (WHS Regulation s243)
- ALL tower cranes require ITEM REGISTRATION before commissioning
- Design registration number must be ACCESSIBLE on site
- Manufacturer's plate must show: date of manufacture, serial number, design registration number

BOOM MODIFICATIONS:
- Boom extended beyond original registered configuration = NEW DESIGN REGISTRATION REQUIRED (WHS Regulation s244)
- Boom shortened with same basic construction = certificate continues; no new registration

TOWER CRANE BASE REGISTRATION:
- Tower crane base = part of crane; MUST BE DESIGN REGISTERED when new base designed
- Base design must be by an ENGINEER
- Detailed drawings showing crane base plus operating configuration required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No design registration number accessible on crane = serious non-compliance
- Boom extended beyond original registered length without new registration = serious non-compliance
- Crane base altered without engineering sign-off = non-compliance
- No manufacturer's plate or plate illegible = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: Crane Stability — Counterweights, Footings, Ties and Wind Limits
Section: CoP s2.2–s2.2.4; WHS Regulation
Topic: Stability requirements including counterweight securing, foundation design, crane ties and operational wind speeds

LOAD CHARTS:
- Must be FIXED IN OPERATOR'S CABIN in CLEARLY VISIBLE LOCATION
- Written in ENGLISH, METRIC UNITS
- Lifting capacities MUST NEVER BE EXCEEDED except during testing by competent person or emergency

COUNTERWEIGHTS:
- Must be SECURED TO CRANE per manufacturer's instructions
- Counterweight too light = risk of overturning toward load
- Counterweight too heavy = risk of falling over backwards

FOOTINGS AND FOUNDATIONS (CoP s2.2.2):
- Must be designed by ENGINEER per engineering principles or relevant technical standards
- Design must consider GEO-TECHNICAL INSPECTIONS specific to location

CRANE TIES (CoP s2.2.3):
- Must be secured to supporting structure at SET INTERVALS per manufacturer's instructions and designer of crane installation
- Design and detail VERIFIED BY ENGINEER
- Must account for structural adequacy and movement/deformation of supporting structure

WIND SPEED LIMITS — OPERATIONS (CoP s2.2.4):
- Traditional maximum operational wind speed (QLD): 54 KM/HOUR (15 m/s)
- Some installations designed for 72 KM/HOUR (20 m/s) per AS 1418.4
- ANEMOMETER (wind gauge) MANDATORY on each tower crane:
  - LUFFING cranes: on TOP OF A-FRAME
  - NON-LUFFING cranes: on A-FRAME or MACHINE DECK HAND-RAIL
- Anemometer must NOT be shielded from wind

WIND SPEED LIMITS — CLIMBING:
- Climbing operations must NOT PROCEED where wind speed EXCEEDS 36 KM/HOUR (10 m/s)
- Must not commence if wind speed UNKNOWN

OPERATING ABOVE 54 KM/HOUR:
- DOCUMENTED RISK ASSESSMENT required
- Only cranes DESIGNED TO OPERATE above 54 km/hr may be used
- Risk assessment must involve consultation between principal contractor, crane owner, operator and crew

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No anemometer fitted = non-compliance
- Anemometer shielded from wind = non-compliance
- Crane operating above 54 km/hr without documented risk assessment = serious non-compliance
- Climbing operations at wind speeds exceeding 36 km/hr = serious non-compliance
- Load chart not fixed in operator's cabin = non-compliance
- Counterweights not secured per manufacturer's instructions = serious non-compliance
- No geo-technical inspection for footing design = non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Rated Capacity Limiters and Motion Limiting Devices
Section: CoP s2.3.2–s2.3.3; AS 1418.4
Topic: Required limiting devices on tower cranes; rated capacity limiter specifications; motion limiters

RATED CAPACITY LIMITERS (CoP s2.3.2):
- MUST be provided on ALL TOWER CRANES regardless of age
- Prevents overloading by STOPPING ALL RELEVANT CRANE FUNCTIONS when overload detected
- Must prevent:
  - Hoisting load EXCEEDING 110% OF MAXIMUM RATED CAPACITY
  - Radius increase when load exceeds 100% at particular radius
- All tower cranes must have BOTH LOAD INDICATORS AND RATED CAPACITY LIMITERS

RELIABILITY STANDARDS (CoP s2.3.1):
- Safety circuits must meet CATEGORY 4 under AS 4024, OR
- SAFETY INTEGRITY LEVEL 3 under AS 62061

MOTION LIMITING DEVICES (CoP s2.3.3):
Must be fitted to prevent motion out of service limits; cause braking/stopping at:
- HIGHEST POSITION OF HOOK
- LOWEST POSITION OF HOOK (when persons lowered in workbox into shaft)
- EXTREME PERMISSIBLE OPERATING POSITIONS OF JIB (luff limiter on luffing cranes)
- END POSITIONS OF TROLLEY TRACK on jib
- END POSITIONS of horizontally telescoping or movable jibs
- END POSITIONS OF TRACKS for rail-mounted travelling tower cranes

DUAL BRAKING SYSTEMS (CoP s2.3.5):
- Required on LUFF FUNCTION of all ROPE LUFFING TOWER CRANES
- Also on any other function specified by manufacturer
- Must comply with AS 1418.1 or other relevant technical standards

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Rated capacity limiter absent or visibly disabled = serious non-compliance (applies regardless of crane age)
- No load indicator fitted = non-compliance
- Motion limiting devices (hook height limiters, trolley end stops) absent or disabled = serious non-compliance
- Dual braking system absent on rope luffing tower crane = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Working Radius Indicator
Section: CoP s2.3.4
Topic: Mandatory radius indicator specifications and accuracy requirements

MANDATORY:
- RADIUS INDICATOR must be fitted on ALL TOWER CRANES
- Displays radius of suspended load (measured from centre of slew ring)
- Must be displayed in METRES
- Accuracy: +10% and -3% of actual radius

REMOTE-CONTROLLED CRANES WITH HORIZONTAL JIB:
- Where crane is remote-controlled AND jib is horizontal AND fully visible to operator:
- Indicator may consist of ONE METRE GRADUATIONS marked on jib
- Numbers at appropriate intervals (e.g. every 5 metres)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No working radius indicator on tower crane = non-compliance
- Radius indicator not displaying in metres = non-compliance
- Graduations on jib exceeding 1 metre spacing = non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Signs on Tower Cranes — Restrictions and Requirements
Section: CoP s2.4
Topic: Prohibited and permitted signage locations; engineering certification requirements

GENERAL RULE:
- Signs must NOT be attached to tower crane BOOMS
- Reasons: affects slew speed; makes slew brake application hazardous; risk of signs detaching

PERMITTED SIGNS — MACHINE DECK ONLY:
- Signs may be used on MACHINE (REAR) DECKS only
- Must be CERTIFIED BY ENGINEER prior to installation
- Engineer must certify design and attachments are FIT FOR PURPOSE
- Must ensure MAINTENANCE ON SIGN NOT REQUIRED for crane installation life

FLEXIBLE SIGNS:
- Made of UV RESISTANT MATERIAL
- SECURELY ATTACHED using tying system that WITHSTANDS POTENTIAL WIND LOADINGS

SOLID SIGNS:
- Attached by BOLTED CONNECTIONS
- APPROPRIATE LOCKING NUTS on bolts required
- PROHIBITED: drilling holes into crane structure; welding joints onto crane structure; strapping and cable ties

WIND SAILS:
- May be mandated by manufacturers (e.g. short boom on hammerhead)
- Design engineer must make ALLOWANCES FOR SIZE AND WEIGHT OF SAIL in crane base drawing

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Signs attached to tower crane boom = non-compliance
- Signs on machine deck without engineer certification = non-compliance
- Flexible signs attached with strapping or cable ties = non-compliance
- Solid signs attached by welded or drilled connections = non-compliance
- Signs showing deterioration or risk of detachment = flag

CHUNK 6: Overhead Powerlines — Exclusion Zones and Control Measures
Section: CoP s4.1–s4.1.3; ES Act s30; ES Regulation s68–s69
Topic: Minimum clearance distances from powerlines; control hierarchy; safety observer requirements

MINIMUM SAFE DISTANCES (ES Regulation s68–s69):
- AT LEAST 3 METRES from overhead powerlines for voltages UP TO 132kV
- Greater distances for voltages above 132kV
- Applies to ANY PART OF CRANE including load being lifted
- Booms MUST NOT operate OVER THE TOP of energised powerlines

CONTROL HIERARCHY (in order of preference):
1. TURNING OFF POWER (de-energising) or RE-ROUTING electricity supply — must be considered FIRST
2. Setting up crane OUTSIDE EXCLUSION ZONE
3. PHYSICAL BARRIER of NON-CONDUCTIVE MATERIALS (may require isolating supply during barrier installation)
4. ENGINEERING CONTROLS (mechanical stops or constraints preventing crane entering exclusion zone)
5. ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS (warning signs, tiger tails, warning devices) — only when higher order not reasonably practicable

TIGER TAILS:
- Tiger tails or line markers are VISUAL AIDS ONLY
- DO NOT insulate wires
- Exclusion zone applies regardless

SAFETY OBSERVER (SPOTTER):
- Required when crane operates in SAFETY OBSERVER ZONE (where any part of crane or load could enter exclusion zone)
- Must be TRAINED AND COMPETENT
- Must NOT carry out OTHER TASKS (such as dogging) while observing
- Must be CLEARLY IDENTIFIABLE by all workers
- DOGGER MUST NOT also perform safety observer role near powerlines

SAFETY OBSERVER ZONE DOES NOT APPLY WHEN:
- Powerlines DE-ENERGISED and cannot be inadvertently re-energised (high voltage also EARTHED)
- LIMITING DEVICES installed preventing crane entering exclusion zone
- PHYSICAL BARRIERS prevent crane entering exclusion zone

WORKING AT CLOSER DISTANCES:
- If not reasonably practicable to maintain exclusion zone: RISK ASSESSMENT required
- Consultation with electricity entity required
- Persons must be AUTHORISED OR INSTRUCTED PERSON under ES Regulation
- All conditions specified by electricity entity must be met

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any part of crane or load within 3 metres of powerline (up to 132kV) without de-energising or documented risk assessment = serious non-compliance
- Tiger tails treated as insulation by crane crew = non-compliance
- No safety observer where crane operates in safety observer zone = serious non-compliance
- Safety observer also performing dogging duties = non-compliance
- Safety observer not clearly identifiable = non-compliance
- No consultation with electricity entity before setup near powerlines = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Collision with Concrete Booms and Adjacent Cranes
Section: CoP s4.2, s4.2.1
Topic: Collision prevention procedures; communication requirements; crane coordinator identification

DOCUMENTED PROCEDURE REQUIRED:
- SWMS or documented procedure must be established EARLY IN PLANNING where tower crane may collide with other plant
- Must identify PERSON RESPONSIBLE for implementation
- ALL PERSONS operating tower cranes and other plant must be AWARE OF AND TRAINED IN procedure

PROCEDURE MUST ADDRESS:
- SITING cranes to minimise other plant operating within crane's operating radius
- Siting cranes and plant with COUNTERWEIGHTS so they CANNOT COLLIDE during slewing
- METHOD OF COMMUNICATION between crane crew and other plant operators
- SCHEDULING OF WORK to minimise time crane and other plant work in same area or height
- TOWER CRANE CLIMBING PROCEDURE to keep crane as far above structures/plant as practicable
- FREQUENCY OF REGULAR MEETINGS to monitor and review control measures

AUDIBLE WARNING DEVICE:
- All tower cranes should be fitted with AUDIBLE WARNING DEVICE

ADJACENT SITES:
- When cranes share air space from ADJACENT WORKPLACES: principal contractor from EACH WORKPLACE must negotiate DOCUMENTED SYSTEMS OF WORK
- Must include NAME OF CRANE COORDINATOR from each site
- EFFECTIVE MEANS OF COMMUNICATION must be provided between crane operators on separate cranes

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Concrete placement boom operating within tower crane's radius without documented procedure = non-compliance
- No audible warning device on tower crane = non-compliance
- Adjacent site cranes sharing air space without documented agreement and crane coordinator identified = non-compliance
- Crane counterweights positioned where they could collide during slewing = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Minimum Clearance — Slung Loads Above Building Worksite
Section: CoP s3; AS 2550.4
Topic: Required clearance between bottom of slung load and highest extremity of building

MINIMUM CLEARANCE:
- AT LEAST 2 METRES between BOTTOM OF SLUNG LOAD and HIGHEST EXTREMITY OF BUILDING WORKSITE
- Required unless not reasonably practicable
- Purpose: minimise risk of crane load colliding with or striking building or persons

CLEARANCES BETWEEN CRANES AND PLACEMENT BOOMS:
- Sufficient clearances to minimise risk of contact between parts of cranes, crane loads and booms
- When cranes operate on ADJACENT SITES sharing air space: negotiations must formulate SYSTEMS OF WORK ensuring sufficient clearances

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Less than 2 metres clearance between slung load bottom and highest extremity of building without documented justification = non-compliance
- No coordination procedure where crane booms share air space with adjacent site cranes = non-compliance

CHUNK 9: Crane Siting — Aircraft Warning Lights and Access Areas
Section: CoP s3.3–s3.3.3
Topic: Requirements for cranes near aerodromes; aircraft warning lights; access area considerations

AERODROMES AND AIRCRAFT FLIGHT PATHS:
- Where tower cranes set up in flight paths: AERODROME OPERATOR must be contacted to ensure CASA requirements met (www.casa.gov.au)
- Where necessary: AIRCRAFT WARNING LIGHTS must be fitted to HIGHEST PART OF CRANE

ACCESS AREA CONSIDERATIONS:
- Location of COMMON ACCESS AREAS for workers and other persons
- Location of PUBLIC ACCESS AREAS (footpaths, roadways, railways) in vicinity of crane

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tower crane near aerodrome or aircraft flight path without aircraft warning lights on highest part = non-compliance
- Crane siting not accounting for collision hazards without documented plan = non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Communication Systems — Radio and Hand Signals
Section: CoP s3.4; AS 2550.1
Topic: Radio communication requirements; safety checks; prohibited use of mobile phones

RADIO COMMUNICATION:
- Common practice in tower crane industry
- DEDICATED RADIO FREQUENCY should be selected for duration of crane operations
- WORK MUST STOP IMMEDIATELY if LOSS OF RADIO COMMUNICATION

RADIO SAFETY CHECKS:
- Operator and dogger must perform checks ensuring radios are:
  - DRY
  - HANDLED WITH CARE
  - PERFORMING SATISFACTORILY
  - FULLY CHARGED BATTERY with SPARE AVAILABLE
- Adopt CONSTANT TALK METHOD between users
- Crane operator normally takes radio instructions from ONE PERSON ONLY

ALTERNATIVE COMMUNICATION:
- Where radio cannot be used: HAND, WHISTLE, BELL AND BUZZER SIGNALS per AS 2550.1

PROHIBITED:
- MOBILE PHONES must NOT be used to direct tower crane operations

DOGGER COMMUNICATION:
- ONLY ONE DOGGER should give visual and audible signals at any time
- When more than one dogger: each must understand when responsibility is handed over

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Mobile phone being used to direct tower crane operations = non-compliance
- Only one radio available with no spare battery = flag
- More than one dogger simultaneously giving signals = non-compliance
- Work continuing after loss of radio communication = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 11: Emergency Plans and Rescue Equipment
Section: CoP s3.5; WHS Regulation s43
Topic: Emergency plan requirements; rescue equipment accessibility; fire-fighting equipment

EMERGENCY PLAN MUST INCLUDE (WHS Regulation s43):
- EFFECTIVE RESPONSES AND EVACUATION procedures
- NOTIFYING EMERGENCY SERVICES
- MEDICAL TREATMENT
- Emergency procedure TRAINING for workers
- Contact numbers EASILY SEEN OR FOUND
- Systems to contact emergency services and HOW TO USE THEM

RESCUE AND EQUIPMENT:
- RESCUE EQUIPMENT must be available and EASILY ACCESSIBLE for quick removal of injured worker (including crane operator)
- FIRE-FIGHTING EQUIPMENT (fire extinguishers, fire blankets) must be in place and OPERATIONAL
- EVACUATION LOCATION SIGNS placed where easily seen

SCOPE:
- Must cover persons NEAR CRANE and persons OPERATING CRANE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No emergency plan prepared for workplace with tower crane = serious non-compliance
- Rescue equipment absent or not accessible = non-compliance
- Fire extinguisher absent from crane or workplace = non-compliance
- Emergency contact numbers not displayed in accessible location = non-compliance
- Workers unable to describe emergency procedures = flag

CHUNK 12: Erecting and Dismantling — Collapse Prevention and Wind Limits
Section: CoP s5.2.1; WHS Regulation s291, s299
Topic: Control measures to prevent crane collapse during erection and dismantling; wind limits; tower bolt requirements

SWMS REQUIREMENT (WHS Regulation s299):
- SWMS must be prepared for HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK of erecting or dismantling tower crane

PREVENTING COLLAPSE (CoP s5.2.1):
- Must NOT erect or dismantle in conditions EXCEEDING MANUFACTURER'S SPECIFICATIONS
- Must NOT erect or dismantle where wind may make components UNCONTROLLABLE when suspended
- WIND LOADING must be considered including FUNNELLING EFFECTS between buildings
- Erecting and dismantling must be SUPERVISED BY COMPETENT PERSON
- Manufacturer's instructions must be followed for ASSEMBLY IN CORRECT SEQUENCE using CORRECT EQUIPMENT AND TOOLS

TOWER SECTIONS:
- Must be CLEARLY AND PERMANENTLY IDENTIFIED with MODEL TYPE AND SERIAL NUMBER
- Tower sections used must be SAME AS SPECIFIED ON ENGINEER'S CRANE BASE DRAWING

TOWER BOLT REQUIREMENTS:
- Use only CORRECT TYPE AND GRADE of tower bolts
- Bolts and pins must be COMPATIBLE WITH CRANE COMPONENTS and not defective
- Tower bolts must be correctly TORQUED (over-tightening as dangerous as insufficient tightening)
- Crane ties installed per MANUFACTURER AND DESIGNER instructions

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Erection/dismantling without prepared SWMS = serious non-compliance
- Erection/dismantling in conditions exceeding manufacturer's wind specifications = serious non-compliance
- Erection/dismantling not supervised by competent person = serious non-compliance
- Tower sections without permanent model type and serial number identification = non-compliance
- Tower bolts not correctly torqued or incorrect grade = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 13: Climbing Operations — Exclusion Zones and Collapse Prevention
Section: CoP s5.2.2
Topic: Exclusion zone dimensions for climbing; prohibited activities during climbing; counterweight inspection

EXCLUSION ZONE FOR CLIMBING:
- Minimum radius: AT LEAST 12 METRES from base of crane where practicable
- Must be in place PRIOR TO INSTALLATION OF CRANE TIES
- Exclusion zone for crane tie installation may be REDUCED where justified by risk assessment

PROHIBITED DURING CLIMBING:
- EXCLUDE ALL UNNECESSARY PERSONS from workplace
- PROHIBIT PERSONS from area DIRECTLY BEHIND TOWER CRANE (under counterweights)
- AVOID SLEW OPERATIONS at ALL TIMES during climbing

PRE-CLIMBING INSPECTION:
- PHYSICAL INSPECTION of counterweight trolleys (side plates, bolting, pins, safety gear, ropes, turnbuckles) BEFORE COMMENCING climbing

CLIMBING SEQUENCE:
- All persons must receive THOROUGH TRAINING AND INSTRUCTION for SPECIFIC MODEL AND TYPE of crane
- Sequence carried out in STRICT ACCORDANCE with MANUFACTURER'S INSTRUCTIONS
- Must NOT attempt at WIND SPEEDS GREATER THAN 36 KM/HOUR
- Must not commence if wind speed UNKNOWN
- Rigging crew may cease at lesser wind speed at their discretion

CRANES WITH MOVING COUNTERWEIGHTS ON SLOPED RAILS (e.g. FAVCO 1500, FAVCO STD 1000):
- Counterweight positioned at TOP OF COUNTERWEIGHT RAIL during climbing
- Kept in position by LATCH locking onto lug on bottom of rail
- SECONDARY MEANS OF SECURING LATCH should be provided (e.g. rope or chain connecting latch lever to machine deck)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Climbing without exclusion zone of at least 12 metres = serious non-compliance
- Slewing operations during climbing = serious non-compliance
- Persons behind tower (under counterweights) during climbing = serious non-compliance
- Climbing at wind speeds exceeding 36 km/hr = serious non-compliance
- Persons not trained in specific model/type climbing procedure participating = serious non-compliance
- Exclusion zone not established before crane tie installation = non-compliance

CHUNK 14: Exclusion Zones — Erection, Dismantling and Falling Objects
Section: CoP s5.4.1; WHS Regulation s54–s55
Topic: Exclusion zone dimensions for erection/dismantling; public area closures; falling object controls

EXCLUSION ZONE — ERECTION AND DISMANTLING (CoP s5.4.1):
- Must be established BEFORE WORK STARTS
- Based on RISK ASSESSMENT; must include where practicable:
  - RADIUS OF AT LEAST 20 METRES from base of tower crane
  - FOOTPRINT RELATIVE TO SIZE OF BOOM
- Must be SUFFICIENT SIZE TO CONTAIN STRUCTURAL FAILURE where practicable
- Only persons IMMEDIATELY INVOLVED in erecting, climbing or dismantling permitted within zone

PUBLIC FOOTPATHS AND ROADWAYS:
- Where public footpaths/roadways form part of exclusion zone: PERMISSION TO CLOSE must be obtained from APPROPRIATE LOCAL AUTHORITY

FALLING OBJECT CONTROLS (WHS Regulation s54–s55):
- Must PREVENT OBJECTS FROM FALLING FREELY so far as reasonably practicable
- If prevention not reasonably practicable: provide SYSTEM TO ARREST FALL
- Examples: secure barriers; safe means of raising/lowering objects; exclusion zones

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No exclusion zone before erection or dismantling = serious non-compliance
- Exclusion zone radius less than 20 metres (erection/dismantling) without documented justification = non-compliance
- Public footpaths/roadways within exclusion zone without local authority approval for closure = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 15: Tool Lanyards and Restraining Systems for Components
Section: CoP s5.4.2, s5.4.4
Topic: Tool lanyard specifications; restraining systems for crane components during erection/dismantling

TOOL LANYARDS (CoP s5.4.2):
- SHORT ROPE OR WEBBING securing tools/equipment to ANCHORAGE POINT
- Anchorage: person using tool, column or beam
- Weight of tool must NOT impose ADDITIONAL RISKS to person
- Material: SYNTHETIC OR NATURAL FIBRE, STEEL ROPE OR WEBBING
- MAXIMUM LANYARD LENGTH attached at wrist: 300 MILLIMETRES (ensures dropped tool cannot hit person below)
- Lanyard length KEPT TO MINIMUM to reduce snagging risk

RESTRAINING SYSTEMS FOR CRANE COMPONENTS (CoP s5.4.4):
- Control measures must RESTRAIN individual crane components (e.g. packers, shims) from falling during erecting, dismantling, climbing
- Example: SHORT CHAIN attached to both crane component and crane structure

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tool lanyards wrist-attached exceeding 300 mm length = non-compliance
- Crane components (packers, shims) not restrained during climbing or dismantling = non-compliance

CHUNK 16: Fall Protection — Edge Protection, Travel Restraint and Fall-Arrest
Section: CoP s5.3–s5.3.3; WHS Regulation s291, s299, s306C–s306E, s306G, s306I
Topic: Fall prevention hierarchy; edge protection specifications; travel restraint and fall-arrest requirements

HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK (WHS Regulation s291):
- Risk of fall MORE THAN 2 METRES = high risk construction work
- Requires SWMS before work commences (WHS Regulation s299)

CONTROL HIERARCHY (in order of preference):
1. EDGE PROTECTION SYSTEMS
2. TRAVEL RESTRAINT SYSTEMS
3. FALL-ARREST HARNESS SYSTEMS (least preferred; must be supplemented with rescue procedure)

EDGE PROTECTION (WHS Regulation s306E):
- Must consist of RAILS, MESH, SHEETING OR OTHER MATERIAL designed to withstand forces if person fell against it
- Where possible: TOWER CRANE SECTIONS DESIGNED so edge protection already in place before installation
- Where impractical: other fall protection (workboxes, travel restraint) must be used

TRAVEL RESTRAINT SYSTEMS (WHS Regulation s306G):
- Must be installed by COMPETENT PERSON
- Anchorage point must withstand any load exerted on it
- PCBU must ensure AT LEAST EVERY 6 MONTHS: competent person INSPECTS system and provides WRITTEN RECORD
- Consists of harness/belt + lanyards attached to static line or anchorage point
- RESTRICTS TRAVELLING RANGE so person cannot get into position where they could fall
- PREFERRED OVER fall-arrest systems

FALL-ARREST HARNESS SYSTEMS (WHS Regulation s306I):
- Each anchorage point DESIGNED BY ENGINEER or INSPECTED AND APPROVED BY COMPETENT PERSON before first use
- Arrests fall — does NOT prevent it; LESS PREFERRED
- Where used: WRITTEN PROCEDURE describing how person can be SAFELY RETRIEVED after fall must exist
- FIRST AID BOX suitable for emergency retrieval must be READILY AVAILABLE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Persons working at height on tower crane without fall protection = serious non-compliance
- Edge protection absent on platforms, landings or machine decks = non-compliance
- Travel restraint system not inspected by competent person within past 6 months = non-compliance
- Fall-arrest harness system without documented rescue/retrieval procedure = non-compliance
- No first aid box on site where fall-arrest harness systems in use = non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Machine Deck and A-Frame Guardrails
Section: CoP s7.3.3, s7.3.4; AS 1657
Topic: Guardrail specifications for machine deck and A-frame; ladder cage dimensions

GUARDRAILS ON MACHINE DECK (CoP s7.3.3):
- All tower cranes should have PERIMETER EDGE PROTECTION around machine deck
- Must consist of:
  - TOP RAIL between 900 MM and 1100 MM HIGH
  - MID-RAIL
  - TOEBOARD AT LEAST 100 MM HIGH
- Must comply with AS 1657

A-FRAME LADDER CAGE (CoP s7.3.4):
- Must be provided on A-frame to confine falling person to land on machine deck
- LOWEST PART of ladder cage: between 2 METRES AND 2.2 METRES above lower deck
- HORIZONTAL SPACING between vertical bars: NOT EXCEED 150 MM
- MESH INFILL may be used instead of vertical bars

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Machine deck guardrail with top rail below 900 mm or above 1100 mm = non-compliance
- Machine deck missing mid-rail or toeboard (minimum 100 mm) = non-compliance
- A-frame ladder cage lowest bar less than 2 metres above lower deck = non-compliance
- A-frame ladder cage bar spacing exceeding 150 mm = non-compliance

CHUNK 18: Tower Ladders and Fall-Arrest Systems
Section: CoP s7.3.1; AS 1418.4; AS 1657
Topic: Tower ladder landing intervals; continuous vertical ladder fall-arrest requirements

TOWER LADDERS (CoP s7.3.1):
- Landings with changes in ladder direction should be provided where space permits
- Preferred: vertical distance between landings SHOULD NOT EXCEED 6 METRES
- Where manufacturer designed otherwise:
  - LOWEST LADDER in tower may be up to 12.5 METRES
  - SUBSEQUENT LADDERS may be up to 10 METRES per AS 1418.4

CONTINUOUS VERTICAL LADDERS:
- Use for total tower length NOT RECOMMENDED
- Where unavoidable: FALL-ARREST SYSTEM that does NOT require person to CONSTANTLY HOOK ON AND OFF must be provided
- System may incorporate VERTICAL RAIL OR ROPE with LOCKING CAM DEVICE
- MAXIMUM LANYARD LENGTH between person and vertical rail/rope: 300 MILLIMETRES (reduces potential fall distance)
- Any ladder fall-arrest system must comply with TYPE 1 FALL-ARREST DEVICES in AS/NZS 1891 Series
- Vertical ropes: material NOT PRONE TO UV DEGRADATION OR CORROSION (e.g. suitable grade stainless steel)
- FOLD-DOWN TYPE REST PLATFORMS NOT recommended (hinder rescue, increase fall-down-ladder risk)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No fall-arrest system on continuous vertical tower ladder = serious non-compliance
- Fall-arrest lanyard on vertical rail exceeding 300 mm length = non-compliance
- Fold-down rest platforms on continuous vertical ladder = flag

NON-LUFFING TOWER CRANES WITH TROLLEY PLATFORMS:

CHUNK 20: Leaving Cranes Unattended — Weathervaning and Tethering

BEFORE LEAVING CRANE UNATTENDED (WHS Regulation s207):
- All LOADS REMOVED from hook
- Hook RAISED to position safely CLEAR OF OTHER OPERATIONS
- All POWERED MOTIONS DISABLED
- KEYS REMOVED from crane
- ADEQUATE SYSTEMS to prevent UNAUTHORISED ACCESS to tower crane base

WEATHERVANING:
- Where no risk of boom contacting other structures: crane SHOULD BE LEFT TO WEATHERVANE per MANUFACTURER'S INSTRUCTIONS

TETHERING (RESTRICTING BOOM MOVEMENT):

VISUAL INDICATORS:

- Keys not removed from unattended tower crane = serious non-compliance
- No access restriction at tower crane base = non-compliance
- Boom tethered by means not specified or certified by engineer = non-compliance

CHUNK 21: High Risk Work Licences — Operators and Doggers
Section: CoP s7.1.3, s7.1.4; WHS Regulation s85, Schedule 3
Topic: Licence requirements for crane operators and doggers; operator and dogger restrictions

CRANE OPERATOR (CoP s7.1.3):
- Must hold HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE as specified under WHS Regulation Schedule 3
- If operator believes lift is DANGEROUS OR UNSAFE: must REFUSE TO PROCEED until concern reported, risks managed, safe conditions confirmed

CRANE OPERATOR MUST NOT:
- Leave cabin or control room while load is suspended — EXCEPT IN EMERGENCY

DOGGER (CoP s7.1.4):
- Must hold HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE for dogging work per WHS Regulation Schedule 3
- PRIMARY ROLE: assist crane operator in safe and efficient crane operation
- Includes: slinging techniques; selection and inspection of lifting gear; directing crane operator when load out of view
- Must be positioned to SAFELY OBSERVE ENTIRE LIFTING OPERATION they are responsible for
- MUST NOT also perform role of safety observer when crane near overhead powerlines
- Must have CONTROL OF LOAD from time hoisted until placed in position

PROHIBITED:
- Crane operator MUST NOT undertake own dogging work (on remote control tower crane)
- Crane operator MUST NOT supervise trainee dogger (even if operator is also licensed dogger)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane operator operating without high risk work licence = serious non-compliance
- Dogger working without high risk work licence = serious non-compliance
- Crane operator leaving cabin while load suspended (non-emergency) = serious non-compliance
- Dogger also acting as safety observer near powerlines = non-compliance
- Crane operator undertaking own dogging duties on remote control crane = non-compliance
- Crane operator supervising trainee dogger = non-compliance

PRE-OPERATIONAL INSPECTION (CoP s10.4):

Items to inspect and test:

CHUNK 23: Lifting Gear Inspection and Tagging
Section: CoP s7.2.1; AS 1353.2; AS 4497.2
Topic: Lifting gear inspection intervals; tagging requirements; basic inspection items

LIFTING GEAR INSPECTION INTERVALS:
- All lifting gear: PERIODICALLY INSPECTED by COMPETENT PERSON
- Maximum period: NOT EXCEED 12 MONTHS (general lifting gear)
- Synthetic sling inspection: AT 3-MONTHLY INTERVALS (per AS 1353.2 and AS 4497.2)
- All lifting gear must be TAGGED identifying date of LAST INSPECTION
- DOCUMENTED MAINTENANCE RECORDS must be AVAILABLE ON SITE

BASIC ITEMS TO CHECK:
- Lifting gear TAGGED with all relevant information (grade, SWL, manufacturer, chain size, Australian Standard marking)
- Lifting hooks provided with OPERABLE SAFETY LATCHES
- Shackles PREVENTED FROM UNSCREWING (e.g. mousing or similar)
- Lifting eyes and inserts COMPATIBLE AND SAME PROPRIETARY BRAND
- Lifting slings NOT DAMAGED (excessive wear, damaged strands, cracks, deformation, severe corrosion)
- Sling APPROPRIATE for loads including adequate capacity and PROTECTION FROM SHARP EDGES

SLINGING TECHNIQUE:
- Slings placed so NOT CROSSED OR TWISTED — ensuring load balanced and stable
- Point of lift DIRECTLY ABOVE CENTRE OF GRAVITY (prevents toppling or falling)
- Synthetic slings (round/sausage): only for APPROPRIATE LOADS — NOT for sharp-edged loads

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lifting gear without inspection tags or tags exceeding 12-month (general) or 3-month (synthetic slings) interval = non-compliance
- Slings crossed, twisted or not appropriate for load type = non-compliance
- Lifting hooks without operable safety latches = non-compliance
- Shackles not prevented from unscrewing = non-compliance

CHUNK 24: Load Chart Compliance and Overload Prevention
Section: CoP s7.2.2; WHS Regulation s219
Topic: Load chart compliance; hoist brake testing; load indicator checking; suspended load restrictions

LOAD CHART COMPLIANCE:
- Crane MUST BE OPERATED WITHIN LOAD CHART at all times
- Hoist rope must hang VERTICALLY OVER LOAD before hoisting
- Ensure load does NOT SWING once lifted

MAXIMUM OR NEAR MAXIMUM LOADS:
After load lifted a few centimetres:
- TEST HOIST BRAKES
- CHECK MASS RECORDED ON LOAD INDICATOR
- RECHECK LOAD CHART

SUSPENDED LOADS (WHS Regulation s219):
- No loads may be SUSPENDED OR TRAVEL OVER A PERSON unless plant specifically designed for that purpose
- Where possible: avoid HANDLING LOADS OVER PUBLIC ACCESS AREAS (footpaths, roads, highways, railways, waterways, buildings)
- Where necessary: control measures must prevent or minimise risk of injury from falling objects

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane operating at or near maximum load without testing hoist brakes and rechecking load chart = non-compliance
- Load suspended over workers = serious non-compliance (WHS Regulation s219)
- Hoist rope not vertical over load before hoisting = non-compliance

MATERIAL BOX REQUIREMENTS:

CHUNK 26: Work Boxes — Design Registration and Features
Section: CoP s7.5.1, s7.5.3; AS 1418.17; AS 2550.1
Topic: Work box criteria; when to use; design registration; fall-arrest requirements; crane features

WHEN TO USE WORK BOXES:
- May be used to gain access to ELEVATED WORK AREAS OTHERWISE DIFFICULT TO REACH for MINOR WORK OF SHORT DURATION
- Before selecting: RISK ASSESSMENT must be undertaken and RECORDED demonstrating scaffolding/EWP is IMPRACTICAL

WORK BOX CRITERIA:
- DESIGN REGISTERED
- CORRECTLY TAGGED LIFTING SLINGS supplied; attached by HAMMERLOCKS OR MOUSED SHACKLES
- Factor of safety for each suspension sling: AT LEAST 8 FOR CHAINS and 10 FOR WIRE ROPE
- FALL-ARREST ANCHORAGE POINTS present
- SWL, TARE MASS AND DESIGN REGISTRATION NUMBER clearly marked (e.g. data plate)
- Door (if provided): INWARD OPENING ONLY, SELF-CLOSING, with LATCH preventing accidental opening
- SIDES AT LEAST 1 METRE HIGH
- HANDRAIL running around INSIDE OF BOX PERIMETER (prevents crush injuries if box contacts obstructions)
- All persons must wear FULL BODY FALL-ARREST HARNESSES at all times, attached to FALL-ARREST ANCHORAGE POINTS in box or MAIN SLING RING above workers' heads
- ENERGY ABSORBERS on lanyards
- AT LEAST ONE PERSON in work box must hold DOGGER'S LICENCE CLASS or equivalent

CRANE FEATURES WHEN USING WORK BOXES (CoP s7.5.3):
- Where reasonably practicable: SECONDARY BACK-UP SYSTEM to prevent load falling if primary lifting device fails
- Minimum SWL: AT LEAST TWICE TOTAL LOAD of workbox and contents at maximum radius, and NOT LESS THAN 1000 KILOGRAMS
- Must be fitted with UPPER HOIST LIMIT stopping hoist and luff functions
- Crane levers and foot pedals: CONSTANT PRESSURE SYSTEM stopping motions when operator removes pressure
- Crane must NOT BE CAPABLE OF FREE FALL

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Work box used without prior risk assessment demonstrating impracticability of scaffolding/EWP = non-compliance
- Work box not design registered or no design registration number visible = serious non-compliance
- Persons in work box not wearing full body fall-arrest harnesses = serious non-compliance
- Work box door opening outward = non-compliance
- Work box sides less than 1 metre high = non-compliance
- No person in work box holding dogger's licence = serious non-compliance
- Crane SWL for work box lift less than twice total load or less than 1000 kg = serious non-compliance
- Crane capable of free fall when used with work box = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 27: First Aid Boxes — Dimensions and Wind Restrictions
Section: CoP s7.5.2, s7.5.3; commenced 1 January 2018
Topic: First aid box specifications; dimensions; wind speed restrictions; emergency retrieval

FIRST AID BOX PURPOSE:
- Must ONLY be used for RETRIEVAL OF INJURED PERSONS
- Should be READILY ACCESSIBLE on site for effective emergency response

FIRST AID BOX CRITERIA:
- DESIGN REGISTERED
- Clearly IDENTIFIED AND MARKED as first aid boxes
- Provided with SIDES AND ROOF (horizontal gap may be provided around all sides)
- Sides at least 1 METRE HIGH, up to horizontal gap NOT EXCEEDING 250 MM in vertical distance
- MINIMUM INTERNAL HEIGHT: 2.2 METRES
- MINIMUM INTERNAL WIDTH: 1.2 METRES
- MINIMUM INTERNAL LENGTH: 2.8 METRES
- Maximum MESH SIZE: NOT EXCEED 50 MM x 50 MM (openings not exceeding 0.0025 m²)
- GRAB RAIL along sides RECESSED to minimise crush injury risk
- Door (if provided): may be OUTWARD OPENING but must be LOCKABLE to prevent inadvertent opening
- Persons in first aid box NOT REQUIRED to use fall arrest harnesses (box is enclosed)
- CORRECTLY TAGGED LIFTING SLINGS attached by HAMMERLOCKS OR MOUSED SHACKLES
- Factor of safety: AT LEAST 8 FOR CHAINS and 10 FOR WIRE ROPE
- SWL, TARE MASS AND DESIGN REGISTRATION NUMBER marked on box
- AT LEAST ONE PERSON must hold DOGGER'S LICENCE CLASS or equivalent
- EMERGENCY RETRIEVAL ARRANGEMENTS in place BEFORE LIFT so workers can safely exit if crane fails

WIND SPEED RESTRICTIONS:
- First aid boxes should NOT be used when wind speeds EXCEED 54 KM/HR
- Should not be used in ADVERSE WEATHER CONDITIONS (e.g. ELECTRICAL STORMS)
- More suitable in LOCATIONS LESS SUSCEPTIBLE TO WIND
- Should be lifted AWAY FROM STRUCTURES to avoid collision
- When wind speeds EXCEED 54 KM/HR: ALTERNATIVE EMERGENCY RETRIEVAL MEANS should be used (e.g. HOISTS)

CRANE FEATURES (same as work boxes):
- Minimum SWL: AT LEAST TWICE TOTAL LOAD at maximum radius and NOT LESS THAN 1000 KILOGRAMS
- UPPER HOIST LIMIT stopping hoist and luff functions
- CONSTANT PRESSURE SYSTEM on levers and foot pedals
- Crane must NOT BE CAPABLE OF FREE FALL

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aid box used for any purpose other than retrieval of injured persons = serious non-compliance
- First aid box internal dimensions less than 2.2m height, 1.2m width, 2.8m length = non-compliance
- Mesh openings exceeding 50mm x 50mm = non-compliance
- First aid box used in wind speeds exceeding 54 km/hr without documented exceptional justification = serious non-compliance
- First aid box without design registration = serious non-compliance
- No emergency retrieval arrangements documented before lift = non-compliance
- No person in first aid box holding dogger's licence = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 28: Self-Erecting Tower Cranes — Remote Control and Barricades
Section: CoP s8–s8.1.2; WHS Regulation
Topic: Remote control requirements; operator restrictions; barricade specifications for self-erecting cranes

OPERATION FROM DESIGNATED AREA:
- Must be operated from DESIGNATED AREA
- Operator must at all times during lifting:
  - REMAIN IN CLOSE PROXIMITY to crane
  - MAINTAIN GOOD VISIBILITY OF LOAD
- Where operator cannot keep load in sight: DOGGER must report on POSITION OF LOAD

OPERATOR RESTRICTIONS:
- Crane operator must NOT undertake own DOGGING WORK (even if also licensed dogger)
- Crane operator must NOT SUPERVISE TRAINEE DOGGER

REMOTE OPERATION (CoP s8.1.1):
- Reliability of remote control circuits: SAME AS CONTROLS IN CABIN
- WIRELESS REMOTES must be UNIQUELY CODED to avoid signal corruption and interference
- DEDICATED OPERATOR must be available
- Number of DOGGERS for MULTIPLE DROP-OFF AND PICK-UP POINTS out of sight of operator
- Operator must FOLLOW DIRECTION OF DOGGER when load out of sight
- Operator must REMAIN STATIONARY when load in motion
- If operator must move to different location: crane must NOT BE OPERATED WHILE MOVING; travel path FREE FROM OBSTACLES, PENETRATIONS AND HAZARDS
- If operator undertakes other tasks: REMOTE CONTROL TURNED OFF AND SECURED to prevent unintended activation
- EFFECTIVE COMMUNICATIONS maintained between operator and other personnel at all times

BARRICADE REQUIREMENT (CoP s8.1.2):
- 1800 MM HIGH BARRICADE (e.g. mesh fence) must be erected around BASE of self-erecting tower cranes
- Purpose: prevent people entering slewing arc and being hit by counterweights
- Barricade positioned to provide ENOUGH ROOM TO AVOID ENTRAPMENT between barricade and counterweights

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Self-erecting tower crane remote control not uniquely coded = non-compliance
- Operator performing dogging work on remote control tower crane = non-compliance
- No 1800 mm high barricade around base of self-erecting tower crane = serious non-compliance
- Operator moving to new location while crane in operation = serious non-compliance
- Remote control left unsecured when operator undertakes other tasks = non-compliance
- Self-erecting tower crane not operated from designated area = non-compliance
