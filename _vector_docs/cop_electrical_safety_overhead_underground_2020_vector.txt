CHUNK 1: Exclusion Zones — Untrained Persons, Overhead Uninsulated Lines
Section: ES Regulation s68, s69, Schedule 2; CoP s3.1, s3.2
Topic: Mandatory minimum clearance distances for untrained workers and plant near overhead powerlines (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

UNTRAINED PERSONS — PERSON/PLANT EXCLUSION DISTANCES (with consultation / without consultation):
- Low Voltage (<1000V): 1000mm with consultation / 3000mm without consultation
- Above LV up to 33kV: 2000mm with consultation / 3000mm without consultation
- Above 33kV up to 132kV: 3000mm
- Above 132kV up to 220kV: 4500mm (Person); 6000mm (Plant)
- Above 220kV up to 275kV: 5000mm (Person); 6000mm (Plant)
- Above 275kV up to 330kV: 6000mm
- Above 330kV up to 500kV: 6000mm (Person); 8000mm (Plant)

UNTRAINED PERSONS — VEHICLE EXCLUSION DISTANCES:
- Low Voltage: 600mm
- Above LV up to 33kV: 900mm
- Above 33kV up to 132kV: 2100mm
- Above 132kV up to 220kV: 2900mm
- Above 220kV up to 275kV: 2900mm
- Above 275kV up to 330kV: 3400mm
- Above 330kV up to 500kV: 4400mm

DC LINES (untrained persons):
- +/- 25kV: 3000mm (Person/Plant); 900mm (Vehicle)
- +/- 85kV and +/- 150kV: 3000mm (Person/Plant); 2100mm (Vehicle)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any part of worker, plant or load within the relevant exclusion distance = serious non-compliance
- Tiger tails/Torapoli pipes on lines = lines are energised and marked, NOT insulated
- Work within 3m of bare line without prior consultation = non-compliant for untrained persons
- No exclusion zone markers (red warning tape) visible near energised lines during plant operation = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: Exclusion Zones — Authorised/Instructed Persons, Overhead Lines
Section: ES Regulation s68, s69, Schedule 2; CoP s3.1, s3.3
Topic: Reduced clearance distances for trained workers with safety observer (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

AUTHORISED/INSTRUCTED PERSONS — OVERHEAD UNINSULATED LINES:
- Low Voltage: No exclusion zone prescribed (no-touch zone applies)
- Up to 33kV: 700mm (Person); 1200mm (Plant with safety observer)
- Above 33kV up to 50kV: 750mm (Person/Vehicle); 1300mm (Plant)
- Above 50kV up to 66kV: 1000mm (Person/Vehicle); 1400mm (Plant)

SAFETY OBSERVER REQUIREMENTS (ES Regulation s69):
- MUST NOT carry out any other work or function that compromises their role
- Should NOT observe more than one crane or operating plant at a time
- Should NOT be located on the work basket of an elevating work platform
- MUST have authority to stop the operation

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Safety observer present and communicating with plant operator = positive compliance
- Safety observer positioned on EWP work basket = non-compliant
- Safety observer visibly doing another task while plant operates near lines = non-compliant
- Plant operating within authorisation zone without safety observer or documented safe system = non-compliant
- Operator and safety observer without two-way communication = flag

CHUNK 3: Operating Plant Near Overhead Lines — Cranes, Tip Trucks, EWPs
Section: CoP s4.1, s4.3, s4.4
Topic: Safety observer zone requirements and plant operation controls

SAFETY OBSERVER ZONE: Any part of plant or load COULD enter the exclusion zone during operation.
- Safety observer MUST be employed
- Observer MUST NOT carry out other work that compromises their role
- Observer MUST have authority to stop the operation

AUTHORISATION ZONE: Plant operated by authorised/instructed person.
- Safety observer OR another safe system of work MUST be used to prevent contact

PROHIBITIONS:
- Crane or operating plant MUST NOT be operated in authorisation zone without safety observer or safe system of work
- Safety observer should NOT be required to observe more than one crane or plant at a time

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tip truck tray raised near overhead lines without spotter = high-risk non-compliance
- Safety observer using red warning tape to mark exclusion zone boundary = positive indicator
- Crane or EWP operating near lines with no safety observer = non-compliant
- Operator remaining in cabin while plant in safety observer zone = positive indicator (isolated from earth)
- Plant operating on uneven terrain near lines where ground movement could cause surge = flag
- Knuckle boom EWP with boom intruding toward lines during repositioning = flag
- Visible stay wires on poles near work area = must be included in risk assessment

CHUNK 4: Work Near Low Voltage Lines on Buildings — No-Touch Zone
Section: CoP s5.1, s5.2, s5.3
Topic: Maintenance, painting and scaffolding near insulated service lines

NO-TOUCH ZONE: Applies to insulated low voltage line examined for visible defects by competent person.
- Worker may go as close as needed but MUST NOT TOUCH the line

VISUAL EXAMINATION REQUIREMENTS — insulated line must be checked for:
- Insulation intact with no tears or cracks
- No exposed live parts anywhere on the line
- If effectively insulated cannot be determined, line MUST BE TREATED AS BARE

BARE LINE STATUS: If insulation defects visible, full exclusion zones apply (1000mm with consultation / 3000mm without consultation for untrained persons).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding erected within 3m of uninspected low voltage line without consultation = non-compliant
- Worker using metal roofing sheet near service line attachment point = high risk of encroachment
- Visible tears, cracks or physical defects in service line insulation = "bare" line status — exclusion zones apply
- No evidence of visual inspection of service line before work commenced = flag
- Work near mains connection box or service line attachment point without inspection = flag

CHUNK 5: High Load Transportation — Height Categories and Escort Requirements
Section: CoP s8.2, s8.3
Topic: Moving loads exceeding 4.6 metres in height on public roads (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

HIGH LOAD CATEGORIES:
- Category 1 (Up to 4.6m): No notification required
- Category 2 (Over 4.6m to 5.0m): Notification to electricity entity required; route scoped by qualified person
- Category 3 (Over 5.0m to 5.5m): Notification required; route scoped; escort may be required
- Category 4 (Exceeding 5.5m): Notification required; route scoped; escort REQUIRED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Load height exceeding 4.6m moving on public roads without escort or scoping record = potential non-compliance
- Presence of dips in road profile = risk of load rising into overhead lines
- High load escort service provider directing road transport operator = positive safety indicator
- No height measuring device visible on oversize load = flag
- Load exceeding 5.5m without escort vehicle = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Underground Electric Lines — Excavation and Service Location
Section: WHS Regulation s304; CoP s9.1, s9.2, s9.3
Topic: Mandatory service location and safe digging requirements

BEFORE EXCAVATION STARTS:
- PCBU MUST obtain current underground essential services information
- Free service: Before You Dig Australia — phone 1100 or www.byda.com.au
- Information MUST include location AND DEPTH of pipes, cables or other plant
- Any conditions specified by asset owner MUST be complied with
- Underground services information MUST be available for inspection until work completed

NO UNIVERSAL "NO DIG ZONE": Specific clearance distance is set by asset owner in conditions provided with underground services information. Those conditions are MANDATORY.

RECORD KEEPING:
- If notifiable incident occurs, records must be kept for at least 2 years after incident

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation (trenching or post-hole digging) starting without BYDA report on site = serious non-compliance
- Conduits or markers visible in trench = positive identification of underground assets — stop and verify
- Worker driving star pickets without checking for buried cables = high-risk activity
- No underground services plans visible on site during excavation = non-compliant
- Cable identification tape (yellow/orange) visible during excavation = underground electrical asset present — stop work
- Excavation proceeding without regard to asset owner conditions = non-compliant
