CHUNK 1: Primary Duties and Electrical Safety
Section: ES Act s30; ES Regulation s11, s68; COP s1.2
Topic: Legislative framework and core safety obligations

PRIMARY DUTY: A PCBU must ensure the person's business or undertaking is conducted in a way that is electrically safe. This includes ensuring that no person, plant or thing at the workplace comes within an unsafe distance of an overhead or underground electric line.

DUTY HOLDERS:
- Officers: Must exercise due diligence to ensure the PCBU complies with the ES Act and ES Regulation
- Workers: Must take reasonable care for their own electrical safety and comply with reasonable instructions
- Treatment of Lines: Electric lines MUST BE TREATED AS LIVE until they have been proven to be de-energised, isolated, and (for high voltage) effectively earthed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Presence of overhead wires, poles, towers, or stay wires = potential electrical hazard
- Workers using conductive tools (e.g. long ladders, metal pipes) near lines = high-risk indicator
- Written confirmation from the person in control of the line that it is de-energised = positive safety indicator

CHUNK 2: Unsafe Distances and Exclusion Zones
Section: ES Regulation s68, s69, Schedule 2; COP s3.1, s3.2, s3.3
Topic: Mandatory approach distances based on training and voltage

EXCLUSION ZONE: A safety envelope around an electric line. No part of a worker, operating plant, or vehicle should enter an exclusion zone while the line is energised.

LOW VOLTAGE DEFINITION: Voltage greater than extra low voltage but not more than 1000V AC RMS or 1500V ripple-free DC.

UNTRAINED PERSONS — OVERHEAD UNINSULATED LINES (Person/Plant exclusion distance; Vehicle exclusion distance):
- Low Voltage (<1000V): 1000mm with consultation / 3000mm without consultation; Vehicle: 600mm
- Above LV up to 33kV: 2000mm with consultation / 3000mm without consultation; Vehicle: 900mm
- Above 33kV up to 132kV: 3000mm (Person/Plant); Vehicle: 2100mm
- Above 132kV up to 220kV: 4500mm (Person); 6000mm (Plant); Vehicle: 2900mm
- Above 220kV up to 275kV: 5000mm (Person); 6000mm (Plant); Vehicle: 2900mm
- Above 275kV up to 330kV: 6000mm (Person/Plant); Vehicle: 3400mm
- Above 330kV up to 500kV: 6000mm (Person); 8000mm (Plant); Vehicle: 4400mm

UNTRAINED PERSONS — DC LINES:
- +/- 25kV: 3000mm (Person/Plant); Vehicle: 900mm
- +/- 85kV and +/- 150kV: 3000mm (Person/Plant); Vehicle: 2100mm

AUTHORISED/INSTRUCTED PERSONS — OVERHEAD UNINSULATED LINES:
- Low Voltage: No exclusion zone prescribed (no-touch zone applies)
- Up to 33kV: 700mm for persons; 1200mm for operating plant with a safety observer
- Above 33kV up to 50kV: 750mm (Person/Vehicle); 1300mm (Plant)
- Above 50kV up to 66kV: 1000mm (Person/Vehicle); 1400mm (Plant)

PROHIBITIONS:
- An untrained person MUST NOT allow any part of their body or anything they are handling or wearing to enter the relevant exclusion zone
- A safety observer MUST NOT carry out any other work or function that compromises their role
- A safety observer should not be required to observe more than one crane or operating plant at a time
- The crane or operating plant MUST NOT be operated in the authorisation zone without a safety observer or another safe system of work
- A safety observer SHOULD NOT be located on the work basket of an elevating work platform

NOTE: Tiger Tails (Torapoli pipes) are used as visual indicators of line position only — they DO NOT insulate wires.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tiger tails/Torapoli pipes on lines = lines are energised and marked, not insulated
- Safety observer or spotter present and communicating with plant operators = positive compliance
- Work within 3 metres of a bare line without prior consultation = non-compliant for untrained persons
- No exclusion zone markers (red warning tape) visible near energised lines during plant operation = non-compliant
- Visible stay wires on poles near work area = must be included in risk assessment — often overlooked
- Safety observer positioned on the work basket of an EWP = non-compliant
- Safety observer visibly doing another task while plant operates near lines = non-compliant
- Plant operating on uneven terrain near lines where ground movement could cause surge toward lines = flag
- Knuckle boom EWP with boom intruding toward lines during repositioning = flag

CHUNK 3: Operating Plant Near Overhead Lines
Section: COP s4.1, s4.3, s4.4
Topic: Requirements for cranes, tip trucks, and elevated work platforms (EWPs)

SAFETY OBSERVER ZONE: A crane or plant is in this zone when any part of the plant or load COULD enter the exclusion zone during operation.
- A safety observer should be employed
- The observer MUST NOT carry out any other work that compromises their role
- The observer MUST have the authority to stop the operation

AUTHORISATION ZONE: Applies when plant is operated by an authorised or instructed person.
- A safety observer OR another safe system of work must be used to prevent contact with the line

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tip truck tray raised near overhead lines without a spotter = high-risk non-compliance
- Safety observer using red warning tape to mark the exclusion zone boundary = positive indicator
- Crane or EWP operating near lines with no safety observer = non-compliant
- Operator remaining in the cabin while plant is in the safety observer zone = positive indicator (isolated from earth)
- Plant operator and safety observer without two-way communication = flag

CHUNK 4: Work Near Low Voltage Lines on Buildings
Section: COP s5.1, s5.2, s5.3
Topic: Maintenance, painting, and scaffolding near structures

NO-TOUCH ZONE: Applies to an insulated low voltage line examined for visible defects by a competent person. A worker may go as close as they need but MUST NOT TOUCH the line.

VISUAL EXAMINATION — an insulated line should be visually checked to ensure:
- The insulation is intact with no tears or cracks
- There are no exposed live parts anywhere on the line
- If effectively insulated cannot be determined, the line SHOULD BE TREATED AS BARE

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Scaffolding erected within 3 metres of an uninspected low voltage line without consultation = non-compliant
- Worker using a metal roofing sheet near the point of attachment for a service line = high risk of encroachment
- Visible tears, cracks or other physical defects in the insulation of a service line = "bare" line status; must maintain exclusion zones
- No evidence of visual inspection of service line before work commenced near it = flag
- Work being performed near the mains connection box or point where service lines attach to a building = flag — requires inspection of insulation before proceeding

CHUNK 5: High Load Transportation
Section: COP s8.2, s8.3
Topic: Moving loads exceeding 4.6 metres in height

HIGH LOAD CATEGORIES AND REQUIREMENTS:
- Category 1 (Up to 4.6m): No notification required
- Category 2 (Over 4.6m to 5.0m): Notification to the electricity entity is required; route should be scoped by a qualified person
- Category 3 (Over 5.0m to 5.5m): Notification required; route scoped; escort may be required
- Category 4 (Exceeding 5.5m): Notification required; route scoped; escort is required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Load height exceeding 4.6m moving on public roads without an escort or scoping record = potential non-compliance
- Presence of dips in the road profile = risk of the load rising into overhead lines
- High load escort service provider directing the road transport operator = positive safety indicator
- No height measuring device visible on oversize load = flag

CHUNK 6: Underground Electric Lines and Excavation
Section: WHS Regulation s304; COP s9.1, s9.2, s9.3
Topic: Safe digging and location of buried services

LOCATION REQUIREMENTS: Before starting excavation, a PCBU must take all reasonable steps to obtain current underground essential services information.
- Free Enquiry Service: Before You Dig Australia — phone 1100 or www.byda.com.au
- Information obtained must include the location AND DEPTH of any pipes, cables or other plant
- Any conditions on the proposed excavation work specified by the asset owner MUST be complied with
- The person with management or control AND any PCBU given the information must have regard to it when carrying out or directing excavation (WHS Regulation s304; COP s9.3)

NOTE: The Code does not prescribe a universal "no dig zone" numerical distance. The specific clearance distance is set by the asset owner in the conditions provided with the underground services information. Those conditions are mandatory.

RECORD KEEPING:
- Underground essential services information must be available for inspection until the work is completed
- If a notifiable incident occurs, records must be kept for at least 2 years after the incident

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavation (trenching or post-hole digging) starting without a BYDA report on site = serious non-compliance
- Conduits or markers visible in a trench = positive identification of underground assets — stop and verify
- Worker driving star pickets into the ground without checking for buried electrical cables = high-risk activity
- No underground services plans visible on site during excavation = non-compliant
- Cable identification tape (yellow/orange) visible during excavation = underground electrical asset present — stop work
- Excavation proceeding without regard to asset owner conditions = non-compliant
