CHUNK 1: Document Purpose, Hierarchy and How to Use
Section: About this document; How to use this document
Topic: Scope, relationship to Australian Standard AS 1742.3-2019, and Queensland precedence

THIS DOCUMENT:
- Provides uniform practices for signing, delineation and device use for works on roads in Queensland
- Promotes safety of workers and safe and efficient movement of road users at roadworks sites
- Is designed to be read and applied TOGETHER with AS 1742.3-2019 Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices Part 3
- HAS PRECEDENCE over AS 1742.3-2019 when applied in Queensland
- Uses the same section and clause numbering as AS 1742.3-2019
- Must be read in conjunction with the Queensland Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (QGTTM)

APPLICABILITY CATEGORIES used in this document:
- ACCEPTED — Australian Standard section or clause applies unchanged
- ACCEPTED, WITH AMENDMENTS — part or all accepted, but with Queensland additions, deletions or differences
- NEW — no equivalent in the Australian Standard (Queensland-only requirement)
- NOT ACCEPTED — Australian Standard clause does not apply in Queensland

KEY RELATED DOCUMENTS:
- Queensland Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (QGTTM) — primary operational reference for TTM
- AS 1742.3-2019 — base national standard; Queensland MUTCD Part 3 takes precedence
- Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (Qld) — TORUM Act — governing legislation
- Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015
- Professional Engineers Act 2002 (Qld) — governs RPEQ certification
- Main Roads Technical Specifications (MRTS) — referenced for specific devices and products
- TMR Traffic Control (TC) signs database — officially approved non-standard signs

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Construction site TTM plan referencing only AS 1742.3-2019 without Queensland MUTCD Part 3 = incomplete compliance basis, flag for review
- Non-standard signs used without TC-series or Queensland MUTCD authorisation = non-compliance
- Traffic control devices on site not matching Queensland MUTCD Part 3 requirements = flag

CHUNK 2: Key Definitions — Competent Person
Section: Clause 1.3.2 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Who is authorised to perform TTM activities in Queensland; training requirements

COMPETENT PERSON (Queensland additions to definition):
- Must be AUTHORISED BY TRANSPORT AND MAIN ROADS to perform that task in Queensland
- A competent person may ONLY undertake TTM activities in accordance with QGTTM Part 8 Appendix A
- Prescribed training for TTM activities: see Clause 1.7 (below)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Person performing TTM functions on site without evidence of Queensland authority card = non-compliance
- TTM activity carried out outside the scope of QGTTM Part 8 Appendix A = non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Key Definitions — Shall / Should / May
Section: Clauses 1.3.5, 1.3.13, 1.3.14 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Mandatory vs recommended vs optional requirements; compliance implications

SHALL (Clause 1.3.13):
- Where certain requirements are described with 'shall' = MANDATORY
- When an installation is made, all 'shall' requirements MUST be met
- Variations to 'shall' requirements: refer to Clause 1.9(b) — requires risk assessment and RPEQ certification

SHOULD (Clause 1.3.14):
- Where 'should' is used = RECOMMENDED but not mandatory
- Variations to 'should' recommendations: refer to Clause 1.9(a) — requires documented risk assessment by TMD or RPEQ

MAY (Clause 1.3.5):
- Where 'may' is used = conditional or optional; no specific requirement for design or application is intended

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Site deviating from a 'shall' requirement without documented RPEQ-certified risk assessment = serious non-compliance
- Site deviating from a 'should' recommendation without documented risk assessment = flag for review

CHUNK 4: Key Definitions — Traffic Controller
Section: Clause 1.3.19 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Who qualifies as a Traffic Controller in Queensland; legal authority

TRAFFIC CONTROLLER (Queensland definition):
- A person who holds an APPOINTMENT as an ACCREDITED PERSON under Section 21 of the TORUM Act 1995
- Performs the functions of a Traffic Controller as prescribed by Subdivision 6 of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management – Accreditation and Other Provisions) Regulation 2015

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Person performing stop/slow bat traffic control without a Queensland Traffic Controller appointment = serious non-compliance
- STOP/SLOW bat in use at site without accredited Traffic Controller present = non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Key Definitions — RPEQ (Registered Professional Engineer of Queensland)
Section: Clause 1.3.24 (NEW)
Topic: Role and authority of RPEQ in TTM; when RPEQ certification is required

RPEQ (Registered Professional Engineer of Queensland):
- A person registered under the Professional Engineers Act 2002 (Qld) with the Board of Professional Engineers of Queensland
- Must be registered to provide the specific professional engineering service required

NOTE: A professional engineering service requires application of engineering principles and data to a design or construction, production, operation or maintenance activity. Services provided only in accordance with a prescriptive standard are excluded.

WHEN RPEQ IS REQUIRED (see Clause 1.9):
- Variations to 'should' recommendations: risk assessment must be undertaken and signed by TMD or RPEQ
- Variations to 'shall' requirements: risk assessment PLUS full TMP/TGS must be certified by RPEQ
- Use of innovative treatments outside Queensland MUTCD scope: TMP/TGS must be RPEQ certified

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Variation to a mandatory ('shall') requirement in TMP or TGS without RPEQ certification = serious non-compliance
- Complex TTM involving significant geometric changes or network diversions without RPEQ involvement = flag for review

Section: Clause 1.6 (NEW)

LEGAL AUTHORITY:

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Temporary roadworks signs installed without authority from TMR or local government = non-compliance
- Signs not complying with Queensland MUTCD methods and standards = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Prescribed Training — Authority Cards and Training Courses
Section: Clause 1.7 (NEW)
Topic: Mandatory training requirements for all TTM roles in Queensland; who needs what card

AUTHORITY CARD REQUIREMENT:
- A person is ONLY authorised to perform a TTM role in Queensland if they hold an AUTHORITY CARD applicable to that role
- Authority cards are a mandatory regulatory requirement where specified
- To obtain an authority card: must complete the relevant prescribed training course AND meet additional requirements
- Prescribed training courses are developed by TMR and delivered by APPROVED REGISTERED TRAINING ORGANISATIONS

PRESCRIBED TRAINING COURSES:

(a) WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC AWARENESS PART 1:
- Required for: persons who work on or adjacent to a road
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

(b) WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC AWARENESS PART 2:
- Required for: persons who have completed Part 1 AND are required to select and implement work method practices under short-term, low-impact works provisions
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

(c) TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT IMPLEMENTATION (TMI):
- Required for: persons implementing Traffic Management Plans (TMPs) and Traffic Guidance Schemes (TGSs)
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A5

(d) TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT DESIGN (TMD):
- Required for: persons who DESIGN, DEVELOP, REVIEW and INSPECT TMPs and TGSs
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A3

(e) TRAFFIC CONTROLLER:
- Required for: persons performing Traffic Controller functions (Clause 1.3.19)
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A4

(f) EVENT TRAFFIC MARSHAL:
- Required for: volunteers undertaking event traffic management
- Applies in accordance with: QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

ADDITIONAL ROLES (no authority card required but must comply with QGTTM):

(g) LOOKOUT PERSON — must have completed Parts 1 and 2 of Working in Proximity training; QGTTM Part 8 Table A7
(h) ROADWORKS PILOT VEHICLE DRIVER — QGTTM Part 8 Table A7
(i) TRUCK-MOUNTED ATTENUATOR (TMA) VEHICLE DRIVER — must have completed TMI; QGTTM Part 8 Table A7
(j) AUTHORISED PERSON — QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any TTM worker on site without a valid Queensland authority card for their role = non-compliance
- TMI worker implementing a TGS without TMI training completion = non-compliance
- TMD worker designing TMP without TMD qualification = non-compliance
- Traffic Controller using stop/slow bat without Traffic Controller authority card = serious non-compliance
- TMA vehicle driver without completed TMI training = non-compliance
- Worker adjacent to live traffic without minimum Part 1 awareness training = non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Variations to Mandatory Requirements — RPEQ Certification Process
Section: Clause 1.9 (NEW)
Topic: The formal process when deviating from 'shall' or 'should' requirements in TTM

VARIATIONS TO 'SHOULD' RECOMMENDATIONS (Clause 1.9(a)):
- Reasons must be based on sound TTM judgement
- Must be DOCUMENTED THROUGH A RISK ASSESSMENT demonstrating acceptability and safety of the varied approach
- Risk assessment must be prepared in accordance with QGTTM
- Risk assessment SHALL be undertaken and SIGNED BY A TMD OR AN RPEQ

VARIATIONS TO 'SHALL' REQUIREMENTS (Clause 1.9(b)):
- Reasons must be based on sound TTM or traffic engineering judgement
- Must be DOCUMENTED THROUGH A RISK ASSESSMENT demonstrating acceptability and safety
- Risk assessment SHALL be undertaken and signed by a TMD or RPEQ
- BOTH the risk assessment AND the TMP/TGS SHALL be CERTIFIED BY AN RPEQ
- RPEQ certification only required for portion of TMP/TGS impacted by or related to the departure
- TMD preparing TMP/TGS SHALL email notification to TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au including all relevant information and RPEQ details (for information only — not for approval)

VARIATIONS FOR INNOVATIVE TREATMENTS (Clause 1.9(c)):
- Risk assessment undertaken by a Competent Person
- TMP/TGS AND risk assessment certified by RPEQ
- ALL innovative treatments require PRIOR APPROVAL by Transport and Main Roads
- Request approval by emailing TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au

USE OF OPTIONS ('MAY') (Clause 1.9(d)):
- Not a variation to the optimal treatment — does NOT require RPEQ certification

SCOPE NOTE: Very few roadworks sites should fall within the RPEQ requirement under (b) or (c). Examples where it IS required: complex geometric changes requiring engineering design principles; complex diversions requiring detailed analysis (e.g. micro-simulation traffic modelling).

RISK ASSESSMENT PREPARATION:
- All TTM risk assessments SHALL be prepared in accordance with QGTTM Part 10 Section 2 Risk Management for TTM

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- TMP/TGS deviating from a 'shall' requirement without documented RPEQ-certified risk assessment = serious non-compliance
- Innovative TTM treatment in use without prior TMR approval = serious non-compliance
- TMD-signed risk assessment used for a 'shall' variation without accompanying RPEQ certification = non-compliance
- No notification email to TMR for 'shall' variation = procedural non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Multi-Message Signs (MMS) — Rules and Requirements
Section: Clause 4.2.2 (Accepted, with amendments — entire clause replaced)
Topic: Mandatory conditions of use for multi-message signs at roadworks; panel configuration rules

MULTI-MESSAGE SIGNS (MMS) — GENERAL:
- Designated within TM, RM, WM and GM Series
- Allow display of up to THREE logically related messages within a single uniform modular frame (see Clause 4.5.2)
- May display messages from the T, R, W and G series combined in one frame

MANDATORY CONDITIONS OF USE (when not used to control pedestrians only):

(a) MINIMUM PANEL REQUIREMENT — MMS SHALL have relevant messages consisting of a minimum of:
- Two 600 mm × 600 mm panels; OR
- One 600 mm × 600 mm panel and one 1200 mm × 300 mm panel; OR
- One 1200 mm × 600 mm panel

(b) REGULATORY SIGN LIMIT — no more than ONE regulatory sign panel in the same MMS frame facing the direction of traffic
- EXCEPTION: Additional regulatory supplementary panels that condition or relate to the other regulatory panel (forming a single regulatory message) may be included

(c) REGULATORY SIGN POSITION — when used, regulatory sign panels SHALL be placed in the TOP POSITION of the frame on the SIDE CLOSEST TO TRAFFIC
- EXCEPTION: No Left (RM2-6A(L)) or No Right (RM2-6A(R)) Turn prohibition panels — installed on the side of the MMS where the turn prohibition applies (see Figure 4.2.2(a))

(d) TEXT-ONLY PANELS — within one frame, no more than TWO message panels consisting of words only
- If 1200 mm × 600 mm space is filled by two 600 mm × 600 mm word-only panels: at least one shall be symbolic or a blank retroreflective yellow panel
- EXCEPTIONS to the two text-panel limit:
  i. MMS under section 46 of the Transport Infrastructure Act 1994 for restricted road use notices — three word panels allowed (see Figure 4.2.2(b))
  ii. Police performing traffic control duties where POLICE CONTROL AHEAD and PREPARE TO STOP word panels are both required (see Figure 4.2.2(c))
  iii. ROAD CLOSED AHEAD and LOCAL TRAFFIC ONLY word panels combined (see Figure 4.2.2(d))

(e) BLANK PANELS — a blank retroreflective yellow panel SHALL be placed in any UNUSED module of the frame — all panel positions must be filled

(f) TM/RM/WM/GM SERIES PANELS — SHALL ONLY be used in multi-message frames (not as standalone signs)

(g) BLANK RETROREFLECTIVE YELLOW PANEL — no more than ONE per MMS
- When a 600×600 blank yellow retroreflective panel is used: SHOULD be located FURTHEST FROM TRAFFIC
- EXCEPTION: where arrows or other direction-related symbol panels are required in that location

(h) BACK-TO-BACK MMS — where a sign is required at the same location for both directions of travel:
- Option i: Use TWO SEPARATE MMS assemblies placed BACK TO BACK
- Option ii: ONE MMS assembly with panels installed back-to-back on one frame (if orientation and sight lines can be achieved)

(i) SPEED LIMIT AHEAD AND END SPEED LIMIT panels in MMS:
- SHALL be placed in the TOP POSITION of the frame on the SIDE CLOSEST TO TRAFFIC

SPECIAL CASE — TM5-1B DETOUR and TM5-1-Q01 DETOUR U-TURN:
- Minimum panel and blank panel requirements in (a) and (g) DO NOT APPLY when:
  - Using the 1200×300 TM5-1B (DETOUR with small arrow) OR TM5-1-Q01 (DETOUR with U-Turn symbol), AND
  - There are no relevant messages to add to the assembly
  - In this case: a single TM5-1B or TM5-1-Q01 panel may be used as the TOP PANEL with two blank panels below (see Figure 4.2.2(e))

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- MMS with only one panel displayed (no second panel, no blank filler) = non-compliance
- MMS with two or more regulatory sign panels facing same direction = non-compliance
- Regulatory sign panel NOT in top position closest to traffic = non-compliance
- MMS with three word-only panels (other than the approved exceptions) = non-compliance
- Unused MMS frame position not filled with blank retroreflective yellow panel = non-compliance
- TM/RM/WM/GM series panel used as a standalone sign (outside an MMS frame) = non-compliance
- Two blank retroreflective yellow panels in one MMS frame = non-compliance
- Blank yellow panel positioned closest to traffic (unless direction panel required there) = flag
- Speed Limit AHEAD or END Speed Limit panel not in top position closest to traffic = non-compliance

CHUNK 11: ROADWORK AHEAD Sign — T1-1, TM1-1, T1-31, T1-Q06
Section: Clause 4.6.2 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Queensland rules for ROADWORK AHEAD signs including the narrow-format T1-Q06

STANDARD ROADWORK AHEAD SIGNS:
- T1-1 — standard format
- TM1-1 — multi-message panel format
- T1-31 — alternate format
- T1-16 — ROADWORK X km AHEAD (advance warning)

QUEENSLAND ADDITION — NARROW FORMAT SIGN T1-Q06 (600 mm × 900 mm):
- MAY be used in Queensland
- SHALL ONLY be used on NARROW MEDIANS AND SHOULDERS where the standard T1-1 or T1-31 would not fit and would be an OBSTRUCTION to traffic
- Listed in Table 4.1(A): Sign = ROAD WORK AHEAD (Narrow format), Sign number = T1-Q06, Size = 600 x 900 mm

ROADWORK X km AHEAD SIGN — QUEENSLAND POSITION REQUIREMENT (replaces AS 1742.3-2019):
- When additional advance warning is necessary, the ROADWORK X km AHEAD sign SHOULD be used X km in advance of:
  - The PRIMARY PREPARE TO STOP sign (if traffic control is used), OR
  - The FIRST CONE in a taper (if a taper is used), OR
  - The START OF THE SAFETY BUFFER AREA
- AND SHALL be at least TWO SIGN SPACINGS in advance of the ROADWORK AHEAD sign

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- T1-Q06 narrow ROAD WORK AHEAD sign used on wide median or shoulder where standard T1-1 or T1-31 would fit = non-compliance
- T1-Q06 sign used as primary ROADWORK AHEAD sign on a standard road = non-compliance
- ROADWORK X km AHEAD sign placed less than two sign spacings before the ROADWORK AHEAD sign = non-compliance
- ROADWORK X km AHEAD sign measured from wrong reference point (not from primary PREPARE TO STOP, first cone, or safety buffer start) = flag

CHUNK 12: ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD Sign — T1-25, TM1-25, T1-27, TM1-27
Section: Clause 4.6.7 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Restrictions on use of ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD and ROAD PLANT ON SIDE ROAD signs; correct alternatives

RESTRICTION — T-INTERSECTIONS (replaces AS 1742.3-2019):
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD and ROAD PLANT ON SIDE ROAD signs SHALL NOT be used on the TERMINATING LEG of a T-intersection to warn of activities on the CONTINUING LEG of the T-intersection
- CORRECT ALTERNATIVE at T-intersection terminating leg:
  - Use ROAD WORK AHEAD (TM1-1A or TM1-1C) OR ROAD PLANT AHEAD (TM1-3-1A or TM1-3-2C)
  - WITH a direction arrow (TM5-8B)
- NOTE: The standard MMS Lane Status panel TM10-1A may be used with or without the ON SIDE ROAD message where the long arrow TM5-8B is not suitable

RESTRICTION — IMPACT ON THROUGH ROAD (Queensland addition):
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign SHALL NOT be used where there is an IMPACT FOR TRAFFIC ON THE THROUGH ROAD
- Impacts on the through road include (but not limited to):
  - Need for reduced speed zones
  - Additional delineation
  - Changes to alignment or surface condition
  - Use of traffic control
  - Traffic queueing which extends onto the through road
- Where there ARE impacts on the through road: the ROADWORK AHEAD sign (Clause 4.6.2) SHOULD be used instead

ON SIDE ROAD MESSAGE USAGE RULE:
- ON SIDE ROAD message MAY be used to warn of activity on ANOTHER ROAD which intersects with the road being travelled (includes cross intersections and roundabouts)
- EXCLUDES: the through/straight direction even if it is technically another road
- ON SIDE ROAD message SHALL NOT be used when travelling on the TERMINATING LEG of a T-intersection to warn of activity on the continuing leg

REFER TO FIGURE 4.6.7 for permitted and prohibited approaches.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign used on the terminating leg of a T-intersection to warn of works on the continuing leg = serious non-compliance
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign used where works impact the through road (e.g. queue extends onto through road, speed zone required) = non-compliance
- ON SIDE ROAD message used for the straight-through direction = non-compliance
- T-intersection with works on continuing leg not using TM1-1A/C + TM5-8B = non-compliance

CHUNK 13: SIDE ROAD CLOSED Sign — T1-32, TM1-32
Section: Clause 4.6.9 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Correct use of SIDE ROAD CLOSED sign; prohibition at T-intersections

RESTRICTION — T-INTERSECTIONS (replaces AS 1742.3-2019):
- SIDE ROAD CLOSED sign SHALL NOT be used on the TERMINATING LEG of a T-intersection to indicate a closure on the CONTINUING LEG of the T-intersection

- SIDE ROAD CLOSED sign absent at intersection where side road is closed to all traffic = non-compliance
- T-intersection with closed continuing leg not using TM1-43A/C + TM5-8B = non-compliance

CHUNK 14: END ROADWORK Sign — T2-16, T2-17, TM2-17
Section: Clause 4.6.10 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: When END ROADWORK signs are mandatory; bridgework and road plant additions

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:
- END ROADWORK sign SHALL be used whenever a ROADWORK AHEAD or ROADWORK NEXT X km sign is used on the approach to the work site
- EXCEPTION: where the requirements of Clause 4.6.11 apply (see below)

QUEENSLAND ADDITION — BRIDGEWORK:
- END ROADWORK sign SHALL be used whenever a BRIDGEWORK AHEAD or BRIDGEWORK NEXT X km sign is used on the approach to the roadworks site

QUEENSLAND ADDITION — ROAD PLANT:
- END ROADWORK sign MAY be used whenever a ROAD PLANT AHEAD or GRADER AHEAD (with or without NEXT X km sign) is used on the approach to the roadworks site

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ROADWORK AHEAD sign installed on approach but no END ROADWORK sign at exit (unless Clause 4.6.11 exception applies) = non-compliance
- BRIDGEWORK AHEAD sign used without corresponding END ROADWORK sign = non-compliance
- END ROADWORK sign absent at end of bridge construction zone = non-compliance

CHUNK 15: Advance Warning and Termination Signs — Complex Scenarios (Clause 4.6.11)
Section: Clause 4.6.11 (NEW)
Topic: Detailed rules for ROADWORK AHEAD, ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD, and END ROADWORK signs in various road configurations

SCENARIO A — ROADWORKS ON A SIDE ROAD WITH NO IMPACT ON THE THROUGH ROAD:
- Use ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD advance warning sign on the through road
- END ROADWORK sign is NOT REQUIRED on the through road
- EXCEPTION: if END ROADWORK sign cannot be installed prior to the end of the side road = then END ROADWORK sign SHALL be installed on the SIDE ROAD prior to exiting onto the through road
- Refer to Figure 4.6.11(a)

SCENARIO B — ROADWORKS ON A SIDE ROAD WHICH IMPACT THE THROUGH ROAD:
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK signs SHALL be used on BOTH the through road AND the side road
- Refer to Figure 4.6.11(b)

SCENARIO C — ROADWORKS ON A 'NO THROUGH ROAD' OR 'CUL-DE-SAC':
- END ROADWORK sign MAY be OMITTED on the departure side (travelling toward the terminating end) when ALL of the following apply:
  - No intersections exist between the end of works and the terminating end of the road
  - The end of the road or cul-de-sac is either VISIBLE FROM or within 500 m of the end of the work area
- Refer to Figure 4.6.11(c)

SCENARIO D — ROADWORKS ON A THROUGH ROAD WITH NO IMPACT ON A SIDE ROAD (within advance warning area):
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK signs SHALL be installed on the THROUGH ROAD
- ROADWORK AHEAD sign WITH A DIRECTION ARROW shall be installed on the side road
- On the side road departing the through road before or after the work area: NO END ROADWORK sign required if works have no impact on the side road or intersection operation
- An END ROADWORK sign MAY be installed on the side road if desired
- Refer to Figure 4.6.11(d)

SCENARIO E — ROADWORKS ON A THROUGH ROAD WHICH IMPACT A SIDE ROAD:
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK signs SHALL be installed on BOTH the through road AND the side road
- Refer to Figure 4.6.11(e)

IMPACTS ON THROUGH/SIDE ROAD include (but not limited to):
- Need for reduced speed zones
- Additional delineation
- Changes to alignment or surface condition
- Use of traffic control
- Traffic queueing which extends onto the road

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Works on side road impacting through road (e.g. speed zone, queue) without ROADWORK AHEAD on through road = non-compliance (Scenario B, not A)
- No END ROADWORK on side road when through road END ROADWORK is omitted and works exit onto through road = non-compliance
- Cul-de-sac works with END ROADWORK omitted but end of road is more than 500 m from end of works AND has intersections in between = non-compliance
- Through road works impacting side road without ROADWORK AHEAD + END ROADWORK on side road = non-compliance (Scenario E, not D)
- Roadwork AHEAD on side road approach missing direction arrow = non-compliance (Scenario D)

CHUNK 16: Manual Traffic Control — STOP/SLOW Bat and LED Enhancement
Section: Clause 4.7.2 (Accepted, with amendments — item e added)
Topic: LED-enhanced STOP/SLOW bats; when they may be used; product approval requirements

STANDARD STOP/SLOW BAT:
- Used by Traffic Controllers (see Clause 1.3.19) for manual traffic control at roadworks
- Standard format — used in normal conditions

LED-ENHANCED STOP/SLOW BAT (Queensland addition — item e):
- Sign numbers: R6-8-QO1 (STOP face) and T7-1-QO1 (SLOW face) on opposite faces of the bat
- MAY be used where ENHANCED VISIBILITY is desirable:
  - During NIGHT WORKS
  - When weather conditions such as RAIN or FOG may limit effectiveness of standard bats
- SHALL comprise R6-8-QO1 (STOP, red LEDs) on one face and T7-1-QO1 (SLOW, yellow LEDs) on the other face

STATE-CONTROLLED ROADS — PRODUCT APPROVAL:
- When used on STATE-CONTROLLED ROADS: LED-enhanced STOP/SLOW bats SHALL be as specified on Transport and Main Roads' Approved Products and Registered Suppliers list

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- LED STOP/SLOW bat used on a state-controlled road that is not on the TMR Approved Products and Registered Suppliers list = non-compliance
- LED bat with incorrect sign numbers (not R6-8-QO1 / T7-1-QO1) = non-compliance
- Standard (non-LED) bat in use during night works or in rain/fog where LED bat would significantly improve safety = flag for risk assessment

CHUNK 17: Sign-Controlled Single Lane Operation — GIVE WAY and ONE LANE Signs
Section: Clause 4.7.3 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Queensland sign sizes for GIVE WAY and ONE LANE signs; traffic volume and lane length thresholds for sign control

QUEENSLAND SIGN SIZES — GIVE WAY (replacing AS 1742.3-2019 table rows):
- R1-2A: 750 ht
- R1-2B: 900 ht

CHUNK 27: Vehicle-Mounted Warning Devices — Three Arrangement Types
Section: Clause 4.14.1 (Accepted, with amendments — entire clause replaced)
Topic: Mandatory configurations for vehicle-mounted warning devices; sight distance requirements

VEHICLE-MOUNTED WARNING DEVICE (VMWD) — THREE ARRANGEMENTS:

ARRANGEMENT 1 — SINGLE FLASHING YELLOW LAMP OR LED LIGHT ASSEMBLY:
- Use conditions:
  - For EMERGENCY or other INFREQUENT use on a vehicle not normally used for roadworks or inspection purposes
  - For use on PLANT ITEMS working within a STATIC WORK AREA

ARRANGEMENT 2 — PAIR OF FLASHING YELLOW LAMPS OR LED LIGHT ASSEMBLIES (placed as far apart as practical):
- Use conditions:
  - For use on VEHICLES ON ALL ROADS WITHOUT THE PROTECTION of a static roadworks site
  - Positioned so that AT LEAST ONE (and preferably both) lamps are visible to all road users from ANY DIRECTION
  - ADDITIONAL flashing yellow lamps/LED assemblies MAY be added to ensure visibility in all directions

ARRANGEMENT 3 — ILLUMINATED FLASHING ARROW SIGN (see Clause 4.14.2):
- For ANY situation where options 1 or 2 are NOT appropriate
- MAY be used for any type of work including mobile works
- MAY be used in COMBINATION with Arrangements 1 or 2
- Supplementary signs (static or VMS) may be mounted with or elsewhere on the vehicle
- The illuminated flashing arrow sign MUST be capable of being REMOVED FROM VIEW (e.g. covering, folding down, or turning off) when NOT NEEDED

MOUNTING REQUIREMENTS:
- SHALL be mounted as HIGH AS PRACTICABLE on the vehicle for best visibility (e.g. on top of cab of truck)
- If cab-mounted sign could be obscured by load: may be placed near rear of vehicle
- Signs mounted on device or elsewhere on vehicle: SHALL be capable of being removed from view when not needed

MINIMUM SIGHT DISTANCE to VMWD (if not specified elsewhere):
- Speed of 60 km/h or less: minimum sight distance = 150 m
- Speed more than 60 km/h: minimum sight distance = 250 m

TRANSITION PERIOD — INSPECTION VEHICLES:
- Inspection vehicles (other than emergency/infrequent use) SHALL comply with Arrangement 2 (pair of flashing lamps) by 31 JULY 2027

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Vehicle on open road without protection of a static work area with only a single flashing lamp = non-compliance (Arrangement 2 required)
- Vehicle-mounted flashing arrow sign that cannot be turned off, covered or folded when not in use = non-compliance
- VMWD mounted at low point on vehicle (not as high as practicable) = flag
- Sight distance to VMWD less than 150 m at ≤60 km/h zone = non-compliance
- Sight distance to VMWD less than 250 m at >60 km/h zone = non-compliance
- Inspection vehicle on state-controlled road after 31 July 2027 with single flashing lamp only = non-compliance
