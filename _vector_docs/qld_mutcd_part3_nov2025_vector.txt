CHUNK 1: Authority Cards — Mandatory Training and Roles
Section: Clause 1.7
Topic: Mandatory Queensland authority cards for all TTM roles; who needs what qualification

AUTHORITY CARD REQUIREMENT:
- Person ONLY authorised to perform TTM role in Queensland if they hold AUTHORITY CARD applicable to that role
- Mandatory regulatory requirement where specified
- Obtain card by: completing prescribed training course AND meeting additional requirements
- Prescribed training delivered by TMR-approved Registered Training Organisations

PRESCRIBED TRAINING COURSES AND ROLES:

WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC AWARENESS PART 1:
- Required for: persons who work on or adjacent to a road
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

WORKING IN PROXIMITY TO TRAFFIC AWARENESS PART 2:
- Required for: persons who completed Part 1 AND select/implement work methods under short-term, low-impact works
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT IMPLEMENTATION (TMI):
- Required for: persons implementing Traffic Management Plans (TMPs) and Traffic Guidance Schemes (TGSs)
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A5

TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT DESIGN (TMD):
- Required for: persons who DESIGN, DEVELOP, REVIEW and INSPECT TMPs and TGSs
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A3

TRAFFIC CONTROLLER:
- Required for: persons performing Traffic Controller functions (stop/slow bat operations)
- Must hold APPOINTMENT as ACCREDITED PERSON under Section 21 of TORUM Act 1995
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A4

EVENT TRAFFIC MARSHAL:
- Required for: volunteers undertaking event traffic management
- Applies per QGTTM Part 8 Table A7

ROLES WITHOUT AUTHORITY CARD (must comply with QGTTM):
- Lookout Person — must complete Parts 1 and 2 of Working in Proximity training
- Roadworks Pilot Vehicle Driver
- TMA Vehicle Driver — must complete TMI training
- Authorised Person

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker performing TTM functions without visible Queensland authority card = non-compliance
- TMI worker implementing TGS without TMI training = non-compliance
- TMD worker designing TMP without TMD qualification = non-compliance
- Traffic Controller using stop/slow bat without Traffic Controller authority card = serious non-compliance
- TMA vehicle driver without TMI training = non-compliance
- Worker adjacent to live traffic without minimum Part 1 awareness training = non-compliance

CHUNK 2: RPEQ Certification — When Required for Variations
Section: Clause 1.9
Topic: Mandatory RPEQ certification process for deviations from 'shall' requirements and innovative treatments

RPEQ = Registered Professional Engineer of Queensland under Professional Engineers Act 2002 (Qld)

VARIATIONS TO 'SHOULD' RECOMMENDATIONS (Clause 1.9(a)):
- Must be DOCUMENTED through RISK ASSESSMENT demonstrating acceptability and safety
- Risk assessment SHALL be undertaken and SIGNED BY TMD OR RPEQ
- Risk assessment prepared per QGTTM Part 10 Section 2

VARIATIONS TO 'SHALL' REQUIREMENTS (Clause 1.9(b)):
- Must be DOCUMENTED through RISK ASSESSMENT
- Risk assessment SHALL be undertaken and signed by TMD or RPEQ
- BOTH risk assessment AND TMP/TGS SHALL be CERTIFIED BY RPEQ
- RPEQ certification only required for portion of TMP/TGS impacted by departure
- TMD preparing TMP/TGS SHALL email notification to TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au (information only — not approval)

VARIATIONS FOR INNOVATIVE TREATMENTS (Clause 1.9(c)):
- Risk assessment by Competent Person
- TMP/TGS AND risk assessment certified by RPEQ
- ALL innovative treatments require PRIOR APPROVAL by TMR
- Request approval: email TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au

SCOPE NOTE: Very few roadworks sites require RPEQ under (b) or (c). Examples: complex geometric changes requiring engineering design; complex diversions requiring detailed analysis (e.g. micro-simulation traffic modelling).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- TMP/TGS deviating from 'shall' requirement without documented RPEQ-certified risk assessment = serious non-compliance
- Innovative TTM treatment in use without prior TMR approval = serious non-compliance
- TMD-signed risk assessment for 'shall' variation without RPEQ certification = non-compliance
- No notification email to TMR for 'shall' variation = procedural non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Multi-Message Signs — Panel Configuration Rules
Section: Clause 4.2.2
Topic: Mandatory conditions for multi-message sign assemblies; panel limits and positioning (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MMS = signs displaying up to THREE logically related messages in single uniform modular frame

MANDATORY CONDITIONS:

(a) MINIMUM PANEL REQUIREMENT — MMS SHALL have minimum of:
- Two 600×600 mm panels; OR
- One 600×600 mm + one 1200×300 mm panel; OR
- One 1200×600 mm panel

(b) REGULATORY SIGN LIMIT — no more than ONE regulatory sign panel per MMS frame facing traffic
- EXCEPTION: Additional regulatory supplementary panels conditioning the regulatory panel allowed

(c) REGULATORY SIGN POSITION — regulatory panel SHALL be TOP POSITION, SIDE CLOSEST TO TRAFFIC
- EXCEPTION: No Left (RM2-6A(L)) or No Right (RM2-6A(R)) — installed on side where turn prohibition applies

(d) TEXT-ONLY PANELS — maximum TWO word-only panels per frame
- If 1200×600 mm space filled by two 600×600 mm word panels: at least one shall be symbolic or blank yellow panel
- EXCEPTIONS: restricted road use notices (3 word panels); POLICE CONTROL AHEAD + PREPARE TO STOP; ROAD CLOSED AHEAD + LOCAL TRAFFIC ONLY

(e) BLANK PANELS — blank retroreflective yellow panel SHALL fill any UNUSED module

(f) TM/RM/WM/GM SERIES PANELS — SHALL ONLY be used in multi-message frames (not standalone)

(g) BLANK RETROREFLECTIVE YELLOW PANEL — maximum ONE per MMS
- When used: SHOULD be located FURTHEST FROM TRAFFIC (unless arrows/direction panels required there)

(i) SPEED LIMIT AHEAD and END SPEED LIMIT panels — SHALL be TOP POSITION, SIDE CLOSEST TO TRAFFIC

SPECIAL CASE — TM5-1B DETOUR and TM5-1-Q01 DETOUR U-TURN:
- Minimum panel and blank panel requirements (a) and (g) DO NOT APPLY when using single 1200×300 TM5-1B or TM5-1-Q01 with no other relevant messages
- May use single panel as top with two blank panels below

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- MMS with only one panel, no second panel, no blank filler = non-compliance
- MMS with two or more regulatory panels facing same direction = non-compliance
- Regulatory panel NOT in top position closest to traffic = non-compliance
- MMS with three word-only panels (other than approved exceptions) = non-compliance
- Unused MMS frame position not filled with blank yellow panel = non-compliance
- TM/RM/WM/GM series panel used standalone (outside MMS frame) = non-compliance
- Two blank yellow panels in one MMS = non-compliance
- Speed Limit AHEAD or END Speed Limit panel not in top position closest to traffic = non-compliance

CHUNK 5: ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD Sign — T-Intersection Prohibition
Section: Clause 4.6.7
Topic: Restrictions on ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign use; correct alternatives at T-intersections

RESTRICTION — T-INTERSECTIONS:
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD and ROAD PLANT ON SIDE ROAD signs SHALL NOT be used on TERMINATING LEG of T-intersection to warn of activities on CONTINUING LEG
- CORRECT ALTERNATIVE at T-intersection terminating leg:
  - Use ROAD WORK AHEAD (TM1-1A or TM1-1C) OR ROAD PLANT AHEAD (TM1-3-1A or TM1-3-2C)
  - WITH direction arrow (TM5-8B)
- NOTE: MMS Lane Status panel TM10-1A may be used with or without ON SIDE ROAD message where long arrow TM5-8B not suitable

RESTRICTION — IMPACT ON THROUGH ROAD:
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign SHALL NOT be used where IMPACT FOR TRAFFIC ON THROUGH ROAD exists
- Impacts include: reduced speed zones, additional delineation, alignment/surface changes, traffic control, queueing extending onto through road
- Where impacts exist: use ROADWORK AHEAD sign instead

ON SIDE ROAD MESSAGE USAGE:
- MAY be used to warn of activity on ANOTHER ROAD intersecting with road being travelled (includes cross intersections and roundabouts)
- EXCLUDES through/straight direction even if technically another road
- SHALL NOT be used on TERMINATING LEG of T-intersection to warn of continuing leg activity

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign on terminating leg of T-intersection warning of continuing leg works = serious non-compliance
- ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD sign where works impact through road (queue extends, speed zone required) = non-compliance
- ON SIDE ROAD message used for straight-through direction = non-compliance
- T-intersection with continuing leg works not using TM1-1A/C + TM5-8B = non-compliance

CHUNK 7: END ROADWORK Sign — Mandatory Use Conditions
Section: Clauses 4.6.10, 4.6.11
Topic: When END ROADWORK signs are mandatory; complex scenario rules

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:
- END ROADWORK sign SHALL be used whenever ROADWORK AHEAD or ROADWORK NEXT X km sign used on approach
- END ROADWORK sign SHALL be used whenever BRIDGEWORK AHEAD or BRIDGEWORK NEXT X km sign used on approach
- END ROADWORK sign MAY be used whenever ROAD PLANT AHEAD or GRADER AHEAD used on approach
- EXCEPTION: where Clause 4.6.11 scenarios apply

SCENARIO A — ROADWORKS ON SIDE ROAD, NO IMPACT ON THROUGH ROAD:
- Use ROADWORK ON SIDE ROAD on through road
- END ROADWORK NOT REQUIRED on through road
- EXCEPTION: if END ROADWORK cannot be installed before end of side road = install END ROADWORK on SIDE ROAD before exiting onto through road

SCENARIO B — ROADWORKS ON SIDE ROAD IMPACTING THROUGH ROAD:
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK SHALL be used on BOTH through road AND side road

SCENARIO C — ROADWORKS ON NO THROUGH ROAD OR CUL-DE-SAC:
- END ROADWORK MAY be OMITTED on departure side (toward terminating end) when ALL apply:
  - No intersections between end of works and terminating end
  - End of road/cul-de-sac VISIBLE FROM or within 500 m of end of work area

SCENARIO D — ROADWORKS ON THROUGH ROAD, NO IMPACT ON SIDE ROAD:
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK on through road
- ROADWORK AHEAD WITH DIRECTION ARROW on side road
- NO END ROADWORK required on side road departing through road if no impact on side road/intersection

SCENARIO E — ROADWORKS ON THROUGH ROAD IMPACTING SIDE ROAD:
- ROADWORK AHEAD and END ROADWORK SHALL be on BOTH through road AND side road

IMPACTS include: reduced speed zones, additional delineation, alignment/surface changes, traffic control, queueing extending onto road

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- ROADWORK AHEAD sign without END ROADWORK at exit (unless Clause 4.6.11 exception applies) = non-compliance
- BRIDGEWORK AHEAD without END ROADWORK = non-compliance
- Works on side road impacting through road without ROADWORK AHEAD on through road = non-compliance (Scenario B, not A)
- Cul-de-sac works with END ROADWORK omitted but end >500 m from works AND has intersections = non-compliance
- Through road works impacting side road without ROADWORK AHEAD + END ROADWORK on side road = non-compliance (Scenario E, not D)

CHUNK 19: Vehicle-Mounted Warning Devices — Three Arrangements
Section: Clause 4.14.1
Topic: Mandatory VMWD configurations; sight distance requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

THREE ARRANGEMENTS:

ARRANGEMENT 1 — SINGLE FLASHING YELLOW LAMP OR LED:
- For EMERGENCY or INFREQUENT use on vehicle not normally used for roadworks/inspection
- For PLANT ITEMS working within STATIC WORK AREA

ARRANGEMENT 2 — PAIR OF FLASHING YELLOW LAMPS OR LED (as far apart as practical):
- For VEHICLES ON ALL ROADS WITHOUT PROTECTION of static roadworks site
- Positioned so AT LEAST ONE (preferably both) visible to all road users from ANY DIRECTION
- ADDITIONAL lamps/LEDs MAY be added for visibility in all directions

ARRANGEMENT 3 — ILLUMINATED FLASHING ARROW SIGN (Clause 4.14.2):
- For ANY situation where options 1 or 2 NOT appropriate
- MAY be used for any work type including mobile works
- MAY be used in COMBINATION with Arrangements 1 or 2
- MUST be capable of being REMOVED FROM VIEW (covering, folding, turning off) when NOT NEEDED

MOUNTING:
- SHALL be mounted as HIGH AS PRACTICABLE on vehicle for best visibility (e.g. top of cab)
- If cab-mounted sign obscured by load: may be near rear of vehicle

MINIMUM SIGHT DISTANCE to VMWD:
- Speed ≤60 km/h: minimum 150 m
- Speed >60 km/h: minimum 250 m

TRANSITION PERIOD — INSPECTION VEHICLES:
- Inspection vehicles (other than emergency/infrequent use) SHALL comply with Arrangement 2 (pair of lamps) by 31 JULY 2027

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Vehicle on open road without static work area protection with only single flashing lamp = non-compliance (Arrangement 2 required)
- Vehicle-mounted flashing arrow sign that cannot be turned off, covered or folded when not in use = non-compliance
- VMWD mounted at low point (not as high as practicable) = flag
- Sight distance to VMWD <150 m at ≤60 km/h zone = non-compliance
- Sight distance to VMWD <250 m at >60 km/h zone = non-compliance
- Inspection vehicle on state-controlled road after 31 July 2027 with single lamp only = non-compliance

CHUNK 21: Variable Message Signs — Alternating Screen Timing
Section: Clause 4.22.3
Topic: Technical requirement for alternating screen timing in VMS (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

ALTERNATING SCREENS:
- 'On' time of each screen SHALL be (0.6 ± 0.1) s per word or number
- TOTAL TIME to read message on BOTH screens taken into account when determining message length and letter height
- Letter height calculation: use AS 1742.2 procedure; apply letter series most nearly matching on-screen font; recommended calculated letter height be DOUBLED

VISUAL INDICATORS:

CHUNK 22: Electronic Signs — Display Colour Rules
Section: Clause 4.24
Topic: Mandatory colour scheme for electronic signs at roadworks

ELECTRONIC SIGNS — GENERAL:
- MAY substitute for static signs
- Electronic variable speed limit signs: SHALL comply with AS 5156
- Variable message signs: SHALL comply with MRTS229 Electronic Traffic Control Signs AND MRTS262 Temporary Variable Message Signs (as appropriate)

DISPLAY PRINCIPLES — all electronic signs SHALL comply:
(a) Symbols/signs in REVERSED COLOUR SCHEME to appropriate static sign
(b) Legends in REVERSED COLOUR SCHEME to appropriate static sign:

  - ORANGE text: for LINE MARKERS AHEAD message
  - YELLOW text: for LINE MARKING AHEAD message
(c) Annulus on speed limit sign: SHALL be RED with speed limit in WHITE legend

- Electronic sign displaying general roadwork message in colour other than yellow = non-compliance

CHUNK 31: Key Compliance Summary — Personnel and Documentation
Section: Clauses 1.3.2, 1.3.19, 1.7, 1.9
Topic: Consolidated personnel qualifications and documentation requirements

PERSONNEL COMPLIANCE:
- All TTM workers SHALL hold valid Queensland authority cards for their roles (Clause 1.7)
- Traffic Controllers SHALL hold appointment under TORUM Act s.21 (Clause 1.3.19)
- All TTM activities SHALL be per QGTTM Part 8 Appendix A (Clause 1.3.2)
- Inspection vehicles SHALL comply with Arrangement 2 (pair of lamps) by 31 JULY 2027 (Clause 4.14.1)

DOCUMENTATION FOR VARIATIONS:
- 'Should' deviation: risk assessment signed by TMD or RPEQ (Clause 1.9(a))
- 'Shall' deviation: risk assessment signed by TMD or RPEQ + TMP/TGS certified by RPEQ + email TMR (Clause 1.9(b))
- Innovative treatment: risk assessment by Competent Person + TMP/TGS certified by RPEQ + prior TMR approval (Clause 1.9(c))
- All risk assessments SHALL be per QGTTM Part 10 Section 2 (Clause 1.9)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker performing TTM without valid Queensland authority card = non-compliance
- Traffic Controller without TORUM Act s.21 appointment = serious non-compliance
- TMP/TGS with 'shall' variations without RPEQ certification = serious non-compliance
- Innovation in TTM without prior TMR approval = serious non-compliance
- No documentation for variations from 'shall' or 'should' requirements = non-compliance
