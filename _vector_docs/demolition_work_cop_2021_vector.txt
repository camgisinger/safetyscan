CHUNK 1: Notifiable Demolition Work — Regulator Notification Requirements
Section: CoP s3.1; WHS Regulation s142
Topic: Which demolition work requires prior written notice to the regulator (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY NOTIFICATION — written notice to WHSQ regulator AT LEAST FIVE DAYS before work commences for:

(a) Demolition of structure or load-bearing element AT LEAST 6 METRES IN HEIGHT
(b) Demolition work involving LOAD SHIFTING MACHINERY ON A SUSPENDED FLOOR
(c) Demolition work involving EXPLOSIVES

HEIGHT MEASUREMENT: from LOWEST LEVEL OF GROUND immediately adjacent to base of structure to its HIGHEST POINT.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of structure 6m+ in height without evidence of regulator notification = serious non-compliance
- Load shifting machinery present on suspended floor during demolition without notification documentation = non-compliance
- Explosives on site for demolition without notification or licensing evidence = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 2: WHS Management Plan — Construction Project Requirements
Section: CoP s3.2; WHS Regulation s293, s308–s315
Topic: Principal contractor duties for projects $250,000 or more

CONSTRUCTION PROJECT exists where construction work costs $250,000 OR MORE.

ONLY ONE principal contractor per project.

PRINCIPAL CONTRACTOR MUST:
- Prepare and review a WHS Management Plan
- Obtain a copy of each SWMS before high risk construction work commences
- Install signs showing: principal contractor's name, contact details and location of site office
- Secure the construction workplace
- Put in place arrangements for: falls, facilities, first aid, emergency plan, traffic management

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No WHS Management Plan present on site for projects $250,000+ = non-compliance
- Signs absent identifying principal contractor name and contact details = non-compliance
- High risk construction work commencing without SWMS sighted by principal contractor = non-compliance
- Construction workplace not secured against unauthorised access = non-compliance

CHUNK 3: Safe Work Method Statements (SWMS) — High Risk Demolition Work
Section: CoP s3.4; WHS Regulation s299, s300
Topic: When SWMS is required and compliance obligations

SWMS REQUIRED FOR HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK including:
- Demolition of load-bearing or structural integrity elements
- Work with fall risk MORE THAN 2 METRES
- Work involving or likely to involve DISTURBANCE OF ASBESTOS
- Work ON OR NEAR energised electrical installations
- Work involving EXPLOSIVES
- Work involving LOAD SHIFTING MACHINERY ON A SUSPENDED FLOOR
- Work ON OR NEAR pressurised gas distribution mains or piping
- Work ON OR NEAR chemical, fuel or refrigerant lines
- Work near trench with excavated depth GREATER THAN 1.5 METRES
- Work where risk of MOBILE POWERED PLANT COLLIDING with pedestrians or other plant

COMPLIANCE: If high risk construction work is NOT being carried out in accordance with the SWMS:
- Work MUST BE STOPPED immediately (or as soon as safe to do so)
- Work MUST NOT BE RESUMED until it is in accordance with the SWMS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- High risk demolition work commencing without a completed SWMS present = serious non-compliance
- Work continuing after deviation from SWMS without stopping and reviewing = non-compliance
- SWMS present but does not identify the specific hazards, risks and controls for the activities being performed = inadequate

CHUNK 4: Asbestos Removal Licensing — Class A, Class B and Unlicensed Work
Section: CoP s3.5, s3.6; WHS Regulation s458
Topic: Licensing requirements for asbestos removal (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

CLASS A LICENCE — can remove:
- Any amount of friable asbestos or ACM
- Any amount of asbestos contaminated dust or debris (ACD)
- Any amount of non-friable asbestos or ACM

CLASS B LICENCE — can remove:
- Any amount of non-friable asbestos or ACM
- ACD associated with removal of non-friable asbestos or ACM

NO LICENCE REQUIRED — can remove:
- Up to 10 m² of non-friable asbestos or ACM
- ACD associated with removal of less than 10 m² of non-friable asbestos or ACM (if not associated with friable asbestos and only a minor contamination)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Asbestos removal underway without licensed asbestos removalist on site = serious non-compliance
- More than 10 m² of non-friable asbestos being removed without at least Class B licence = serious non-compliance
- Any friable asbestos removal without Class A licence = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Adjacent Buildings — Lateral Support and Protection Requirements
Section: CoP s3.7
Topic: Obligations to protect adjacent structures from structural impact and vibration

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- No part of demolition process should ADVERSELY AFFECT THE STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY of any other building
- LATERAL SUPPORT for adjoining structures must be EQUAL TO OR GREATER THAN that provided by the structure being demolished
- Before existing lateral support is disturbed, TEMPORARY SUPPORTS must be erected and checked for effectiveness as demolition proceeds
- Other buildings must not be adversely affected by VIBRATION or CONCUSSION
- No part of demolition process should cause FLOODING or WATER PENETRATION to an adjoining building

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition adjacent to other occupied buildings with no shoring, propping or lateral support installed = non-compliance
- Demolition by demolition equipment creating significant vibration near sensitive facilities without vibration monitoring = flag
- Evidence of water penetration or flooding into adjoining property from demolition site = non-compliance

CHUNK 6: Essential Services — Disconnection and Underground Services Location
Section: CoP s3.8; WHS Regulation s304, s305
Topic: Requirements for identifying, disconnecting and managing essential services

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- All electric, gas, water, sewer, steam and other service lines NOT required in demolition process must be SHUT OFF, CAPPED or OTHERWISE CONTROLLED at or outside the building line BEFORE demolition work starts
- The relevant utility agency MUST be notified in advance
- Services retained for demolition work must be ADEQUATELY PROTECTED

UNDERGROUND ESSENTIAL SERVICES:
Before work starts, PCBU must:
- Take ALL REASONABLE STEPS to get CURRENT underground essential services information for the work area AND adjacent areas
- Contact DIAL BEFORE YOU DIG — phone 1100 or online enquiry at www.1100.com.au
- Contact relevant authorities: electricity supply authorities, communication companies, local government, water authorities

UNDERGROUND ESSENTIAL SERVICES INFORMATION MUST BE:
- Made available to any worker, principal contractor and subcontractors
- Retained until excavation work is completed, OR if there is a notifiable incident: for TWO YEARS after the incident

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No evidence of Dial Before You Dig enquiry on site = non-compliance for underground services
- Essential services not isolated or capped before demolition commences = serious non-compliance
- No SWMS for work near energised electrical installations = non-compliance
- Workers excavating near underground services without locating equipment or potholing = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 7: Asbestos — Pre-Demolition Removal and Register Requirements
Section: CoP s4.2; WHS Regulation s422, s426, s448, s449, s450, s451, s458
Topic: Asbestos identification, assumption, registers and mandatory pre-demolition removal

IDENTIFICATION (WHS Regulation s451):
- PCBU with management or control MUST ensure ALL asbestos or ACM at the workplace (or assumed present) is IDENTIFIED BY A COMPETENT PERSON

ASSUMPTION OF ASBESTOS — PCBU carrying out demolition MUST ASSUME asbestos or ACM is present if:
- The competent person is, on reasonable grounds, UNCERTAIN whether asbestos is fixed to or installed in the structure, OR
- Part of the structure is INACCESSIBLE and likely to be disturbed

PRE-DEMOLITION REMOVAL — MANDATORY:
- ALL asbestos likely to be disturbed MUST be identified and, so far as reasonably practicable, REMOVED BEFORE demolition starts
- If only part of a building is being demolished: only asbestos likely to be disturbed in that part needs to be removed before that part commences

ASBESTOS REGISTER REQUIREMENTS:
- PCBU carrying out demolition must OBTAIN A COPY OF THE ASBESTOS REGISTER from the person with management or control before commencing
- Person with management or control must: (a) review and revise register before demolition, and (b) give a copy to the demolition PCBU before work starts

IF NO ASBESTOS REGISTER EXISTS:
- PCBU must NOT carry out the work until the structure has been inspected by a COMPETENT PERSON

DISPOSAL:
- Asbestos removal MUST be carried out by a LICENSED ASBESTOS REMOVALIST
- Asbestos waste can ONLY be disposed of at a SITE LICENSED BY THE EPA
- MUST NEVER be disposed of in the general waste system

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition proceeding without asbestos register obtained = serious non-compliance
- No asbestos identification by competent person before demolition = serious non-compliance
- Asbestos disturbed or removed without licensed removalist present = serious non-compliance
- Asbestos waste in general waste bins or skips = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 8: Lead, PCBs and Synthetic Mineral Fibres — Hazardous Material Controls
Section: CoP s4.2
Topic: Specific controls for lead, polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) and synthetic mineral fibres

LEAD:
Sources of lead by construction date:
- 1920–1978: Lead-based PAINT
- 1920–1978: Lead PLUMBING fittings, pipes
- 1923–1986: Automobile EXHAUST ACCUMULATION (ceiling dust)

TESTING: If lead-based paint is suspected, a test MUST be conducted. Testing can identify dried paint film with MORE THAN 1% LEAD (by weight) as lead-containing paint. Refer to AS 4361.1.

POLYCHLORINATED BIPHENYLS (PCBs):
Workers may be exposed when dismantling electrical capacitors and transformers or cleaning up spills.

CONTROL MEASURES for PCBs:
- Place equipment or parts in a polyethylene bag, then into a marked sealable METAL CONTAINER
- PPE: gloves made of materials RESISTANT TO PCBs — polyethylene, nitrile rubber or neoprene

SYNTHETIC MINERAL FIBRES:
Used extensively in building insulation (walls, ceilings, air-conditioning ductwork).

CONTROL MEASURES:
- SUPPRESS DUST by damping down

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of pre-1980 building without lead paint testing documented = flag
- Workers removing old electrical transformers or capacitors without PCB-resistant gloves = non-compliance
- Insulation removal in progress without dust suppression or respiratory protection = flag
- Ceiling void dust present in pre-1987 building without contamination assessment = flag

CHUNK 9: Securing the Work Area — Exclusion Zones and Public Protection
Section: CoP s4.3
Topic: Requirements for exclusion zones, barricading and overhead protection (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

EXCLUSION ZONES REQUIRED DURING:
- Stripping, removal and/or dropping of debris
- Operation of demolition plant or equipment
- Pre-weakening activities for deliberate collapse
- Deliberate collapse or pulling over of buildings or structures

EXCLUSION ZONE PLANNING MUST INCLUDE:
- Erecting SECURE IMPASSABLE BARRICADES with adequate signage and appropriate lock-out procedures
- Providing SUPERVISION so no unauthorised person enters an exclusion zone

PUBLIC ACCESS AND PROTECTION:
- Where demolition is adjacent to a public place with risk of falling debris or hazardous noise: a method of protection must be:
  (a) Erected BEFORE commencement of demolition work
  (b) Kept in position AT ALL TIMES during the work
  (c) Regularly inspected and maintained

OVERHEAD PROTECTIVE STRUCTURES:
- Must be provided for public walkways in conjunction with perimeter fencing
- Must be designed to WITHSTAND AN APPROPRIATE LOAD

WORKPLACE SECURITY:
- PCBU must ensure, so far as reasonably practicable, the workplace is SECURED to prevent unauthorised access

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition debris falling or able to fall into public areas without exclusion zones = serious non-compliance
- No hoarding or security fencing separating demolition site from public access = serious non-compliance
- Overhead protective structure absent for public walkway adjacent to demolition = non-compliance
- No signage on barricades identifying exclusion zones = non-compliance
- Unauthorised persons able to freely access demolition workplace = non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Emergency Plans
Section: CoP s4.4; WHS Regulation s43
Topic: Requirements for emergency plans at demolition workplaces

MANDATORY REQUIREMENT:
- A PCBU at a demolition workplace MUST ensure there is an EMERGENCY PLAN for the workplace

EMERGENCY PLAN MUST PROVIDE FOR:
1. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES including:
   - Effective response to an emergency
   - Evacuation procedures
   - Notifying emergency service organisations at the EARLIEST OPPORTUNITY
   - Medical treatment and assistance
   - Effective communication between the person authorised to coordinate the emergency response and all people at the workplace
2. TESTING of emergency procedures — including the FREQUENCY of testing
3. INFORMATION, TRAINING AND INSTRUCTION to relevant workers in implementing emergency procedures

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No written emergency plan available at the demolition workplace = non-compliance
- Workers unaware of emergency procedures or evacuation routes = non-compliance
- No evidence of emergency procedure testing (drill records) = flag

CHUNK 11: Plant and Equipment — Operator Protective Structures and Licensing
Section: CoP s4.5, s4.6; WHS Regulation s206
Topic: Requirements for plant protective devices and licensing (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

OPERATOR PROTECTIVE STRUCTURES — MANDATORY:
Powered mobile plant used for demolition work must be fitted with a SUITABLE COMBINATION of operator protective devices, typically:
- FALLING OBJECT PROTECTIVE STRUCTURE (FOPS)
- OPERATOR RESTRAINTS (e.g. seat belts)
- ROLL-OVER PROTECTIVE STRUCTURE (ROPS)

OPERATOR PROTECTIVE STRUCTURES must:
- Allow operator to EXIT FROM THE CABIN DURING AN EMERGENCY — exits must be maintained and cleaned regularly
- Windows and openings: protected against penetrating objects by wire mesh, steel bars or suitable polycarbonate material

IF A DOOR IS FITTED: door must be CLOSED while machine is operating.

HIGH RISK WORK LICENCE required for:
- Some cranes
- Elevating work platforms (EWPs)
- Forklifts

CRANES used to hold loads to be cut: rated capacity must be NOT LESS THAN 1.5 TIMES THE ASSESSED LOAD.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavator or mobile plant without FOPS operating in demolition environment = serious non-compliance
- Machine door open while operating = non-compliance
- Crane, EWP or forklift operator unable to produce high risk work licence = serious non-compliance
- Crane lifting loads without load capacity calculations documented = non-compliance
- Plant operating on uneven or uncompacted ground without assessment = flag

CHUNK 12: Removal of Debris — Drop Zones, Chutes and Floor Openings
Section: CoP s4.7
Topic: Management of debris including chutes and floor openings (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Debris must be PROGRESSIVELY REMOVED to prevent build-up that could affect integrity of a suspended floor, affect workplace entry and exit, become a fire hazard, or cause a health and safety hazard

FALLING DEBRIS CONTROL:
- Demolished materials MUST NOT be allowed to fall freely UNLESS confined within a chute or similar enclosure, shaft and/or exclusion zone

MULTI-STOREY INTERNAL FLOOR OPENINGS (e.g. lift shafts, debris drop zones):
AT THE WORKING LEVEL:
- Each opening must be protected by an ADEQUATE VEHICLE BUFFER during removal of debris by mobile plant
- Must be GUARDED BY SUITABLE BARRIERS at all other times
- Vehicle buffers must be HIGH ENOUGH to prevent mobile plant riding over them and SOLID ENOUGH to stop fully loaded mobile plant

AT ALL LEVELS BELOW THE WORKING LEVEL:
- Access to area through or onto which material is falling must be PREVENTED either by:
  (a) Sealing off the opening with guarding from floor to ceiling, OR
  (b) Erecting signs and barricades to prevent persons coming near the openings

DEBRIS CHUTES:
- Vertical chutes must be FULLY ENCLOSED with a cover or barrier at the top to PREVENT A PERSON FALLING INTO THE CHUTE
- Overhead demolition must CEASE during removal of debris bins
- Signs warning of risk from falling or ejected material must be placed at the DISCHARGE END of every chute

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Debris allowed to fall freely outside exclusion zones or chutes = serious non-compliance
- Debris accumulating on suspended floors without progressive removal = non-compliance
- Debris chute without top cover preventing persons falling in = non-compliance
- No vehicle buffer at floor opening during mobile plant debris removal = non-compliance
- No barricading below active debris drop zone = serious non-compliance
- No signage at discharge end of debris chute = non-compliance

CHUNK 13: Falls — Hierarchy of Fall Controls
Section: CoP s4.8; WHS Regulation s78
Topic: Hierarchy of fall controls for demolition work (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK threshold: work involving risk of a person falling MORE THAN 2 METRES = high risk construction work; SWMS must be prepared before commencing.

HIERARCHY OF FALL CONTROLS — in order (next control can only be used if previous is not reasonably practicable):

1. CARRY OUT WORK ON THE GROUND or solid construction
2. FALL PREVENTION DEVICE — e.g. temporary work platforms, guard rails, secure fencing, edge protection, covers
3. WORK POSITIONING SYSTEM — e.g. industrial rope access systems
4. FALL ARREST SYSTEM — e.g. catch platforms, industrial safety nets, safety harness system

SOLID CONSTRUCTION must have:
- Surface structurally capable of supporting all persons and things
- Barriers around perimeter and any openings to prevent a fall
- Even and readily negotiable surface and gradient
- Safe means of entry and exit

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers at height above 2m without any fall prevention measures = serious non-compliance
- Harness system used as primary fall control without establishing that fall prevention devices and work positioning systems were not reasonably practicable = flag
- Unguarded floor openings with fall-through potential = non-compliance
- No SWMS for demolition work where fall of more than 2m is possible = non-compliance
- Edge protection absent at perimeter of partially demolished structure = non-compliance

CHUNK 14: Electricity — Disconnection and Isolation Requirements
Section: CoP s4.9
Topic: Electrical disconnection requirements before demolition

HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK: Construction work carried out ON OR NEAR energised electrical installations or services — SWMS must be prepared before this work commences.

MANDATORY CONTROLS:
- Before demolition commences: ALL LIVE ELECTRICAL WIRING AND COMPONENTS (apart from temporary electrical installations provided for the work) must be DISCONNECTED, ISOLATED or clearly marked and rendered safe by a COMPETENT PERSON — e.g. licensed electrical worker or, where necessary, the local electrical supply authority

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Live electrical services not disconnected before demolition commences = serious non-compliance
- Demolition equipment operating near overhead powerlines without exclusion zone or spotter = serious non-compliance
- No SWMS for demolition work near energised electrical installations = non-compliance
- Electrical work being carried out by unlicensed person = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 15: Fire Prevention — Equipment and Welding/Cutting Controls
Section: CoP s4.10
Topic: Fire prevention equipment requirements and hot work controls

GENERAL FIRE PREVENTION:
- Adequate fire prevention equipment must be provided and maintained AT ALL TIMES during demolition
- If a SPRINKLER SYSTEM is installed: must be maintained in operable condition at each storey, so far as reasonably practicable
- Portable fire extinguishers must be kept in working areas AT ALL TIMES and maintained in operable condition

WELDING AND CUTTING — FIRE HAZARDS:
- Where floor, walls or ground cover are combustible: protect by SPRAYING WITH WATER, SPREADING DAMP SAND, laying fireproof blankets or other suitable means
- Where serious fire might quickly develop: a FIRE SPOTTER should be assigned to the area
- Fire extinguishing equipment must be readily available and ALL WORKERS trained in its use

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No portable fire extinguisher in working areas = non-compliance
- Sprinkler system disconnected without alternative fire suppression in place = flag
- Hot work (welding/cutting) adjacent to combustible materials without fire suppression measures = non-compliance
- No fire spotter present when serious fire risk exists during cutting operations = non-compliance
- Workers not trained in use of fire extinguishers = non-compliance

CHUNK 16: Manual Demolition — Roofs, Walls and Floors
Section: CoP s4.11
Topic: Controls specific to manual demolition including fragile roofs (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

GENERAL:
- Manual demolition should only be considered if it is NOT REASONABLY PRACTICABLE to use mechanical demolition

FRAGILE ROOFS:
Brittle or fragile roofing materials include: asbestos cement, cellulose cement, glass panels, fibreglass, acrylic, or other similar synthetic moulded/fabricated material.

When working on or adjacent to fragile roofs:
- Complete work from a TEMPORARY WORK PLATFORM
- Provide temporary WALKWAYS for entry and exit
- On HIGH PITCH ROOFS (slope exceeding 1:6): secure and fix CLEATS to walkways
- On STEEP ROOFS (exceeding 35 degrees): provide temporary ROOF LADDERS

PURLIN TROLLEYS:
- Must have a HOLDING BRAKE
- Must have a device to prevent ACCIDENTAL DISLODGMENT from purlins
- If workers are supported by trolley: must have SUITABLE SAFETY HARNESS ANCHORAGE POINTS

MANUAL DEMOLITION OF WALLS:
- Glass must be removed from windows, doors and openings BEFORE demolition commences
- Walls and gables must be demolished COURSE BY COURSE
- Work must be performed from SAFE WORKING PLATFORMS
- Workers must NOT work from the TOP OF A WALL being demolished

MANUAL DEMOLITION OF FLOORS:
- Floor openings must be PROPERLY GUARDED or BOARDED OVER and boarding SECURED against accidental removal
- Covers/boarding must be of SUFFICIENT STRENGTH to withstand expected loads

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers demolishing walls by standing on top of the wall = non-compliance
- Floor openings not boarded over or guarded = non-compliance
- Workers on fragile roofs without fall protection or temporary walkways = non-compliance
- Purlin trolleys in use without holding brake or dislodgement prevention = non-compliance
- Glass not removed from windows before wall demolition commences = non-compliance

CHUNK 17: Mechanical Demolition — Operator Protective Structures and Suspended Floors
Section: CoP s4.12
Topic: Requirements for mechanical demolition including FOPS and suspended floor operations (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

OPERATOR PROTECTIVE STRUCTURES — MANDATORY:
Powered mobile plant used for demolition work must be fitted with a SUITABLE COMBINATION of operator protective devices, typically:
- FALLING OBJECT PROTECTIVE STRUCTURE (FOPS)
- OPERATOR RESTRAINTS (e.g. seat belts)
- ROLL-OVER PROTECTIVE STRUCTURE (ROPS)

IF A DOOR IS FITTED: door must be CLOSED while machine is operating.

WORKING ON SUSPENDED FLOORS:
- If powered mobile plant will operate on a suspended floor: a COMPETENT PERSON (e.g. structural engineer) must VERIFY AND DOCUMENT:
  (a) Type, size, weight and usage of specified plant
  (b) Floor is capable of sustaining static and live loads of plant (including attachments and demolished materials) without excessive deformation or collapse
- Plant must be moved between suspended floor slabs by HOIST EQUIPMENT or APPROPRIATELY FABRICATED RAMP
- If load-shifting equipment is to be used on suspended floors: NOTIFICATION TO REGULATOR is required (at least 5 days before)

LOAD SHIFTING EQUIPMENT must, so far as reasonably practicable, be LOCATED ON A BEAM.

DEMOLITION OF WALLS (mechanical):
- At least 900 MM OF WALL must be LEFT INTACT above floor level to provide a PROTECTIVE BARRIER at the perimeter of the building and around lift wells, stairwells, light wells and other places where persons or objects could fall

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Excavator or mobile plant without FOPS operating in demolition environment = serious non-compliance
- Machine door open while operating = non-compliance
- Plant operating on suspended floor without structural engineer's written floor capacity verification = serious non-compliance
- Less than 900mm of wall left intact above floor level when using mechanical demolition at perimeter = non-compliance
- Workers in exclusion zone while shears operating = serious non-compliance
- Plant overloaded beyond manufacturer's rated capacity = non-compliance

CHUNK 18: Induced Collapse — Wire Rope Pulling and Pre-Weakening
Section: CoP s4.13
Topic: Requirements for induced collapse including wire rope pulling (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- Expert advice must be sought from a COMPETENT PERSON (e.g. structural engineer) BEFORE this method is used
- Induced collapse must ONLY be used on DETACHED, ISOLATED STRUCTURES on REASONABLY LEVEL SITES
- SUFFICIENT CLEAR SPACE must be available into which collapsing material will fall
- Explosives MUST NOT be used to induce collapse UNLESS APPROVED BY THE REGULATORY AUTHORITY

WIRE ROPE, SLINGS AND CHAIN PULLING:
- ROPE/CHAIN LENGTH: must be long enough to ensure HORIZONTAL DISTANCE from the demolition work to the pulling medium is AT LEAST TWICE THE HEIGHT OF THE HIGHEST PART TO BE PULLED
- No person in a position where they could be struck by the wire rope, sling or chain in event of failure
- Plant operator must be PROTECTED from rope breakage and flying debris
- VERTICAL REINFORCING BARS must NOT be cut until after the wall has been pulled over

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Induced collapse method used without structural engineer involvement = serious non-compliance
- Insufficient clear space for collapse zone = serious non-compliance
- Horizontal distance to pulling medium less than twice the height of structure being pulled = non-compliance
- Persons present in collapse zone during induced collapse = serious non-compliance
- Reinforcing bars cut before wall pulled over = non-compliance
- Explosives used for induced collapse without regulatory approval = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 19: Explosives in Demolition — Licensing and Notification
Section: CoP s4.14
Topic: Requirements for use of explosives in demolition

MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
- Construction work involving use of explosives = HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK; SWMS must be prepared BEFORE work commences
- Explosives MUST NOT be used to induce collapse of a structure UNLESS APPROVED BY THE REGULATORY AUTHORITY
- Explosives must ONLY be used by a COMPETENT PERSON who is LICENSED in the use of explosives AND has experience in the work
- A licensed competent person must develop and have AUTHORISED a BLAST MANAGEMENT PLAN prior to blasting starting
- A NOTIFICATION MUST BE MADE TO THE REGULATOR if explosives are planned (at least 5 days before commencement)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Explosives on demolition site without evidence of regulatory approval and notification = serious non-compliance
- Explosives being handled or used by unlicensed person = serious non-compliance
- No blast management plan documented before blasting = serious non-compliance
- No SWMS for demolition work involving explosives = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 20: Pre/Post-Tensioned Concrete — Tendon Management
Section: CoP s5.1
Topic: Requirements for demolishing pre-tensioned and post-tensioned concrete

KEY RISK:
- Tensioned tendons require CONTROLLED REMOVAL because the HIGH LEVEL OF POTENTIAL ENERGY stored in the tendons poses a risk to health and safety of workers
- Tendons can be subject to CORROSION that weakens them — damage is NOT USUALLY EVIDENT EXTERNALLY

CONTROL MEASURES:
- Use STEEL PLATES or other restraint measures at locations adjacent to pedestrian areas or where concrete cover is reduced — to minimise risk of personal injury or property damage from unexpected release of stored energy

A COMPETENT PERSON must advise on the demolition sequence.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of post-tensioned or pre-tensioned concrete without structural engineer involvement = serious non-compliance
- No restraint measures in place adjacent to pedestrian areas during tensioned element demolition = non-compliance
- Tendons being cut without controlled release procedure in place = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 21: Fire-Damaged and Structurally Unsound Buildings
Section: CoP s5.2
Topic: Assessment requirements for fire-damaged or structurally unsound structures

ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENT:
- Before demolition: assessment must be undertaken to identify ASBESTOS, HAZARDOUS MATERIALS and STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY ISSUES
- PCBU must request a WRITTEN REPORT by a COMPETENT PERSON specifying:
  (a) Hazards associated with the design and current state of the structure
  (b) Control measures to be applied to the demolition

CONTROL MEASURES:
- Where possible: fire-damaged, ruinous or structurally unsound structures should be demolished by MECHANICAL MEANS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Demolition of fire-damaged or ruinous structure without written structural assessment = serious non-compliance
- Manual demolition used on structurally unsound building where mechanical means could be used = flag
- Workers entering fire-damaged building without hazardous materials assessment = non-compliance

CHUNK 22: Chimneys, Precast Panels and Facade Retention
Section: CoP s5.6, s5.8, s5.9
Topic: Requirements for demolishing chimneys, precast panels and retained facades (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

INDEPENDENT CHIMNEYS AND SPIRES:
- Hand demolition: must be carried out PROGRESSIVELY FROM THE TOP and from SAFE WORKING PLATFORMS
- INDUCED COLLAPSE requires clear space of approximately 1.5 TIMES THE TOTAL HEIGHT OF THE CHIMNEY (of sufficient width)

PRECAST CONCRETE PANELS:
- ORIGINAL LIFTING POINTS must NOT be reused if they have DETERIORATED OR CORRODED
- Before removing an individual panel: must be FULLY SUPPORTED, both vertically and horizontally, ABOVE ITS CENTRE OF MASS, to prevent sudden rotational movement during detachment

FACADE RETENTION:
- Retention must be PLANNED AND DOCUMENTED before demolition commences
- A COMPETENT PERSON (e.g. structural engineer) may need to supervise during various stages to monitor facade movement or cracking
- Structure must be inspected after: HEAVY RAIN OR WIND, EARTH TREMOR, or ACCIDENTAL IMPACT on facade or supports

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Chimney hand demolition not proceeding top-down = non-compliance
- Precast panel removed using deteriorated/corroded original lifting points = serious non-compliance
- Precast panel removed without full vertical and horizontal support = serious non-compliance
- Facade retention proceeding without documented plan and engineer involvement = non-compliance
- Facade not inspected after extreme weather event = non-compliance

CHUNK 23: Storage Tanks and Pipelines — Demolition Controls
Section: CoP s5.10
Topic: Requirements for demolishing storage tanks and pipelines

PRE-DEMOLITION:
- Delivery lines and vent pipes must be PURGED
- Tank must be EMPTIED and CERTIFIED BY A COMPETENT PERSON as being free of gas, flammable vapours or other hazardous chemicals

GENERAL PRECAUTIONS:
- HOT WORK (welding, oxyacetylene cutting) MUST NOT be carried out where there is a chance flammable material may be present as a result of leakage/spillage OR after cleaning out the tank/pipe

CONTAINERS THAT HAVE HELD FLAMMABLE OR COMBUSTIBLE MATERIAL:
A container is considered UNSAFE until confirmed otherwise by a COMPETENT PERSON if it has held:
- Petrol, kerosene, solvents or light oils
- Acids and alkalines (can react with metal to produce explosive or toxic gases)
- Heavy oils, tars or solids (can release combustible gases when exposed to heat)
- Flammable solids (finely divided particles may form explosive dust cloud)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Hot work (welding, cutting) on storage tank or pipeline without competent person certification of gas-free status = serious non-compliance
- Underground tank excavation without soil contamination check = non-compliance
- Workers entering storage tank for demolition work without confined space assessment = serious non-compliance
- Flammable waste from tanks entering stormwater drainage = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 24: PPE — Personal Protective Equipment in Demolition
Section: CoP s1.2, s4.2, s4.11, s4.12, s4.13; WHS Act s28; WHS Regulation s46
Topic: PPE requirements applicable to demolition workers

TYPICAL PPE FOR DEMOLITION:
- HARD HAT (safety helmet) — mandatory for all demolition work
- HIGH VISIBILITY VEST — mandatory in construction zones and near mobile plant
- SAFETY BOOTS — mandatory
- EYE PROTECTION — when working with power tools, in areas with falling debris, or near hazardous dust
- HEARING PROTECTION — when noise at or near the exposure standard (LAeq,8h of 85 dB(A) or LC,peak of 140 dB(C))
- RESPIRATORY PROTECTION — when exposed to dust (including crystalline silica, lead dust, asbestos), chemical vapours or fumes
- GLOVES — for manual demolition, hazardous chemical handling, PCB-resistant gloves (polyethylene, nitrile rubber or neoprene) for PCB exposure
- FALL PROTECTION EQUIPMENT (harness, lanyard, inertia reel) — when working at heights where fall arrest is required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers on demolition site without hard hat = serious non-compliance
- Workers near mobile plant without high visibility vest = non-compliance
- Jack hammer or power tool operators without hearing protection = non-compliance
- Workers in dusty demolition environment without respiratory protection = flag
- Asbestos removal workers without appropriate respiratory protection = serious non-compliance
- Workers handling PCBs without PCB-resistant gloves = non-compliance
