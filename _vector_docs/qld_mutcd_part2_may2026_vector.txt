CHUNK 1: Innovative Treatments — TMR Approval Requirement
Section: Clause 1.4.15
Topic: Approval process for new or innovative traffic control devices and treatments

MANDATORY APPROVAL:
- New or improved devices, treatments or practices require APPROVAL BY TMR PRIOR to use or adoption (Clause 1.4.15)
- For TRIALS of new or innovative traffic control devices: submit written submission to TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au
- Submission must comply with Clause 1.13 of Queensland MUTCD Part 1

COMPLIANCE:
- Using a traffic control device or treatment not in the Queensland MUTCD or AS 1742.2-2022 without TMR approval = non-compliance
- Site-specific innovation on construction workzone TTM layouts requires pre-approval

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unusual or non-standard traffic control device on construction site = verify TMR approval exists
- Temporary traffic control arrangements that deviate from Queensland MUTCD standard layouts = flag for verification of prior approval

CHUNK 30: Wildlife Warning Signs — Installation Requirements
Section: Clause 4.11.6.1
Topic: When wildlife warning signs must be installed on Queensland roads

WILDLIFE WARNING SIGNS SHALL be installed when (Clause 4.11.6.1):
(a) Wildlife FREQUENTLY ENCROACHES onto the road or roadside in LOCALISED AREAS when there is a SIGNIFICANT TRAFFIC VOLUME, OR
(b) Where DRIVERS ARE UNLIKELY TO EXPECT wildlife

RESTRICTIONS:
- Wildlife warning signs SHOULD NOT be provided for MIGRATORY animals
- Signs SHALL be CONTINUALLY ASSESSED for need and relevance
- Signs SHALL be REMOVED IF NO LONGER RELEVANT

SPACING FOR REPEAT SIGNS:
- Rural areas: may be repeated (if warranted) at intervals of NOT LESS THAN 5 km
- Urban areas: repeated as appropriate

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Construction in wildlife-sensitive area without wildlife warning signs where wildlife frequently crosses the road = non-compliance
- Wildlife warning signs installed for migratory species = non-compliant use
- Expired or irrelevant wildlife warning signs not removed after conditions change = non-compliance

CHUNK 33: Wildlife Conservation Area (WCA) Signing — Eligible Locations
Section: Clause 4.11.6.4
Topic: Conditions for installing Wildlife Conservation Area (WCA) signs; eligible location types

SIGN: CONSERVATION AREA FOR WILDLIFE (W5-Q19)
- Should include the ANIMAL SYMBOLIC WARNING SIGN of the most prevalent animal in the area
- SHALL ONLY be installed where the WARRANTS for the included warning sign (Clause 4.11.6) are MET (Clause 4.11.6.4)

ELIGIBLE LOCATIONS — WCA signs SHALL ONLY be installed within:
(a) Protected areas under the Nature Conservation Act 1992 where vehicle/animal collisions are deemed likely: National parks, Conservation parks, Resources reserves, Nature refuges, Coordinated conservation areas, Wilderness areas, World Heritage management areas, International agreement areas
(b) Land within state forests, timber reserves or reserves for environmental purposes (Land Act 1994)
(c) World Heritage Areas under the World Heritage Convention
(d) Areas supporting a critically endangered or endangered ecological community (EPBC Act list)
(e) Declared RAMSAR wetlands (EPBC Act)
(f) Areas of high nature conservation value (Vegetation Management Act 1999)
(g) Areas (other than state-controlled land) identified in a local government planning scheme as having special environmental significance for native wildlife

PLACEMENT:
- WCA signs should be erected AS CLOSE AS POSSIBLE TO THE BOUNDARY of the conservation area
- SHALL be placed so as not to COMPETE WITH other traffic control devices or POSE A SAFETY HAZARD to road users
- GREEN RRPMs SHOULD be used in conjunction with WCA signs (Clause 4.11.6.6)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- WCA sign installed outside eligible location types = non-compliant use
- WCA sign installed without underlying wildlife warning warrant being met = non-compliance
- WCA sign location competing with other traffic control devices creating legibility issues = non-compliance

CHUNK 35: Green RRPMs — Wildlife Conservation Areas Spacing and Installation
Section: Clause 4.11.6.6
Topic: Green RRPM requirements for WCA sections; installation rules and spacing

GREEN RRPMs FOR WILDLIFE CONSERVATION AREAS:
MAY be used at locations with WCA signs (Clause 4.11.6.4) to highlight areas of greater wildlife significance or where vehicle/animal collisions are significantly overrepresented (Clause 4.11.6.6).
SHALL NOT be installed with wildlife INFORMATION signs.

WHERE GREEN RRPMs ARE USED FOR WCA SIGNING, THEY SHALL:
(a) Be installed OVER THE LENGTH of the WCA
(b) Be REMOVED where no longer required
(c) Be BI-DIRECTIONAL and installed ADJACENT TO THE ROAD EDGE LINE on BOTH SIDES of the road
(d) Be installed IN ADDITION TO standard RRPM requirements (Section 5.9)
(e) NOT be installed on MOTORWAYS
(f) NOT be installed on roads or in locations where they may POSE A HAZARD to other road users
(g) SPACING: MINIMUM 48 m, MAXIMUM 96 m — spacing (m) SHALL be UNIFORM over the length of the WCA area

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Green RRPMs installed on motorway = non-compliance
- Green RRPM spacing less than 48 m or greater than 96 m = non-compliance
- Green RRPMs installed on one side only (not bi-directional, both sides) = non-compliance
- Green RRPMs with non-uniform spacing over WCA length = non-compliance
- Green RRPMs remaining after WCA conditions are no longer applicable = non-compliance

CHUNK 36: Vehicle Activated Wildlife Warning Devices — Type 1 and Type 2
Section: Clause 4.11.6.7
Topic: Vehicle activated flashing lights and signs for wildlife warning; operational parameters

VEHICLE ACTIVATED DEVICES — MAY be used where (Clause 4.11.6.7):
- There is an INCREASED RISK of animal/vehicle collisions, OR
- Due to ELEVATED IMPACTS with animals

TYPE 1 — VEHICLE ACTIVATED FLASHING LIGHTS ("WIG WAG" arrangement):
- Must be installed with a RADAR DEVICE capable of detecting vehicle speeds
- Activates flashing of lights in a WIG WAG arrangement
- MUST be used with HIGH-IMPACT WILDLIFE WARNING SIGNS (Clause 4.11.6.3)
- Flashing lights operate alternately when activated by an approaching vehicle EXCEEDING a nominated speed within prescribed operation times, OR during set times when animals are likely to be encountered
- Lights SHALL CONTINUE TO FLASH until the vehicle speed reduces below the nominated speed or the vehicle passes the sign
- SHOULD be used SELECTIVELY at critical locations where high-impact wildlife warning signs need enhancement to improve driver speed compliance

TYPE 2 — VEHICLE ACTIVATED SIGNS:
- MAY be used where presence of animals is expected AND there is POOR COMPLIANCE with posted speed
- Installed with a RADAR DEVICE preset with the POSTED SPEED LIMIT
- Displays a relevant animal symbol easily understood by drivers
- ACTIVATION:
  • Vehicle AT OR BELOW posted speed: activates, cycles between GREEN symbolic of animal and "THANK YOU" — cycle 1–2 seconds
  • Vehicle ABOVE posted speed: activates, cycles between RED symbolic of animal and "SLOW DOWN" — cycle 1–2 seconds

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Vehicle activated flashing lights not installed with high-impact wildlife warning signs = non-compliance
- Vehicle activated sign preset to incorrect speed (not the posted speed limit) = non-compliance
- Cycle speed other than 1–2 seconds for thank you/slow down messaging = non-compliance

- For additional guidance on WCLT in Queensland: refer to RPDM PART 3 (Clause 5.4.2.1)

WIDTH — QUEENSLAND DIFFERENCE (replaces AS 1742.2-2022):
- AS 1742.2-2022 allows width reduction to minimum 0.6 m where seal width is insufficient
- QUEENSLAND RULE: Where seal width is NOT sufficient to provide a separation width of 1 m, a REDESIGN OF THE TREATMENT is required in accordance with RPDM PART 3 (Clause 5.4.2.2)

- Width of WCLT over a narrow bridge structure = RESIDUAL WIDTH AVAILABLE while maintaining lane and shoulder widths (Clause 5.4.2.6)
- These widths MAY VARY at SUCCESSIVE STRUCTURES dependent on residual width available
- Queensland does NOT apply the 0.6 m minimum through a structure (see redesign requirement above)

SIGN REQUIREMENTS — QUEENSLAND ADDITION (Clause 5.4.2.7):
The following G9-Q14 series signs may be used to inform motorists about overtaking permissions:
- G9-Q14_1: OVERTAKING NOT PERMITTED (black on white, rectangular)
- G9-Q14_2: OVERTAKING NOT PERMITTED (black on white, smaller format)
- G9-Q14_3: OVERTAKING PERMITTED WHEN SAFE (black on white, rectangular)
- G9-Q14_4: OVERTAKING PERMITTED WHEN SAFE (black on white, smaller format)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- WCLT with separation width less than 1 m but no evidence of redesign per RPDM Part 3 = non-compliance
- WCLT through a narrow structure where width has been reduced to 0.6 m (following AS 1742.2-2022 fallback) rather than redesigned per RPDM Part 3 = non-compliance in Queensland
- Road with WCLT installed but no overtaking information signs (G9-Q14 series) where required = flag
