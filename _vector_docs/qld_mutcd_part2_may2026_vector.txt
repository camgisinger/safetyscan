CHUNK 1: Document Purpose, Hierarchy and How to Use
Section: About this document; How to use this document
Topic: Scope, relationship to Australian Standard AS 1742.2-2022, and Queensland precedence

THIS DOCUMENT:
- Specifies requirements for regulatory and warning signs, pavement markings and other devices for GENERAL USE on roads, including expressway-type roads
- Sets out how these are applied at intersections, interchanges, between intersections, and at specific situations including curves, structures, pavement width changes, climbing/overtaking lanes, steep grades and water crossings
- Is designed to be read and applied TOGETHER with AS 1742.2-2022 Manual of Uniform Traffic Control Devices Part 2
- HAS PRECEDENCE over AS 1742.2-2022 when applied in Queensland
- Uses the same section and clause numbering as AS 1742.2-2022

APPLICABILITY CATEGORIES used in this document:
- ACCEPTED — Australian Standard section or clause applies unchanged
- ACCEPTED, WITH AMENDMENTS — part or all accepted, but with Queensland additions, deletions or differences
- NEW — no equivalent in the Australian Standard (Queensland-only requirement)
- NOT ACCEPTED — Australian Standard clause does not apply in Queensland

REFERENCES RELEVANT TO CONSTRUCTION:
- Queensland Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (QGTTM) — key reference for construction workzone TTM
- Queensland Guide to Traffic Management (QGTM)
- Road Planning and Design Manual (RPDM) 2nd Edition — Parts 3 and 6 referenced for design details
- Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (Qld) — TORUM Act
- TMR Traffic Control (TC) signs database (officially approved non-standard signs)
- Main Roads Technical Specification MRTS45 Road Surface Delineation
- Main Roads Technical Specification MRTS110 Coloured Surface Treatments

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Traffic control devices on a Queensland construction site that do not match the Queensland MUTCD (not just AS 1742.2-2022) = possible non-compliance
- Non-standard signs used without TMR Traffic Control Signs Database authorisation = non-compliance
- Reference to outdated version of MUTCD on construction documentation = flag for review

CHUNK 2: Innovative Treatments — Approval Requirements
Section: Clause 1.4.15 (NEW)
Topic: Approval process for new or innovative traffic control devices and treatments on construction sites

INNOVATIVE TREATMENTS — encouraged where they provide improved safety, efficiency or value-for-money outcomes. May include:
(a) Innovative use of current devices
(b) Alternative device layouts using existing and/or improved devices
(c) New devices or practices

APPROVAL REQUIREMENT:
- New or improved devices, treatments or practices require APPROVAL BY TMR PRIOR to use or adoption
- For TRIALS of new or innovative traffic control devices, treatments or practices:
  → Submit a written submission to TrafficEngineering.Support@tmr.qld.gov.au
  → Submission must comply with Clause 1.13 of Queensland MUTCD Part 1

COMPLIANCE IMPLICATIONS FOR CONSTRUCTION:
- Using a traffic control device or treatment not in the Queensland MUTCD or AS 1742.2-2022 without TMR approval = non-compliance
- Site-specific innovation on construction workzone TTM layouts requires pre-approval

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Unusual or non-standard traffic control device on construction site = verify TMR approval exists
- Temporary traffic control arrangements that deviate from Queensland MUTCD standard layouts = flag for verification of prior approval

CHUNK 4: Intersection Direction Control — KEEP LEFT/RIGHT Signs (R2-3 Series)
Section: Clause 2.8.2 (Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Queensland-specific KEEP LEFT and KEEP RIGHT signs; restrictions on use of R2-3-Q02 series

STANDARD SIGN — KEEP LEFT / KEEP RIGHT (R2-3) (L or R):
- Standard R2-3 is the primary KEEP LEFT or KEEP RIGHT sign
- Used where traffic must keep to a specified side of a median or island

QUEENSLAND-SPECIFIC SIGN — R2-3-Q02 (KEEP LEFT — vertical format):
- RESTRICTION: The R2-3-Q02 series KEEP LEFT sign shall ONLY be used where the width of a median or island is INSUFFICIENT to provide adequate clearance to the standard KEEP LEFT (R2-3) sign
- This is a Queensland TC-series sign in vertical format to suit narrow medians/islands

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- R2-3-Q02 series vertical KEEP LEFT sign installed on wide median where a standard R2-3 could fit = non-compliant use
- KEEP LEFT sign missing entirely at divided road or roundabout entry = flag for review

CHUNK 31: Wildlife Warning Signs — Installation Requirements (Clause 4.11.6.1)
Section: Clause 4.11.6.1 (NEW — Queensland only)
Topic: When wildlife warning signs must be installed on Queensland roads

WILDLIFE WARNING SIGNS SHALL be installed when:
(a) Wildlife FREQUENTLY ENCROACHES onto the road or roadside in LOCALISED AREAS when there is a SIGNIFICANT TRAFFIC VOLUME, OR
(b) Where DRIVERS ARE UNLIKELY TO EXPECT wildlife

IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS:
- Wildlife warning and information signs are NOT specifically intended to perform an animal preservation role
- Research indicates they are NOT particularly effective for animal preservation
- If animal preservation is critical: other traffic management AND animal management treatments will be required; signs alone are not adequate
- Wildlife warning signs SHOULD NOT be provided for MIGRATORY animals
- Signs SHALL be CONTINUALLY ASSESSED for need and relevance
- Signs SHALL be REMOVED IF NO LONGER RELEVANT

SPACING FOR REPEAT SIGNS:
- Rural areas: may be repeated (if warranted) at intervals of NOT LESS THAN 5 km
- Urban areas: repeated as appropriate
- May also be appropriate to sign short, individual lengths of road where the risk of animal/vehicle collision is greatest

RELEVANCE TO CONSTRUCTION:
- Construction in areas where wildlife movements are likely disturbed or increased (e.g. near national parks, wildlife corridors) may require wildlife warning signing
- Vegetation clearing may increase wildlife/road interactions — assess need for wildlife warning signs

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Construction in wildlife-sensitive area without wildlife warning signs where wildlife frequently crosses the road = non-compliance
- Wildlife warning signs installed for migratory species = non-compliant use
- Expired or irrelevant wildlife warning signs not removed after conditions change = non-compliance

CHUNK 37: Vehicle Activated Wildlife Warning Devices (Clause 4.11.6.7)
Section: Clause 4.11.6.7 (NEW — Queensland only)
Topic: Vehicle activated flashing lights and signs for wildlife warning; operational parameters

VEHICLE ACTIVATED DEVICES — MAY be used where:
- There is an INCREASED RISK of animal/vehicle collisions, OR
- Due to ELEVATED IMPACTS with animals

Two types:

TYPE 1 — VEHICLE ACTIVATED FLASHING LIGHTS ("WIG WAG" arrangement):
- Must be installed with a RADAR DEVICE capable of detecting vehicle speeds
- Activates flashing of lights in a WIG WAG arrangement
- MUST be used with HIGH-IMPACT WILDLIFE WARNING SIGNS (Clause 4.11.6.3)

- Lights SHALL CONTINUE TO FLASH until the vehicle speed reduces below the nominated speed or the vehicle passes the sign
- SHOULD be used SELECTIVELY at critical locations where high-impact wildlife warning signs need enhancement to improve driver speed compliance

TYPE 2 — VEHICLE ACTIVATED SIGNS:
- MAY be used where presence of animals is expected AND there is POOR COMPLIANCE with posted speed
- Installed with a RADAR DEVICE preset with the POSTED SPEED LIMIT
- Displays a relevant animal symbol easily understood by drivers
- ACTIVATION:
  • Vehicle AT OR BELOW posted speed: activates, cycles between GREEN symbolic of animal and "THANK YOU" — cycle 1–2 seconds
  • Vehicle ABOVE posted speed: activates, cycles between RED symbolic of animal and "SLOW DOWN" — cycle 1–2 seconds

RELEVANCE TO CONSTRUCTION:
- Construction zones near wildlife hotspots where speed compliance is poor may benefit from vehicle activated devices
- Must be used with high-impact wildlife warning signs (Type 1)
- Radar device must be preset to posted speed limit (Type 2)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Vehicle activated flashing lights not installed with high-impact wildlife warning signs = non-compliance
- Vehicle activated sign preset to incorrect speed (not the posted speed limit) = non-compliance
- Cycle speed other than 1–2 seconds for thank you/slow down messaging = non-compliance

- Road construction creating or reinstating bicycle lanes less than 1.5 m wide must locate RRPMs outside the lane
- Rehabilitation projects reinstating RRPMs adjacent to cycle infrastructure must follow this rule

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- RRPMs placed within a bicycle lane less than 1.5 m wide = non-compliance
- RRPMs augmenting edge line of cycle path less than 1.5 m wide placed inside the cycle path = non-compliance
- Bicycle lane 1.5 m wide or more — standard RRPM placement applies

CHUNK 52: Exit Ramp Pavement Marking — Green RRPMs for Delineation (Clause 5.11.7)
Section: Clause 5.11.7 (NEW — Queensland only)
Topic: Queensland-standard exit ramp marking using green RRPMs

NORMAL EXIT RAMP MARKING — QUEENSLAND STANDARD (see Figure 5.11.7):
To DELINEATE the approach to and entrance of an OFF RAMP, GREEN UNI-DIRECTIONAL RRPMs are used in ASSOCIATION WITH THE EDGE LINE

SPACING AND ARRANGEMENT (from Figure 5.11.7):
- GREEN REFLECTORISED MARKERS at 6 m spacing for 150 m (approach section)
- CLUSTER OF GREEN REFLECTORISED MARKERS at 1.5 m spacing for 7.5 m (at ramp nose)
- GREEN REFLECTORISED MARKERS at 6 m spacing for 150 m (beyond ramp nose)
- Used in conjunction with a CONTINUITY LINE at the ramp nose area

KEY MEASUREMENTS:
- 6 m spacing — standard section (approach and beyond nose)
- 1.5 m spacing — cluster at nose (7.5 m length)
- 150 m — length of approach and exit RRPM runs

RELEVANCE TO CONSTRUCTION:
- Expressway construction or modification creating or reinstating exit ramps must install green RRPMs per this standard
- Replacement or temporary delineation during construction must maintain the green RRPM delineation
- All RRPMs are uni-directional green (not the standard bi-directional type used in other applications)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Exit ramp without green RRPMs (white or red RRPMs used instead) = non-compliance with Queensland standard
- Green RRPM spacing on exit ramp approach not at 6 m = non-compliance
- Cluster spacing at ramp nose not at 1.5 m over 7.5 m = non-compliance
- Green RRPMs at exit ramp bi-directional instead of uni-directional = non-compliance

CHUNK 53: Wildlife Awareness Signs — Queensland Rules (Appendix H)
Section: Appendix H (Normative — Accepted, with amendments)
Topic: Queensland-specific rules for wildlife awareness signs; species, sizing and contact information

SPECIES RULE:
- Wildlife awareness signs shall indicate ONLY ONE ANIMAL SPECIES that is likely to be present
- If DIFFERENT TYPES of animals encroach on the roadside within an area and signing is required for MULTIPLE species: only the animal MOST AT RISK FROM TRAFFIC STRIKES shall be installed

SIZING:
- SIZE A: 750 mm x 600 mm — where speed limit posted is ≤80 km/h
- SIZE B: 900 mm x 720 mm — where speed limit posted is >80 km/h

CONDITIONS FOR WILDLIFE AWARENESS SIGNS:
- SHALL NOT be provided for MIGRATORY animals
- SHOULD ONLY be provided where the PRESENCE OF ANIMALS ON THE ROAD IS EXPECTED
- SHALL be CONTINUALLY ASSESSED for need and relevance
- Signs that are NO LONGER APPLICABLE SHALL BE REMOVED
- Wildlife awareness signs SHOULD NOT be erected on the SAME POST as wildlife WARNING signs (except W8-Q18)
- Wildlife WARNING signs take PRECEDENCE over wildlife AWARENESS signs
- Signs should be located WHERE ANIMALS ARE LIKELY TO BE ENCOUNTERED — not too far in advance

CONTACT INFORMATION — 1300 ANIMAL SIGN (W8-Q18):
- MAY be used to supplement wildlife awareness signs to provide a single contact for reporting injured animals
- NO OTHER PHONE NUMBERS shall be included on the sign
- RSPCA MUST be contacted FIRST prior to installation of a sign bearing the 1300 ANIMAL number to verify adequate services operate in the area

AVAILABLE QUEENSLAND WILDLIFE AWARENESS SIGNS:
- W5-Q20 series: "CARE FOR OUR WILDLIFE" with various animal symbols (freshwater turtle, snake, bird, possum/glider, and others)
- W8-Q18: REPORT INJURED ANIMALS PHONE 1300 ANIMAL (supplementary plate)
- W8-Q19: NEXT [x] km (supplementary plate)
- W5-Q21: CARE FOR OUR ISLAND'S WILDLIFE REPORT INJURED ANIMALS PHONE 1300 ANIMAL (for island areas)
- W5-Q22: FAUNA CROSSING (with vehicle and animal symbols)

RELEVANCE TO CONSTRUCTION:
- Construction projects disturbing habitat or increasing wildlife/road interactions should consider wildlife awareness signs
- Any construction site adjacent to conservation areas must use correct sign sizes for the posted speed limit
- Only one species to be shown; select the one most at risk from traffic
- Verify RSPCA service coverage before installing 1300 ANIMAL signs

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Wildlife awareness sign displaying more than one animal species = non-compliance
- Size A sign (750x600mm) on road with speed limit >80 km/h = wrong size, non-compliance
- Size B sign (900x720mm) on road with speed limit ≤80 km/h = oversized, flag
- Wildlife awareness sign provided for migratory species = non-compliance
- Wildlife awareness sign on same post as wildlife warning sign (except W8-Q18) = non-compliance
- 1300 ANIMAL number on sign without prior RSPCA consultation/approval = non-compliance

CHUNK 55: General Compliance Summary — Queensland Construction Site Checklist Reference
Section: All sections
Topic: Consolidated quick-reference compliance summary for SafetyScan construction site AI assessments

MANDATORY "SHALL" REQUIREMENTS RELEVANT TO CONSTRUCTION:
1. R2-9-Q01 (RIGHT LANE MUST U TURN) SHALL be provided at exclusive U-turn lanes at signalised intersections
2. R6-33-Q01 (OVERALL WIDTH LIMIT) — if used, SHALL be on immediate approaches, and SHALL be accompanied by detour signs
3. W5-Q11 (BOOM BARRIER) — SHALL be installed in advance of boom barriers across roadways (not railway crossings)
4. R6-020-Q01 / R6-020-Q02 — SHALL be installed where lane filtering is to be prohibited
5. R6-28-Q01 (TRUCKS AND BUSES USE LEFT LANE) — SHALL be used to indicate mandatory lane requirement
6. W5-36-Q01 (EMERGENCY VEHICLES) — SHALL be used where two or more emergency services are co-located
7. G9-21-1-Q01 (ON SIDE ROAD) — SHALL be used with flood signs where floodway on side road is too close to main road
8. Green RRPMs: SHALL be installed at ALL wildlife threshold pavement marking installations
9. Green RRPMs in WCAs: SHALL be bi-directional, on both sides, minimum 48 m / maximum 96 m spacing, uniform spacing, over full WCA length
10. W5-Q19 (WCA signs) — SHALL ONLY be installed where wildlife warning warrants are met, and ONLY in eligible locations
11. Wildlife awareness signs — ONLY ONE ANIMAL SPECIES per sign; use size A (750x600mm) for ≤80 km/h, size B (900x720mm) for >80 km/h
12. Give way line at roundabout — SHALL be placed across the entering road; set-back SHALL NOT exceed 5 m
13. RRPMs adjacent to cycle lanes <1.5 m wide — SHALL NOT be placed within the lane; place on opposite side of line
14. WCLT width less than 1 m — redesign required per RPDM Part 3; no Queensland fallback to 0.6 m
15. Innovations / non-standard devices — MUST have TMR approval prior to use
16. Exit ramp green RRPMs — 6 m spacing approach/exit runs, 1.5 m spacing in nose cluster (7.5 m length)

KEY "SHOULD" DEFAULTS FOR CONSTRUCTION:
- Maintenance marker posts at items requiring regular maintenance (SD1358)
- Hazard marker posts at bridges and culverts (SD1851)
- W5-Q13 (QUEUED TRAFFIC) where stopping sight distance to end of queue may not be achieved
- Active devices with queued traffic signs
- 1300 ANIMAL signs — consult RSPCA first

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
- Non-compliance with Queensland MUTCD requirements may constitute a breach of the Transport Operations (Road Use Management) Act 1995 (TORUM Act)
- WHS obligations may also apply where traffic control failures expose workers or the public to risk
