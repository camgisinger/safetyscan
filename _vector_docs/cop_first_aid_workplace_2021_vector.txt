CHUNK 1: First Aid Kit Identification and Signage
Section: CoP s3.1; AS 1319
Topic: Mandatory kit marking and visual identification standards

MANDATORY MARKING:
- Each first aid kit MUST display a WHITE CROSS ON GREEN BACKGROUND prominently on the outside
- Red or blue background = non-compliant
- Kit must be immediately identifiable as first aid equipment

LOCATION SIGNAGE:
- First aid equipment locations must be clearly marked with directional signage
- Emergency floor plans must display first aid kit locations

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aid kit with white cross on red or blue background = non-compliant identification
- No visible first aid signage or directional arrows = non-compliant
- Kit locked or inaccessible to workers = non-compliant
- Emergency evacuation plan not showing first aid station location = gap

---

CHUNK 2: First Aid Kit Contents — Prohibited Items
Section: CoP s3.1; Appendix E
Topic: Medication prohibition and restricted contents

PROHIBITED CONTENTS:
- Paracetamol MUST NOT be included
- Aspirin MUST NOT be included
- Any analgesic medication MUST NOT be included

PERMITTED EXCEPTIONS (with appropriate training):
- Asthma-relieving inhaler and spacer
- Epinephrine auto-injector

MAINTENANCE EVIDENCE:
- Inventory list must be signed and dated after each check
- Checks required after each use OR at least once every 12 months

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Paracetamol or aspirin visible in general-access first aid kit = non-compliant content
- Expired sterile products or tampered seals = maintenance failure
- No inventory checklist or check date visible in kit = maintenance failure
- Opened and resealed saline bottle = non-compliant (must be discarded after opening)

---

CHUNK 3: Emergency Showers — Proximity and Access
Section: CoP s3.3; AS 4775
Topic: Immediate access requirements for chemical burn risks

MANDATORY PROVISION where risk of:
- Serious burns to large area of face or body (chemical/electrical burns)
- Burns LARGER THAN A 20 CENT PIECE
- Hazardous chemical exposure causing skin absorption or contamination

LOCATION:
- Must provide IMMEDIATE ACCESS from hazard area
- Must be within immediate reach of chemical handling area

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No emergency shower within immediate reach of chemical handling area = non-compliant
- Emergency shower blocked or obstructed = non-compliant
- Portable plastic shower hose attached to tap in low-risk area = acceptable portable facility
- Shower located in area exposed to extreme temperatures = non-compliant storage

---

CHUNK 4: Eye Wash Equipment and Sterile Solution
Section: CoP s3.3; AS 4775
Topic: Eye wash provision and sterile solution handling

MANDATORY PROVISION where risk of:
- Hazardous chemicals causing eye injuries
- Infectious substances causing eye injuries

STERILE SOLUTION RULE:
- Sterile saline or sterile water for irrigating eye wounds MUST BE DISCARDED AFTER OPENING
- Resealed bottles = non-compliant

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No eye wash station near hazardous chemical use area = non-compliant
- Opened but resealed saline bottle in first aid kit = non-compliant
- Eye wash equipment inaccessible or obstructed = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 5: AED Placement and Storage
Section: CoP s3.3
Topic: Automated External Defibrillator location requirements

RECOMMENDED where:
- Risk of electrocution exists
- Delay in ambulance services reaching workplace
- Large numbers of public present

STORAGE:
- Must not be exposed to extreme temperatures

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- AED located in area exposed to extreme temperatures = non-compliant storage
- AED cabinet locked without worker access = non-compliant
- No AED visible at high-risk electrical work site with delayed ambulance access = gap

---

CHUNK 6: First Aid Room — Size and Access Requirements
Section: CoP s3.4
Topic: Physical specifications for dedicated first aid rooms

THRESHOLDS:
- Low risk workplaces: 200 workers or more
- High risk workplaces: 100 workers or more

MANDATORY SPECIFICATIONS:
- Corridors and entrances MUST be wide enough to permit transport of persons on a stretcher or wheelchair
- Entrance MUST be clearly marked with first aid signage
- Must have easy access to sink with hot and cold water (if not in room)
- Must NOT be used as a thoroughfare or for general storage

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- High-risk site with 100+ workers but no dedicated first aid room = non-compliant with recommendations
- Corridors to first aid room cannot accommodate horizontal stretcher = design failure
- First aid room entrance without clear signage = non-compliant
- First aid room used as storage or thoroughfare = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 7: First Aid Room — Mandatory Equipment
Section: CoP s3.4; AS 4031
Topic: Required contents and equipment specifications

MANDATORY CONTENTS:
- Bowl or bucket with MINIMUM 2 LITRES CAPACITY
- Sharps container (rigid-walled, puncture-resistant)
- Waterproof examination couch

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No sharps container in first aid room = non-compliant
- Bowl or bucket visibly less than 2 litres capacity = non-compliant
- No waterproof examination couch = non-compliant
- Sharps container not rigid-walled or puncture-resistant = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 8: First Aider Ratios — Worker Count Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s42; CoP s3.5; Appendix C
Topic: Minimum first aider to worker ratios by risk level

MANDATORY RATIOS:
- Low risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 50 WORKERS
- High risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 25 WORKERS
- Remote high-risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 10 WORKERS

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Construction site (high risk) with 60 workers and only 1 first aider visible = non-compliant ratio (requires minimum 3)
- Site with 30+ workers and no trained first aider on site = non-compliant
- Remote site with 25 workers and only 1 first aider = non-compliant (requires minimum 3)

---

CHUNK 9: First Aider Identification and Training Currency
Section: CoP s3.5; Appendix C
Topic: Visual identification and qualification renewal requirements

TRAINING RENEWAL:
- CPR refresher: ANNUALLY
- First aid qualification renewal: EVERY THREE YEARS

IDENTIFICATION:
- First aiders should be visibly identifiable to workers
- High-visibility vests of particular colour = acceptable identification method

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aiders identified by high-visibility vests of particular colour = positive identification method
- No first aider identification visible on site = flag — workers cannot identify who to call
- First aider certificate showing "last trained" date more than 3 years ago = non-compliant refresher status

---

CHUNK 10: Infection Control — PPE and Sharps Disposal
Section: Appendix F; AS 4031
Topic: Mandatory precautions and waste handling

MANDATORY PRECAUTIONS:
- Disposable gloves MUST be worn when providing first aid
- Resuscitation masks MUST be used for CPR
- Hands must be washed with soap and water before and after administering first aid

SHARPS DISPOSAL:
- Sharps (needles, syringes) MUST be disposed of in RIGID-WALLED, PUNCTURE-RESISTANT SHARPS CONTAINER
- Must be disposed by the person who used them

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aider performing CPR without resuscitation mask = non-compliant infection control
- Sharps being placed in general plastic waste bag = life-safety breach
- Disposable gloves being reused between injured persons = infection control failure
- No sharps container available on site where sharps are used = non-compliant
- No disposable gloves in first aid kit = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: High Risk Workplace Classification — Visual Triggers
Section: Appendix A
Topic: Activities that trigger high-risk first aid ratios (1:25)

HIGH RISK CLASSIFICATION applies when workers:
- Use hazardous machinery (mobile plant, chainsaws, power presses)
- Carry out CONFINED SPACES work
- Carry out WELDING
- Carry out DEMOLITION
- Carry out ELECTRICAL WORK on energised equipment
- Carry out ABRASIVE BLASTING
- Are exposed to extreme heat or cold (foundries, prolonged outdoor work in Queensland summer)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Presence of welder, demolition activity, or abrasive blasting pot = site automatically HIGH RISK (1:25 ratio applies)
- Outdoor work in extreme summer temperatures = high risk classification
- Confined space entry work on site = high risk classification
- Electrical work on energised equipment = high risk classification
- Mobile plant, chainsaws or power presses in use = high risk classification
