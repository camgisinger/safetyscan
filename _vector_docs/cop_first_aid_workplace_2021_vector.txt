CHUNK 1: Primary Duty and Legal Requirements
Section: WHS Regulation s42; WHS Act s19, s27, s28
Topic: Mandatory provision of first aid and duty holder responsibilities

PRIMARY DUTY — a PCBU MUST ensure:
- Provision of first aid equipment
- Each worker at the workplace has access to the equipment
- Access to facilities for administering first aid
- An adequate number of workers are trained to administer first aid, OR workers have access to an adequate number of other people who have been trained to administer first aid

DETERMINING REQUIREMENTS — must have regard to all relevant matters including:
- The nature of the work being carried out at the workplace
- The nature of the hazards at the workplace
- The size and location of the workplace
- The number and composition of the workers and other persons at the workplace

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aid kits clearly accessible to night shift and overtime workers = positive compliance
- Workers on a shared site with a formal agreement to access another business's first aiders = coordinated duty (acceptable)
- Lack of clean water supplies for administering first aid = facility deficiency
- First aid kit locked or inaccessible = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: First Aid Kits — Design and Prohibitions
Section: COP s3.1; Appendix E
Topic: Mandatory kit standards and restricted contents

DESIGN:
- Each kit should be immediately identifiable with a WHITE CROSS ON GREEN BACKGROUND prominently displayed on the outside
- Should be made of material that will protect the contents from dust, moisture and contamination

MEDICATION PROHIBITION: Medication including analgesics like PARACETAMOL and ASPIRIN MUST NOT be included in first aid kits because of their potential to cause adverse health effects. Workers requiring these should carry their own for personal use.

EXCEPTIONS — workplaces may consider including:
- An asthma-relieving inhaler and a spacer
- An epinephrine auto-injector
Provided first aiders have appropriate training for these items.

MAINTENANCE:
- A person should be nominated to maintain the kit
- Regular checks should be carried out after each use OR at least once every 12 months
- An inventory list should be signed and dated after each check

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aid kit with a white cross on a red or blue background = non-compliant identification (must be green background)
- Paracetamol or aspirin found inside a general-access first aid kit = non-compliant content
- Expired sterile products or tampered seals in kit = maintenance failure
- No inventory checklist or check date visible in kit = maintenance failure
- Kit stored in a locked cabinet without worker access = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Specialised Equipment — AEDs, Emergency Showers, and Eye Wash
Section: COP s3.3; AS 4775
Topic: Requirements for high-risk injury equipment

AUTOMATED EXTERNAL DEFIBRILLATORS (AEDs):
Should be considered where:
- There is a risk of electrocution
- There is a delay in ambulance services reaching the workplace
- There are large numbers of members of the public

EMERGENCY SHOWERS — immediate access should be provided where there is a risk of:
- Serious burns to a large area of the face or body (including chemical/electrical burns or burns LARGER THAN A 20 CENT PIECE)
- Exposure to hazardous chemicals resulting in skin absorption or contamination

EYE WASH:
- Equipment should be provided where there is a risk of hazardous chemicals or infectious substances causing eye injuries
- Sterile saline solution or sterile water provided for irrigating eye wounds MUST BE DISCARDED AFTER OPENING — resealed bottles are non-compliant

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- AED located in an area exposed to extreme temperatures = non-compliant storage
- Opened but resealed saline bottle in a first aid kit = non-compliant (must be discarded after opening)
- No emergency shower within immediate reach of chemical handling area = non-compliant
- No eye wash station near hazardous chemical use area = non-compliant
- Portable plastic shower hose attached to tap in a low-risk area = acceptable portable deluge facility

CHUNK 4: First Aid Rooms — Recommendation Thresholds
Section: COP s3.4
Topic: When a dedicated first aid room is required

RECOMMENDED THRESHOLDS — a first aid room should be established for:
- LOW RISK workplaces with 200 WORKERS OR MORE
- HIGH RISK workplaces with 100 WORKERS OR MORE

ROOM SPECIFICATIONS:
- Sink: Must have easy access to a sink with hot and cold water (if not in the room)
- Entrance: Must be CLEARLY MARKED with first aid signage
- Size: Corridors and entrances must be wide enough to permit transport of persons supported on a stretcher or wheelchair
- Contents must include:
  - A bowl or bucket with a MINIMUM 2 LITRES CAPACITY
  - A sharps container
  - A waterproof examination couch
- Should be a safe distance from hazardous operations
- Must not be used as a thoroughfare or for general storage

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- High-risk site with 100+ workers but no dedicated first aid room = non-compliant with recommendations
- First aid room used as storage or thoroughfare = non-compliant
- Corridors to first aid room that cannot accommodate a horizontal stretcher = design failure
- First aid room entrance without clear signage = non-compliant
- No sharps container in first aid room = non-compliant

CHUNK 5: First Aiders — Ratios and Training Renewal
Section: WHS Regulation s42; COP s3.5; Appendix C
Topic: Worker-to-first-aider ratios and mandatory refreshers

RECOMMENDED RATIOS:
- Low risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 50 WORKERS
- High risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 25 WORKERS
- Remote high-risk workplaces: ONE FIRST AIDER FOR EVERY 10 WORKERS

TRAINING RENEWAL:
- CPR refresher training should be carried out ANNUALLY
- First aid qualifications should be renewed EVERY THREE YEARS

MINIMUM TRAINING LEVEL: First aiders should hold a nationally recognised Statement of Attainment for the unit of competency PROVIDE FIRST AID or equivalent.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Construction site (high risk) with 60 workers and only 1 first aider = non-compliant ratio (requires minimum 3)
- First aiders identified by high-visibility vests of a particular colour = positive identification method
- First aider certificate showing a "last trained" date more than 3 years ago = non-compliant refresher status
- No first aider identification visible on site = flag — workers cannot identify who to call
- Site with 30+ workers and no trained first aider on site = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Emergency Plans and Information Provision
Section: WHS Regulation s43; COP s3.6, s3.7
Topic: Mandatory emergency response and worker information

EMERGENCY PLAN MUST include:
- Medical treatment and assistance
- Effective response and evacuation procedures
- Notifying emergency services at the earliest opportunity

INFORMATION THAT MUST BE PROVIDED TO WORKERS:
- The LOCATION of first aid equipment and facilities
- The NAMES AND LOCATION of people trained to administer first aid
- The PROCEDURES to be followed when first aid is required

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Emergency floor plans displaying the location of first aid kits = positive compliance
- Directional arrows on first aid signs leading to equipment = compliant signage
- Workers unable to identify their nearest first aider during site induction = training failure
- No first aid location signs visible on site = non-compliant
- Emergency evacuation plan not displaying first aid station location = gap

CHUNK 7: Infection Control and Waste Disposal
Section: Appendix F; AS 4031
Topic: Standard precautions for first aiders

STANDARD PRECAUTIONS — first aiders should assume they could be exposed to infection:
- Wash hands with soap and water BEFORE AND AFTER administering first aid
- Wear disposable gloves when providing first aid
- Use resuscitation masks for CPR

SHARPS DISPOSAL: Sharps (needles, syringes) MUST be disposed of in a RIGID-WALLED, PUNCTURE-RESISTANT SHARPS CONTAINER by the person who used them.

SPILL CLEANING: Surfaces contaminated with blood or body substances should be wiped with paper towelling and cleaned with warm soapy water.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- First aider performing CPR without a resuscitation mask = non-compliant infection control
- Sharps being placed in a general plastic waste bag = life-safety breach
- Disposable gloves being reused between injured persons = infection control failure
- No sharps container available on site where sharps are used = non-compliant
- No disposable gloves in first aid kit = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: High Risk Workplace Definitions
Section: Appendix A
Topic: Identifying work that requires higher first aid ratios and equipment levels

HIGH RISK WORKPLACES include those where workers:
- Use hazardous machinery (e.g. mobile plant, chainsaws, power presses)
- Use hazardous substances (e.g. chemical manufacture, horticulture, petrol stations)
- Carry out hazardous forms of work including: CONFINED SPACES, WELDING, DEMOLITION, ELECTRICAL WORK, ABRASIVE BLASTING
- Are exposed to extreme heat or cold (e.g. foundries, prolonged outdoor work in Queensland summer)
- Are at risk of physical violence (e.g. cash handling, aggressive customers)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Presence of a welder, demolition activity, or abrasive blasting pot = site automatically classified as HIGH RISK for first aid ratios (1:25)
- Outdoor work in extreme summer temperatures = high risk classification applies
- Confined space entry work on site = high risk classification applies
- Electrical work on energised equipment = high risk classification applies
