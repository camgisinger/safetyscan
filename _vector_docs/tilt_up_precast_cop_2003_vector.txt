CHUNK 1: Panel Mass Marking and Suspension Angle
Section: CoP s14.1; AS 3850
Topic: Mandatory panel marking and suspension requirements during lifting

MANDATORY:
- Panel MASS must be CLEARLY MARKED on panel PRIOR TO LIFTING (CoP s14.1)
- Panels suspended with face as NEAR TO VERTICAL AS PRACTICABLE — usually 5–15 DEGREES OFF VERTICAL (CoP s14.1)
- BOTTOM EDGES must be HORIZONTAL — bottom corner must NOT be noticeably lower than other corner (CoP s14.1)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Panel mass not marked on panel = non-compliant
- Panel hanging at more than approximately 15 degrees from vertical = flag; check rigging
- Bottom edge of panel visibly not horizontal during lifting = flag; rigging incorrect or insert locations wrong

CHUNK 2: Lifting Insert Edge Distances and Depth
Section: CoP s3.1; AS 3850
Topic: Minimum edge distances, depth and installation requirements for cast-in lifting and bracing inserts

MANDATORY COMPLIANCE WITH MANUFACTURER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Minimum EDGE DISTANCES from panel edge (CoP s3.1)
- Minimum DEPTH of insert (CoP s3.1)
- Minimum CONCRETE STRENGTH (CoP s3.1)
- Additional REINFORCEMENT requirements — some inserts require tying to reinforcement steel (CoP s3.1)

FACTOR OF SAFETY:
- MINIMUM factor of safety NOT LESS THAN 2.5 against failure (AS 3850; CoP s3.1)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Inserts installed closer to panel edge than manufacturer specifications allow = non-compliant; failure risk
- Inserts not tied to reinforcement steel where manufacturer specifies this is required = non-compliant
- Number of lifting inserts appears insufficient for panel size = flag; request engineering certification
- No brand/type markings visible on inserts and no documentation = flag

---

CHUNK 3: Strongback Installation — Hard Against Panel
Section: CoP s3; AS 3850
Topic: Strongback bolting requirements for large panels or panels with cut-outs

MANDATORY WHERE SPECIFIED BY ENGINEER:
- Strongbacks MUST be bolted HARD UP AGAINST the panel (CoP s3)
- If not hard against panel: strongbacks may be unable to adequately resist bending moment during lifting, causing panel damage (CoP s3)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Strongbacks not tight against panel surface (visible gap) = non-compliant; risk of panel failure
- Large panels or panels with cut-outs being lifted without strongbacks where specified = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 4: Concrete Strength Before Lifting — 25 MPa Minimum
Section: CoP s3.2; AS 3850; AS 1379
Topic: Minimum concrete strength for lifting panels and testing requirements

MINIMUM CONCRETE STRENGTH FOR LIFTING:
- MINIMUM 25 MPa for most proprietary lifting inserts (CoP s3.2)
- Some inserts MAY require HIGHER strength than 25 MPa — refer to insert manufacturer (CoP s3.2)

STACKED PANELS:
- Panels CANNOT be lifted until LAST panel cast on top of stack has reached adequate strength (CoP s3.2)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Panels being lifted without concrete test results available on site = serious non-compliant
- Panels in stack being lifted before top panel has reached minimum strength = serious non-compliant
- Water being added to concrete mix at site = flag; risk of sub-specification concrete
- No sampling program in place = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 5: Brace Minimum Number and Fixings
Section: CoP s5; AS 3850
Topic: Minimum number of braces per panel and fixing requirements

MANDATORY:
- MINIMUM TWO BRACES must be secured to each panel UNLESS specified otherwise by engineer and shown on erection drawings (CoP s5)
- Where ONLY ONE BRACE is used (engineer must specify): minimum TWO FIXINGS at each end of the brace (CoP s5)

FACTOR OF SAFETY:
- Braces designed to provide FACTOR OF SAFETY OF TWO against failure (CoP s5)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Fewer than two braces on a panel without documented engineer approval = non-compliant
- Single brace without two fixings at each end = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Brace Marking — WLL and Manufacturer
Section: CoP s5; AS 3850
Topic: Permanent marking requirements for panel braces

PERMANENT MARKINGS — braces SHOULD be permanently marked with (CoP s5):
- Manufacturer's name or trademark
- Model type
- WORKING LOAD LIMIT (WLL) for MAXIMUM and MINIMUM extensions

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Brace without permanent marking (manufacturer, model, WLL) = non-compliant
- Brace feet showing components from a different manufacturer/model = non-compliant

CHUNK 7: Brace Anchor Bolt Slot Clearance — 2 mm Maximum
Section: CoP s5; AS 3850
Topic: Maximum slot clearance for anchor bolts in brace feet

MANDATORY:
- Slot width on brace foot SHOULD NOT EXCEED diameter of thread or sleeve on anchor bolt by more than 2 mm (unless otherwise stated by anchor supplier) (CoP s5)
- Bolt head must be large enough to apply adequate load to brace foot (CoP s5)
- Anchor bolt and washer combination on brace foot MUST have ADEQUATE BEARING CAPACITY (CoP s5)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Anchor bolt slot clearance exceeds 2 mm without anchor supplier guidance = non-compliant
- Washer visibly undersized for brace foot slot = flag

---

CHUNK 8: Brace Locking Pins — Retaining Devices Required
Section: CoP s5; AS 3850
Topic: Locking pin retaining device requirements

MANDATORY:
- Locking pins SHOULD be provided with RETAINING DEVICES to prevent unintentional dislodgement (CoP s5)
- WHERE PUBLIC ACCESS IS POSSIBLE: consider retaining devices requiring a TOOL to remove (CoP s5)
- Greater importance where locking pin can be reached by person standing on ground (CoP s5)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Locking pins without retaining devices = non-compliant
- Brace on public-accessible site with locking pins that can be removed without tools = flag

---

CHUNK 9: Brace Pre-Use Inspection and Integrity
Section: CoP s5; AS 3850
Topic: Pre-use inspection requirements and prohibited brace modifications

PRE-USE INSPECTION — braces SHOULD be inspected PRIOR TO USE to verify (CoP s5):
- No DAMAGE
- COMPLETE and FUNCTIONAL (extendable and foot length adjustable)

BRACE INTEGRITY:
- Braces SHOULD NOT have parts interchanged with other makes or models (e.g. different feet, smaller pins or bolts, different tube wall thickness) (CoP s5)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Braces showing visible damage (kinks, bends, corrosion) = serious non-compliant; replace before use
- Brace feet showing components from a different manufacturer/model = non-compliant

CHUNK 10: Wind Zones Queensland — Region A, B, C
Section: CoP s6; AS/NZS 1170.2
Topic: Queensland wind zones affecting brace design and number required

WIND ZONES — QUEENSLAND (AS/NZS 1170.2; CoP s6):
- REGION A (Normal): most of Queensland excluding coastal areas. Includes Toowoomba, Mt Isa
- REGION B (Intermediate): coastal areas SOUTH of Bundaberg; narrow band behind Region C. Includes Brisbane, Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast
- REGION C (Tropical Cyclones): ALL coastal areas NORTH OF and INCLUDING Bundaberg. Includes Townsville, Cairns, Mackay

CRITICAL RELATIONSHIP:
- Number of braces for a tilt-up panel in COASTAL TROPICAL AREA (Region C) will OFTEN BE GREATER than that required near Brisbane (Region B) (CoP s6)
- Number of braces required is NOT DIRECTLY PROPORTIONATE to basic wind speed (CoP s6)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Bracing design for Region C (cyclone zone) site using same brace count as Region A or B without engineer certification = flag; likely under-braced
- No wind zone or terrain category specified on erection drawings = non-compliant
- Region C or B coastal site with brace count based on region A parameters = serious risk flag

CHUNK 11: Deadman Dimensions and Soil Type
Section: CoP s7; AS/NZZ 1170.2
Topic: Deadman sizing requirements for different soil types

SOIL TYPE EFFECTS ON COHESION (CoP s7):
- STIFF CLAY: best cohesive properties; good resistance to pull-out
- GRAVEL: less cohesion than clay
- SAND: very LITTLE cohesion — deadman must be LARGER in sandy soils

CRITICAL RULE:
- Deadman adequacy based on BOTH MASS and COHESION — NOT a simple ratio of panel surface area (CoP s7)
- Cannot simply scale up deadman size linearly based on smaller panel calculation (CoP s7)
- Cohesion is reduced where ground is DISTURBED at top of hole (particularly in clay) (CoP s7)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Deadmen built in sandy soil without enlarged dimensions = flag; inadequate cohesion
- Ground disturbed at top of deadman hole in clay = flag; reduced cohesion

---

CHUNK 12: Deadman Reinforcement Bar — Bent vs Straight
Section: CoP s7; AS/NZS 1170.2
Topic: Cast-in reinforcement bar requirements for deadmen

CAST-IN REINFORCEMENT BAR (CoP s7):
- Usually a BENT bar at right angles — greatly assists bond strength between steel and concrete
- STRAIGHT bars (pushed into wet concrete): engineer MUST check design; correct steel section MUST be inserted to MINIMUM required depth
- If not inserted correctly: may PULL OUT of deadman or cause CONE FAILURE of concrete under load

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Straight reinforcement bar in deadman with no engineer check of design = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 13: Deadman Concrete Strength and Curing
Section: CoP s7; AS/NZS 1170.2
Topic: Minimum concrete strength for deadmen before anchor installation

CONCRETE STRENGTH (CoP s7):
- Deadmen typically cast using LOW STRENGTH CONCRETE (20–25 MPa)
- Must be LEFT for SUBSTANTIAL TIME before cast-in or drill-in anchors can be used (ensure anchors will not pull out)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No concrete test results confirming deadman has reached minimum strength = non-compliant
- Deadman construction records absent = flag

CHUNK 14: Deadman Documentation and Verification
Section: CoP s7; AS/NZS 1170.2
Topic: Required documentation for deadman design and wind load verification

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED ON SITE (CoP s7):
- Drawings showing: deadman DIMENSIONS, CONCRETE STRENGTH, STEEL CONTENT and LOCATION (if applicable), TERRAIN CATEGORY (for wind), and SOIL TYPE
- Documentation verifying each deadman CONSTRUCTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH DRAWINGS
- Documentation verifying CONCRETE HAS OBTAINED minimum required strength prior to brace/anchor installation
- Documentation verifying anchors comply with minimum EDGE DISTANCES specified by engineer or anchor manufacturer
- VERIFICATION that deadmen can resist wind loadings per AS/NZS 1170.2 — either:
  (a) Engineer's WRITTEN CERTIFICATION specific to that site, OR
  (b) TEST RESULTS with correlation to actual site conditions

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No engineer certification or test verification for wind load resistance on site = serious non-compliant
- Single deadman anchor supporting two braces when engineer specified one each = non-compliant

CHUNK 15: Post-Installed Anchors — Permitted Types for Bracing
Section: CoP s8.1; AS 3850
Topic: Permitted and prohibited anchor types for brace connections

PERMITTED ANCHOR TYPES for brace connections (CoP s8.1):
- UNDERCUT type anchors (do NOT rely on friction)
- EXPANSION anchors — HIGH-LOAD SLIP, TORQUE CONTROLLED type: working load AT LEAST 60% of first slip load
- COIL BOLTS — correct installation CRITICAL per manufacturer's specs

PROHIBITED ANCHOR TYPES for brace connections (CoP s8.1):
- DEFORMATION CONTROLLED EXPANSION anchors
- DROP-IN TYPE anchors
- LOW-LOAD SLIP DEFORMATION CONTROLLED anchors (often called "wedge anchors")
- SELF-DRILLING anchors
- CHEMICAL ANCHORS — unless EACH fixing individually proof tested to working load limit AND documentation available on site

MINIMUM CONCRETE STRENGTH: 20–25 MPa (CoP s8.1)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Drop-in anchors, wedge anchors (thin shield type), or self-drilling anchors used for brace connections = serious non-compliant; replace
- Chemical anchors without individual proof test documentation on site = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 16: Post-Installed Anchor Permissible Load — 0.65 Rule
Section: CoP s8.1; AS 3850
Topic: Maximum permissible load on post-installed anchors

PERMISSIBLE LOAD RULE (CoP s8.1):
- Permissible load on anchor must NOT EXCEED 0.65 of the FIRST SLIP LOAD
- First slip load = load causing 0.1 mm movement when installed per manufacturer's specifications
- If capacity quoted as first slip load: multiply by 0.65 (reduce by 35%) to get permissible load

PHYSICAL SIZE CONSTRAINT (CoP s8.1):
- Slot on most brace feet NOT WIDE ENOUGH for anchors exceeding 20–22 mm outside sleeve diameter
- LARGEST EXPANSION ANCHOR USABLE usually incorporates M12 bolt
- Permissible tensile load on high-load slip, torque controlled M12 anchor: GENERALLY NOT MORE THAN 12–14 kN

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Anchor sleeve diameter greater than 22 mm in standard brace foot slot = flag
- Brace capacity exceeds 20 kN but only one M12 expansion anchor per foot = flag; anchor limits system

CHUNK 17: Anchor Setting Torque — Documentation Required
Section: CoP s8.2; AS 3850
Topic: Torque verification and documentation requirements for all post-installed anchors

MANDATORY (CoP s8.2):
- Verification of setting torque for ALL post-installed anchors MUST BE DOCUMENTED and available on site
- Torque MUST be set using: appropriate TORQUE WRENCH or calibrated 'RATTLE GUN'
- WRITTEN RECORDS of setting torque for ALL drill-in anchors must be available on site

TYPICAL SETTING TORQUES (CoP s8.2):
- HIGH-LOAD SLIP, TORQUE CONTROLLED — M12 bolt: 60–80 Newton-metres (Nm)
- COIL BOLTS — 19 mm: 220–360 Nm (VERY HIGH — requires bigger lever arm than shifting spanner)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No torque records available for post-installed anchors = non-compliant
- Setting torque applied with shifting spanner only for coil bolts = flag; torque of 220–360 Nm requires proper equipment

CHUNK 18: Post-Installed Anchors for Lifting — Proof Load Required
Section: CoP s8.3; AS 3850
Topic: Mandatory proof loading requirements when post-installed anchors used for panel lifting

MANDATORY PROOF LOAD REQUIREMENT (CoP s8.3):
- WHERE post-installed anchors used for lifting panels (in tension or partial tension): EVERY ANCHOR MUST BE PROOF LOADED
- Proof load must be DIRECT PULL-OUT LOAD
- Proof load EQUIVALENT TO LOAD TO BE EXPERIENCED during lifting
- FACTOR OF SAFETY OF AT LEAST 2.5 must apply to anchor
- RECORDS of proof testing MUST BE DOCUMENTED

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Post-installed anchor being used for panel lifting without proof load documentation = serious non-compliant
- No proof test records available for anchors used in panel lifting = serious non-compliant
- Post-installed anchors for lifting without individual records for EVERY anchor = non-compliant

CHUNK 21: Crane Support Duration — Until All Braces Secured
Section: CoP s14.1; AS 3850
Topic: Mandatory crane support duration during panel erection

MANDATORY (CoP s14.1):
- CRANE SUPPORT to be MAINTAINED until ALL BRACES have been installed and effectively secured to panel and footings
- Hoist rope and lifting slings must NOT go slack until this is achieved
- BRACES — whenever possible SHOULD BE ATTACHED TO PANEL PRIOR TO LIFTING (reduces need for work at height and minimises time panel is crane-supported)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane slack before all braces are bolted and verified = serious non-compliant
- Braces attached after panel is placed and crane released = non-compliant; crane must remain supporting

CHUNK 24: Suicide Lifting — Prohibited
Section: CoP s14.2; AS 3850
Topic: Prohibition of suicide lifting configuration

SUICIDE LIFTING — PROHIBITED (CoP s14.2):
- Each tilt-up panel MUST be lifted so that, while suspended, it LEANS AWAY FROM THE CRANE
- "SUICIDE LIFTING" = panel tilts TOWARDS crane — if lifting system fails, panel falls against crane and causes crane to OVERTURN
- SUICIDE LIFTING MUST BE AVOIDED due to very high risk of serious injury

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Panel leaning towards crane during lift = serious non-compliant; suicide lifting; stop work immediately

---

CHUNK 25: Panel Suction — Excessive Force Prohibited
Section: CoP s14.2; AS 3850
Topic: Maximum force permitted to overcome panel suction from casting bed

MANDATORY (CoP s14.2):
- Force required to overcome panel suction should NOT BE EXCESSIVE
- If panel does NOT come free when crane indicator registers COMBINED WEIGHT of panel, rigging and other suspended dead loads: LIFTING MUST BE STOPPED
- Any wedging or jacking to assist release MUST BE APPROVED BY THE ENGINEER

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane indicator reading higher than panel + rigging + strongback mass before panel is free = stop; do not continue
- Bond breaker not applied to casting bed = risk of excessive lifting force overloading inserts

CHUNK 27: Pre-cast Panel Rotation — 75% Capacity Rule
Section: CoP s14.3; AS 3850
Topic: Crane capacity requirements for rotating pre-cast panels from horizontal to vertical

INDUSTRY RULE FOR CRANE CAPACITY (CoP s14.3):
- ONE CRANE: AUXILIARY WINCH capacity MINIMUM 75% of panel mass
- TWO CRANES: crane handling LESSER LOAD must also have MINIMUM 75% of panel mass capacity

CONDITIONS THAT MUST ALL BE COMPLIED WITH (CoP s14.3):
- Distance from AUXILIARY WINCH ROPE LIFTING POINT to END OF PANEL: NOT TO EXCEED 1/4 OF PANEL LENGTH
- INCLUDED ANGLE between main hoist rope and auxiliary hoist rope: NOT TO EXCEED 45 DEGREES
- Combined weight of AUXILIARY HOOK BLOCK and LIFTING GEAR: NOT TO EXCEED 200 KG
- ONE CRANE: main winch and auxiliary winch drives must be INDEPENDENT (separate clutches)
- NO CUT-OUTS in HALF OF PANEL CLOSEST TO MAIN WINCH ROPE LIFTING POINT

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Panel rotation with included angle between ropes exceeding 45 degrees = non-compliant
- Auxiliary winch block and gear exceeding 200 kg = non-compliant
- Cut-outs present in half of panel closest to main winch lifting point = flag; engineer review required
- One crane used for rotation without independent winch clutches = non-compliant

CHUNK 29: Exclusion Zones — Panel Erection
Section: CoP s13.1; AS 3850
Topic: Mandatory exclusion zone requirements during panel lifting and erection

MANDATORY EXCLUSION ZONE REQUIREMENTS (CoP s13.1):
- ALL PERSONS NOT INVOLVED IN ERECTION must be prevented from accessing erection zone
- Workers must NOT be located where they could be struck by panel in event of lifting gear failure or strong winds
- Exclusion zone MUST prevent unauthorised persons from gaining access
- Methods: PARA-WEBBING, SIGNAGE, FENCING depending on ease of access

EXCLUSION ZONE EXTENT (CoP s13.1):
- Must include area where persons cannot be struck by:
  - The CRANE FALLING OVER
  - The PANEL BEING LIFTED falling over
  - Another STRUCTURAL ELEMENT FAILING and falling as result of being struck by crane or panel

DURATION (CoP s13.1):
- Exclusion zone maintained DURING PANEL LIFTING until:
  - Braces have been SECURED
  - Lifting slings have been REMOVED

ROADS AND TRAFFIC (CoP s13.1):
- WHERE road or vehicular access way is in exclusion zone: TRAFFIC must be prevented from passing through while panels are being lifted and until panels are secured with braces

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No exclusion zone established around panel lifting area = serious non-compliant; stop work
- Exclusion zone not maintained after panel lifted — braces not yet secured and workers entering zone = non-compliant
- Traffic passing through area during panel lifting = non-compliant
- Workers without involvement in erection standing in panel fall zone = non-compliant
- Para-webbing or barriers present but gaps allowing access = flag

CHUNK 30: Lifting Over Workers — Back-Up System Required
Section: CoP s13.2; AS 3850
Topic: Mandatory conditions when lifting pre-cast elements over workers

MANDATORY CONDITIONS for lifting pre-cast elements over workers (CoP s13.2):
- EFFECTIVE BACK-UP LIFTING SYSTEM must be provided for element in event of primary system failure
- Back-up system DESIGNED and CERTIFIED by SUITABLY QUALIFIED ENGINEER
- PRIMARY and BACK-UP LIFTING SYSTEMS must NOT be removed until concrete element SECURED IN PLACE and no longer suspended by crane
- WIND LIMIT: Lifting must NOT continue when wind speed EXCEEDS 45 km/hr (12.5 m/s) under ANY circumstances

BACK-UP LIFTING SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS (CoP s13.2):
- Must withstand ADDITIONAL IMPACT LOADING as it takes up slack between primary and secondary systems
- Must EFFECTIVELY CONTAIN THE LOAD in event of primary system failure (prevent any part falling to ground)

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lifting pre-cast elements over workers without engineer-certified back-up lifting system = serious non-compliant; stop work
- Back-up system removed before element is secured in place = serious non-compliant
- Lifting in wind conditions above 45 km/hr = non-compliant; stop work

CHUNK 31: Brace Impact Hazard — Mobile Plant Controls
Section: CoP s15; AS 3850
Topic: Hazards of mobile plant near installed panel braces and mandatory control measures

WHY BRACE IMPACT IS EXTREMELY DANGEROUS (CoP s15):
- Braces designed to resist WIND LOADS only — NOT impact loads from moving plant
- When brace STRUCK and BENT ("kinked"): panel pulled towards brace side; braces exposed to DEAD-WEIGHT of panel
- KINKED brace has MUCH LOWER compressive strength than undamaged condition
- Consequences: RAPID and UNCONTROLLED COLLAPSE — HIGH RISK OF FATALITIES
- DOMINO EFFECT: falling panel likely to strike other panels

GENERAL RULE (CoP s15):
- Mobile plant SHOULD GENERALLY NOT be operated or travel CLOSE TO erected panels and braces UNLESS there is very good reason
- UNDER NO CIRCUMSTANCES shall mobile plant ACTUALLY CONTACT an installed brace

CONTROL MEASURES (CoP s15):
- SPOTTER: to signal plant operator
- HAZARD TAPE, PARA-WEBBING and/or SIGNAGE to make brace positions obvious
- ALTERNATIVE TRAVEL ROUTES: plant should NOT travel close to braces if alternative route exists
- NEVER DRIVE UNDERNEATH an installed brace

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Mobile plant operating within brace zone without any control measures = serious non-compliant; stop work
- Brace showing kink or deformation from previous contact = serious non-compliant; panels must be re-assessed by engineer before work continues
- Structural steel erection crane with no exclusion or control for counterweight/load near panel braces = serious non-compliant
- Plant travelling underneath installed braces = non-compliant
- Spotter present but no additional physical controls (no tape, barriers, or route segregation) = non-compliant as sole control

CHUNK 34: Scaffolding Free-Standing Height — 3× Base Width Maximum
Section: CoP s16.2; Scaffolding Code of Practice
Topic: Maximum free-standing height for mobile scaffolding

CHUNK 38: Overhead Powerlines — 3 Metre Exclusion Zone

MANDATORY (Electrical Safety Regulation 2013; CoP s17):

- HIGH VOLTAGE LINES: 6 METRES and 8 METRES EXCLUSION ZONES depending on voltage

CHUNK 40: Crane Ground Slope — 1 Degree Limit for Pick and Carry
Section: CoP s12.5; AS 1418.5
Topic: Maximum ground slope for pick and carry cranes without de-rating

GROUND SLOPE — PICK AND CARRY CRANES (CoP s12.5):
- WHERE ground slope EXCEEDS 1 DEGREE: WRITTEN EVIDENCE that crane can safely walk load for that gradient must be provided
- Evidence from CRANE MANUFACTURER or COMPETENT PERSON
- SIDE SLOPE of only 2–3 DEGREES can have DRASTIC EFFECT on crane stability
- EXAMPLE: capacity should be DE-RATED BY MORE THAN 70 PERCENT at 5-degree side angle (maximum boom extension, luff angle and steering articulation)
- SIDE GRADIENTS generally MORE CRITICAL than gradients in direction of travel

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane operating on visibly sloped ground (greater than 1 degree) without written de-rating documentation = non-compliant
- Ground slope of 2–3 degrees with no capacity de-rating = serious non-compliant; overturn risk

CHUNK 44: Crane Capacity for Tilt-Up — 3× Panel Mass Guide
Section: CoP s12.2; AS 1418.5
Topic: General crane capacity guideline for tilt-up panel lifting

CRANE CAPACITY REQUIREMENTS (CoP s12.2):
- Crane capacity MUST be ADEQUATE for LARGEST panel mass, RADIUS and BOOM LENGTH used
- Mobile cranes used on tilt-up usually have maximum rated capacity of AT LEAST THREE TIMES the mass of heaviest panel as GENERAL GUIDE (e.g. 20-tonne panel = 60-tonne crane capacity minimum as guide)

RADIUS ADJUSTMENT FOR FACE LIFTERS (CoP s12.2):
- When lifting with FACE LIFTERS: panel's centre of gravity at GREATER RADIUS than bottom edge of panel
- ADD AT LEAST 1.5 METRES to working radius to account for this (more for very tall panels)
- Crane MUST have adequate capacity at GREATER (adjusted) radius

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Crane operating radius not adjusted by at least 1.5 m for face lifting = flag; may exceed capacity
- Crane rated capacity less than three times heaviest panel mass without specific engineering justification = flag

CHUNK 45: Rubber Tyred Articulated Cranes — 8 Tonne Panel Limit
Section: CoP s12.3; AS 1418.5
Topic: Limitations of rubber tyred articulated cranes for tilt-up work

RUBBER TYRED ARTICULATED CRANES — GENERALLY NOT SUITED for tilt-up (CoP s12.3):
- Maximum capacity of largest model: 25 TONNES — generally only suitable for panels of 8 TONNES OR LESS
- MORE PRONE TO OVERTURNING than crawler cranes

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Rubber tyred articulated crane being used for panels exceeding 8 tonnes = serious flag; review capacity and stability

COUNTERWEIGHT HAZARD (CoP s12.4):

CHUNK 49: Licence Requirements — Riggers, Crane Operators, Doggers
Section: CoP s18; WHS Regulation
Topic: Mandatory licence classes for panel erection personnel

PANEL ERECTION CREW (CoP s18):
- Must hold INTERMEDIATE RIGGING (RI) or ADVANCED RIGGING (RA) licence classes, OR be TRAINEES
- TRAINEES: must be SUPERVISED; LOGBOOKS showing previous training must be kept

MOBILE CRANE OPERATOR LICENCE CLASSES (CoP s18):
- CN: Non-slewing mobile crane with capacity MORE THAN 3 TONNES
- C2: Slewing mobile crane with capacity 20 TONNES OR LESS
- C6: Slewing mobile crane with capacity 60 TONNES OR LESS
- C1: Slewing mobile crane with capacity 100 TONNES OR LESS

- Crane operator licence class insufficient for crane capacity or type (e.g. C2 operating 60-tonne crane) = serious non-compliant; stop work

- Person performing dogging without dogger or rigger licence = serious non-compliant

CHUNK 50: Variations from Design — Engineer Approval Required
Section: CoP s9.3; AS 3850
Topic: Prohibited variations without engineer written approval

ALL VARIATIONS from design MUST be checked by engineer and EITHER (CoP s9.3):
- CERTIFIED IN WRITING by engineer as acceptable (complying with AS 3850), OR
- ALTERED per engineer's written directions to comply with AS 3850

SPECIFIC EXAMPLES requiring engineer action (CoP s9.3):
- Two braces anchored to ONE DEADMAN when engineer specified separate deadmen
- Brace angle in VERTICAL PLANE not within range specified in erection drawings
- Brace angle in HORIZONTAL PLANE EXCEEDS 5 DEGREES from perpendicular
- Absence of KNEE and/or LATERAL BRACING when originally specified by engineer
- ONLY ONE BRACE on narrow panels when AS 3850 states minimum two
- Removal of braces from panels PRIOR to all permanent structural members being attached

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Two brace feet anchored to single deadman that appears standard-sized = flag; likely engineer variation not obtained
- Brace visibly off-perpendicular by more than 5 degrees horizontally = non-compliant; engineer review required
- Evidence of brace removal before structural connections are complete = serious non-compliant

CHUNK 51: Transport and Unloading — Side Slope 2 Degree Maximum

Topic: Maximum side slope for semi-trailer unloading

UNLOADING FROM TRUCK (CoP s11.2):

- SIDE SLOPE must not exceed 2 DEGREES (2 degrees usually just discernible to human eye)

- SEMI-TRAILER MUST NEVER BE MOVED after restraint system is released

CHUNK 52: Slinging Load at Height — Walkway or EWP Required
Section: CoP s11.3; AS 1418.10
Topic: Safe access methods for doggers slinging panels on A-frames

SLINGING LOAD AT HEIGHT (CoP s11.3):
- Pre-cast panels on A-frames typically have lifting inserts on TOP EDGE — dogger may need to work MORE THAN 2 METRES above ground
- USE OF LADDERS TO SLING LOAD may be UNACCEPTABLE — dogger needs both hands free and ladder difficult to secure

ACCEPTABLE ALTERNATIVES (CoP s11.3):
- WALKWAY along length of A-frame with barrier AT LEAST 900 mm HIGH on both sides; ladder access at rear of trailer
- PRE-ATTACHED SLINGS on panels reachable from ground
- BOOM TYPE EWP — basket can be moved close to sling connection points
- MOBILE SCAFFOLDING with edge protection and safe access
- LOADING BAY beside semi-trailer

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- A-frame delivery without accessible walkway or EWP for dogging at height = non-compliant
- Dogger using unsecured ladder with both hands occupied to attach slings = non-compliant

CHUNK 54: Lift Plan — Dimensioned Site Plan Required

LIFT PLAN — REQUIRED PRIOR TO LIFTING (CoP s12.1):
Must include DIMENSIONED SITE PLAN showing:
