CHUNK 1: Engineering Certification Thresholds — Height and Thickness Triggers
Section: CoP s2.2.1, Table 1, Table 2
Topic: Mandatory RPEQ certification requirements based on measurable dimensions

VERTICAL FORMWORK (walls/columns):
- 2.4m to 3.5m high: RPEQ certification required
- MORE THAN 3.5m high: RPEQ certification required
- Single-sided vertical formwork MORE THAN 2.4m: RPEQ certification required

SOFFIT FORMWORK:
- MORE THAN 3m high OR MORE THAN 250mm thick: RPEQ certification required
- Pre-pour sign-off MUST be by an ENGINEER (not just competent person)

STAIR AND LANDING FORMWORK:
- MORE THAN 3m high OR MORE THAN 200mm thick: RPEQ certification required

MULTISTOREY FORMWORK AND BACK-PROPPING:
- Engineer required for BOTH design AND pre-pour inspection

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Tall columns (>3.5m) being poured without engineer certification visible = non-compliant
- Soffit formwork visibly >3m high without engineer pre-pour sign-off = serious non-compliance
- Thick slab (>250mm) being poured without engineer documentation = non-compliant
- Multi-storey construction without visible reshoring plans = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: Working Platform Width — Minimum 450mm
Section: CoP s4.1, s4.1.2
Topic: Mandatory working surface dimensions for formwork erection

PLATFORM WIDTH:
- Minimum: 450mm WIDE (typically two planks)
- Single bearer or single plank = NOT ACCEPTABLE

FALSE DECK SPECIFICATIONS (when working at 2m or more height):
- Maximum gap width: 225mm
- Point load capacity: 2kN over 100 x 100mm area
- UDL capacity: 2kPa

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker standing on single plank or bearer = serious non-compliance
- Gaps between planks in false deck exceeding 225mm = non-compliant
- Working platform visibly less than 450mm wide = non-compliant

---

CHUNK 3: Base Plates, Sole Plates and U-Head Loading
Section: CoP s4.1.2
Topic: Prop foundation and bearing requirements

BASE PLATES:
- MUST be provided under props and standards on formwork frames unless prop has integral foot OR engineer documents it is unnecessary

SOLE PLATES (on natural ground):
- Should be used under props and standards unless engineer states otherwise
- MUST NOT BE ECCENTRICALLY PLACED
- Must be on adequate foundations

U-HEADS:
- Single bearers MUST BE PLACED CENTRALLY unless engineer states otherwise

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Base plates missing from under props/standards on suspended slab = non-compliant
- Props on natural ground without sole plates = non-compliant
- Sole plates visibly offset or eccentrically placed = non-compliant
- Bearer not centred in U-head = flag for engineer review

CHUNK 4: Leading Edge Protection — 4 Joists at 450mm Centres
Section: CoP s4.1.5, s4.1.6
Topic: Physical fall protection at deck edges

LEADING EDGE REQUIREMENTS (when false deck is OVER 2m below):
- Minimum: FOUR JOISTS at 450mm CENTRES (totalling 1.8m) located next to worker

UNATTENDED LEADING EDGES must have:
- Perimeter edge protection, OR
- Barricade 1.8m BACK FROM EDGE with "Keep Out" signs

HARNESS USE:
- Travel restraint or fall arrest harness systems NOT RECOMMENDED for formwork erection/dismantling due to inadequate free fall distance

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers laying ply with fewer than 4 joists extended in front = high-risk non-compliance
- Open deck edge without handrails or 1.8m set-back barricade = non-compliant
- Unattended edge without physical protection or barricade = serious non-compliance
- Worker wearing harness as only fall protection during formwork erection = non-recommended practice

CHUNK 5: Penetration Covers — Mechanical Fixing and Mesh Size
Section: CoP s4.1.8
Topic: Hole cover specifications and fixing requirements

HORIZONTAL PENETRATION COVERS:
- MUST be MECHANICALLY FIXED to floor
- MUST REQUIRE A TOOL TO REMOVE (e.g. insert anchor)
- Cannot simply be laid over holes
- Must be painted bright colour
- Must be marked "DANGER HOLE UNDER"

VERTICAL PENETRATION MESH (e.g. lift shafts):
- MUST be FULLY ENCLOSED WITH MESH
- Maximum mesh size: 50 x 50mm

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ply cover over hole not mechanically fixed (can be slid aside without tool) = life-safety hazard
- Cover not painted bright colour or missing "Danger Hole Under" marking = non-compliant
- Unsecured ply sheets sitting over penetrations = serious non-compliance
- Lift shaft mesh larger than 50 x 50mm = non-compliant
- Penetration exposed during stripping without protection = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Pre-Pour Inspection Sign-Off Requirements
Section: CoP s4.2, Table 2
Topic: Mandatory verification before concrete placement

PRE-POUR INSPECTION MANDATORY — sign-off required before pour commences.

SIGN-OFF AUTHORITY:
- Soffit formwork >3m high or >250mm thick: MUST be signed by ENGINEER
- Simpler formwork: may be signed by COMPETENT PERSON

INSPECTION MUST VERIFY:
- Correct spacing of frames, props, timbers (as per design)
- Correct joist and bearer sizes
- Acceptable jack extensions
- Quality of materials
- Back-propping correct
- Adequate bracing
- Base plates and sole plates correctly installed
- U-heads correctly loaded

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Pour commencing without signed engineer or competent person sign-off = regulatory breach
- No pre-pour inspection checklist visible on site = non-compliant
- Jack extensions visibly excessive or near maximum = flag for engineer review
- Formwork props visibly out of plumb or leaning = structural risk

CHUNK 7: Stripping Zone Cordoning and Drop Stripping Prohibition
Section: CoP s4.3, s4.3.4
Topic: Formwork removal safety controls

DROP STRIPPING PROHIBITED:
- All supports removed allowing ply to drop = MUST NOT BE USED
- Ply must be PROGRESSIVELY REMOVED as support system gradually lowered

STRIPPING ZONE REQUIREMENTS:
- Area MUST be cordoned off
- Signs: "DANGER — FORMWORK STRIPPING IN PROGRESS"
- Other trades MUST NOT work on floor where soffit stripping is active without cordon and signage

STRIPPING CERTIFICATION:
- COMPETENT PERSON from principal contractor MUST provide WRITTEN CERTIFICATION before stripping
- Based on: engineer's specifications, concrete strength, elapsed time since pour

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Formply falling in uncontrolled manner = drop stripping = serious non-compliance
- Other trades working on floor where soffit stripping active without cordon/signage = non-compliant
- Stripping area not cordoned off with danger signs = regulatory breach
- No written stripping certification visible = non-compliant

CHUNK 8: Back-Propping Edge Distance — 2/3 Prop Length Rule
Section: CoP s4.3, s5.1
Topic: Reshoring requirements and edge prop anchoring

BACK-PROPPING REQUIREMENTS:
- Multi-storey formwork requires ENGINEER for BOTH design AND pre-pour inspection
- Back-propping plans and elevations MUST be AVAILABLE ON SITE

EDGE PROP ANCHORING:
- Exposed back props located CLOSER THAN 2/3 THE LENGTH OF THE PROP from exposed edge MUST be BOLTED TO CONCRETE

POST-TENSIONING:
- Back-propping must be checked AFTER post-tensioning

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Multi-storey construction without visible reshoring on lower floors = structural risk
- Back-propping plans not available on site = non-compliant
- Props near exposed edges not bolted to concrete = non-compliant
- Back-propping installed without engineer design documentation = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Lifting Box Chain Configuration and Formply Orientation
Section: CoP s4.4.1, s4.4.4
Topic: Crane lifting requirements for formwork materials

LIFTING BOXES:
- MUST be ENGINEER-DESIGNED AND CERTIFIED
- MUST HAVE FOUR CHAINS fitted — one in each corner
- Specifically-designed boxes MUST be used for small components (Z-bars, spigots)

FORMPLY LIFTING:
- MUST be lifted FLAT — NOT in vertical position

SLING INSPECTION:
- Lifting slings MUST be inspected EVERY 12 MONTHS
- Inspection tags must be current

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Lifting box without four chains (one per corner) = non-compliant
- Formply loads lifted in vertical position = safety breach
- Small components lifted in bin with open mesh larger than components = falling object risk
- Missing or expired inspection tags on lifting slings = non-compliant
- Materials stacked on formwork deck beyond engineered load limits = structural risk

CHUNK 10: Ladder Extension and One-Hand Operation Rule
Section: CoP s6.1
Topic: Ladder access requirements for formwork

LADDER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Person's feet MUST NOT be HIGHER THAN 900mm FROM TOP of ladder
- Ladder MUST EXTEND AT LEAST 1m ABOVE accessed surface
- Materials MUST NEVER BE CARRIED while using ladder
- Equipment used on ladder MUST be operable with ONE HAND

PRIMARY ACCESS:
- Stair tread systems are PRIMARY access method — ladders are secondary

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using both hands to operate equipment while on ladder = serious breach
- Ladder not extending 1m above landing = non-compliant
- Worker carrying materials while climbing ladder = non-compliant
- Worker's feet higher than 900mm from top of ladder = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: Jump Form Platform Gap — Maximum 100mm
Section: CoP s7.2.1, s7.3
Topic: Slip form and jump form access requirements

PLATFORM GAP:
- Gap between trailing platforms and building MUST NOT EXCEED 100mm

ACCESS AND EGRESS:
- TWO FORMS of access and egress must be maintained AT ALL TIMES
- Trailing access stair system should be PRIMARY means over ladder

TRAPDOORS:
- MUST be constructed from SINGLE PIECE OF MATERIAL
- Should be marked "DANGER HOLE UNDER"
- MUST BE CLOSED except when actively accessed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gap between jump form platform and building wall wider than 100mm = fall hazard
- Single ladder as only exit from jump form cell = emergency egress failure
- Trapdoor left open when not being accessed = non-compliant
- Trapdoor made from multiple pieces of material = non-compliant
