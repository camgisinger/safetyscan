CHUNK 1: Definitions and Primary Duties
Section: s1.1, s1.2; WHS Act s19, s22
Topic: Legal definitions and core safety obligations

FORMWORK means the surface used to contain and shape wet concrete until it is self-supporting, including the forms, frames, and bracing providing stability.

PRIMARY DUTIES:
- PCBU must ELIMINATE risks arising from formwork activity, or if not reasonably practicable, MINIMISE the risks so far as is reasonably practicable
- Designers must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, that plant or structure is designed to be without risks to the health and safety of persons who construct the structure
- Workers must take reasonable care for their own health and safety and must comply with any reasonable instructions

NOTE: Formwork erection and dismantling is HIGH RISK CONSTRUCTION WORK under the WHS Regulation (risk of fall >2m) and requires a SWMS.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers assembling frames or bracing = formwork activity identified
- Wet concrete being poured into temporary structures = active formwork
- Worker performing high-risk construction work without a site-specific induction = non-compliance
- No SWMS visible on site for formwork erection/dismantling = non-compliant

CHUNK 2: Engineering Design and Certification
Section: s2.2.1, Table 1, Table 2
Topic: Mandatory thresholds for Registered Professional Engineer Queensland (RPEQ) involvement

RPEQ CERTIFICATION REQUIRED FOR:
- Vertical formwork (walls/columns) 2.4 metres to 3.5 metres high
- Vertical formwork (walls/columns) MORE THAN 3.5 metres high
- Single-sided vertical formwork MORE THAN 2.4 metres
- Soffit formwork MORE THAN 3 metres high OR MORE THAN 250mm thick
- Stair and landing formwork MORE THAN 3 metres high OR MORE THAN 200mm thick
- Multistorey formwork and back-propping (reshoring) — engineer required for both design and pre-pour inspection

PRE-POUR INSPECTION SIGN-OFF:
- Sign-off by an engineer or competent person is MANDATORY following the final pre-pour inspection
- For soffit formwork >3m high or >250mm thick: sign-off must be by an ENGINEER
- For simpler formwork: sign-off may be by a COMPETENT PERSON

VARIATIONS: Any variation from the original design (e.g. thicker slab, fewer frames, changed loading) MUST be certified in writing by an engineer before proceeding.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Engineer's certification letter missing from site documentation = regulatory breach
- Tall columns (>3.5m) being poured without evidence of pre-pour engineer inspection = non-compliance
- Variations from original design not certified in writing by an engineer = serious safety risk
- No formwork drawings visible on site = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Erection and Working Platforms
Section: s4.1, s4.1.2
Topic: Mandatory dimensions and load ratings for work surfaces

PLATFORM WIDTH:
- A person MUST be provided with a working platform of AT LEAST 450mm WIDE (typically two planks)
- It is NOT ACCEPTABLE for a person to stand on a single bearer or a single plank

FALSE DECKS — required if a person must stand at heights of 2 METRES OR MORE to install timbers:
- Gap limit: No gap must EXCEED 225mm WIDTH
- Point load capacity: Must withstand 2kN distributed over an area of 100 x 100mm
- UDL capacity: Must withstand a uniformly distributed load of 2kPa

BASE PLATES AND SOLE PLATES:
- Base plates MUST be provided under props and standards on formwork frames unless the prop/standard has an integral foot or an engineer documents it is unnecessary
- Sole plates (sole boards) should be used under props and standards on NATURAL GROUND unless an engineer states otherwise
- Sole plates must be on adequate foundations and MUST NOT BE ECCENTRICALLY PLACED
- U-heads: Where only single bearers are placed in the U-head, the bearer MUST BE PLACED CENTRALLY unless an engineer states otherwise

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker standing on a single plank or bearer = serious non-compliance
- Gaps between planks in a false deck exceeding 225mm = safety breach
- Base plates missing from under props/standards on a suspended slab = engineering failure
- Props on natural ground without sole plates = non-compliant
- Eccentrically placed sole plates = non-compliant
- Bearer not centred in U-head = flag

CHUNK 4: Fall Protection and Leading Edges
Section: s4.1.5, s4.1.6
Topic: Physical controls for preventing falls from heights

LEADING EDGE PROTECTION:
- If a false deck is OVER 2 METRES BELOW, a minimum of FOUR JOISTS AT 450mm CENTRES (totalling 1.8 metres) must be located next to the worker
- If a leading edge is left unattended, it MUST be provided with PERIMETER EDGE PROTECTION or a BARRICADE 1.8 METRES BACK FROM THE EDGE with "Keep Out" signs

HARNESS RESTRICTION:
- Use of travel restraint or fall arrest harness systems is NOT RECOMMENDED and DISCOURAGED for formwork erection and dismantling due to inadequate free fall distance
- Primary fall protection method must be physical — platforms, edge protection, and barricades

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Workers laying ply with fewer than 4 joists extended in front of them = high-risk activity
- Open deck edge without handrails or a 1.8m set-back barricade = non-compliance
- Formwork only zone not clearly demarcated by signage and flagging = safety breach
- Worker wearing harness as the only fall protection during formwork erection = non-recommended approach — check for alternative physical controls

CHUNK 5: Penetrations
Section: s4.1.8
Topic: Requirements for covering and signing holes in floors

COVER SPECIFICATIONS:
- Fall protection cover MUST be able to withstand the impact of a fall
- MUST be SECURELY FIXED IN PLACE to prevent accidental removal
- Horizontal covers MUST be MECHANICALLY FIXED to the floor and REQUIRE THE USE OF A TOOL TO REMOVE (e.g. insert type anchor) — covers cannot simply be laid over holes

MESH REQUIREMENTS for vertical penetrations (e.g. lift doors):
- MUST be FULLY ENCLOSED WITH MESH as a minimum standard
- Maximum MESH SIZE: 50 x 50mm

COVER MARKING:
- Covers should be painted a bright colour
- Must be marked with the words "DANGER HOLE UNDER"

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Ply cover over a hole not painted a bright colour or missing the words "Danger Hole Under" = non-compliant
- Unsecured ply sheets sitting over penetrations (not mechanically fixed) = life-safety hazard
- Penetration exposed during stripping without prior protection installation = procedural failure
- Lift shaft opening enclosed with mesh larger than 50 x 50mm = non-compliant
- Penetration cover that can be slid aside without a tool = non-compliant

CHUNK 6: Pre-Pour Inspection
Section: s4.2, Table 2
Topic: Mandatory checks before concrete is poured

PRE-POUR INSPECTION IS MANDATORY — sign-off required before any pour commences.

INSPECTION MUST VERIFY:
- Correct SPACING OF FRAMES, PROPS, AND TIMBERS (as per design)
- Correct JOIST AND BEARER SIZES
- Acceptable JACK EXTENSIONS
- QUALITY OF MATERIALS being used
- BACK PROPPING (reshoring) is correct
- ADEQUATE BRACING to ensure stability
- BASE PLATES AND SOLE PLATES correctly installed
- U-heads correctly loaded

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED ON SITE:
- Formwork design drawings
- Engineer certification (where required)
- Pre-pour inspection checklist signed by engineer or competent person
- Concrete supplier documentation verifying concrete specification (available on request)
- Minimum stripping times and stripping procedures

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No pre-pour inspection checklist visible on site = non-compliant
- Pour commencing without signed engineer or competent person sign-off = regulatory breach
- Jack extensions visibly excessive or near maximum extension = flag for engineer review
- Formwork props visibly out of plumb or leaning = structural risk
- No concrete supplier documentation on site = flag

CHUNK 7: Stripping and Removal
Section: s4.3, s4.3.4
Topic: Managing hazards during formwork removal

STRIPPING CERTIFICATION: Prior to stripping, a COMPETENT PERSON from the principal contractor MUST PROVIDE WRITTEN CERTIFICATION that formwork can be removed. This must be based on:
- The engineer's specifications for the building
- The strength of the concrete mix
- The time period that has elapsed since the pour

NOTE: The Code does not provide universal MPa or hour thresholds — these are site-specific and must be determined by the project engineer for the specific structural design. Minimum stripping times must be documented in project documentation.

DROP STRIPPING: This method (where all supports are removed and ply is allowed to drop) MUST NOT BE USED. Ply must be PROGRESSIVELY REMOVED as the support system is gradually lowered.

STRIPPING ZONE SAFETY:
- Area must be cordoned off with "DANGER — FORMWORK STRIPPING IN PROGRESS" signs
- Other trades must not work on a floor where soffit stripping is active without cordon and signage

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Other trades working on a floor where soffit stripping is active without cordon/signage = non-compliance
- Formply falling in an uncontrolled manner = drop stripping identified = serious non-compliance
- Stripping area not cordoned off with danger signs = regulatory breach
- No written stripping certification visible on site = non-compliant
- Stripping commencing before minimum time period elapsed = structural risk

CHUNK 8: Reshoring (Back-Propping)
Section: s4.3, s5.1
Topic: Multi-storey formwork and back-propping requirements

RESHORING REQUIREMENTS:
- Multistorey formwork and back-propping requires an ENGINEER for both DESIGN and PRE-POUR INSPECTION
- Details of back-propping that may be required and MINIMUM TIME INTERVALS BETWEEN POURS must be included in project documentation
- Back-propping plans and elevations (including tying in) must be AVAILABLE FOR INSPECTION ON SITE
- A competent person must ensure back-propping is correct as part of the construction checklist

POST-TENSIONING: Special requirements include checking of back-propping AFTER POST-TENSIONING.

EDGE SAFETY: Exposed back props located CLOSER THAN 2/3 THE LENGTH OF THE PROP from an exposed edge must be BOLTED TO THE CONCRETE (specific checklist requirement for climbing screens).

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Multi-storey concrete construction without visible reshoring on lower floors = structural risk
- Back-propping plans not available on site = non-compliant
- Props near exposed edges not bolted to concrete = non-compliant
- Back-propping installed without engineer design documentation = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Crane and Load Handling
Section: s4.4.1, s4.4.4
Topic: Mechanical lifting and point loading safety

LOAD HANDLING:
- Materials MUST NOT be placed on the formwork deck if documentation prohibits those loadings
- Lifting gear boxes must be ENGINEER-DESIGNED AND CERTIFIED
- Lifting boxes MUST HAVE FOUR CHAINS fitted — one in each corner
- Specifically-designed boxes MUST BE USED for small components like Z-bars or spigots
- Formply loads MUST be lifted FLAT — not in a vertical position

INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Lifting slings must be inspected EVERY 12 MONTHS — inspection tags must be current

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Smaller formwork components (U-heads, base plates) lifted in a bin with open mesh larger than the components = risk of falling objects
- Missing or expired inspection tags on lifting slings = non-compliance
- Loads of formply being lifted in a vertical position = safety breach
- Lifting box without four chains = non-compliant
- Materials stacked on formwork deck beyond engineered load limits = structural risk

CHUNK 10: Vertical Forms, Access, and Wind Bracing
Section: s4.5, s6.1
Topic: Wind bracing and ladder safety requirements

WIND BRACING:
- Vertical elements MUST BE FULLY BRACED prior to and during stripping until the construction provides adequate support
- Bracing MUST BE VERIFIED AND SIGNED OFF BY AN ENGINEER

LADDER SAFETY:
- Stair tread systems are to be used as the PRIMARY FORM OF ACCESS — ladders are secondary
- If a ladder is used:
  - A person's feet should NOT be HIGHER THAN 900mm FROM THE TOP of the ladder
  - Ladders MUST EXTEND AT LEAST 1 METRE ABOVE the accessed surface
  - Materials MUST NEVER BE CARRIED while using a ladder
  - Equipment used on a ladder must be capable of being operated with ONE HAND

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Worker using both hands to remove a tie bar while standing on a ladder = serious breach
- Columns braced with only one anchor point or using nails instead of through-bolts = engineering failure
- Mobile work platform (scaffold) used with unlocked castors during a pour = non-compliance
- Ladder not extending 1 metre above the accessed landing = non-compliant
- Worker carrying materials while climbing a ladder = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: Slip Forms and Jump Forms
Section: s7.1, s7.2.1, s7.3
Topic: Specialised climbing formwork systems

ACCESS AND EGRESS:
- TWO FORMS OF ACCESS AND EGRESS to the form should be maintained AT ALL TIMES
- A trailing access stair system should be the PRIMARY MEANS over a ladder system
- The gap between trailing platforms and the building MUST NOT EXCEED 100mm

TRAPDOORS:
- Must be constructed from A SINGLE PIECE OF MATERIAL
- Should be marked with the words "DANGER HOLE UNDER"
- MUST BE CLOSED except when being actively accessed

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Gap between the jump form platform and the building wall wider than 100mm = fall hazard
- Single ladder being the only exit from a jump form cell = emergency egress failure
- Workers on a trailing platform being crane-lifted without a rescue procedure in place = non-compliance
- Trapdoor left open when not being accessed = non-compliant
- Trapdoor made from multiple pieces of material = non-compliant
