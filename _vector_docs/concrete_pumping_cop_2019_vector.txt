CHUNK 1: Licensing and Staffing — Boom Operators and Line Hands
Section: CoP s3.1.3, s4.3.2; WHS Regulation s316
Topic: Licence requirements and minimum staffing for concrete placing boom operations

MANDATORY:
- Operators of concrete placing booms MUST hold a High Risk Work Licence (concrete placing boom)
- All construction workers must hold a White Card

SATELLITE BOOM STAFFING:
- Boom operator and pump operator (at hopper) must be SEPARATE people — one person cannot monitor both positions
- Single operator managing both boom and hopper = non-compliant

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Single operator managing boom and hopper on satellite installation = non-compliant
- No spotter or line hand visible during active pour = flag

---

CHUNK 2: Registration Plate and Compliance Marking
Section: CoP s2.2; WHS Regulation s243
Topic: Plant design registration and identification requirements for concrete placing booms

MANDATORY:
- Concrete placing boom design must be registered with the regulator
- Registration number must appear on a compliance/identification plate fixed to the boom
- Manufacturer's plate must show: date of manufacture, serial number, max working pressure, max drop hose length, recommended delivery pipe size

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- No compliance plate or registration number visible on boom = flag — may be unregistered plant
- Boom modified or welded without evidence of re-registration = potential non-compliance
- Manufacturer's plate missing or illegible = non-compliant

CHUNK 3: Outrigger Proximity to Excavations — Exact Distances
Section: CoP s4.2.2, s4.2.2.1, s4.2.2.2
Topic: Minimum outrigger setback distances from excavations and trenches (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

COMPACT, NON-FRIABLE GROUND — 1:1 RULE:
Outrigger timbers/pads must be at least EQUAL TO THE DEPTH OF THE EXCAVATION horizontally from the nearest edge.
Example: 3m deep trench → outrigger timbers at least 3m from trench edge.

LOOSE OR BACKFILLED GROUND — 2:1 RULE:
Outrigger timbers/pads must be at least TWICE THE DEPTH OF THE EXCAVATION horizontally from the nearest edge.
Example: 3m deep trench in backfill → outrigger timbers at least 6m from trench edge.

Pump must NOT be positioned over or adjacent to:
- Previously disturbed or backfilled ground
- Excavations, trenches, holes, cellars, basements or pits
- Soft, wet or inadequately compacted ground

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Mobile boom pump adjacent to open excavation without visible distance checking = flag
- Outrigger pads close to trench edge in disturbed/backfilled ground = serious non-compliance risk
- Ground visibly soft, wet or muddy under or near outrigger pads = flag
- Outrigger pads sinking or tilting = immediate non-compliance

CHUNK 4: Outrigger Timber and Pad Specifications
Section: CoP s4.2.2.3, s4.2.2.4; Table 1
Topic: Minimum timber/pad dimensions and permissible ground pressures (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

TIMBER/PAD MINIMUM SPECIFICATIONS:
- Minimum width: 200mm
- Minimum thickness: 75mm
- Timbers must be laid with NO GAPS and wider than the outrigger foot

MAXIMUM PERMISSIBLE GROUND PRESSURES (t/m²):
- Hard rock: 200
- Shale/sandstone: 80
- Compacted gravel: 40
- Asphalt: 20
- Compacted sand: 20
- Stiff clay (dry): 20
- Soft clay (dry): 10
- Loose sand: 10
- Wet clay: <10

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Outrigger pads/timbers visibly undersized for visible ground type = flag
- Single small pad on soft or wet ground = non-compliant
- Timbers with visible gaps between them = non-compliant
- No pads used at all on soft ground = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 5: Short Legging — Partially Extended Outriggers
Section: CoP s4.2.2.5
Topic: Requirements when one or more outrigger legs is not fully extended

Short legging must be AVOIDED wherever practicable. If unavoidable:
- Manufacturer's manual must explicitly permit short legging for that unit
- Outriggers must be marked showing permissible extension distances
- A documented safe work procedure with diagram of permissible boom zone must be prepared
- If fitted, a slew limiting device must prevent boom swinging into the overturning risk zone

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Outriggers visibly not fully extended on one or more sides = short legging — check for documented SWP and manufacturer's authorisation
- Boom operating toward the short-legged side without slew limiter = serious non-compliance risk
- No slew limiting device visible on unit with short-legged outriggers = elevated risk flag

CHUNK 6: Working Near Overhead Powerlines — Exclusion Zones
Section: CoP s4.2.3; ES Regulation s68, s69
Topic: Minimum clearance distances for boom operations near powerlines (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MINIMUM EXCLUSION ZONE:
- AT LEAST 3 METRES from any overhead powerline for voltages UP TO 132kV
- Greater distances apply above 132kV
- Applies to ANY PART of the boom AND the drop hose — not just the truck body

Booms MUST NOT be operated OVER THE TOP of energised powerlines.
Pipes must be kept PARALLEL TO GROUND when setting up or unloading near powerlines.

Tiger tails/line markers DO NOT insulate powerlines — exclusion zone applies regardless.

SPOTTER REQUIRED when risk of boom entering exclusion zone — must remain in clear communication with operator.

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Any part of boom or drop hose within 3m of overhead powerline = serious non-compliance
- Boom operating over or across energised powerlines = serious non-compliance
- No spotter present when boom operating near powerlines = non-compliant
- No exclusion zone markers or signage near powerlines = flag

CHUNK 7: Hose Whip Controls and Line Hand Working Surface
Section: CoP s4.1.4
Topic: Hose whip risk controls including working surface and hose handling requirements (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY CONTROLS:
- NEVER use rubber delivery hose with metal fittings on free end
- Maximum 3–4 metres of 125mm diameter hose may hang from boom end
- Pump only with hopper grate in CLOSED POSITION
- Clean line with WATER, not air, where possible

LINE HAND WORKING SURFACE:
- Minimum width: 450mm
- Line hand MUST NOT stand on block walls or at unprotected edges

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Line hand standing on block wall or at unprotected edge = serious non-compliance
- Working surface for line hand visibly less than 450mm wide = non-compliant
- Worker stretching delivery hose to reach further = non-compliant
- Rubber hose with metal fittings on free end = non-compliant
- More than 3–4m of 125mm hose suspended from boom = flag

CHUNK 8: Pipeline Restraint, Pipe Clamps and Hopper Grate
Section: CoP s4.1.3
Topic: Anchor bracket spacing, pipe clamp requirements and hopper grate specifications (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

ANCHOR BRACKETS:
- Maximum spacing: 3 METRES APART (unless engineer specifies otherwise)
- Cranes, hoist towers, scaffolding or formwork MUST NOT be used to secure the line

PIPE CLAMPS:
- Must sustain maximum concrete pressure applied by pump
- Locking pins must be used and engaged
- Damaged or deformed clamps must be immediately replaced
- Quick release clamps without locking pin provision must NOT be used
- Clamps must be marked with manufacturer, pressure rating and size

HOPPER GRATE:
- Grate bar spacing MUST NOT EXCEED 75mm
- Distance from top of grate to any moving part must be AT LEAST 100mm
- Grates that open must have an interlock that de-activates paddles when lifted

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Anchor brackets at intervals greater than 3m with no engineer spec = non-compliant
- Pipeline secured to scaffolding or crane tower = non-compliant
- Pipe clamps without visible locking pins = non-compliant
- Hopper grate open or missing during operation = serious non-compliance
- Grate bar spacing exceeding 75mm = non-compliant

CHUNK 9: Concrete Delivery Trucks — Reversing and Hopper Safety
Section: CoP s4.3.1; WHS Regulation s214
Topic: Safe truck reversing procedures and spotter requirements at the pump hopper

CONCRETE TRUCKS MUST have:
- Flashing hazard lights activated when reversing
- Audible reversing devices

NO PERSON must stand BETWEEN a reversing concrete truck and the hopper.

When more than one truck approaches the hopper:
- A dedicated spotter or traffic controller must direct truck movements
- Spotter must remain in clear view of the truck driver — if not visible, truck must stop

SATELLITE BOOM DELIVERY AREAS must have demarcation barriers on all sides except the entry side. Signs must read "CONCRETE DELIVERY AREA — STAY OUT".

GANTRY REQUIREMENT:
- Boom MUST NOT be set up over site sheds (lunch rooms, amenities) without a gantry rated at LEAST 10kPa
- Boom MUST NOT be set up over worker access ways without a gantry rated at LEAST 10kPa

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Concrete truck reversing without audible alarm = non-compliant
- Person standing between reversing truck and hopper = serious non-compliance
- No spotter with multiple trucks manoeuvring = non-compliant
- No barriers or exclusion zone signage at satellite boom delivery area = non-compliant
- Boom set up directly over site shed or worker access path without rated gantry = serious non-compliance

CHUNK 10: Traffic Control for Roadside Concrete Pumping
Section: CoP s4.2.4; WHS Regulation s315M
Topic: Traffic control requirements for pump setups on or adjacent to public roads

MANDATORY:
- Permits must be obtained from the relevant authority before setting up on or adjacent to public roads
- All traffic control must comply with the MUTCD
- Workers in the exclusion zone must wear HIGH VISIBILITY VESTS
- Exclusion zone signage must require hi-vis vests

CONCRETE TRUCKS:
- Must have audible reversing alarms
- Must have flashing amber lights when reversing

LINE HAND STAFFING by hose diameter:
- 76.2mm (3 inch) or more: one line hand per 10 METRES of workable hose
- Less than 76.2mm: one line hand per 20 METRES of workable hose

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Pump on public road without visible traffic control = non-compliant
- Workers in exclusion zone without hi-vis vests = non-compliant
- Multiple trucks manoeuvring without dedicated spotter = non-compliant
- No exclusion zone signs at pump setup = non-compliant

CHUNK 11: PPE Requirements and Equipment on Pump Unit
Section: CoP s6.1, s6.2
Topic: Mandatory PPE and equipment that must be present on every concrete pump (KEY MEASUREMENTS)

MANDATORY PPE for all concrete pumping operations:
- Safety helmet (hard hat)
- Eye protection (AS/NZS 1336 compliant) — mandatory for line hand
- High visibility safety vest
- Rubber safety boots

MANDATORY EQUIPMENT on each pump unit:
- First aid kit (must include eye wash)
- Fire extinguishers
- Reflective traffic cones — minimum 450mm high
- Exclusion zone signage and high visibility vest requirement notices

VISUAL INDICATORS:
- Pump operator or line hand without hard hat = non-compliant
- Line hand without eye protection = non-compliant
- Workers in exclusion zone without hi-vis vests = non-compliant
- No first aid kit visible on pump unit = non-compliant
- No traffic cones at pump setup = non-compliant
- Traffic cones present but visibly less than 450mm high = non-compliant
